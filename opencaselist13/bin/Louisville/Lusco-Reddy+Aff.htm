Empire Aff

Tournament: Wake Forest | Round: 1 | Opponent: Michigan ST | Judge: Mikaela Maslin
From the deserts of the Middle East to the cities of the US, US empire has used fear to cast these areas of the globe and specific groups as evil in order to justify its expanding global domination both foreign and domestic.

Kumar 2012 (Deepa, Associate Professor of Media Studies and Middle Eastern Studies at the Rutgers University, “Islamophobia and the Politics of Empire)

The executive has expanded imperial powers throughout multiple sections of the government, ensuring Islamophobia’s existence. The current means of creating policies over war powers have been configured to serve the goals of the War on Terror, a process that has led to systematic violations of the rights of Muslims and Arab communities both home and abroad.

Kumar 2012 (Deepa, Associate Professor of Media Studies and Middle Eastern Studies at the Rutgers University, “Islamophobia and the Politics of Empire)

Actively fighting against the mechanisms that have created oppression both in government and debate will help to create a political climate where all viewpoints over policy and debate can be ensured to have a space to speak.

Kumar 2012 (Deepa, Associate Professor of Media Studies and Middle Eastern Studies at the Rutgers University, “Islamophobia and the Politics of Empire)

In order to ensure the restriction of the war powers, we must begin to remove the legislation that has given the president unrelenting power in his conquest on the War on Terror. 

Thus, the United States federal government should repeal the Authority for use of Military Force Act, which has given the president unlimited authority.

Grimmett 2006 (Richard F.,” Authorization for Use of Military Response to the 9/11 Attacks (P.L. 107-40): Legislative History”)

Militarism

Tournament: Cedanats | Round: 2 | Opponent: Rutgers-Newark Haughton-Stafford | Judge: Glass
Militarism Definition
Mirra 2008 (Carl, Adelphi University, “Countering Militarism through Peace Education”)

If we continue to allow the government to achieve being the dominant narrative over war in public schools, status quo war decisions by the United States will continue to exist and the endangerment of our world will be inevitable.
Harding and Kershner 2011 (Scott and Seth, School of Social Work University of Connecticut and Simmons College, “Just say No”: Organizing Against Militarism in Public Schools”) 

The use of discourse for the desire of obtaining power and the annihilation of competing interpretations is a clear representation of militarism being expressed in our overly competitive nature of debate.
Reid-Brinkley, 08 (Assistant Professor PhD and Debate Coach at University of Pittsburgh "THE HARSH REALITIES OF “ACTING BLACK”: HOW AFRICAN-AMERICAN POLICY DEBATERS NEGOTIATE REPRESENTATION THROUGH RACIAL PERFORMANCE AND STYLE" Pgs. 68-69)

Debate should be a space that seeks to reject militarism seen within the United States federal government as well as within academia. The ballot should be to endorse the team that provides the best form of resistance against the military structures of the United States government as well as debate.

Prefer our interpretation: The embedded militarization in government and within debate has resulted in the lack of introspection over its existence and vice versa becoming a root cause of the topic areas described in the resolution, expressing the necessity of its critique in order to begin restricting its functionality within these rounds.
Harding and Kershner 2011(Scott and Seth, School of Social Work University of Connecticut and Simmons College, “Just say No”: Organizing Against Militarism in Public Schools”)

Vote affirmative to provide the best form of education and necessary conflict resolutional skills over war powers within debate.
Clarke-Habibi 2005 (Sara, Associate Director and Coordinator of Curriculum Development International Education for Peace Instiute)

Thus our advocacy is for the members of this debate community to counter recruit in the struggle against militarism.
Harding and Kershner 2011 (Scott and Seth, School of Social Work University of Connecticut and Simmons College, “Just say No”: Organizing Against Militarism in Public Schools”)

It requires that members of this community such as the judge to endorse methods to produce an education necessary to break down militarism to end the desire for war by our government.
Allison 2007 (Aimee, Army of None: Strategies to Counter Military Recruitment, End War, and Build)

Voting affirmative endorses a political movement that seeks to promote to rejection of anti-blackness’ existence through a confrontation of militarism within debate allowing it to progressively change and develop new ideals.
Sexton 2006 (Jared, Professor at the University of California-Irvine, “Race, Nation, and Empire in a Blackened World”)
