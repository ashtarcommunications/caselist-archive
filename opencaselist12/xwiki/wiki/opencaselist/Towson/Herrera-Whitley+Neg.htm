The 1NC K was basically anti-politics. They argued that the aff's model of debate leads to never taking any action to affect real world change, and that instead we just feel good about the ballot itself, as though we feel like it did something in the real world (almost identical to our policymaking/spectatorship DA).

They argued instead that we should engage in grassroots institutions like the Leaders of a Beautiful Struggle, which is a Baltimore-based non-profit that tries to support minority rights as well as encourages debater participation in the Baltimore UDL.

Their role of the ballot was about changing the way we orient the knowledge produced by the community to help minorities and those groups affected by the aff's impacts. They said bottom up solved best, and talked about some empirical things the LBS had done, though they were still more theoretical than empirical.
