Afro-Pessimism Kritik

Tournament:  | Round:  | Opponent:  | Judge:
We begin this discussion the way it should end not with a question of whether or not the US should make any action regarding it’s domestic energy policy but a demand, an insatiable, unsatisfiable demand one that robs humanity of its corporeal integrity. An ensemble of questions that problematizes the ethicality of the world itself. A demand to repair the flesh of the black and the return the land to the native

Wilderson, Associate Professor of African-American Studies at UC Irvine, 2010

(Frank B., Ph.D. from UC Berkeley, “Red, White and Black: Cinema and the Structure of U.S. Antagonisms,” Pages 1-2. pdf, Written in 2010, Accessed 08-08-2012, AJH)

 

 

The current political discourse is centered on an ensemble of questions that are fixated on social conflicts through experience as opposed to the grammar of suffering that undergirds the United States and its foundational antagonisms. Continuation of the current discourse only mystifies the paradigmatic relation of the Slave and the ‘Savage’ and their respective demands in the name of “social stability.”

Wilderson, Associate Professor of African-American Studies at UC Irvine, 2010

(Frank B., Ph.D. from UC Berkeley, “Red, White and Black: Cinema and the Structure of U.S. Antagonisms,” Pages 6-7. pdf, Written in 2010, Accessed 08-08-2012, AJH)

 

 

Blackness and Redness exist in a state of perpetual violence through a life constituted by disorientation as opposed to contingently interrupted by it. This is the structural position of being the Slave and Savage position external to the realm of the Settler/Master; violence as paradigmatic necessity subsumes cognitive maps and conceptual frameworks both systemically and epistemic contexts

Wilderson, Associate Professor of African-American Studies at UC Irvine, 2011

(Frank B. , Ph.D. at UC Berkeley, “The Vengeance of Vertigo: Aphasia and Abjection in the Political Trials of Black Insurgents,” pdf, http:~/~/www.yorku.ca/intent/issue5/articles/pdfs/pdf,  Pages, 2-6, Written in Winter 2011, Accessed 08-08-2012, AJH)

 

 

The alternative is an unflinching paradigmatic analysis that calls for the end of America. If we win the question of how the US has ethically distanced itself from Black and Red bodies then the round becomes a question of how we orient our intellectual discourse; the alternative calls for a revolutionary zeitgeist aimed at the overthrow of the political-project that is America.

Wilderson, Associate Professor of African-American Studies at UC Irvine, 2010

(Frank B., Ph.D. from UC Berkeley, “Red, White and Black: Cinema and the Structure of U.S. Antagonisms,” Pages 2-4. pdf, Written in 2010, Accessed 08-08-2012, AJH)
