    • 1AC 1/8
      Observation 1 – Democracy as we know it.

      The “State” not only provides the realm in which the debate on democracy takes place, but it also limits thought and discourse based on predetermined assumptions about politics as being based around enactment of policy. This means that we focus on USFG action instead of ourselves as being the site of politics.

      Badiou 2005 (Alain, “Metapolitics” Pg. 41)

      This category of ‘factory’ authorizes the capture of the intellectuality of an unnameable name which is not ‘worker’ as such,...its category is the factory, while the factory as a specified place and the figure of the worker are the places of the pairing.

      And furthermore, this creates a normative discussion on the nature of the “good State” rather than politics as an end instead of means.

      Badiou 2005 (Alain, “Metapolitics” Pg. 82)

      Thus, our original starting point, according to which there are no grounds for recognizing ‘democracy’ as a philosophical concept, ...which leads us back to the third negative condition of our three Leninist hypotheses.

      1AC 3/8
      The inability of the State to think means that there can be no politics as thought in the resolution which address democracy. This means that we must turn to another site to debate the merits of democracy and the political.

      Badiou 2005 (Alain, “Metapolitics” Pg. 87)

      In passing, the fact that the State does not think is the source of all sorts of difficulties for philosophical thought about politics... ‘democracy’s is a category of philosophy only when it designates something other than a form of the State. But what?

      1AC 4/8
      The impact is that tyranny is inevitable. The restriction of democracy to the state instead of a philosophical concept means that we are unable to escape the constant downward spiral toward domination.

      Badiou 2011 (Alain “The Democratic Emblem” in “Democracy in What State” Agamben et. al pg. 12-14)

      Plato is a sure and perceptive guide to the panorama of modern society...For the democratic nonworld is a leakage of time. Consumption is consuming it.

      1AC 5/8
      Observation 2 – Refocusing the political

      In order to establish a truly productive debate about democracy we must re-consider democracy in terms of politics, not as a form of the State. Thus, this we must take this year’s resolution as an opportunity to interrogate what it really means to “assist democracy.”

      Agamben 2011 (Giorgio, “Introductory Note on the Concept of Democracy” in the book “Democracy in What State?” pg. 4)

      Today we behold the overwhelming preponderance of the government and the economy over anything you could call popular sovereignty... is likely to collapse into mere chatter.

      1AC 6/8
      Resolved: The United States Federal Government should substantially increase democracy assistance for Tahrir Square.

      Badiou 2011 (Alain, “The Universal Reach of Popular Uprisings” http://www.lacan.com/thesymptom/?page_id=1031)

      The popular uprising we speak about is obviously without a Party,...the organized faithfulness to the communism of movement taking on the state.

      1AC 7/8
      Observation 3 - Solvency

      The affirmative is a prerequisite to any better form of democracy. Any attempts at democracy assistance in the status quo are an endorsement of state-based democracy.

      Badiou 2005 (Alain, “Metapolitics” Pg. 80)

      If one now represents philosophy as that which designates, ...and so too the end of all relevance for the word ‘democracy’.

      This is not anarchy, but rather a relegation of the State to a distance from the sphere of politics. There are certain issues and functions that can be mediated by the state.

      Badiou and Hallward 1998 (Alain and Peter, Politics and Philosophy An Interview with Alain Badiou available at http://dx.doi.org/10.1080/09697259808572002)

      The third and final point of change concerns the state....we have to work more through prescriptions against the state than in any radical exteriority to the state.    

      and Bensaid 2011 (Daniel “Permanent Scandal” in Democracy in What State? Agamben et al. pp. 27-28)

      The withering away of the State should not be interpreted as the absorption of all its functions by social self-management or the simple “administration of things.” ...The seizure of power is no more than a first step, a beginning, the onset of a process and not its completion.

      For instance, from the point of view of feminist discourse, modern humanism appears to be a mode of patriarchal discourse ...The subject is thus produced by a linguistic/discursive strategy in which the denial of dependence on the other guarantees an illusion of autonomy and freedom.

      The “crisis of modernity” has been most acutely expressed in the invariable exigency of a Manichean division between an emergent Europe and its abject Other...Following and Deleuze and Guattari, they contend that cultural racism needs to be conceived as a “strategy of differential inclusion,” as opposed to the absolute exclusion of the Other…Subordination is enacted in regimes of everyday practices that are more mobile and flexible but that create racial hierarchies that are nonetheless stable and brutal (Hardt and Negri 2000: 194)

      To elaborate, this paranoid authority inscribed in contemporary Orientalism – where the ethical drive of global postmodern capitalism is to work through cultural deterritorialization, difference, and the regulation of ethnicity – has required the obsessive mediation of otherness in the form of multicultural commodification... The emergent global commodity culture, with its fetishization of difference and ethnicity, “screens-out” these abject figures, to produce a conflict-free, “post-ideological” aestheticized world of desirable otherness.

       

      And this function of fixing the distance to the Orientalized other is both repugnant and essential to the continuation of white hegemony. This is the structural pathology of whiteness which allows the presence of the other as a source of anti-identification.

       

      Sharma and Sharma 2003 (Sanjay and Ashwani “White Paranoia in the age of Empire” in Fashion Theory)

Let us not forget that Said’s central provocation was that the Orient does not exist.

Orientalism was defined by Edward as the process of the West defining itself as a superior civilization by constructing itself in opposition to an “Exotic” but inferior “Orient.”...Their privilege is not a signal that they will be assimilated, but that they will be marked as perpetual foreign threats to the US world order.
