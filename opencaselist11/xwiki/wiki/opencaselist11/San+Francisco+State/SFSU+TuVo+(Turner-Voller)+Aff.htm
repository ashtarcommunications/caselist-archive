Observation one is the absolute uncertainty of it all.

 1. Tunisia is symptomatic of larger structural flaw of the west to understand the nature of revolution and democracy as a process of ongoing struggle and resistance to forces of domination and not as something that we can claim to own or assist in.

Dosemeci 10-19-11

(Mehmet Dosemeci is a post-doctoral fellow at the Institute for Comparative Literature and Society at Columbia University in New York, “Occupation: Rediscovering Democracy in Revolution”, The Policy Innovations, http://www.policyinnovations.org/ideas/commentary/data/000225)

Observation two is the neoliberal world order

2. The revolutions in Egypt and Tunisia have been answered by projections of aid in the billion dollar range from the USFG. But this aid is not benign and is conditioned on an expectation of liberal democracy formation that suppresses radical alternatives and original reasons for revolution in the first place, a rejection of US influence and the hope for control over the political landscape of your own country.

Stoner 6-2-11

(Eric Stoner is a writer based in New York, and an adjunct professor at St. Peter's College. His articles have appeared in The Guardian, Mother Jones, The Nation, “The neoliberal threat to the Arab Spring”, Waging NonViolence, http://wagingnonviolence.org/2011/06/the-neoliberal-threat-to-the-arab-spring/)

 

3. AFL-CIO missions in Tunisia are currently being deployed to de-power the movements and reify imperialist interests, in methods similar to military coups in Central America. 

Walsh 10-8-11

(David, WSWS arts editor,  “From Tunisia to “Occupy Wall Street”: Who is the AFL-CIO’s Stuart Appelbaum?”, World Socialist Web site, http://www.wsws.org/articles/2011/oct2011/appe-o08.shtml)

4.Neo-liberalism has emptied democracy so that civic participation is completely valueless in the face of the market. This justifies endless expansionist wars in the name of us interests.

Brown 2003 (Wendy, political theorist at UC Berkeley, “Neoliberalism and the End of Liberal Democracy” Theory and Event 7:1)

OBS 3

5.U.S. democracy assistance in Tunisia is designed to target civil society in ways that manufacture revolutions that have already internalized U.S. style values and democracy. This hegemonic project uses organizations like IMET to generate consent about Western influence from within.

Roberts 4-1-11

(Dr. Paul Craig Roberts is the father of Reaganomics and the former head of policy at the Department of Treasury., “The New Colonialism: Washington’s Pursuit of World Hegemony”, empirestrikesblack, http://empirestrikesblack.com/2011/04/the-new-colonialism-washington%E2%80%99s-pursuit-of-world-hegemony/)

6. This is obviously attached to western fears of Islamic Radicalism in Tunisia and the region. This leads to the creating of ridiculous media driven scenarios to justify directing the outcomes of Arab politics

BAROUD 11-1-11

(Ramzy, is an internationally-syndicated columnist and the editor of PalestineChronicle.com, “Western reaction to Tunisian elections”, Arab News, http://arabnews.com/opinion/columns/article527556.ece)

7.This form of hegemony is uniquely similar to the process of control over the meaning of democracy defined as only concrete institutions which excludes alternative understandings. This becomes the critical export component of dominant ideology that attempts to universalize the West.

Markakis 10

(Dionysis, London School of Economics & Political Science, “US Democracy Promotion in the Middle East The Pursuit of Hegemony?”, SGIR 7th Pan European Conference on International Relations Stockholm, Sweden 2010)

8. This has two impacts

A. This form of hegemonic ideological export becomes equivalent to control over reality itself as it moulds our relation to the world.

Markakis 10

(Dionysis, London School of Economics & Political Science, “US Democracy Promotion in the Middle East The Pursuit of Hegemony?”, SGIR 7th Pan European Conference on International Relations Stockholm, Sweden 2010)

B. This also drives epistemological racism and essentialist understandings of identity and democracy, that believe that democracy either  can only be liberal or that some cultures are inherently anti-democractic.

Grosfoguel 10

(Ramón Grosfoguel is Associate Professor of Ethnic Studies at the University of California, Berkeley, and a

Senior Research Associate of the Maison des Sciences de l’Homme in Paris, “Epistemic Islamophobia and

Colonial Social Sciences”, H UMAN ARCHITECTURE : JOURNAL OF THE SOCIOLOGY OF SELF-KNOWLEDGE, VIII, 2, FALL 2010, 29-38)

Therefore

Resolved: Kyle and I affirm the potential for radical democratic relations in the ongoing struggles in Tunisia.

Observation four is Radical Democracy

 9.Radical Democracy is a politics of resistance.

Brown 10

(Wendy Brown is Emanuel Heller Professor of Political Science at the University of California, “We Are All Democrats Now ...”, Theory & Event, Volume 13, Issue 2, 2010 (Article))

10. Worker struggle is challenging centralized oppression and keeping the spirit of revolution alive in Tunisia as a radically democratic process.

Swagler 3-24-11

(Matt Swagler is a reporter with Socialist Worker “Tunisia's Ongoing Revolution”, The   B u l l e t, http://www.socialistproject.ca/bullet/481.php)

 

11.The occupations of space and time in Tunisia and North Africa are radically altering dominant understandings of autonomy and the conception of a political subject to the state.

Dosemeci 10-19-11

(Mehmet Dosemeci is a post-doctoral fellow at the Institute for Comparative Literature and Society at Columbia University in New York, “Occupation: Rediscovering Democracy in Revolution”, The Policy Innovations, http://www.policyinnovations.org/ideas/commentary/data/000225)

12. Your ballot affirms the method of radical democracy that contests the normative invisible practices that sustain neoliberal hegemony. This also allows for the formation of solidarities across borders that mutually reinforce struggle in the Middle East and North Africa.

Springer 11

(Simon, Department of Geography at the University of Otago as a Lecturer in December 2010. Prior to this he was Assistant Professor at the National University of Singapore “Public Space as Emancipation: Meditations on Anarchism, Radical Democracy, Neoliberalism and Violence”, Antipode, 43: 525–562. doi: 10.1111/j.1467-8330.2010.00827.x)

13. This act challenges neoliberalism and hegemony as they are supported by democracy promotion in the status quo. Only a radical reconceptualization of democracy can reinvigorate civil society and the power of the individual to shape politics.

Brown 2003

(Wendy, political theorist at UC Berkeley, “Neoliberalism and the End of Liberal Democracy” Theory and Event 7:1)

14. Radical Democracy resists definition. It’s a constantly changing contingent process of the people that traverses essentialist identity politics.

Enck-Wanzer 8

(Darrel Enck-Wanzer is a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Latina/Latino Studies Program at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign., A Radical Democratic Style?, Rhetoric & Public Affairs Volume 11, Number 3, Fall 2008)

Evidence we've read before for the 1ac but not most recently

Democracy is the empty signifier of the whole of this topic, the vacuous base of a political project yet to come. This uncertainty leaves us caught in-between advocacy for pluralism and worshipping at the democratic alter of imperialism.

Brown 10

(Wendy Brown is Emanuel Heller Professor of Political Science at the University of California, “We Are All Democrats Now ...”, Theory & Event, Volume 13, Issue 2, 2010 (Article))

The Middle East and North Africa have seen a host of revolutions in the name of rights, equality, liberty, and the pursuit of Democracy but what does any of this mean in the current geopolitical landscape? Democracy promotion is hidden behind an ideological backdrop of assumed neutrality that privileges neoliberal forms of governance, and makes the assumption of choices in democracy a non-issue. The Revolutions in Egypt and Tunisia have highlighted the ways in which in a new radical left is needed to intervene and challenge overall uncertainty of the status quo.

Callinicos 8-14-11

(Alex Callinicos, professor of European Studies in King's College, London, “Alex Callinicos: 'London shows that democracy neoliberal style doesn’t work'”, Ahram Online, http://english.ahram.org.eg/NewsContent/2/9/18830/World/International/Alex-Calinicous-London-shows-that-democracy-neolib.aspx)

This kind of Neo-liberalism is designed to suppress resistance from below, lead to greater inequalities, and wars in the name of neo-liberal control and US regional dominance.

Smith August 11

(Ashley Smith is a member of the United National Antiwar Committee, “U.S. imperialism in the age of Obama

Rehabilitating U.S. intervention”, International Socialist Review, http://www.isreview.org/issues/78/feat-imperialism.shtml)

The second impact is that this process is used to commit countless genocides and ethnic cleansing under the guise of peace and freedom.

Roy 1

(Arundhati, Book prize winner essayist and activist,  “War Is Peace”, October 18, Published on OutlookIndia.com)\

Our act of the 1AC is one that remakes the debate space in the name of radical democracy. An act that understands civil society occurs at the level between the individual and the state and seeks to create a new liberty of its own. This fosters contestation and struggle critical to transform transnational politics spaces.

Springer 11

(Simon, Department of Geography at the University of Otago as a Lecturer in December 2010. Prior to this he was Assistant Professor at the National University of Singapore “Public Space as Emancipation: Meditations on Anarchism, Radical Democracy, Neoliberalism and Violence”, Antipode, 43: 525–562. doi: 10.1111/j.1467-8330.2010.00827.x)

Our politics are transversal. We can’t limit civil society to the international or domestic-

Blieker 2k (Roland  , Human Agency and Global politics. Pg 184)
