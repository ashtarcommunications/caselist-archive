      A.      Interpretation: the affirmative must substantially democracy assistance to one of the countries specified in the resolution.

       

      B.      Violation: the aff establishes an International Counter Narcotics and Law Enforcement program with the intent of promoting democracy but based solely on its effects. This is not in and itself is democracy. They even said it’s “by any means necessary” in cross, meaning that they don’t even commit intellectually to democray assistance but the EFFECT of the plan.

       

      C.      Standards.  Effects topicality is bad because

       

      a.      Limits: it explodes the limits of the topic, allowing the affirmative to make advantages off of action not listed in the resolution.  This should be negative ground for Counterplans

       

      b.      Predictability: the negative cannot possibly predict the advantages claimed off of visas not listed in the resolution.  This kills education because we lose knowledge about the topic area, and are unprepared to debate untopical advantages. Make them produce a specific card that demonstrates how “democracy assistance” as a term of art includes their plan – the burden is on them to demonstrate they’re topical.

       

      IMPACT: Effects topicality removes all ability to debate and leads to decreased competition because no one wants to play a game where the rules are constantly broken. Effects topicality is completely unfair because it gives the aff enough room to explode the topic so long as they construct some tenuous link to “democracy.”

       

      D.      Voter: Extra t is a voter for education and fairness.
