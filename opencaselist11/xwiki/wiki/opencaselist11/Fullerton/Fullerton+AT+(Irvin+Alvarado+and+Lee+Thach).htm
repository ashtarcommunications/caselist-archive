BAHRAIN:

 

1—The US provides security support for Bahrain in exchange for King Al Khalifa providing US naval headquarters and military facilities for the civilizing mission in Iraq and Afghanistan. US diplomats described Al Khalifa as "personable and engaging, He rules as something of a ‘corporate king,’ giving direction and letting his top people manage the government.[1] King Al Khalifa rules an apartheid government by appointing all the members of the Shura Council who make up half the government bureaucracy. Protests have erupted where security forces surrounded demonstrators and shot rubber bullets and tear gas, killing and injuring protestors.[2]

 

EGYPT:

 

2—The national perception is that the US supports democracy abroad, such as Obama’s support for the democratic uprisings in Egypt, where any skim through history would shown otherwise. The US settled a peace treaty between Egypt and Israel as a way to defect Egypt from the Soviet Union (1973). The US sent $2.3 billion in aid, and in return the US can defend Egypt’s oil, and against the Palestinians who were left out of the Egypt-Israel Peace Treaty. [3]

 

The US support helped Mubarak suppress democratic participation for the last 30 years before getting ousted and put on trial. However, the military establishment under Mubarak is now in power, accomplishing a sleeper military coup. The US has directed aid towards democracy promotion, though the military has used this fund to discredit civil society organizations.[4]

 

The US uses puppet governments to install ‘democratic’ regimes that only benefit US interests.

SYRIA:

 

3—The US supported Israel’s action in stealing the Golan Heights in 1973 . [5] And in the aftermath of Israel’s attack on Syrian forces in 1982, the Reagan administration unconditionally supported Israel’s interests in the region.[6] As a result, the US have restricted trade with Syria, stopping aid and material from entering the country for al Assad’s authoritarian regime.[7]

 

YEMEN:

 

4—The Obama administration has provided a total of $52.5 million in economic and military assistance to Yemen, in return for a signed cooperation agreement to combat terrorism, smuggling, and piracy, where as of January 2010 there was an estimated 91 Yemen detainees incarcerated in Guantanamo Bay.[8]

 

The process of civilizing depends on the US deciding who is worthy of their support. The propagation of civilization and democracy requires the inevitable underbelly of those who are discarded from civilization.

Palumbo-Liu 02 (Multiculturalism Now: Civilization, National Identity, and Difference Before and After September 11th

David Palumbo-Liu boundary 2 29.2 (2002) 109-127)

 

TUNISIA:

 

5—In Tunisia, President Bourguiba stood unopposed for re-election and was named "President for Life" in 1974 by a constitutional amendment. Tunisia is a police state, with little freedom of expression or association, and serious human rights problems, with rising unemployment and regional inequities.[9]

 

LIBYA:

 

6—The US lifted sanctions on Libya in 2003-2004 and took Libya off the State Sponsor of Terrorism list for the government policy reversals on WMD, crackdown on terrorists (where Gadaffi jails and tortures journalists and dissenters), followed by economic liberalization international investments.[10] However, Gaddafi’s policies also forced major oil corporations, (Exxon Mobil, Petro-Canada, Repsol, Total, ENI, and Occidental) to sign business contracts that put them in less favorable terms. US ambassadors have warned that ‘putting pressure on US companies ‘crossed a red line’.[11] Washington backed the NATO intervention because Libya produces a lot of oil that oil corporations could free up for neoliberal market practices.[12]

 

The United States is not interested in stopping a dictator or providing democracy, but are only interested in opening up countries to the neoliberal market.

Democracy assistance has imposed a civilizing mission in the name of technological progress through a growth economy, where the growth economy is the ‘grow-or-die’ mentality that is predetermined by and reproduces social inequality and the oligarchic control of technology that skews society’s democratic framework to support transnational elite.
