1AC Ranciere Zapatistas

Tournament: Long Beach | Round: 1 | Opponent: Loyola AC | Judge: Laila McClay
CONTENTION 1: ANTI-POWER AGAINST POWER

Under the guise of economic engagement with Mexico the people of Chiapas have become invisible and are uncounted. This allows for people in Chiapas to suffer endless exploitation and be murdered in the name of economic growth and security.
Schulz 98 (Markus, Professor of Sociology at the New School, “Collective Action across Borders: Opportunity Structures, Network Capacities, and Communicative Praxis in the Age of Advanced Globalization”, Sociological Perspectives, Vol. 41, No. 3, (1998), pp. 587-616, JSTOR)SK

In the contemporary postmodern era (Harvey 1990; Lyotard 1984; Garcia Canclini 1990; Burbach 1994), global capitalism and international politics have integrated the world in a new way...NAFTA exposes them to North American dumping prices (e.g. for corn) with which they cannot compete (Harvey 1994; Nigh 1994; Saxe-Fernandez 1994).

Economic engagement is rooted in the desire to maintain police order control over the uncounted. This management of capital contracts the uncounted into subordinate categories to maintain their inferiority.
Ranciere 98 (Jacques, Professor of Philosophy at the European Graduate School Emeritus and Professor of Philosophy, “Disagreement: Politics and Philosophy”, Page 113)                                                                                                                                                                          

The legitimacy of state power is thereby reinforced by the very affirmation of its impotence...a power that internalizes the identity of the great power of enterprising and contracting individuals and groups with the powerlessness of the demos as a political force.

This practice of intervention on the behalf of the rights of the Other produces an irresolvable opposition between Good and Evil than sanitizes the violence of the West because it’s done in the name of ethics – without fracturing this model of action politics becomes impossible
Ranciere 04 (Jacques, French Philosopher, “Who is the Subject of the Rights of Man?”, South Atlantic Quarterly, 103.2/3, 2004, 297-310, CL)

"The Rights of the Other" is the title of an essay written by Jean-François Lyotard, originally a paper given within the auspices of the Oxford Lectures on the Rights of Man, organized in 1993 by Amnesty International...I think that we had rather leave the ontological destiny of the human animal aside if we want to understand who is the subject of the Rights of Man and to rethink politics today, even if out of its very lack.

This myth of American virtue has resulted in the formulation of freedom-as-gift – the Western desire to liberate the world and purify it of its evils is a violent psychosis that turns the entire world into a warzone
Rogers 05 (Juliet Rogers, University of Melbourne Law School, 2005.  “Unquestionable Freedom in a Psychotic West,” Law, Culture and the Humanities, 1)

This article was inspired by several encounters I have had with the representation of "freedom," as if freedom is a thing-in-itself...I will extrapolate as precisely the psychotic condition of West in the contemporary West.

ADVOCACY

THUS WE AFFIRM TODOS SOMOS MARCOS. (WE ARE ALL MARCOS)

CONTENTION 2: WE WANT A WORLD WHERE MANY WORLDS FIT

¡Ya basta! Enough is enough. The time for the revolution is now. We must take on the stance of the Zapatistas to rupture the exclusion in current discourses. Our dis-identification with the concept of economic engagement in favor of creating a space in which the people of Chiapas and the other uncounted can render their claims to politics intelligible. ¡Queremos Un Mundo Donde Quepan Muchos Mundos!
Tormey 06 (Simon, Political Theorist based in the School of Social and Political Sciences at the University of Sydney, “‘Not in my Name’: Deleuze, Zapatismo and the Critique of Representation”, Parliamentary Affairs, Vol. 59 No. 1, 2006)SK

One of the reasons, it seems, for the loose-limbed ‘anarchic’ nature of the CCRIs is the shared perception of the impossibility of generating representational structures even on a delegated basis—a point that in turn echoes Deleuze’s critique of identity...t is ‘dignity’ and ‘respect’ for the singular voice that animates this struggle against representation, not a desire to fulfill the historical or foreordained destiny to which all voices are or will be subject.

Against the promise of the economic engagement we must state the impossible. We are all German Jews. We are all Maquiladoras Workers. Todos Somos Marcos. We are all Marcos. We must dis-identify with ourselves and the subjects of a wrong to break apart the police logic that puts those wronged in their proper place.
Ranciere 98 (Jacques, Professor of Philosophy at the European Graduate School ,Disagreement: Politics and Philosophy Pg 126)

The transformation of the democratic stage into a humanitarian stage may be illustrated by the impossibility of any mode of enunciation...by asserting the impossibility of presenting the victim of the crime against humanity and of providing a sufficient reason why the executioner would have perpetrated it. 

This dis-identification is a springboard for the creation of dissensus that politicizes the gap between the West’s construction of politics and the claims to politics of those who are denied the right to have their politics. The aff is a starting point for reframing the global discussion of politics.
Ranciere 04 (Jacques, French philosopher, “Who is the Subject of the Rights of Man?”, South Atlantic Quarterly, 103.2/3, 2004, 297-310, CL)

If we want to get out of this ontological trap, we have to reset the question of the Rights of Man—more precisely, the question of their subject—which is the subject of politics as well...It does not mean the population of the poor; it means a supplementary part, an empty part that separates the political community from the count of the parts of the population.
