2. Framework:

a. Debate is traditionally structured in a comic emplotment, representing a fundamentally conservative view of the world. Comedy produces foreclosure by positing that everything is okay in the end—preexisting structures are capable of solving the problem in the end. The demand that affirmatives provide solvency reifies this view.

White 73 (Hayden V. Metahistory: the historical imagination in nineteenth-century Europe. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins)








b. This conservative mentality must be challenged to open up new avenues of thought.

Mannheim 36 (Karl, professor of sociology at the London School of Economics and Political Science, Ideology and Utopia: an Introduction to the Sociology of Knowledge. New York: Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, 1955. p. 229-230)

c. Thus debate should instead be constructed as a tragedy, which allows us to fully recognize the import of the events around us. We do this through identifying a problem without foreclosing or mandating a quick and easy solution.

White 73 (Hayden V. Metahistory: the historical imagination in nineteenth-century Europe. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins UP. p. 27)

d. Tragic narratives are the best representation of the human condition.

White 90 (Hayden. The Content of the Form Narrative Discourse and Historical Representation. New York: The Johns Hopkins UP, 1990. p. 179-181)
