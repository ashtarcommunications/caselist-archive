The Problem with debate today is its unwillingness to confront its own
desires. Debaters have historically been willing to explore the
limits of antagonism, but have never thought to consider the
possibility that there is no neutral space or reality from which
antagonism can be derived. Indeed, our perceptions of the world are
distorted by what we will call “the Real”, an excess or lack which
eludes to both our symbolic representations of the world as well as
our imaginary fantasies of how the world should be. Lurking beneath
every debate fantasy game about achieving peace and stability lies the
threat of disorder and chaos. Debate today misperceives the status of
this element of the Real, interpreting it as if it was a historically
specific situation that could be overcome by plans, counterplans, etc.
These attempts to understand the Real miss the point that there is a
lack inherent in the social edifice itself, there will always be the
lurking threat of catastrophe, such is the nature of our being.

Daly and Zizek, 2005 (Conversations with Zizek, pg. 76-79)
