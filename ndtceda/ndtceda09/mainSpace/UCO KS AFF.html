1ac description

The aff indicts Western metaphysics' desire to create the world into binaries, with one being considered full and productive and the other void of value, fit to be objectified and abused. This has been the root of American imperialism, with things like nuclear testing, mining, and waste all being done in the desert. This is because the desert is seen as absent of life and value-the unproductive end of a binary. The affirmative affirms the nothing of the resolution, acknowledging that any metaphysical statement contains within it both a supposedly filled material logic and a space of emptiness that is glossed over, and thus becomes an exceptional space that must be conquered or made productive. Affirming the desert is key because it is devoid of metaphysics. Theory has for too long abandoned the category of ontology, so we must begin there. SPECTERS OF DEBATE

broke round 7 @ harvard

We stand before you two years from one if the biggest spectacles in debate history. Our History. The release of this video on youtube quickly spread to the teleprompters and talking points of the news anchors and pundits from CNN to Fox News. Another instance of our ‘tolerance’ of the liberal university system? unprofessionalism and a loss of decorum – he wasn’t wearing any fucking shoes!?!!? This video of ceda 2008 has provoked many different responses. The affirmative Runs through a few of these responses that extend to ceda 2008 and beyond... including excerpts from Stephanie Dillard, Josh Gonzales, Adam Jackson, Steve Mancuso, Andy Ellis, Jack Stroube, Jason Russell and Deven Cooper.

Our affirmative is a stance against the CEDA Code of Conduct which threatens to sanction individuals who do not meet the standards of professional conduct.

Over the year and past years we have investigated many different ways that our debate practices replicate the arbitrary standards of exclusion found in the world - when confronted with difference they operate under the metaphysical dictates of accomodate/banish from the way the desert is made productive via forcing crop production to banishment and nuclear weapons testing when it fails to be a suitable ground for production; - this also extends to the imperial "errand in the wilderness" where the exceptionalist puritan drive was relentless in accomodating this virgin land to become a city on the hill and simultaneously banishing the inhabitants: produced as nothing.

When confronted with the difference of topic: we affirm the topical nothing that is a-part of and a-part from dominant instrumental debate practices.

Indeed, we affirm bill shanahan

The foundation of CEDA was supposed to be a ‘big tent’ to house all the different crazieness going on in the community – we prided ourselves on inclusion of even the most radical critiques – indeed, CEDA is known by most as ‘The People’s Tournament.” But as big as this tent is, we drew a line and said no to one of those more extreme outbursts – we reigned it in, domesticated it: played damage control. The subjective standards that allow elimination of criticism deemed ‘uneducational’ is part of a dictatorial gaze that disciplines debaters to become model citizens - we question what that model produces.

Part of our exilic affirmation takes a note from the idea of le differend. We believe that debate is fucking awesome. Perhaps even Where the Wild Things Are. We are not too naive to see that conflicts will arise: even big ones. The point is not to determine a objective rule or vantage point to discipline individuals who speak in radically different idioms, the point is to keep the idea of justice open and affirm this difference as a differend. We then read from Kennith Jenkins.
"This capacity to keep open even truthful discourse" ... " as opposed to homogeneity"

This issue has been swept under the rug for two years – now is time that debaters and judges alike keep the agonstic spirit of debate open – or when looking in the mirror, we very well might not like the community we find.

CIRCLES


Roland Bleiker explains:
“the puzzles [we believe, of the resolution] are understood most adequately through an interdisciplinary approach, which is necessary to deal with circles of revealing and concealing that surround transversal struggles. Such an approach entails constant moving back and forth not only between unconnected bodies of literature and different levels of analysis, but also between theory and practice, abstraction and everydayness, epistemology and ontology, space and time, discursive domination and possibilities for dissent that arise from fissures in them.”

We read different, conflicting, affirmations of the resolution from George W. Bush, Barack Obama, Abbie Hoffman, Margaret Thatcher, and General Lee Butler, not as multiple worlds or conditional advocacies or anything of the sort, but as pieces in one larger puzzle. Otherwise, we would risk collapsing into our disciplinary holes, like the nuclear weapons scientist who sees Moscow as nothing but lines of rads and pressure, or the anarchist who refuses to ever engage traditional politics. Though technology has been pushing us forward and forward insisting on it's objectivity and accuracy, no one site can make claim to absolute truth. So we should take a breath on our forward march of progress, and take a moment to move in circles.

We also believe, following Kierkegaard, that "a decision is a terrible thing", and no amount of knowledge or debate can absolve us of responsibility for our actions. We try to calculate our actions, weigh the costs and benefits, consider every potential scenario - this is nothing but an attempt to assuage our anxiety at our always imperfect knowledge. While it may make us highly educated, it leaves us ignorant to the moral and political consequences of our action.

How the Resolution at Last Became a Fable: History of an Error

Friedrich Nietzsche. Twilight of the Idols. 1888.

  1. The true world, attainable to the sage, the pious man and the man of virtue, - he lives in it, he is it. (The most ancient form of the idea was relatively clever, simple, convincing. It was a paraphrase of the proposition “I, Plato, am the truth.“)
  2. The true world which is unattainable for the moment, is promised to the sage, to the pious man and to the man of virtue (”to the sinner who repents”). (Progress of the idea: it becomes more subtle, more insidious, more evasive, - it becomes a woman, it becomes Christian.)
  3. The true world is unattainable, it cannot be proved, it cannot promise anything; but even as a thought, alone, it is a comfort, an obligation, a com­mand. (At bottom this is still the old sun; but seen through mist and skepticism: the idea has become sublime, pale, northern, Königsbergian.)
  4. The true world - is it unattainable? At all events it is unattained. And as unattained it is also unknown. Consequently it no longer comforts, nor saves, nor constrains: what could something unknown constrain us to? (The grey of dawn. Reason stretches itself and yawns for the first time. The cock-crow of positivism.)
  5. The ”true world” - an idea that no longer serves any purpose, that no longer constrains one to anything, - a useless idea that has become quite superfluous, consequently an exploded idea: let us abolish it! (Bright daylight; breakfast; the return of common sense and of cheerfulness; Plato blushes for shame and all free-spirits kick up a shindy.)
  6. We have suppressed the true world: what world survives? The apparent world perhaps? …Certainly not! In abolishing the true world we have also abolished the world of appearance!
(Noon; moment of the shortest shadows; end of the longest error; man-kind’s zenith; Incipit Zarathustra.)”

IN THE NEIGHBORHOOD OF ZERO

Rd 4: D3 Districts, <3

It seems that each year, William V. Spanos comes out with a book somewhat relevant to the resolution. This year is no exception.
In his new 2010 book, Spanos sketches a memoir of his experience in Dresden and more generally as an American soldier and POW in WWII. It was while he was captured as a prisoner of war that he witnessed the firebombing of the "open city" of Dresden for 24 hours. Further, he was forced to confront this exceptionalist violence up close when he was assigned the task of cleaning up the bodies of this devastated city. He refers to this as the "zero zone": meaning spatially, the razed city of Dresden/Hiroshima/Nagasaki and temporally as that moment when the benign american national identity confronts its malignant praxis.

In response to this violence, we affirm the topic in time, attuned to the differences that time disperses. This interesseted affirmation allows us to be reflexive and political.
