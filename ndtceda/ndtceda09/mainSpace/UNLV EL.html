Between 1951 and 1992, there were 1021 nuclear tests conducted at the Nevada Test Site. Thousands of Nevadans worked at the Nevada Test Site. The rates of work-related illness, cancer, and accidental deaths among these workers is astounding. But those working at the Nevada Test Site are not the only ones affected by the nuclear poisoning. Other “innocent bystanders” living in rural areas of Nevada are referred to as “downwinders,” or those who lived down-wind from the explosions.

Until 1962, nuclear tests were actually performed above ground and could be seen nearly 100 miles away. The people of Nevada could go outside and watch the nuclear explosions. What many did not know then was that they were being poisoned, every single time a nuclear explosion was conducted. The wind carried radiation that people inhaled, ate, and drank. Every day that passed and every additional nuclear test just increased the rate at which they and the earth under their feet was being poisoned.
