In order to completely understand the problems inherent within nuclear policy, we must first understand the cultural values and social filters that create and reproduce these problems throughout our society. The topic provides us with a unique place to question these previously uncontested ideologies and challenge how we as individuals can begin to alter the cycle. But how do you question that which has previously gone uncontested? We argue by, “Understanding the ways in which the dominant culture is produced and reproduced in the simplest, most mundane relational acts between the adult and the child reveals that whatever happens between warring nation-states, between the dominant and the subjugated, between the rich and the poor, happens first between the adult and the child, no more and no less than it ever has…” (Zornado pg.27) We are choosing in this round to highlight the mundane act of reading and how it carries within it the ability to expose and challenge the power structures of the dominant ideology that drive the nuclear dilemma.
