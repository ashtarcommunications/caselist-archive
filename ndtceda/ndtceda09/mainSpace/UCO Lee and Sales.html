OUR FIRST ARGUMENT IS THE GENERAL ECONOMY –
What we know as reality operates around the focal point of solar energy. The Sun shits energy in all directions. Energy is an amazing phenomena that under no circumstance wishes to be restrained. Energy pushes itself in whatever direction it wishes to go. To restrict energy is to operate in contradiction to its path. This explanation of reality gives the best explanation about the notion of violence. Violence is an inevitable part of life due to our relationship with energy, the most egregious examples of violence are related to a restricted relationship to energy. Our affirmative identifies with the nonproductive useless expenditure of energy as a better understanding of relating to violence, Kenneth Itzkowitz explains in 1999.

Itzkowitz 99 [Kenneth, Associate Professor, Philosophy Vassar College) “To witness the Spectacle of Pain: The Hypermorality of Georges Bataille”]-AC



Our squandering of the notion of good as a basis for action divorces the concept of war from the political aspects of it. War does not have to be reduced to policy instrumentality but can be understood in base as beautiful. This is Nick Land in 1992
[Nick, lecturer in "Continental Philosophy" from 1987 to 1999 at the University of Warwick, UK, The thirst for Annihilation: Georges Bataille and Virulent Nihilism, 0415056071,



Our Second argument is the catastrophe of the atom –
The relationship with nuclear weapons is an example of restricting energy to its catastrophic extreme. There is never an ability to expend the energy built into the bomb, the irony is that their existence is intended for non-use via deterrence. Arms races and weapons increases represent a massive investment of energy into a restricted economy instead of a general economy. The status quo’s ignoring of the relationship to energy makes nuclear use inevitable. This is Fred Botting and Scott Wilson in 2001.
Botting and Wilson 01 ( Fred, Scott Engligh professors, University of Lancaster) “The Bataille Reader” pg. 29-30



NUCLEAR WAR UNDER THE GUISE OF SELF-ACCUMULATION AND INTEREST IS INEVITABLE UNDER A SYSTEM WHICH TENDS TO EXPEND WITH THE DEMAND FOR UTILITY AND SELF INTEREST. This is Allen Stoekl in 2007.

The aff indicts Western metaphysics' desire to create the world into binaries, with one being considered full and productive and the other void of value, fit to be objectified and abused. This has been the root of American imperialism, with things like nuclear testing, mining, and waste all being done in the desert. This is because the desert is seen as absent of life and value-the unproductive end of a binary. The affirmative affirms the nothing of the resolution, acknowledging that any metaphysical statement contains within it both a supposedly filled material logic and a space of emptiness that is glossed over, and thus becomes an exceptional space that must be conquered or made productive. Affirming the desert is key because it is devoid of metaphysics. Theory has for too long abandoned the category of ontology, so we must begin there.
