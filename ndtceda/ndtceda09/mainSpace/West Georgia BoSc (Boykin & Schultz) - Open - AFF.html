Contention One: Preemption
There should be no preemptive roles or missions of the nuclear arsenal.
Deterrence, the operative logic of the Cold War, has given way to the new logic of preemption. This logic is based on an epistemology of objective uncertainty – it sees a world filled with omnipresent potentially emergent threats. These “unknown unknowns” can only be collectively defined collectively as an “axis of evil” to justify what would otherwise be considered crimes against humanity undertaken to preempt potential threats.
Massumi 7 (Brian, a professor in the Communication Department of the Université de Montréal, “Potential Politics and the Primacy of Preemption”, Theory & Event, Vol. 10, Iss. 2, ProQuest, GAL)
“Deterrence does not work across different orders of magnitude. … The operative logic will function on an entirely different plane.”

Preemption of the unspecified “terrorist threat” instates a co-productive logic driven to constantly defuse potential attacks before they unfold- fear incites you to leap into action and trigger the production of what you fear. This isn’t just about the Bush doctrine- preemption is a self-perpetuating logic which expands to encompass and respond to ever-changing threats.
Massumi 7 (Brian, a professor in the Communication Department of the Université de Montréal, “Potential Politics and the Primacy of Preemption”, Theory & Event, Vol. 10, Iss. 2, ProQuest)
“The situation within which the logic of preemption arises is far-from-equilibrium by nature. ….a movement of actualization may be triggered that is not only self-propelling but also effectively, indefinitely, ontologically productive, because it works from a virtual cause whose potential no single actualization exhausts.”

The logic of preemption has even influenced thinking about the role of the US nuclear weapons arsenal. Contrary to traditional notions about the defensive role of nuclear weapons, Stratcom’s new global strike command includes a preemptive nuclear option.

The impact is a permanent state of emergency- perpetual undeclared war. A new operative logic must be found to disarm preemption- returning to prevention or deterrence, or voting a particular administration out of office is not sufficient.
