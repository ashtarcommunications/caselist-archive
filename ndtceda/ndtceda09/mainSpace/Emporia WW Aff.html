1ac w/ cites

The affirmative is a series of stories and narratives about Ryan's experiences in high school. These stories focus on what it was like to attend Kansas City Central High School. Ryan talks about the low quality of education there to try to put in perspective structural differences in education. Playing spades in Chemistry class, playing monopoly in social studies -- these stories reflect on the low quality of education that is characteristic of impoverished areas getting written-off when it comes to education. Ryan talks a lot about how debate became his chance to escape this fatalistic system.
Security measures at KCC were very strict -- 2 metal detectors were used to filter 1500 students through every morning. In a way this means that security threats (e.g. someone bringing a gun to school) were more important to the school than quality education.
After school, students would race out to the parking lot. some would sell drugs to each other, some to teachers, and there would almost always be a fight that often times would later end in gunfire.
These stories are interlaced with memories about watching the show Mighty Morphin Power Rangers as kids. The MMPR were a bunch of regular teenagers who Morphed -- became something else, something powerful and fantastic -- to fight against the villains, demons, and what not. Similarly, the affirmative identifies with these fantastic images in a comparable fashion to their own condition and discuss the relationship of this identification with structural violence and the contemporary arcane, insular, and exclusive nature of college debate.
We conclude by saying that nuclear weapons never really had a lot to do with the daily forms of structural violence that Ryan encountered and thus read one of the following advocacy statements.
