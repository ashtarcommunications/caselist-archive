Simplistic narratives that attempt to outline a global harm an a simplistic, state based solution, are not value neutral inevitabilities but are part of a larger discursive method. Which means chaos and violence are always situated out there, never in here. Unfortunately, this global speak is cyclical; harms are never systemic and can always be "solved for" with more beautiful narratives of clean orders like the affirmative.
Nayar 1999 (Jayan, Transnational Law and Contemporary Problems, Fall 1999, pp. Lexis Nexis)
"It has become a convienience...times is the order of the day."

Nayar 1999 (Jayan, Transnational Law and Contemporary Problems, Fall 1999, pp. Lexis Nexis)
"Following around the state as...of mythic solidarities and common destinies."
