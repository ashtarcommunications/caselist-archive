B SUBPOINT: The Myth Becomes A Nuclear Fetish

The logic of nuclear destruction creates a closed loop of nuclear policy-making where decreasing, increasing and maintaining deterrence can all be shown to simultaneously increase and decrease crisis stability. This paradoxical form of paralysis is no accident but rather is the result of the political construction of nuclear weapons as a fetish, whose awesome destructive power cannot be truly wielded by mere mortals and whose value is only irrationally determined by the beliefs of others. This fetishism of the value of force constructs nuclear weapons as both natural and inevitable and literally writes value into social reality and creates a perverse relationship between quantity and value, where increasing thresholds and stockpiles devalues the credibility of actual use and thus necessitates ever-greater armaments
