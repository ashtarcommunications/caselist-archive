Lets take a step back and think about our world. When I talk about our world, I’m not talking about the world as it is, I’m talking about the world as perceived, as it is for you. We’re here, in the same room undergoing the same experience of a debate round, but in spite of being in the presence of the same experience we all perceive this event differently.

This is the result of our own individual paradigms for viewing life through. In general, people aren’t meant to experience the reality of their surroundings, instead they experience the perception of that reality. And that perception is unique to all people. This is the individualizing, authenticating process of the world. What makes you you and me me. In a sense, the world around you is an extension of yourself because it is built around your own perceptions.

But there is a nihilistic paradigm that has been slowly engraining itself in our collective mindsets since the age of Plato. A hegemonic and destructive paradigm of viewing the world that seeks to see the world as it is, rather than how it is perceived. This viewpoint sees the world, specifically other individuals in terms of computability, facts, numbers, charts and in doing so it dangerously blurs what it means to be an individual in the world. This levels all being and experience to cold hard numbers, ignoring the joys of life. The paradigm becomes productivity. To take objects in the world and mathematically use them to their fullest potential. To value a thing is to be being towards that thing. To value progress is to be being towards being towards. It is an endless cycle of progression for the sake of more progression that leaves us fitter, happier, and more productive. Empty robots.

A directionless, unmoving and infinitely self-referential will that shrugs off any attachment to the subject.

Individuals see others not as anything intrinsically good, but as tools to be used for specific tasks. This cycle closes when individuals view themselves as tools- forever divorced from being and the essential nature of life that is given to them by their own unique paradigms.
