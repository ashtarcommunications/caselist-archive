1) Federal support for biofuels in the status quo comes in the form of
the Volumetric Excise Ethanol Tax Credit, a subsidy to biofuel blenders
and owners of ethanol processing plants, which supports using ethanol to
power cars. There are also two market-price supports: the Renewable
Fuels Standard, a mandate by President Bush to meet certain production
levels of ethanol each year, and the import barrier, a tariff on
Brazilian sugarcane ethanol. These market-price supports constitute a
form of protectionism that allows biofuel producers to make more money
than they otherwise would. There are no direct payments or
countercyclical payments given to farmers for biofuels.
Koplow in 07 (Douglas, Biofuels - At What Cost? Government support for
ethanol and biodiesel in the United States: 2007 update October 2007)
2) The ethanol tariff acts as an import barrier to Brazil, who wants to
trade its sugarcane ethanol with us and fill in the supply gap left by
ethanol producers struggling to meet US demand.

fuel? Cane do, Brazil says," Orlando Sentinel,

Act, which is currently in-committee, would repeal the Renewable Fuels

imported ethanol.
