Contention 1: The chef d’Eat

Biopolitical sovereignty begins not with a contract but a meal. Managing heterogeneous bodies requires the production of a population, humans, in opposition to a dangerous outside, animals. The reduction of animals to ‘units of meat’ to be consumed by the populace is the first step in the production of this line between the inanimate energy source known as Animal and the lively consumer called Human. What results is a national dietetics, a prescribed order of consumption, that is focused on optimizing the human-health of the population against the dangers of animality, which range from sexual ‘predation’ to society-threatening ‘parasites.’

The American Chef proffers an experience in concentrated consumption.. Carcass factories and ghettos. This massifying isolation of zones of animality from the rest of humanity forms a standing-feast upon which the political order must constantly feeds to stabilize its existence.
Young, Ph.D candidate in Political Science at CSU, 2006
[Katherine."Animal Politics: Sovereignty and the Animal Body" Paper presented at the annual meeting of the Western Political Science Association, Hyatt Regency Albuquerque, Albuquerque, New Mexico, Mar 17, 2006 <Not Available>. 2008-09-13 __http://www.allacademic.com/ meta/p97264_index.html__
Animals are denied politics because they have no sense outside of their own survival. Animals and... (separating nomos and physis), and, therefore, substantiating sovereign power to judge life and death before the law. In order to grasp the motor of the anthropological machine, it looks like we need to go back to what feeds it first – the animal body.”

The fixity of the boundary between the Human and the Animal is not natural, but an active production of an ensemble of governmentality.

The reduction of animals-as-multiplicity to already-dead matter is coded into the federal government’s depiction of the farm. The relationship between what goes on at a flesh factory and the consumers of its so-called products is obscured by legal discourse that construes agricultural production in purely economic terms. The notion of the farm as a site of cultural production is emptied in favor of a sanitized ‘agricultural sector.’

Thus the mass concentration of animals into mechanized zones of death is reframed:
<definition of CAFO from EPA site>

These “Regulatory Definitions” are “Size Thresholds.” More dead animals means YOU (the farm) become intelligible as a CAFO.

Despite receiving substantial subsidization from the government, the relationship between the Sovereign’s consumption pattern and the CAFO remains concealed by a legal language of absence. The phrase Concentrated Animal Feeding Operation is not mentioned in the 2008 Farm Bill.

This omission of the flesh factory from the law constitutes an a priori ontological subsidy, which frames the human relationship to animals in terms of management and control. To quote the law:
<section of farm bill on “carcass management technology”>


Animal beings are reduced to a series of inanimate carcasses to be optimized, sold, or destroyed. This political order of the Human necessitates the never-ending extermination of all entities deemed ‘animal.’
Derrida, 2008

Because the sovereign controls the line, every subject under its purview becomes standing-reserve, always already in danger of being pushed over the line into a zone of extermination. Exet

Taste: the United States federal government should eliminate its subsidization of Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations.

This motion is not a call for a transcendental government solution, but rather part of an immanent deconstruction of the diet of the biopolitical body. Calling for the elimination of subsidies for that which does not textually exist in subsidy law, the CAFO, positively marks the negative exclusion of animality that conditions the current social order. Drawing attention to absence as the ontological subsidy that enables the massification and elimination of populations deemed ‘animal’ renders fragile the smooth digestion of the Animal as mere source of energy. This deconstructive work challenges the stability of the human-animal divide and opens new space for ethical interaction.

Contention 2: Eat Well

The confident utilitarian position from which current decision-makers gaze upon the world and predict future crises is founded upon an invisble, yet an ever increasing mountain of bodies deemed ‘animal.’ In order to unsettle the stomach of this method of consumption..

There is no choice between sovereignty or no sovereignty, ethics or no ethics, consumption or starvation. One must eat. And when confronted with the genocidal diet of current subsidy law one must make a decision between passively consuming the animal as mere-energy or actively intervening into the biopolitical digestive system.

We are left, then, with a single question: will you join us in eating well? In finding the meaning of Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations that could satisfy our desire to share a meal of the good life?
Derrida, 1991
[ Jacques, “Eating Well: an Interview” in Who Comes After the Subject p. 114-116 ]
