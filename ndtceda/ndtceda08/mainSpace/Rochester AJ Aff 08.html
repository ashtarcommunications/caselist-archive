Afghanistan Affirmative- Updated post Cornell (February '08)

Observation 1: Inherency

The government of Afghanistan is near collapse; the Taliban insurgency has gained ground, and the impact of the collapse is intensified by strife in Iraq.
LA Times August 27, 2007 ("Don't Fail Afghanistan: Iraq may be hopeless, but Afghanistan is worth defending. Here's why and how")

Civilian casualties from U.S. forces have served to increase support for the resurgent Taliban, and patience with western forces is wearing thin. Inderfurth May 29,2007 ("Losing the 'other war' in Afghanistan?)

Violent attacks are on the rise in 2008; insurgents are gaining popularity where people have become dissatisfied with the Kabul government. Smith 2008 ("Optimism turns to gloom in Karzai's hometown")

Observation Two: Harms
A) Afghan Instability

Although Karzai has expressed opposition to civilian casualties, his inability to stop them is leading to increasing opposition, including calls for Karzai's resignation. Kay May 5, 2k7 ("US Massacres of Civilians spartks protests in Afghanistan")

Civilian casualties from bombings have driven civilians to support the Taliban as a means of defense. Bayani 2K7 ("Afghanistan: Reconstruction Can Help Win War Against Taliban")

Civilian deaths have resulted in calls for Karzai's resignation and have also helped the Taliban insurgents recruit. Johnson 2K7 ("On the edge of the Big Muddy: The Taliban Resurgence in Afghanistan")

Empirically, the Taliban in Afghanistan has been the source of violent attacks and its return to power would be devastating. LA Times 2K7 ("Don't Fail Afghanistan: Iraq may be hopeless, but Afghanstan is worth defending. Here's why and how."

If Karzai's government fails to lead, collapse and instability will follow. The Guardian 2K2 ("Afghan staying power: Karzai needs friends he can count on."

Instability in Central Asia leads to competitiont between nuclear powers, threatening world peace. Star 2K1 ("The War Against Terrorism and U.S. Bilateral RElations with the Nations of Central Asia")

B) Pakistan Spillover
Instability in Aghanistan risks spillover into Pakistan and the ousting of Musharraf. Nixon Center 2K2 ("Afghanistan and Pakistan: Is the glass half empty or half full? A Panel Discussion")

A coup in Pakistan risks a pre-emptive strike from India. San Francisco Chronicle 2K1

A coup in Pakistan risks omnicide. Caldicott 2002 (The New Nuclear Danger by Dr.Helen, Nobel Peace Prize Nominee)

C)Nato
Civilian deaths have hurt U.S. relations with its NATO allies.
Associated Press 2K7 ("Nato partners chide US over civilian deaths: Recent operations that killed 90 Afghans causing friction over U.S. Mission")

Afghanistan is the key test for the future and cohesion of NATO. Koenders 2K5 ("Afghanistan and the Future of the Alliance")

The Nato Alliance is key to stability among its member states and preventing nuclear war. Duffield '94 ("Nato's Functions after the Cold War")

Observation 3: Solvency

The U.S. must establish a policy of zero civilian casualties and coordinate more closely with local authorities in Afghanistan Inderfurth 2K7 ("Losing the 'other war' in Afghanistan?")

There must be a status of force agreement in order to provide stability; it is the only way to affirm the sovereignty of the Afghani government. Rubin 2K6 (Afghanistan: Interview with regional analyst Barnet Rubin)

Warfare in the status quo privileges the lives of American soldiers over those of civilians: we must reject this thinking. Shaw 2K2 ("Risk transfer militarism: small massacres and the historic legitimacy of war")
