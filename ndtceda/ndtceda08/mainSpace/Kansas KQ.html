Contention Two: Openess to Time Itself

Every decision we make will be violent in that it we always exclude someone, something. The ghosts of those excluded will always come back to haunt us and spoil the best laid plans. A vote affirmative is no different. No matter how hard we try, we cannot be the Messiahs that usher in a new world. Accepting the plan will not get you a world of peace, it may not even bring peace with Iran. The Islamic Republic may say no, the neocons may swoop in and mess everything up. Every problem is haunted by these same possibilities – of violent response by another, by the possibility of failure, of being co-opted by others. All these anxieties come from the same impossible desire to pre-comprehend the limits of the Event. The question the Affirmative poses is this: Do we accept the ghosts and let ourselves bring them into expression by refusing to pre-comprehend the future or do we accept a politics that poses ourselves as the Messiah calling-forth the End of History.

The plan does not occur and then fade away. The plan is not a finite, limited event but an ever emerging possibility. The anxiety and even frustration that we feel by refusing to bracket an event with the “what comes next” is the very nature of ethics. We can only conceive of an ethical politics when the future is open not closed.

Rose (University of Hull) 7
(Mitch, 8 May, The problem of power and the politics of landscape, Trans Inst Br Geog)

Derrida also has reservations about … No response. We throw the dice and wait.

This all may seem debilitating and scary. The drive to be timely is a powerful one, they tell you not to guess wrong or anticipate to late. This politics where every decision is made in the nick of time can never come to terms with time as anything but linear. It cannot come to terms with Events that do not simply begin and end but instead effect with the future and the past. Understanding and accepting these ghostly events is a prerequisite to addressing all global problems.

It may seem that this politics is indifferent to those it attempts to help – it may seem that the we are ruling the reactions of Iran, Israel, the US and others outside the bounds of our content but only in marking an openness to the future can will begin to make ghosts a part of our lives and gain an understanding that breaks the chain of timely failures.
