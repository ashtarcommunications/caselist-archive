Subsidies are an example of our desire to manage and control the world around us as a means of manipulating our surroundings. Under the guise of conserving the environment for our gain in the present and future, policymakers technologically order the world around us and obstruct our ability to continually question and rediscover our relationship with Being and Being in the world.
Biofuels are seen as a solution to the current energy problem and are kept alive through the government’s subsidies. Subsidizing biofuels is an act of ontological concealment that prevents society from discovering a multiplicity of possible solutions.
Furthermore, the science and technology used to calculate and make these decisions as they exist and operate in the status quo can never actually be included in the solution – clearly they are part of the problem
