Jenny Edkins, Lecturer at University of Wales, Whose Hunger? Concepts of Famine, Practices of Aid, 2000, p. 123-124

This creation of lost wholeness is ultimately responsible for the possibility of violence. Those who stand in the way of our fantasy become worthy of destruction and elimination.
Slavoj Zizek, “Ideology between Fiction and Fantasy,” Cardozo Law Review, 1995, p. lexis

Our criticism also reveals why they cannot access their impacts. The affirmative assumption of a simple cause and effect function of subsidies is not only wrong, but reflective of a general ideology which ignores the necessity of evaluating our justifications and methodologies. The affirmative is guaranteed to fail on its own account. We must not be afraid to focus on theory.
Nick Srnicek, “Complexity and the Food Crisis,” The Accursed Share, May 13th, 2008 (http://accursedshare.blogspot.com/2008/05/complexity-and-food-crisis.html)
