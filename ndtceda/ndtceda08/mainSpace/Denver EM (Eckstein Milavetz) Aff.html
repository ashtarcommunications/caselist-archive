The current paradigm for understanding the text is through one of two lenses. First is the clinical view which views the text as something to be diagnosed. Like a doctor examining a patient, we look for symptoms (indicators) as evidence for our conclusions. In literature, the clinical frame means that we take specific words of a text from those, deduce their meaning in terms of the larger narrative in place. For example we would look at the term supreme court and determine that we are discussing legal concepts and ideas. In contrast, the critical view attempts to determine the underlying meanings of a text. For example, one would evaluate the term "man," in terms of how identity is portrayed in a text. These divergent views of the text limits our own understanding of the text, because there is an undisclosed “truth” in the words, this objectifies the text into something that can be consumed by the market.
