How much of international relations have changed since the days of antiquity? Stuck without wind, unable to help his brother Menalaous-Agamemnon couldn’t leave for troy. In order to set sail and gain favorable winds he offered his daughter, Iphigeni, up as a sacrifice. When the ships finally sailed to Troy it was assume that the God’s were appeased. By this economy of sacrifice doesn’t end here. When Agamemnon returned home his wife, Clymestra and her lover Aegisthus killed Agamemnon to avenge Iphigea. Later, Orestes (Agamemnon’s son) killed his mother and her lover in order to avenge his father. Perhaps this all started with a boast, hubris, the killing of one of Arthemis’ dears, of the rape of Helen, but the idea that the economy of sacrifice is unending as long as the sacrifice is able to be justified and moralized. Agamemnon’s actions served as a sort of way to moralize morality- that in order to fulfill his ethical duty to his bother a sacrifice is needed and is justified by the overall good. It is this unproblematized idea of morality that facilitates war.

Tracy Strong comments on this moralizing of morality in 2003, Nietzsche and Legal Theory Part I- Where are we when we are Beyond Good and Evil, Cardozo Law Review, January 2003, 24 Cardozo L. Rev. 535

Thus, Nietzsche’s opposition to a faith in morality…..and then an eternity of deadly silence.

In deciding how to act it is important to recognize that every decision is locked into an economy of sacrifice. What then is needed is action that sacrifices the very sacrifice of ethics, of an immediate action that demands no condition and calculation. This is the difference between Agamemnon and Abraham. Agamemnon’s sacrifice of his daughter is calculative-her life is weighted against the lives that can be saved by sailing to troy sooner. Agamemnon’s action is invariably caught up in the sacrificial logic of ethics where as Abraham sacrifices ethics in his action and accepts responsibility for the possible sacrifice of Isaac. When go calls upon him to act, Abraham responds by saying “I am here”.

This year’s resolution calls upon us, the individual debater to make a similar sacrifice. We can choose an ethic that is in line with Agamemnon or Abraham. In answer to the hail of the resolution. Instead of calculating and choosing a why to our affirmation- we choose to affirm the resolution such and sacrifice any ethical determinations.
