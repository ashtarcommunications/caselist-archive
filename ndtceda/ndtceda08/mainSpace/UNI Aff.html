OBSERVATION TWO – FRAMEWORK AND OUR METHODOLOGY

A. OUR ASSUMPTIONS

1. LANGAUGE STRUCTURES REALITY –Language shapes and is shaped by reality in a symbiotic relationship, where our understandings of the world are created by the language used to describe it. This communicative co-production of reality gives meaning to our surroundings thru the nature of our research and the context of the debate. As Burke explains, language directs our attention to see some things and to ignore others, such that many of our observations are but implications of the terms we use.

2. DEBATE AS CRITICISM AND CRITICISM AS ARGUMENT –

a. debate is inherently rhetorical - Debate is a language game and the words we use to justify our strategic choices matter.

Little 1. The plan is not the exclusive focus. The community’s validation of discourse criticisms prove the ways we justify policies have become as important as the policies themselves. For example. Birth control policy justified by racist eugenics arguments, but would accept that very same policy if it was justified with women’s rights.

Little 2. Rhetorical effects outweigh the myth of policy fiat – debate is not a game of competing truths, rather it is a means to evaluate probabilities ground in communicative choices. Policy only carries meaning insofar as rhetoric structures it.

Little 3. the power of being resolved - The word resolved justifies this conception of debate. The federal government is not the entity that must stand resolved. Rather, we as individual agents reach a conclusion regarding the resolution. We “make a firm decision about” or “ cause [a person] to reach a decision” (American heritage dictionary)

b. Rhetorical Criticism is Argument – we do not abandon the dialectical core of debate – argumentation, refutation and deliberation. Instead, we offer an alternate way to practice the skills of debate. Good criticism is inherently argumentative.

B. ROLES PLAYED IN ROUND – who are we?

1. THE ARGUER AS CRITIC – We are rhetorical critics, and the 1AC is a rhetorical criticism. Given on our interpretation of debate and law this is both a natural and necessary role for debaters to play. Rhetorical criticism advances an argument about how we frame and are framed by discourse. Rhetorical criticism constructs an argument by providing a description, analysis and evaluation of a rhetorical artifact. The artifact we evaluate is the Supreme Court decision ex parte Quirin and the way that its shaped the policies governing and perceptions of Germans during World War Two, and how its present day use shapes policies and perceptions of the administrations war on terror

2. THE OTHER TEAM – as critical respondent or reviewer, as in every debate, the job of the negative is to answer the affirmative advocacy. Our framework does provide argumentative ground. A text can produce multiple criticisms. As Karlyn Campbell, Forbes Hill and Robert Newman’s opposing criticism of Nixon’s Vietnamization speech prove. And if you have ever submitted a criticism for publication, you know that reviews offer many reasons to reject the article and many answers to your argument.
