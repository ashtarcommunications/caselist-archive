B. Their Interpretation. They see constructive engagement and an offer of a security guarantee or increased assistance as a done deal. They don’t wait to see what the other government does in the plan text.

C. Our Interpretation is Better.

1. Limits for preparation. There are hundreds of possible security guarantees or small aid programs that could be given. We cannot be expected to predict and have relevant arguments prepared for each one. Defending for a process of constructive engagement gives affs good options that are specified in the literature.

2. Ground for clash. Our counterplan ground and disad links depend on the reaction of the other government to the US offer. Their interpretation tries to avoid this clash by being unconditional. This destroys clash.

3. Bright line standard for evaluation. The plan text should state some conditions for the offer. They talk about effects of their unconditional decision, and you end up making an arbitrary decision about whether they lead to constructive engagement to be topical, which is unfair.
