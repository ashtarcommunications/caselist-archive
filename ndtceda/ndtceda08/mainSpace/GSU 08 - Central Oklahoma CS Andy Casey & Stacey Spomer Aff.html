The desire to conquer and tame land is abundant throughout western disciplines. Land is represented as an adversary, a volatile entity to be subordinated and cultivated by the West. This desire has manifested itself into a syndrome that paves the way for colonial domination. Thus, the conquering of land involves multiple levels of enclosures of the land that classify the land into territory, field, and landscape. This places a narrative of control that alters the essence of the land. The land is merely a subject to the imperial state: deprived of its foundational offering it becomes placed on a grid of violent oppositions.
