Kritik of the Extended Will

The supreme court’s rulings are based upon greed, inertia, and fear which establishes a kantian style universalization that embodies the will of the state into the individual transforming the body into the potential site of the little Eichmann, We become capable of all evil solely for sake of evil while maintaining that we are doing our good duty.
Slavoj Zizek from the Parallax View in the former year of our lord, 2006
Pg. 66-67 “This is how we….to be truly evil”.

The dark alter-ego of the affirmative is that its attempt to establish and improvement of life further reifies the possibilities for its harms to continue – it does not address the worsening aspects of egoistic human nature and the international system – the impact is more violent scenarios for willed violence and inevitable extermination.
Joan Copjec, in Andy Casey’s personally favorite book Imagine there’s no woman which debuted in 2004. pages 135-138 “When we contemplate the….with his own arguments.”

Our alternative is to embrace life as it is now, this means to do nothing. This is the status quo. This is not permutable so don’t try…no not even once…no not even on your birthday. We aren’t attempting to establish a non-violent harmony. To be honest, authentic revolutionary liberation is often much more directly identified with violence. Freedom is not harmony, but the very violent act that disturbs the balance. The plan is an attempt at creating balance, we are its antithesis. We do not strive for inner peace or even peace of mind, we embrace the tension, the gap, the violence at the very heart of our own being, only when we accomplish can we truly upset the will’s need to engage in external violence against the other.
This is again Slavoj Zizek in 2006, pg. 280-282 “This late work of Heidegger….in aggression against others.”

Batailles Kritik

Life is in constant repetition, desire flows in whatever way it chooses. The earth literally rotates on its axis because people and animals have sex, and they have sex because the world turns o its axis. The is the type of useless expenditure that creates the basis of life. This is Georges Batailles from the Solar Anus. “it is clear that….the anus is night”

Even the great star in the middle of our galaxy is guilty of this expenditure. It burns without any expectation of return. The affirmative is an attempt to stop the excess expenditure of the status quo. The plan is an attempt at regulating the uninhibited flow of expenditure and excess. This puts energy in the service of the future which constrains desire Nick land in 1992 [The thirst for annihiliation: Georges Bataille and Virulent Nihilism, pg 33-34 “Belonging alongside….does not serve anything”

The focus on death is misplaced as an attempt to hide and avoid our inevitable encounter with death. This establishes that there is a hope for the future that must be encountered through plan. This is a replication of an obsession with obtainng the object of the fantasy of life. This allows us to remove ourselves from the spectacle that is death by privatizing it and brushing it aside. This is the fear associated with death because it interrupts the constant constrained movement of capitalist aims. Instead an embracing of objectlesness and meaninglessness allows a new encounter with life nick land 92 pg 109-112 “An utter intoxication such as this…alone comes to rule”

Our alternative is to sacrifice the affirmative. We abandon the entire hope of the affirmative project. This radical rejection of the object of the 1ac is key to ending the obsession with productive activity in the status quo this is Georges Batailles in 2001 [ The unfinished system of nonknowledge, page 86-87 “The domination of activity….crime, stripped, unveiled”

Western politics condemns its actions to one of servitude to utility – Death is feared because it is useless and the end of utility. Death is feared because it has no value. The ‘good’ of society is colonized by this fear, our sacrifice violently liberates us from servility an collapses the transcendent object of the world. This erotically allows us to transcend all taboos and prohibitions against desire in the status quo allows us to release life back into its unpredictable path Land in 92 [pg xviii-xix “life appears as a pause…infernal extravagance of the sun”

Life is constantly trying to return to its origin of non-existence, this is the death drive, the affirmative subdues the death drive by attempting to conserve life and nature – our alternative advocates useless expenditure which invigorates the death drive land in 92 pg 46-47 “Psychoanalysis, as the science…if not the sun?”

This erotic intolerance toward life and survival thirsts for disaster allowing a new conception for life this is land in 92 pg. 189-190 “every production and articulate…without reason to be”

Death and disorder are inevitable, the question is how we situate ourselves toward it, the affirmative by constraining excess and desire leads to a perpetual imprisonment of life and death – only our radical sacrifice avoids this land in 92 [ pg 205-210 “particles decay, molecules disintegrate…nor are words about words”
