Approved by Congress in 1996, The Environmental Quality Incentives Program set out to address the individual resource allocation issues, and environmental concerns of agricultural producers across a wide spectrum of the industry. Yet, with the passage of the 2002 Farm Bill all this changed – Federal EQIP Subsidies now began to take on a broader scope and with it forged a new mission – the demand for a sanitization network to exist beyond all rational and pragmatic need, through the construction of new transportation systems and storage facilities for factory farm waste – shit management par excellence.

The shit lagoons found on CAFOs cannot be isolated in terms of their localized site of waste containment, rather each plays a unique part of a much larger sanitation network established by the moral principles of cleanliness set up under regulations such as EQIP subsidies that govern and define the “good life” – free from shit - hence laying the foundation for “bare life”. This desire for cleanliness extends far beyond the needs of sanitation required for human or environmental health; it is a demand associated with a human will of the good, which can never be satisfied or fulfilled no matter how hard we scrub, or far we cast off excess. By its mere exclusion, the contaminated site of the CAFO lagoon is central to the organization and construction of a bio-political utopian fantasy

Biopolitics take on a unique form within the context of the utopian city’s sanitization network, one operating at the level of the individual psyche of the subject, and therefore must be addressed according to its own psychosomatic terms. We will call this brand of bio-power “Scato-power” - the ability of the hygienic apparatus to cause or produce constipation and diarrhea. Scato-power serves as a means of abjection, thriving off the continuous alienation of the individual from their own body, scato-power promotes a mode of fascism and self-discipline which poses an ultimate danger to all of human kind

Architecture begins with shame and ends with expulsion – It kicks in at that moment when the subject realizes it is no longer (assuming it ever was) at home in the world as serves as its means to make itself at home, or rather it serves in the making a simulacrum of that utopian home it wishes to be grounded.

Plan advocacy does not attempt to transfer a new projection of subjective analysis, or offer up an alternative utopian image for us to strive towards – rather it serves only as a representation of a new laceration within a lacerated nature - an analytic embrace of that abject site which has been omitted from an affective position which allows the distance between the identity that is affirmed and the one that is negated, the gap between the subject and the object of desire, or between the city and its utopian counterpart to be exposed. Plan approaches the city from “another perspective” – employing a much more seductive method, one which enslaves “science through the use of weapons borrowed from it, by making it itself produce the paralogisms that limit it”

Kansas State AF - Heidegger Affirmative



Our affirmative deals with several concepts related to the philosophy of Martin Heidegger (particularly what is called thinking, strangeness, guidewords/concepts, singularity, resolve, challenge, and provocation), yet the embrace of this philosophy is loose, and in no way direct or fixated. Much of the 1AC itself resembles a path to thinking, that which is itself already underway in its discussion of the way – the thinking taking place is our own, a response to the resolution which compels such on its own accord.



We begin with a discussion of Heidegger’s 1949 remarks on industrial agriculture: the event of the lecture, the response by academics, and our modern relationship to his words. We do not take a position on the statement itself; rather we argue that we are no longer able to, and that such an attempt would be a mere regression. We then discuss that which calls us forth to respond at this moment, the resolution – and respond in affirmation, an affirmation of the topical strangeness of the resolution, embracing the resolution as guidewords on the way to thinking. Finally, we discuss that which thought provoking to us, in our thought provoking society, in term of the fact that we are still not thinking – the banality of the good, the ease in which modernity leaves the problematic of questions behind, often purging all that cannot be conceptually grasped in the name of production – an enframed world of conceptual sterility.



Below is a rough outline of the 1AC – we do not read “tags” per se, but do read segments of text from the authors listed below. If you have any questions about the 1AC itself or our affirmation of the topic we would be more than happy discuss any and all portions of our advocacy in depth before the round.

“the vain quarrel over Heidegger has no philosophical significance...are the only way we have left to feel alive.”

“The most thought provoking thing, in our thought provoking society, is that we are still not thinking.”


“Thinking itself is a way. We respond to that way by remaining underway.” And, our response is one of affirmation – affirmation of that which calls us forth to respond – affirmation of the topical strangeness of this year’s resolution, “the strangeness which lies in the matter itself:”


Resolved: that the United States Federal Government should substantially reduce its agricultural support, at least eliminating nearly all of the domestic subsidies, for biofuels, Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations, corn, cotton, dairy, fisheries, rice, soybeans, sugar and/or wheat.


This affirmation takes place on the path of thinking itself, always remaining resolved, underway, and attentive to that which provokes thought - carefully listening and heeding to the resolutional guidewords from which the topical hale is issued and heard.
