Profanations Aff

“Political Theory and Partisan politics” p 181-2

In our political community, what is it that we all hold sacred? What subjects have been removed from contention, what issues are up for debate? These questions help us answer how it is that we as a community stand resolved, to what end is it that our decisions should strive towards in order that the activity know as policy debate remains policy debate

It seems to us that the question of what is scared in debate was once much easier to answer. There was once a time when debate was assumed to be an exclusive forum for future policymakers, government officials, and public figures that would play a central and direct rule in the management of society. In this long forgotten past, debate’s continue existence as a coherent, consistent community needed no external justification

What is sacred about debate is debates won self importance, that debate is good and proper grounded as an activity. This is not a concrete set of principle or dies but instead presupposed idea that standard for judging our arguments is their usefulness or productivity. Debate’s special location as a nexus between scholarship and competition meant that for policy debate to say policy debate it had to maintain its sacred foundation as an educational activity. This sacred education required a structure, a set of rituals that debaters had to learn to properly communicate, to make the game function, and to also keep the game on track. The maintance of the sacred requires ritual acts of sacrifice.

The sacred mythology of debate has required, primarily, a focus on truth seeking. Knowledge, authority, certainly, and resolution. What had to be sacrificed were those events which created uncertainty, which claimed no authority nor any desire for certainty of useful debate.

As advanced research and increase technicality became part of the game of debate, the activity was force to confront the truth of political interactions. Debaters began to make arguments about why certain plans were politically unfeasible This posed a challenge to sacred nature of debate as a productive activity that teaches us how to master an challenge the world rather than experience the world as it is.

The subject of the resolution also went though a challenging period subjected to scrutiny. These perspectives are now commonplace in debate rounds, and it is well known that in nay round one maybe responsible for knowing and responding to claims based out many of those views, this evolution in debate has shown us that there is no way to stand resolved, that here is no one definite way in which one could err resolve themselves on the resolution.

Today, the advertisements of the sacred resolved debate community are sued in rounds as absolutes, prima facia issues that define what is and isn’t good, productive, debate. To force debate to be proper or sacred causes us to labor constantly for our won unhappiness and find solace only in death of the debate community itself
