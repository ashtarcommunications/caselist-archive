A- sub point: Let’s make believe

Policy debate rounds function around wholly contrived mechanisms for decision-calculus that have more to do with abstract theoretical principles of behavior and prediction than they do with how the world actually works. We ask you for this debate to assume the position of a radical empiricist, to strip away the nonsense and encounter the world as if you are discovering everything for the first time, like a scientist on an alien planet

Gilles Deleuze provides a possible model for doing this in ‘86
famous philosopher, Professor of Philosophy at the Sorbonne, Dialogues II, European Perspectives, with Claire Parnet, translated by Hugh Tomlinson and Barbara Habberjam, 2002 «Preface to the English Language Edition» pgs.1-2 or 54-56

As an empiricist, you find the affirmative’s scenarios somewhat far-fetched. It is not that they are impossible, but given what you know about the world they do not seem plausible. In fact, it is the very specificity of their scenarios makes them mathematically improbably, and the emphasis in debate on specificity is a heuristic device that is the exact opposite of how risk should be assigned
