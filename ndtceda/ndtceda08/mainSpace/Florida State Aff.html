Observation Two: Harms

The Court allows the military to operate independently, allowing to function under the ?separate but equal? doctrine.
TURLEY 2002 (Jonathan, Shapiro Professor of Public Interest Law at The George Washington University Law School, Tribunals and Tribulations: The Antithetical Elements of Military Governance in a Madisonian Democracy, GWU Law Review 2002, Lexis)

And, the rules of military tribunals are designed to deny judicial review in order to secure convictions.

Thus, we offer the following plan:

The United States Supreme Court should overrule Ex Parte Quirin [317 U.S. 1 (1942)] on the grounds that military judicial jurisdiction over non-disciplinary matters violates due process.

We reserve the right to clarify intent.
