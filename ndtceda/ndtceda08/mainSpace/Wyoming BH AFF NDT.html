Schopenhauer explains in 59

(Arthur, doctorate in philosophy at Jena, “The World as Will and Presentation, Volume 1”, translated by Richard Aquila, edited by Daniel Kolak and republished in 2008, pp. 438-441)
“After this digression…seeks to solidify in himself the greatest indifference toward all things”

She says I am like a god now. I tell her I don’t think there is a god. And if there is I’m nothing like him. If I were a god, even I could not eliminate all the suffering in the world. Each instance of suffering reveals the entire character of existence.

Schopenhauer explains in 2004.

(Arthur, “The Essays Of Arthur Schopenhauer”, Republished and Translated by T. Bailey Saunders, M.A., http://www.gutenberg.org/ etext/10739)
It is marvelous how every man’s…better alone than amongst traitors.

There is no future. There is no past. Do you see? Time is simultaneous, an intricately structured jewel that humans insist on viewing one edge at a time, when the whole design is visible in every facet. All time is present and preordained by the will, why should mere phenomena be saved from doom?

Schopenhauer explains in 2004

(Arthur, “Studies in Pessimism” online, re-released in 2004, http://www.gutenberg.org/ files/10732/10732-8.txt, MB)
“This vanity finds…and now it is over.”

The only thing humans live for is to die. Why preserve humanity from their inevitable destiny? There can be no value to an existence where the best that can be said about it, is that it is a delayed dying.

Jacquette explains in 2000

(Dale, Department of Philosophy @ The Pennsylvania State University, Schopenhauer on the Ethics of Suicide, Continental Philosophy Review, 33:43-58, 2000)
“What is death?...individual will to life.”

Humans live in constant fear of their inevitable deaths. Life to them is a constant battle for existence itself, with the weight of the certainty of losing it in the end. What allows them to endure is not a love of life, but a fear of death. What value can be found in an existence maintained by fear?

Schopenhauer in 1859

(Arthur, doctorate in philosophy at Jena, “The World as Will and Presentation, Volume 1”, translated by Richard Aquila, edited by Daniel Kolak and republished in 2008, pp. 365-367)
“On every level…all the reefs he has avoided”

I think about all the representational phenomena that result from the will. The most basic drive to live is to eat. Food provides the basic sustenance for the existence of life. Its cultivation is considered a way of life and its consumption a definite affirmation of life. Agriculture subsidies embody the will to life in America. The maintenance of the food supply through government support is a constant striving for life, an object of desire to maintain existence.

Humans, plants and animals spend their whole lives willing and cultivating the fruits of existence to doom more generations to their same fate when in reality the earth would be better off like Mars, devoid of all life.

In fact, all that would exist is peace, the complete stillness of the sea of the spirit.
