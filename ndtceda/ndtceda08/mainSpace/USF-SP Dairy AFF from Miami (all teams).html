OBSERVATION ONE: MILK MONEY

YEOMAN 2003
Barry Yeoman, "Unhappy Meals" Mother Jones Jan. 2003 http://www.motherjones.com/news/feature/2003/01/ma_207_01.html

Second: This “win-win” scenario actually embodies a form of institutional and economic racism because non-white school children are proportionately much more likely to be lactose intolerant. Thus, for 70 percent of black kids in public schools, crippling digestive issues are practically mandated by the US Government though its subsidy program for dairy products.
GREENE 2002
Che, July 9, "Not Milk: The USDA, Monsanto, and the U.S. Dairy Industry" AlterNet http://www.alternet.org/story/13557/

THE PLAN: The United States Federal Government should should substantially reduce its agricultural support, at least eliminating nearly all of the domestic subsidies for dairy by eliminating all federal dairy subsidy programs. We reserve the right to clarify intent.

OBSERVATION TWO: WHITENESS

First: An examination of the racial component behind dairy subsidies wouldn’t be complete without understanding milk as a racialized commodity, a totem of whiteness. The industrialization of the dairy industry through government subsidy goes hand-in-hand with the state-sponsored promotion of milk as an ideal beverage – the influence of whiteness is concealed behind these narratives, and we are obliged to examine the role of our own racial priviledge in the creation of the modern dairy industry.
DUPUIS 2006
ASA Paper "White Food: Milk, Race and the Politics of Perfection" Melanie DuPuis (http://www.allacademic.com/meta/p_mla_apa_research_citation/1/0/3/2/3/p103230_index.html)
What the story ... attention to food and to rural places.

Second: Racism is the fundemental mechanism of biopower. Since institutional racism is intrinsically an action excercised by the government, we are obliged to center our resistance against racism with demands against the state. We demand that the US government remove its support for the dairy industry in the form of economic subsidies.
SHEIN 2004
Avital Shein, A Foucauldian explanation of racism beyond Foucault's. http://www.panopticweb.com/2004conference/3.shein.pdf

We know that judges don’t cause nuclear wars, but we understand that because we are entangled in the webs and systemic operations of whiteness, we can open a space for renegotiating our white identity by participating in the continuous project of reinventing ourselves.
