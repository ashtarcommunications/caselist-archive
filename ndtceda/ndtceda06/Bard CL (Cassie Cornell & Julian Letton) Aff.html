Thus we affirm the resolution: The United States Supreme Court should overrule planned parenthood v. Casey, 505 U.S. 833 (1992)

We affirm the going over of rules in the literal sense. We need to renounce the power that rules have over us, and engage in a process of questioning and crating our own rules. We affirm the overrule of Casey because we believe we should be free from laws that exert power over our bodies as well as our minds, but we do not need the 9 justices in Washington to take this action for us. We affirm overrule as the going over of this rule, which is an action we can and should take for ourselves, in order to take the stance that we can determine our own moralities. We go over the rule of casey, and we go over the rule that the court must do the ruling for us. Our overrule goes over the rule and it also goes over the rule of who must rule. This establishes us as the rulers of our won individual beings, as well as the rulers of our own rules. Our mission is to affirm our radical freedom from the limitations that are placed upon us every day by meaningless laws. Voting affirmative means that you affirm our resolve to go over the law and liberate our uique potentials for creativity and deep thought, unrestricted by anyone else’s arbitrary interpretations of how we should act or think. You need not affirm that debate is bad; far from hurting the game of debate we allow for the maximum amount of thought and education if thought and education are living the fullest of our potentials.

We consider monitoring abortion rights to be a despicable practice of imposing ones will on another. We advocate a worldview in which each person denies his or her own laws and beliefs, and no person is subject to the belief system of another. This affirmation of free life must be taken up by each unique human being, thus we do not need nine justices to bestow this freedom upon us.

Dolan ’03, “Symposium: Nietzsche and legal theory: niezsche’s gnosis of law. 24 Cardozo L. Rev. 757 January 2003.

The ability to create life under one’s own terms is essential to what it means to be human – living under someone else’s laws causes the state of humanity to degenerate and have less worth.

This means creating our own, new game.

The reasons to follow the rules of the game are false and trivial, there is no ultimate truth to the rules because they have only been made up fictitiously to limit our actions.

We do not leave the game and we are not nihilists. The nihilist does not play the game but removes to the sidelines and thus ends the game; we stay within the game and only change the way we act inside it.
