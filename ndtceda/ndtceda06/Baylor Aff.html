BAYLOR LT's AFF

i can't really give you "cites" to our aff because we don't read "cards" but here is some general info about our aff:
all of our speeches are a dialoge between the two of us; the aff is in essay form, so we don't necessarily cite any authors, but the "quotes" from our 1ac are from baudrillard's The Intelligence of Evil.
our argument is that any system (for example, debate) that tries to reach its perfection by enforcing certain rules/standards (like topicality or framework arguments) will ultimately lead to the collapse of that system.
we advocate baudrillard's idea of "becoming" and talk a lot about the idea of reversibility between the self and the world (the systems around us (ie debate) change our perceptions and actions rather than us (as debaters) changing the debate system)
we talk about how truth-production is bad and linear ideas of time/progress are bad. we argue that there is no concrete meaning to the topic or resolution.
we don't read a plan text or talk about any of the four cases in the 1ac.

there is obviously more wanky philosophy, but that is the "debate argument" contained in our 1ac. below is a copy of the aff from UNI (its the only aff we have run so far this year) - its not word-for-word (somebody from another team typed it up) but its pretty acurate




The "shadow of a debater speaks"- Our world is in excess! Everywhere everything is excessively good and factual, every person smart and free, every event predictable and comparable,



Our World is in excess- Debate has become an excessive game with fictional rules to be found no where, written nowhere with referent nowhere except in the virtual space of the modern framework of debate. Modern debate practices have focused heavily on a narrow understanding of topicality that takes so many assumptions for granted, like timeliness, reality, competition fair division of ground and so on. Debate have attempted to make the resolution reveal itself fully and it has responded in a dual way.



On the other hand, the resolution seems to have a meaning- giving meaning makes objects strike a pose. ( Baudrillard, intelligence of evil, p 101)



For there to be transference on to the real, there must be a counter transference on to the image, and that counter transference must be resolved. (Ibid 92)



Everything in us crystallizes negatively before the material imagining of our presence. (Ibid 104)



And the same is surely true for the resolution and for the debate community, every ballot that is case on the framework, every attempt to materially imagine debate and the rez,



Objects always get their revenge.

All of the processes, framework, etc tend towards unification one debate style, one topic, one way to read the rez. Our entire system tends towards oneness at the cost of extraordinary simplification. Any system that achieves this even virtual totalization either automatically fractures or duplicates itself to infinity in a simulacrum of itself.



Everything that comes close to it sdefinitive formula or its absolute potency can only repeat itself indefinitely or produce a monstrous double, whether it be terrorism or clones. (Baudrillard intelligence of evil, 185)



The only rule, as everywhere else, is the dual rule of gift and counter gift. (pg 179)



What we are given we must return with something equal or greater. Our debate world has lost its double. He who has lost his shadow is merely the shadow of himself, in an attempt to expunge its double from debate, the opposite effects have been produced and debate is a shadow.



Every exponential form leads to the critical threshold at which the process reverses its effects. The accumulation of the signs of truth produces an irresistible effect of uncertainty in this way the passage from the true to the false from the real to the simulacrum from good to evil is like a critical mass effect, a fateful logic of excess. (Ibid 92)



Most of us think of the world in metaphysical terms and categories imply performance and are under girded by ideas of inearity.



We're debating about gibberish- it wouldn't make sense to you because you keep trying to make sense out of it. The very way that you think about debate centers around representation, the flow to make sense of it all, and making the world better. What I call interpretation others call judge intervention. There is no decision without intervention. (Sartre, existentialism as humanism)



Our world is perfect- perfect- most perfect in its operations of duality in evil's inevitable transparency and the long term failure of any totalizing system, our world, even our debate world is itself intelligent. (Ibid 41)



The fatal mistake of our community to this point is that we believe that our knowledge exists outside of the world. It is the object which thinks us, and which sometimes thinks better than we do, and quicker than we do, which thinks us before we have thought it. (Ibid 42)



There is too much information, too much meaning, to the point where the situation in no longer bodies in search of a soul but innumerable souls in search of a body. Or an incalculable knowledge in search of a knowing subject. (Ibid 181)



If one did not look at the world within the world's eyes, the world already in one's own gaze, it fell apart into meaningless details that live as sadly far apart from each other as the stars in the night sky. (Ibid 183)



We are debate's natural defense mechanism, debate sees itself as a certain terminal state in history with two opposing forces, to continue to replicate itself by predictable it is not a question of whether it will happen, it already has happened. That whole moralizing impulse to question is bound up with the subjective logic of the debater that thinks they can know the world, or even what is best for the debate activity. Debate runs itself. Through our encounters with other which are fated by debate itself., there is no freedom here, and certainly no choice.



Our argument makes sense- nonsense. Everything which offends against duality which is the fundamental rule everything which aims to be integral, leads to disintegration through the violence resurgence of duality. It is duality and reversibility which everywhere govern the principals of evil. It is duality that fractures integral reality that smashes everywhere unitary or totalitarian system by emptiness, crashes, viruses, or terrorism. (Ibid, 185-186.)

Traditional debate practices call us to define the resolution, to interpret the resolution and then to affirm the resolution in that statement, that actuality. We use our intelligence and research to master the topic, limit the topic and give the topic meaning. It’s the affirmative’s job to turn every no into a yes, and the negative dances the same jig in reverse. And with this mastery and intelligence we attempt to know the resolution, and say that we have learned the resolution.

“Intelligence, Give me,” by Juan Ramon Jimenez:

Hold on now, this is bullshit! I mean, I’m a pretty smart guy and I can usually understand things, but I can’t ever tell what “the thing itself” actually is, precisely. For in some way, my description of a thing never seems to capture the thing itself, it’s always somehow missing. This isn’t anything more than the same problem humanity has been facing forever. We seem to be social creatures and cannot act, exist, or think without language. Language is the way we communicate, but it is imperfect. No description can ever really capture all of what is; yet we refuse to even admit this.

So where does this leave us, those who seek to affirm in a world that we cannot master? Certainly, we have found ourselves in a destitute time. And what is it that is so destitute about this age? Put simply, humanity has lost its gestures. Our capacity to truly communicate has been lost. That’s not to say that we can no longer speak anymore, but what was really at the heart of communication has all been presupposed. Language has been utterly sterilized, stripped of its


potential to simultaneously reveal and hide itself, so that we may seek comfort in the idea that we can achieve Truth. By our own choices we have removed the possibility of engaging in pure communication. The active refusal to seek the thing itself in language cripples our ability to experience authentic community and communication. We have come to presuppose the thing itself within language.

How can we conceive of such a thing in language, the thing itself of language?

To help us understand, we should back the story up a bit, way back, all the way to Plato and Aristotle

You see, Plato spent a lot of time contemplating being. For every being and every thing, Plato discovered five things: the name, the definition, the image, the knowledge of the thing, and the thing itself. For Plato, the thing itself is very much a real thing. It exists as that by which the name, definition, and image of a thing is revealed and thus by which knowledge of a thing becomes possible. It was therefore that part of being within the world that came to be known to humanity only through language, yet that language was always insufficient to describe, and master. This is because the thing itself conceals itself within language. When we speak about things without presupposing the thing itself, things in the world communicate themselves through our language. The reason why we cannot master language is simply because the very muse and inspiration of our language simultaneously hides and reveals itself. The thing itself is the part of being, of every being, that communicates and reveals itself. Thus, the thing itself is at the heart of communication.

On the other hand, you have Aristotle, the great granddaddy of Western logic. Aristotle’s idea of language and existence eliminated the thing itself entirely from language. For Aristotle our name and linguistic signification of a thing, our knowledge of a thing, and the sensible object of the thing, were entirely compartmentalized. There was no thing itself in this logic, to express itself through language. For him, the human capacity to master and define would be sufficient. Thus, the letter, the written definite linguistic signification becomes the final interpreter of language, giving language a definite and absolute meaning, or more precisely, commanding language to its finite and absolute meaningfulness, while simultaneously creating the essential foundation of truth, rationality, and logic.

So what does it mean to remove the thing itself from language? Let’s remember the thing itself as that which communicates only itself in language. Being thus participates in out language, has a role in our language, dwells in our language. When we remove the thing itself in language, by forcing language into a one-dimensional disclosure, we presuppose and obscure being, thus reifying language’s command over being.

A world that can be so easily defined may also be mastered, or at least objectified. This self-assertion and domination over being founds limitless violence.
