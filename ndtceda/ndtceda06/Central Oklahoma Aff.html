QUIRIN


Quirin is grounded in the herd mentality, in which the masses desire their own repression based on the fear of terrorism and national security. However this fascism is ignored by the public because they are grounded in the ressentiment that “terrorists” are evil and thus Americans are good. We however argue that this situation smells bad, and that we want a breath of fresh air.
Deleuze and Guattari in ’72 (Gilles, and Felix, Anti-Oedipus: Capitalism and Schizophrenia, p. XVI-II)

Specifically the talking heads love to bombard us with the images of the sadistic pornography of the US military torturing the “non-American” prisoners. The security apparatus uses these images to scare the citizens into obeying without question the policies of the state apparatus. But the images serve another purpose, marking bodies designating them as subjected groups, “others” in which to be destroyed, while at the same time aiding in the endocolonization of the bodies which are marked “American.” The state then uses the law as a tool in which, the “Terrorist” body is marked as being an illegal enemy combatant, being outside the law. It is through this that ex parte quirin allows the US to turn the world into one giant panopticon.
McClintock ’04 (Scott, Antioch university, Los Angeles, “The Penal Colony: Inscription of the subject in literature and Law, and Detainees as Legal Non-person at camp x-ray, Comparative Literature Studies 41.1 (2004) 153-167avery)

The United States Supreme Court should overrule Ex Parte Quirin, 317 U.S. 1(1942). People will ask us why we overrule this decision; our answer is that it is fucking sweet.

It is time to take a breath of fresh air, politics is an experiment. It is time to abandon the subjected groups based on reactionary politics, and instead “roll the dice.” We are going on a strange journey and there will be no transcendence over us for comfort. We embrace the insecurity of not being able to understand what will ever happen. We are a constant becoming-revolutionary.
May ’05 (Todd, Gilles Deleuze, p. 148-153)

Finally we are the Pink Panther, living life like a rhizome, because for the nomad the adventure is in the journey and not the destination.
Deleuze and Guattari ’87 ( A Thousand Plateaus, Capitalism and Schizophrenia, p.24-25)
