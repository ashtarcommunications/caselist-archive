Scrooge, living a life with his partner, was queer in the most basic form of the word. Leaving church, never raising a family and refusing to buy into the notion that the future rests on Tiny Tim, he had the potential to destroy and remove the fantasy that the town refused to deny: that life itself depends on the survival of the child. Scrooge gave in when Ghosts revealed to him the horrible fate he would suffer if he did not sacrifice his way of life for the child. Like the Ghosts in A Christmas Carol, the 1AC is a specter that haunts us with an impending doom of tomorrow, and persuades us to orient our every action towards saving the child of today’s world. The hidden message behind the 1AC is the same as that of the ghosts: the social and the political is not for the Queer.
It is the political action of the 1AC and their commitment to preserving the social order that proves their inability to perceive of the value of life without its investment in the future.
