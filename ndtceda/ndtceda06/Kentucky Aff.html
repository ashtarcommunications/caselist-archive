Contention One is a tale of two cities

Moreover, the civil rights era’s efforts to desegregate schools without inter district remedy resulted in massive white flight to the suburbs and geographic isolation of minorities – racial boundaries are being re-established

However, the failure of the courts to consider the relationship between race and place in the Milliken decision

Bradley on the grounds that de facto segregation between school districts violates the equal protection clause of the 14th Amendment. The Supreme Court should recognize the relationship between race and place in cities and allow interdistrict remedies to elevate segregation.

Contention Two is the Re-Mapping

The territorial boundaries that divide cities and suburbs are not fixed, rather they are governed by the law. Overturning the Milliken decision is critical to foregoing desegregation efforts across districts – this deconstructs the boundaries between the city and the suburbs
Ford, 2004 “Brown at fifty: Brown’s ghost”

Furthermore, judicial action overturning the Milliken decision on the grounds of equal protection is critical to remapping the process of segregation within the greater contesxt of space and geography. This is the only productive way to reduce racial inequality in the status quo.
Oh, 2004 “Critical Race Theory: history evolution, and new frontiers: article: remapping equal protection jurisprudence: a legal geography of race and affirmative action.”

Moreover, our examination of the geographic and spatial assumptions in the Milliken decision is the first to a geographic understanding of the law
