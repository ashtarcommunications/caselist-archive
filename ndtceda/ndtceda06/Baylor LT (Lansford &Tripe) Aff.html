Observation One: Dr. Feelgood
Motherhood has always been a colonized concept, and today the womb continues to be a key site of colonization by a plethora of actors, agents and social forces. The colonizers of the womb are not adversaries who wage war against each other in an effort to individually secure the womb as a bio-geographical territory of their own, but rather allies which form inter-related systems of domination in order to uniquely oppress womyn across all intersections of the social body. We begin with a story; or rather two divergent stories of pregnancy which stem from different locations, follow different trajectories, and conclude in different abuses – yet share a similar theme of oppression.
Ehrenreich 1993 (Nancy, Assistant Professor of Law, University of Denver College of Law. B.A., 1974, Yale University; J.D., 1979, University of Virginia; L.L.M., 1982, University of Virginia. THE COLONIZATION OF THE WOMB. Duke Law Journal. December, 1993)
Underlying each of these stories of oppression we find a conception of hegemonic power which acts positively rather than negatively in producing the reality of oppression in each of the stories. One story of power is subtle and indirect, while the other is deployed in ideological terms, hence making it more obvious being that it is implemented through direct force. In the context of pregnancy, modern medicine constructs a medical model of reproduction which power cumulates in the act of the cesarean section.
Ehrenreich 1993 (Nancy, Assistant Professor of Law, University of Denver College of Law. B.A., 1974, Yale University; J.D., 1979, University of Virginia; L.L.M., 1982, University of Virginia. THE COLONIZATION OF THE WOMB. Duke Law Journal. December, 1993)
Cesarean sections have become the total embodiment of standard and legitimate medical birthing practices. The medical model of reproduction constructs a “normal” vision of pregnancy founded upon notions of technocratic and scientific rationality; ceding unquestionable Truth, knowledge and power over the physical body to medical authority under the guise of expertise. This model of reproduction asserts pregnancy to be a “pathological” disease managed through self-sacrificial gestures to the dominant ideology of interlocking white supremacy, patriarchy and capitalism. Failure to offer this gesture of consent to a cesarean section is viewed as irresponsible, hysterical and dangerous on behalf of the “bad mother” labeled according to the western dualisms employed by the medical model. This act of resistance in refusing to submit to a physician’s call for a cesarean section justifies overt coercion in the form of court-ordered cesarean sections issued from the legal system which derives its power from the same claims of objectivity, neutrality and Truth as modern medicine. Not only do these court-ordered cesarean sections represent a unique form of violence against the individual womyn subjected to the specific violation, but this act of judicial deference to medical authority creates a mutually reinforcing system of oppression which simultaneously reifies the legitimacy of both Law and Medicine. Placing the power of the state behind medical Truth creates a medico-legal juggernaut of bio-political control which obscures race, class, and gender oppression in denying individual resistance and alternatives to the hegemony of medico-legal tyranny with a mask of objectivity and neutrality. All of this was first made possible with the 1973 ruling of Roe v. Wade.
Ehrenreich 1993 (Nancy, Assistant Professor of Law, University of Denver College of Law. B.A., 1974, Yale University; J.D., 1979, University of Virginia; L.L.M., 1982, University of Virginia. THE COLONIZATION OF THE WOMB. Duke Law Journal. December, 1993)
Unfortunately, the Casey decision did nothing to alter this situation. While the decision increased restrictions on abortion, with provisions such as the 24 hour waiting period and informed consent, none of this applies in the context of Cesarean sections. Casey’s central holding which reaffirms Roe’s essential holding that the State has legitimate interest in the protecting the health of the womyn and fetus remains legitimate law.
Levine 1994 (Eric M. B.S. from the University of Miami, Coral Gables, Florida, and is a J.D. candidate at Albany Law School of Union University, Albany, New York. THE CONSTITUTIONALITY OF COURT ORDERED CESAREAN SURGERY: A THRESHOLD QUESTION. Albany Law Journal of Science & Technology)
