Ex Parte Quirin concerned the detention, trial, and execution of eight German saboteurs during World War II. Roosevelt pushed for the creation of an independent military tribunal in order to mete out a series of executions that civilian courts would have never allowed. The executions were carried out in a matter of weeks and made possible by the application of a novel and accelerated timetable for the hearing, conviction, and execution of the detained.
