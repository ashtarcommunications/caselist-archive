Observation 1: The Current System
Racism is still alive in all elements of society.
Feagin, 2000 (Joe, Prof. of Liberal Arts at Texas A&M Univ., Racist America, p.2)CY

Observation 2: Educational Apartheid
Re-segregation of public schools is on the rise and in some areas schools are more racially segregated than before Brown V. Board of Education.

Kermit Hall, Professor History, SUNY, Marquette Law Review, Fall 2005 [89:115]

Students in predominately minority schools struggle for a quality education while predominately white school thrive from quality resources and teaching.
Street, 2005 (Paul, Vice President for Research and Planning at the Chicago Urban League/History Prof. @ Northern Illinois Univ., Segregated Schools, p. 3-4)

The Latino/Latina community suffers from educational disparities as well.
Bowman, 2001 (Kristi, Duke Law Journal, The New Face Of School Desegregation, April, L/N) CY

Segregation upholds and reproduces the white racist frame

Feagin, 2006 (Joe R, Prof. of Liberal Arts at Texas A&M Univ., Systemic Racism: A Theory Of Oppression, pg. 307) CY

Allowing one act of racism to go overlooked leads to its social reproduction
Feagin, 2000 (Joe, Prof. of Liberal Arts at Texas A&M Univ., Racist America, p.32)CY

Fighting Racism must be our number one priority; our survival is contingent on its elimination.
Feagin, 2006 (Joe R, Prof. of Liberal Arts at Texas A&M Univ., Systemic Racism: A Theory Of Oppression, pg. 322) CY

Plan: Ruling to uphold desegregation of American public schools, The United States Supreme Court should overturn Milliken v. Bradley, 418 U.S. 717 (1974) on a 9-0 decision. The United States Supreme Court should overturn on the basis that both de jure and de facto segregation are unconstitutional.

Observation III: Emancipation
The Supreme Court is the best channel for African American victories and race reforms.
Combs, 1995 (Michael, Blacks and the American political System, p.162) CY

Milliken v. Bradley decisions blocks schools districts from implementing desegregation plans and preventing white flight. Reversal of the decision is the prerequisite for integration.

Erwin Chemerinsky, Prof Law USC, American University Law Review, August 2003 [52:1461]

Racial diversity within the education system is key to dismantling the white racial frame.
Feagin, 2006 (Joe R, Prof. of Liberal Arts at Texas A&M Univ., Systemic Racism: A Theory Of Oppression, pg. 308) CY

We can’t rely on human agency alone for emancipation; we must challenge and reform the system that oppresses us.

Iglesias, 2002 (Elizabeth, Prof. of Law @ Univ. of Miami, Crossroads, Directions, and a New Critical Race Theory, p. 318) CY
