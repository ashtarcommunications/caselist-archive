Methodology PIC

The 1AC engages in dangerous politics of prioritizing methodology and assisting intellectual prerequisites political strategy. Their use of the experience of oppression as the status that defines those who should guide politics harms progressive political strategies. Their particular strategy is part of a larger discourse of recapturing agency through privileging experience as the basis for politics.
Craig Ireland, American Culture @ Bilkent ‘2 (The Appeal to Experience and its Consequences” Cultural Critique 52 Fall 2002 p.87-89
“Once an arcane philosophical term…’black’ or ‘lesbian’ or ‘homosexual’ for ‘working class’ (Scott, 786).”

The pedagogy of the oppressed sounds appealing, but regulates consciousness under the disguise of difference Making the speaker the gateway for listening to the speech demands ever-greater levels of purification and escalating rhetoric of oppressive history as the source for authority
Rob Moore Cambridge and Johan Muller University of Cape Town ’99 “The Discourse of Voice and the Problem of Knowledge and Identity in the Sociology of Education” British Journal of Sociology of Education 20 (2) p. 199-200
“The pedagogic device (Bernstein, 1990)…assert an alternative perspective or standpoint.”

Our alternative: debate should include methodologies of the oppressed. We shouldn’t assign absolute importance to social location or the experience of oppression because all perspectives are partial no one standpoint can direct a complex society.
Our alternative acknowledges the relevance of experience without turning a particular category of experience, like oppression, into a prerequisite for participation in political dialogue. Their strategy prevents most effective exchange between viewpoints and values by claiming that there is a single location that must initiate any dialogue.
Susan Carle Law @ American ‘5 “Theorizing Agency” 55 Am. U.L. Rev. 307 L/N
“One debate that could have benefited from…including scholarly production.”

There should be no methodological prerequisites for participation in education and argument. This is the only way to give the force of argument and rigorous testing of any idea that is necessary for progressive politics to win the public sphere.
Rob Moore Cambridge and Johan Muller University of Cape Town 99 “The Discourse of Voice and the Problem of Knowledge and Identity in the Sociology of Education” British Journal of Sociology and Education 20 (2) p191-2
“Our purpose in this paper is to raise some issues…untenable, allegedly related, epistemological ones’ (ibid, p.34).”
