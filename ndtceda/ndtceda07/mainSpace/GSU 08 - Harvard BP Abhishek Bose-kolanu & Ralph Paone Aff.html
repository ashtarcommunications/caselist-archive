The spatial distancing of dead-flesh production is bound up with a co-emergent metaphoric distancing in the legal system’s production of the ‘national farm.’

Techniques of governmentality aimed at ordering, controlling, and integrating farms into the so-called national imaginary deploy statistics to re-define the dead animals factory in terms of economic value. The farm is dead, long live the ‘agricultural sector.’

These “Regulatory Definitions” are nothing more than “Size Thresholds.” More dead animals means YOU (the farm) are understood by the law as CAFO.[3]

This sanitized understanding of the dead animals factory removes it from consideration in the social habitus. The ethical relationship between consumptive social body and dead animals factory is elided. The farm becomes an economic unit. Animals are reduced to food-producing units for the food-consuming units that make up the mechanized, depoliticized Human social body of America.

The subsidization of the so-called CAFO is part and parcel of this production of the ‘national farm’ as a zone of food-production, which distances us from the whirring blades of the kill floor AND disciplines both farmers and consumers to understand food-production in terms of raw economics.

The primary subsidy mechanism, the EQIP, presents itself as an open program in which any “land used for the production of livestock” is ‘eligible’ for funding.[4]

In practice, the program functions to erase the excrement of the ‘national dead animals factory.’

In the dead animals factory, there is no death, only production. When the shiny rod enters the cow’s skull, it simply becomes stratified into the meat and other products which it was destined to become.

The components of the dead animals factory are not intelligible outside of the reach of this ontological framing. things do not go in as "animals" and come out "meat" in any meaningful way. The dead animals factory and its subsidies precode the bodies that enter as Meat (that which is only to be understood as the fuel of our production-centers). Workers and animals alike shrug through a death dance as their bodies are transmuted into regimented, lurching pieces of meat (energy). CONTENTION TWO: YOU ARE WHAT YOU EAT

The animal's absence in the law leaves a trace, the remains of a presence excluded. To excavate that animal that therefore I am, is to think the way what is present makes absent. To call for the elimination of the subsidization of Concentrated Animal Feed Operations by the United States federal government is to recognize our very selves as an essential component of this ensemble of lines traced through the animal, the state, and back again through us.

To eliminate the presence of subsidization is not simply to blot factory farming from the law, but to present the absence of animals in the social habitus. Marking the structure of this difference exposes the state as an aesthetic of governance where "we" are not outside omniscient activists who demand that "they," the evil government withdraw its support for CAFOs. RATHER, this recognition that the dead animals factory that is both within and outside of our subjectivity and the law motivates a new line of flight. A reorganization of the order of governance that radically alters the current political order, which is constituted precisely through the erasure of this trace of animals.
Wolfe, post-human, professor at Rice, 2003
[Cary, Animal Rites: American Culture, the Discourse of Species, and Posthumanist Theory, p. 66-78]
And yet Derrida's recent work moves... upon this experience of compassion" (40).


The dead animal factory looms in the bowels of the social body, purging the Human of its animality. Recognizing the not-just economic function of this Concentrated Animal Feeding Operation requires the prior act of unearthing its ontological subsidization in contemporary governance.

The 1AC is not a closure of this criticism, but instead represents a crucial opening of the political to the question of animality. To vote affirmative is to mark the trace of the excluded animals, which run through the factory farm and subsidy law, down to our own subjectivities. Such a trace deconstructs the boundaries between Human and Animals and opens new spaces for ethical action.
Wolfe, post-human, 2003
[Cary, Animal Rites: American Culture, the Discourse of Species, and Posthumanist Theory, p.78-80]
