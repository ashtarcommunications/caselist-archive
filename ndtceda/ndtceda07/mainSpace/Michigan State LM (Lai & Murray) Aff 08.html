The United States Federal Government should offer the government of Iran a comprehensive settlement of diplomatic issues. This offer should include –

- A security guarantee which commits to not preemptively use force to change the borders or form of government of Iran;
- A commitment to end unilateral sanctions against Iran;
- The reestablishment of diplomatic relations;
- A commitment to support Iran’s application for accession to the World Trade Organization;
- A commitment to terminate Iran’s designation as a state sponsor of terrorism; and
- An agreement to the commencement of an ongoing strategic dialogue with Iran as a forum for assessing each side’s implementation of its commitments.

The United States Federal Government will make clear that its implementation of this offer is dependent on Iran’s acceptance of the following conditions:

- Iran should agree to limit its fuel cycle activities to a delayed limited enrichment program.
