The First Fight

Human activity is characterized by negation: When I eat an apple, I negate the apple in its current form in order to create something else - energy for myself. When I build a house, I must negate a forest in it's current form to transform it into something else. However, this negation isn't enough to sustain us. This can't explain why people engage in such wholesale destruction and negation - like when we kill and die for country, honor, or love. Human behavior is unique in our desire for the recognition of others; we want respect, we want love, and we want adoration. Go back to the beginning of civilization and you will find The First Fight, a fight to the death between two humans for pure recognition.

Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel. "Autonomy and Dependence of Self-Consciousness: Mastery and Slavery." Phenomenology of Spirit. Originally published in 1807. Reproduced in Introduction to the Reading of Hegel: Lectures on The Phenomenology of Spirit. By Alexandre Kojeve. Assembled by Raymond Queneau. Edited by Allan Bloom. Translated from French by James H. Nichols. 1980. Pages 3-9.
