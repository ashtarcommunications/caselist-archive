Kentucky GO (Mike Gentile & AJ O'Donnell) Pre-Tournament Disclosure

Observation One: Eyes Wide Shut
Status quo representations of modern “Factory Farms” revolve around discursive practices of euphemisms to water down and conceal the inherent violence towards nonhuman animals for the purposes of convenience and economics. These off-topic discussions precludes any ethical questions surrounding mass slaughter
Glenn 2004 /Cathy, a doctoral candidate @ the Department of Speech Commutations @ Southern Illinois University, “Constructing Consumables and Consent: A Critical Analysis of Factory Farm Industry Discourse,” Journal of Communication Inquiry; 28/

Thus the plan- Resolved: The United States federal government should substantially reduce its agricultural support by eliminating all United States’ domestic subsidies for Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations.

Observation Two: Apocalypse Cow
CAFOs have been transformed by modern social practices that allow a social and ethnical distancing from the act of killing an animal – this reinforces the distinction between human and non-human and absolves all individual responsibility to non-human animals
Smith 2002 /Mick, Division of Sociology @ the University of Abertay, “The ‘Ethical’ Space of the Abattoir: On the (In)huma(e) slaughter of Other Animals, Human Ecology Review, Vol. 9, No. 2/

This unethical distancing from non-human animals results in a slippery slope that blurs the lines between what can and cannot be killed, or dominated. Violence towards non-human animals is historically the root cause of all forms of violence and domination
Charkowski 2004 /Elaine, Free Lance, Santa Cruz based writer for feminist and environmental causes, “An injusry to one is an injury to all! Animal Rights linked to human rights,” Indymedia, Newswire, November, http://santacruz.indymedia.org/newswire/display/12905/index.php/


The exclusion of non-human animals from our ethical and moral considerations put humans value on a pedestal. These arguments are inherently self-imploding and are the drawing of arbitrary lines that allow anything to be excluded. Only by ending factory farming can we redraw our decision calculus to include all sentient beings
Wilson 2006 /Scott, Department of Philosophy @ the University of California, Santa Barbara, “Animals and Ethics,” The Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy, http://www.iep.utm.edu/a/anim-eth.htm#SSH3a.iii/

Observation Three- Fowl Play (Yes, We think we’re clever)
Studies show that Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations are responsible for the deaths of over 11 billion non-human animals per year. These factory farms represent the largest facet of any other animal slaughter programs
Animals and Society Institute 2006 /A nonprofit and independent research and educational organization, “Animals in Agriculture Position Paper, July, http://www.animalsandsociety.org/resources/details.php?id=3/

Factory farming is a moral issue; there is a prima facie burden to withdraw our support for factory farms because it is inherently an unethical slaughterhouse of innocent animals
Engel 1978 /Mylan, Professor of Philosophy @ Northern Illinois University, “The Immorality of Eating Meat,” The Moral Life: An Introductory Reader in Ethics and Literature, p. 867-70/
Contention Four- Viva La Cow
The government pumps billions of dollars a year into CAFOs; these subsidies are the life blood of the factory farms. Only by ending these subsidies can CAFOs be put out of business for good
Starmer 2007 /Elanor, Agriculture policy/communications consultant, Bucking the CAFO tax: A plea for conscientious objection,” Referencing the Union of Concerned Scientist’s Report, July, http://www.ethicurean.com/2008/04/24/buck_the_cafo_tax/

The most important decision is individual stances against CAFOs by personally choosing it withdraw our support from a system that constantly perpetuates mass slaughter. This first step is critical to call into question the relation of factory farm violence toward non-human animals and other facets of societal violence
Scully 2003 /Matthew, Author, Journalist, and former speech writer for George W. Bush, Dominion: The Power of Man, the Suffering of Animals, and the Call to Mercy, p. 127-130/

Finally, our challenging of the discourses surrounding the factory farms is the first and most ethical step towards breaking down constructions of nonhuman animals as our other
Glenn 2004 /Cathy, a doctoral candidate @ the Department of Speech Commutations @ Southern Illinois University, “Constructing Consumables and Consent: A Critical Analysis of Factory Farm Industry Discourse,” Journal of Communication Inquiry; 28/
