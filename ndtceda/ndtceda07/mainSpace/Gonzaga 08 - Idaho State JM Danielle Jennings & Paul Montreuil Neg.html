The 1ac is a speech given by Senator Paul Monttreuil at the 2028 Democrublican national convention, the convention is being held in Iowa and is focused on agricultural policy. During the speech Senator Montreuil will buck the usual party line (pro-subsidies) by advocating the following statement: Resolved: that the United States Federal Government should substantially reduce its agricultural support, at least eliminating nearly all of the domestic subsidies, for biofuels, Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations, corn, cotton, dairy, fisheries, rice, soybeans, sugar and/or wheat. Paul will discuss a few key issues, which happen to be supported by research:

1. the nature of politics as a desiring practice: he will contend that much of what gets said at the convention is simply ritual human calibration of desire [Thorstein Veblen, (1919) The Place of Science in Modern Civilization and Other Essays, New York: Huebsch pp. 73-74] politics has become a game of fantasizing about utopias.

2. agricultural policy typifies this desire: here is a quote from the 1ac:

"Like gods we looked across the landscape and saw it all within our omnipotent grasp, and we grasped at the earth and dug into it, pulled by the fantasy of progress. Fed by crops and domesticated animals but also the dreams of a utopia lost. The Garden could never be regained. Toiling in the fields, planting, picking, sowing, could not restore the glories of Eden. Unable to live there, but unable to forget it, Eden became part of our collective memory, and archetype longed for. We decided to transform the earth into a heaven of its own, as Thoreau wrote, We now no longer camp as for a night, but have settled down on earth and forgotten heaven. We have adopted Christianity merely as an improved method of agriculture."

3. subsidies represent our greatest utopian desires: they are rely upon a delusional nostalgia for the sacred family farmer, the dream of an abscract solution to the vulnerabilities posed by the market, and the fantasy of economic order and political stability. Senator Montreuil's speech will include quotes like these to support this premise: “Make no mistake… farm program payments have been critical to the economic survival of this nation's traditionally family-owned farm structure… Farm payments cost the average taxpayer 22 cents per day. "That's less than a pack of cheap gum! You can't even buy a can of Coke for that. Folks… I encourage each of you today to speak up for agriculture. My grandmother reminded me this week: agriculture is the backbone of this nation." [Delta Farm Press /March 10, 2006 /Budget, WTO and politics driving debate on farm bill, lexis] “There's no specific terrorist threat against our food supply, but everybody in the world should pay attention to it. We (at the USDA) are part of an overall programme to combat terrorism. We have people here in Washington and throughout the US devoting their full time and effort to this… Third US president Thomas Jefferson dreamed of a rural democracy where every prosperous family had a small farm or business… has that dream turned sour? It has not… This president has said that rural policy is very important. Therefore, family farms have a future,… I believe farmers are the same the world over. They have entrepreneurial spirit and are looking for the latest developments to keep agriculture successful. I just believe in agriculture.” [Farmers Weekly /October 6, 2006 /USA farm policy needs to change, lexis]

4. Utopianism is bad [Yannis Stavrakakis, teching fellow at the department of government at the University of Essex, 1999 Lacan and the Political]
