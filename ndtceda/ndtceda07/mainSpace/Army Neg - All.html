Pre-emptive Strikes Procedural


Their plan and their evidence confuse the terms ‘preemptive strikes’ and ‘preventive strikes’ – preemptive strikes are solely in self-defense against an imminent attack and are widely accepted as legitimate in international law. The strikes their plan WANTS to ban are preventive – these are wars like Iraq, which are fought whenever the aggressor wants, in anticipation of a threat that may materialize any time in the future. This means the plan does NOTHING about the ACTUAL way Iran strikes would go down.
Gray 07 [Colin S., Professor of International Politics and Strategic Studies at the University of Reading, England, July 2007, online: http://www.strategicstudiesinstitute.army.mil/pdffiles/PUB789.pdf, accessed September 27, 2007


This is NOT just a semantic distinction – it’s reflected in actual U.S. warfighting doctrine. The DOD dictionary defines preemption and prevention just like our evidence does – which means legally the plan doesn’t oblige the U.S. to take preventive strikes off the table, it only bans the FAR more limited category of preemptive strikes. The plan BANS the MORAL, SELF-DEFENSE type of attack, while EXPLICITLY ALLOWING the PREVENTIVE strikes that ALL their evidence indicts. Regardless of what they INTENDED, this is what their plan would ACTUALLY DO
Barela 04, Professor of Law at the University of Geneva, Winter 2004, Denver Journal of International Law and Policy, 33 Denv. J. Int'l L. & Pol'y 31, p. 32-33


Here’s how you should assess this debate – reject any and all of their evidence that doesn’t make clear its assumptions regarding the definitions of preemption and prevention. Every one of our authors makes this distinction clear and clarifies that when they’re talking about preemptive war, they’re ACTUALLY talking about preemptive war. Only setting the bar super-high for what evidence you consider in debates over preemptive war can rescue discussions of these policy choices from the hopeless lack of clarity people like the affirmative have created.
Gray 07 [Colin S., Professor of International Politics and Strategic Studies at the University of Reading, England, July 2007, online: http://www.strategicstudiesinstitute.army.mil/pdffiles/PUB789.pdf, accessed September 27, 2007
