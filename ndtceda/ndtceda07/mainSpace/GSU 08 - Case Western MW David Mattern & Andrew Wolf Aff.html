Case Western MW (David Mattern & Andrew Wolf) Pre-Tournament Disclosure

Case Western Reserve MW – GSU 2008

Plan Text: The United States Federal Government should substantially reduce its agricultural support, at least eliminating nearly all of the domestic subsidies for biofuels.

Contention One:

1. The status quo environmental policy frames the environment as an energy stockpile to be pillaged for modern technology and industry.
Foltz 1995. Bruce Foltz, Professor of Philosophy at Eckerd College and Director of the Senior Honors Program, Inhabiting the Earth, New Jersey: Humanities Press International, 1995, p.103.
2. The U.S. Federal Government provides extensive biofuel subsidies to incite exploration into alternate energy.
Christian Science Monitor 2008. Editorial, The Christian Science Monitor, 12 August 2008, Lexis.
3. Federal subsidies are indiscriminately linked to the production and consumption of biofuels.

Karey 2007. Gerald Karey, columnist, Platt’s Oligram News, October 27, 2007, Lexis.

4. Technology’s turn of matter and energy into products is coupled with the transformative of Nature into “natural resources.”
Luke 95. Timothy W. Luke, Professor of Political Science, Virginia. TechOn Environmentality: Geo-Power and Eco-Knowledge in the Discourses of Contemporary Environmentalism, Cultural Critique, No. 31, The Politics of Systems and Environments, Part II, Autumn, pp. 57-81, JSTOR), p.58.
5. Modern technology strives for the endless pursuit of efficiency in the exploitation of resources.

Thiele 95. Leslie Paul Thiele, professor of political science at the University of Florida. Timely Meditations: Martin Heidegger and Postmodern Politics. 1995 (p. 195-196)

6. The managerial environmental approach brought by the drive of modern technology leads to conceptualizing the earth as a mere standing-reserve—constructing the environment as a resource and power source.

McWhorter 1992. Ladelle, Professor of Philosophy and Women’s Studies at Richmond, Heidegger and the earth: Essays in environmental philosophy, Thomas Jefferson UP, p. 6

7. This environmentality governs by restructuring through elaborate managerial designs to realize tomorrow’s environmentally sustainable economy.
Luke 95. Timothy W. Luke, 95, University Distinguished Professor of Political Science in the College of Liberal Arts and Human Sciences @ Virginia TechOn Environmentality: Geo-Power and Eco-Knowledge in the Discourses of Contemporary Environmentalism, Cultural Critique, No. 31, The Politics of Systems and Environments, Part II, Autumn, pp. 57-81, JSTOR)
8. The power to “sustain” the population is the counterpart of the ability to destroy it.
Bernauer 1990. James Bernauer, Professor of Philosophy, Boston College, Michel Foucault’s Force of Flight, 1990, p. 141-142
Contention Two:

1. Modern man’s approach to reality—viewing everything as something that can be scientifically and mechanically understood—has emerged in the search for certainty.

Rohkramer 2005. Thomas Rohkramer, senior lecturer in Modern European History at Lancaster University, How Green Were the Nazis? Athens, Ohio: Ohio UP, 2005, p. 184.

2. This construction of political arithmetic makes even extinction seem like a viable goal.

Dillon 1996. Michael Dillon, senior lecturer in Politics and International Relations at the University of Lancaster, Politics of Security, 1996.

3. Our policy solution facilitates the cause of ontological questioning.

Dillon 96. Michael Dillon, The Politics of Security, 1996. Michael Dillon is the Senior Lecturer in Politics and International Relations at the University of Lancaster, p. 6.

4. Through “releasement” in attitudes toward technology, we can learn to value the environment as something other than raw materials.

Rohkramer 2005. Thomas Rohkramer, senior lecturer in Modern European History at Lancaster University, How Green Were the Nazis? Athens, Ohio: Ohio UP, 2005, p. 191.

5. Ontological questioning resolves conflicts produced by modern technology and environmental management.

Rohkramer 2005. Thomas Rohkramer, senior lecturer in Modern European History at Lancaster University, How Green Were the Nazis? Athens, Ohio: Ohio UP, 2005, p. 190.

6. Only through reformulating our conception of politics can we resolve the implications of environmental management – the status quo ensures the perpetuity of violence.

Duarte 2005. Andre Duarte, “Thinking the Shadow of the Rupture: Politics and Philosophy in Hannah Arendt,” 27 April 2005. < http://hannaharendt.net/>.


7. U.S. action is necessary – European producers use a “splash and dash” scheme to exploit U.S. biofuel subsidies.
Macalister 2008. Terri Macalister, columnist, “Demands for crackdown on biofuels scam: US ‘splash and dash’ loophole undermines climate change fight,” The Guardian, 1 April 2008, Lexis.
8. Solvency requires a fusion of theory and praxis for which our conceptualization of the environment plays a crucial role.

Hirokawa 2(Keith, Stanford Environmental Law Review, June 2002, 21 Stan. Envtl. L.J. 225)
