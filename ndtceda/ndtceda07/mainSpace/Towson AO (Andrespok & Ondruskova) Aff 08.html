1/Status Quo
  • 2006 – Middle East unstable – cultural, religious, political, strategic importance
  • Cohen of BBC points out most of Gaza receives UN rations
  • Changes coming to restrict movement, work, electricity, services in Gaza
  • Bombings of Hamas
  • Arab-Israeli conflict eludes resolution – all solutions are met with the response that they are unviable
  • Fatigue setting-in in the Middle East and elsewhere
  • Peace process failings used to divert attention from countries domestic woes
  • 2007 – Wake up call – Hamas won election, considered legit
  • Hamas triumph is part of a pattern – Morocco, Egypt (Muslim Brotherhood), Hezbollah in Lebanon – all Islamic victories
  • Western governments won’t engage Islamists – concerned about autocratic allies’ stability
  • Active dialogue is necessary
  • UN and NGOs say situation is reaching humanitarian crisis
  • UK diplomat who went to Northern Ireland and engaged says West in perfect position to engage in ME
  • US must realize it can’t just take sides, Hamas is key to long term solution, must engage Hamas
  • Need constructive engagement of Hamas – open dialogue

2/Future

  • In terms of this year’s resolution, we advocate that to enable solvency for the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, the United States Federal Government should offer Hamas a security guarantee by taking them off the Foreign Terrorist Organizations list and include them in talks about the future of the Israeli-Palestinian disputes and the future of the Middle East.
  • This means inviting the Israeli government and the leadership of Fatah, Hamas, Israel to the table. Must include non-state entities in negotiations. Must be no preconditions – those would start dialogue with unequal treatment.
  • We need to win 3 things – 1) Removal from the terrorist list is key to peace process, 2) Hamas will cooperate, 3) US has to take the first step
  • 1) Terrorist list stops negotiations now – can’t cooperate with terrorists
    • Hamas is not going away, so need to satisfy their demands.
    • Labeling as terrorists is dehumanizing and legitimizes violence – quote says it discredits those labeled, treats them outside the norm, not to be rationalized with, stops negotiations, legitimizes US violence against
    • Blurs distinction between liberatory forces and fringe fanatics
    • Rhetoric of terrorism justifies violence
    • Need to denounce dichotomies
  • 2) Hamas will cooperate
    • They have strategic incentive to cooperate, but perceive efforts now are not genuine
    • SQ makes violence necessary as response
