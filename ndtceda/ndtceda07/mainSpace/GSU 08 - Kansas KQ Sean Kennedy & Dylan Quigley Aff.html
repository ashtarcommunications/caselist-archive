Having detailed what … a return to the particular.

This fidelity to the Event demands from us a fundamental reframing of the question of rice subsidies – Via Campesinas shows that our understanding of the corporate rice subsidy system must break with the economism that depoliticizes the question of food security, one that relies on the dumping of artificially cheap commodities on the international market. Failure to actualize this shift leads to cycles of dispossession and destruction, physically expelling peasants from their land, and epistemologically removing them from history.

And yet we are drawn to be unfaithful – we are reminded of how engrained this view of food security is, how surely our meager act will fail to break free from it – and yet, despite the seemingly impossibility of the odds of success in the face of the dominance of status quo political economy the Event of Lee’s death calls to us again and again to keep going, breaking through the knowledges of the dominant order - By what means could we ever rationalize such a momumentous act, what value can we find in this man’s senseless destruction of his own life in a demand simply to “let them farm again!” We find here an agarian ontology – one that demands we reframe the question of subsidies in terms of the primacy of food itself and forces us to shape policies that allow for an empowering relation of all peoples to the food they eat and produce.

The ability of this poor farmer’s narrative to cut through existing epistemic codes calls us to realize that his sacrifice was not one person’s particular demand, but instead pointed towards a foundational political truth unique to this moment in the world- it is thus around this Event’s universal re-framing of food and political ontology that we must unite if we are to fight abstract economism and make a radical break with the status quo.
