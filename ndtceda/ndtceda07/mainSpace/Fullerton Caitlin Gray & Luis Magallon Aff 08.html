Across the border of Mexico and the United States, dozens of crosses spot the landscape—people that have lost their lives crossing a “no man’s land.” “In spots all along the border wall, those who have lost loved ones have been memorializing those who have died while crossing the border. At one of these memorials where the wall reaches the ocean is a tally of the lives that have been lost under Operation Gatekeeper. And a question is posed--"How many more?" Reading the names, ages, and state of origin is a vivid reminder that these aren't statistics--these are individuals. Quite a few of those who died could not be identified, so the listing often reads ‘No Identificado.’”[1]

“As long as migrants have been around, they have had their own saints to pray to, taking their consoling images with them on their treacherous journeys. … Many come to … a small Mexican highland town, to pay tribute to Saint Toribio Romo… a priest [that was shot and killed in an ambush,] martyred…when a vengeful government persecuted the Catholic church … Many believers say Romo has returned to help them face down thirst, hunger and the U.S. Border Patrol in their …crossing to find jobs in the United States. … Romo is only the latest Mexican saint to answer the migrants’ prayers. The U.S.-Mexican border is an open wound where the Third World grates against the first and bleeds. And before a scab forms it hemorrhages again, the lifeblood of two worlds merging to form a third country—a border culture. Borders are set up to define the places that are safe and unsafe, to distinguish us from them. … A borderland is a vague and undetermined place created by the emotional residue of an unnatural boundary. It is in a constant state of transition. The prohibited and forbidden are its inhabitants: the perverse, the queer, the troublesome, the mongrel, the mulatto, the half-breed, the half dead; Do not enter, trespassers will be raped, maimed, strangled, gassed, shot. Ambivalence and unrest reside there and death is no stranger. This is what it’s like to be in the borderlands.

Similar to the U.S.-Mexican border, the Israel-Palestine wall border is an open wound where the communities grate against barbed wires and bleed. The West Bank wall is a barrier consisting of vehicle-barrier trenches, high-tech surveillance, and concrete linings. Built along the 1949 Armistice line, or “Green Line”, the wall has been creeping into Palestinian territory, and the barrier is said to continue its construction, which was supposed to end in 2005. “In January 2006 there were 471 physical obstacles [checkpoints] placed by the Israeli army on roads … manned by army personnel, through which both vehicles and pedestrians must pass. Currently there are approximately fifty manned checkpoints throughout the West Bank. … New terminals are designed to provide maximal protection to the soldiers. … When the terminals were designed, parental considerations regarding the well-being of the soldiers at the checkpoints gave this issue precedence.”[1]

A small group of women who became aware of the expansion and growing significance of the checkpoints…our summary of the Saturday shift, as any other day, is that the soldiers of the Israeli army, stood there according to their nature and the instructions of the day and abused by the Palestinian people because it is Palestinian. That is more or less all.

The US- Mexican border and the Israel- Palestine barriers’ colonizing agendas are maximized at these checkpoints. The psychic injury that is created by the separation of the wall and the everyday form of humiliation at checkpoints is the biggest impact. Ruchama Marton and Dalit Baym describe the psychological effect that the wall has where splitting is the psychological defense mechanism. It does not reduce the anxiety, only blocks it; it does not enable working through the causes of the anxiety, but just distances them. The physical separation from the wall cretes alienation between the Mexicans Americans and Israelis- Palestinians creating a system of distrust and the erasure of the other’s face in the realm of the local, domestic, foreign and international. Psychic injury through thepsychic splitting is what drives individuals to destroy each other, distrust each other eithin their communities and against their neighbors. Psychic injury propogates the injurious forms in politics in which every form of engagement between indivduals is tainted by distrust, frar, and violent opposition. Our method is not intended to demonize either the Israelis or Palestinians since we recognize that violence occurs against both sides. Our affirmative is not a stance against all borders, the Israeli nation, or a mere criticism of security! Rather, our intention is to recognize the psychic destruction of the invisible border peoples that resulted from the western separation of Israelis and Palestinians, just like we relate to immigrant families in the border of Mexico and the US.
