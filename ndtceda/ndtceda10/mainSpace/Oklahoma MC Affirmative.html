In Arizona and all across the nation, laws have been passed restricting and controlling the unrestrained flow of outsiders into the country. However, these policies didn’t come from nowhere: somewhere in the political machinery the masses demanded a channeling and containment of the flows from the outside. The people must have desired the regulation and organization of immigrant populations based upon their utility and legal status. And so we begin our investigation into the resolution with a few questions: WHY is it the majoritarian masses desire the coding and capture of the minor? What is the linkage between this desire and State control? And why, when confronted with difference, does the majority always respond with fear and a process of territorialization and nationalism?
