Dear Mr. Repko: Thank you for your correspondence of October 23, 2010 to the U.S. Consulate General in Hyderabad. A valid non-immigrant visa is just one criterion for entry into the United States. It does not automatically permit entry. A visa simply indicates that one is eligible to apply for entry to the country for a specific purpose. Permission to enter the United States and how long the applicant may remain in the U.S. is determined by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Bureau of Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) at the port of entry. A visa allows the visa holder to travel to the United States as far as the port of entry (airport or land border crossing). The USCIS Officer at the port of entry determines whether to allow a visa holder to enter the country and also to decide how long the person can stay for any particular visit. More information can be found on the website: www.uscis.gov. We hope this information is useful to you.
