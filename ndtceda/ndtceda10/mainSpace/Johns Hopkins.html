Soul Death is not simply a “quality of life” argument, but is the root cause behind all forms of conflict and war. In order to heal the wounds of soul death, we must create a site for breaking down the border.

Therefore, our framework is the judge should vote for who best solves for soul death.

Our advocacy statement is the debate community should affirm the topic as a spiritual act against all borders that exist in our lives.





Our advocacy to solve for soul death is to actively resist systems of bordered identities through affirming the topic spiritually. This affirmation recognizes the soul in all beings around us and the connectedness that the revelation of the soul reveals. We do not deny or desire to destroy difference, but instead we orient the soul as the starting point for our activism against systems of domination. This orientation allows us to build a new narrative where what connects us is more important than what divides us, allowing us to break down the borders that exist in our lives.
ANZALDUA IN 2002 [Gloria, This Bridge We Call Home, pg. 257-258]










We understand that affirming a topic about visas to break down borders sound contradictory, but we affirm this contradiction. Bordered identities are built to create order and destroy contradictions, ambiguities, and difference. The only way to create a new consciousness and solve for soul death is to affirm the contradictions that do not fit in an ordered society. Affirming contradictions helps to bridge divisions and heals our souls in the process
ANZALDUA IN 1999 [Gloria, “Towards a New Consciousness”, Borderlands/ La Frontera, pg. 16-18]









Normally the ballot is a tool for voting for or against a policy. We rupture this interpretation by viewing the ballot as a bridge-building tool. Voting affirmative is an act of connection where the judge connects their decision to our advocacy, legitimizing the place of the spirit in debate. Tim Wise, a former policy debater, speaks to the power of the ballot in 2007.
[Tim Wise, 2007, White Like Me]

The ballot is not for the creation of a movement to change debate, but instead creates a moment of rupture where change is birthed from the old through building bridges across the borders that normally separate us. The multiplication of these moments creates a movimiento, a collection of small acts that creates a quiet storm that changes debate, our nation, and our world from the inside out.
ANZALDUA IN 1990 [Gloria, “Haciendo Caras, Una Estrada”, Making Face, Making Soul, pg. xxvii]
