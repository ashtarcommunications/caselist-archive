T – Admissibility

INTERPRETATION. Admissibility standards apply to all visas under INA §212(a), while eligibility standards relate to specific categories of visas. Eligibility allows you to receive a visa before you travel, while admissibility gets you into the US once you have arrived.
A. Tilman, "An Overview of Immigration Law," http://www.pbl.net/IMMIGRANT_SELECTION_SYSTEM.doc [date of essay checked with author by email on 7-25-10 DBH]
B. The Act establishes a two check system as the main mechanism for deciding which immigrants are accorded lawful permanent residence.[1] These two checks correspond to two distinct types of limitation on the incoming flow of immigrants. The first limitation is based on the concept of ELIGIBILITY TO FILE AN APPLICATION TO IMMIGRATE. It corresponds to the limits in the Act on the number of immigrants that can be admitted in different categories each year. The second limitation is based on the concept of the individual alien’s ADMISSIBILITY, i.e., certain individual characteristics or attributes that the prospective immigrant must have, or must NOT have, in order to be “lawfully admitted for permanent residence.”[2]
