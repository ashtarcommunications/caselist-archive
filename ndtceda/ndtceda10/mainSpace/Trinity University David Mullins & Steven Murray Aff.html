Status quo movements for same sex visas are failing miserably. Couples are regulated by the state, forced to conform to a predetermined vision of the “good” Americanized queer.
Capitalism has overcoded visa applications – enough cash money can overcome moral failings and green-light entrance to the United States. All the while, any successful applicants reinscribe a model of the proper citizen properly following proper protocols – a model which is impossible to match for a majority of individuals across the globe – which means that same sex visa applicants are unknowingly and unwillingly creating the means for their own exclusion.

In order to understand why this micropolitical movement is failing, we must understand the history of capitalist accumulation and power politics which have resulted in the modern political ethos. In order to fight for the queer rights, we must fight the very conception of a proper family, proper interpersonal connections, and a proper relation towards the state. We must fight for a vision of the family which has no predetermined image, accepting that what an individual considers their family can look any which way at all and has no transcendent meaning. Because the point of contention upon which we fight is an ever shifting line of struggle to be successful requires a fluid political strategy that can overcome the blockages of neo-imperialism
