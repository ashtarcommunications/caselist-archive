Narrative on white privilege

Immigration as a phenomenon, understood within the context of American nationalism produces a ladder of assimilation for the immigrant. How does the nation-state produce itself to define the belonging of Others? How does collective identity justify violence? The policy-oriented discussions that is brought forth by the resolution attempts to liberalize and expand the bodies that exist within America, but these bodies do not necessarily belong. Bodies are relegated to become calculable figures to justify one policy option or another. Lives are disposable to the logic of capital, and personhood erased. Immigration unfolds into the ways that the state purports an ideological figure—that of the normative citizen. Immigrants are juxtaposed and bodies are marked and exiled into the periphery. We don’t account for the lives that are affected after these policies. Violence becomes possible because they are undesirable, they are threats to the stable narrative that legitimizes America’s imperialism domestic and abroad.

We begin with a critical affirmation of the immigrant within the resolution. It is to affirm the lives of those who would be affected by visas, by illegality and by the ways America define its populous and does violence to those who are relegated to the margin of the nation state. The ballot is an endorsement of our politics that centers around the figure of the immigrants. It sends a message to the debate community that we need to reorient our politics toward the material realities of immigrants.

The resolution functions as the law that codifies experiences of immigrant debaters and debaters of color and erases the lived experience by treating oral histories as afterthought. Narratives are threats to the stable coherence and otherwise smooth functioning debate space. Those who are intimately tied to the topic are crowded out in the argumentative bombardment of policy debate through instrumentalization of the topic.

Reid-Brinkley (08). "The Harsh realities of acting black: How African American policy debaters negotiate representation through performance and style" pg 15-17


Instead, we should center our political alternatives around the violence that immigrants undergo and to better their material realities. The ballot should be awarded to who better performatively deconstructs white citizenship within debate. We best do this because our advocacy centers on the figure of the immigrant in order to elucidate the complex legalistic forms of oppression which constraint the political possibilities for meaningful and happy lives for immigrants.

Thus, we affirm the figure of the immigrant within the topic
