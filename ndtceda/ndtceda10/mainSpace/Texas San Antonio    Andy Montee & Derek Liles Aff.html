Resolved: the United States Federal Government should substantially increase the number of and/or substantially expand beneficiary eligibility for its visas for one or more of the following: employment-based immigrant visas, nonimmigrant temporary worker visas, family-based visas, human trafficking-based visas.

Against the grain of the perfect set of language games we have been given, we assert the un-speak-ability of the resolution. Our affirmation is a bearing witness to those stories left out by a resolution which extends the differential valuation of migrants in its prescription of what forms of immigration are worth discussing. It is a respect for difference and alterity within the resolution and is a solidarity with the stories unable to be told by the immigrant survivors of exploitation and oppression. The affirmative is an encounter with affect, with the stories of domestic labor on a visceral level, that ties us to the very bodies of labor that have been left behind in the social story of America.
