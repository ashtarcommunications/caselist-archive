Scene 2: Alternative


On November 30, 1999, activists from around the globe descended on Seattle to protest the WTO Ministerial Conference of 1999. On the streets of Seattle, Teamsters united with Turtles to issue a new demand for fair, democratic global trade. As noted ecofeminist and seed activist Vandana Shiva argues, “when labour joins hands with environmentalists, when farmers from the North and farmers from the South make a common commitment to say ‘no’ to genetically engineered crops, they are not acting in their special interests. They are defending the common interests and common rights of all people, everywhere” (para. 11). The announcement that the W.T.O. Ministerial would not happen was a “watershed towards the creation of a global citizen-based and citizen-driven democratic order” (para. 16). The W.T.O. protests were the birth of a new global citizen’s movement. “In their diversity,” Shiva concludes, “citizens were united across sectors and regions” (para. 12).

Our performance is a demand for global citizenship. Global citizenship implies an end to the tyranny of state citizenship and enables the people to reappropriate biopolitical control.
