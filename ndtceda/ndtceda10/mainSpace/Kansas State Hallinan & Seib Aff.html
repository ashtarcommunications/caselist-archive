My stories, woven through words, emerge from a play with shadows; the way in which I position myself aligns with all around me so that we can be the results. An alteration of ideas, drawn from the chaotic ether, ephemeral until placed into a given form. What have I formed? My stories and self take on a particular meaning, leaning towards the tragic, as fate would have it, the borderland art. Douglass Smith explains
tragedy “represents a fusion of the Apollonian and the Dionysian, a combination of the plastic art of sculpture with the formless art of music which produces a powerful visible representation of a spiritual state.”[[file:/C:/Users/Steven/Downloads/1ac round 3.doc#_ftn2|[2]]]

This state is embodied in presentation, offered for affirmation, a permutation of two strands. Act 3: The Resolution


The confrontation operates on various levels. Levelling first with the criticism aimed at the Bakkai, that Euripides shot too high in his desire for tragedy to make sense rationally, imposing the Socratic promise of knowledge, never knowing the ecstatic release of tragic mythic art, he departs with aggressive rationalism, rejecting pessimism, we return both with the chorus, affirming tragic as joyous. Creating balance.

The second level operates in the world of the round, how the sound presents a mode for affirmation, consideration called for the tragic approach, approach this as a member of the chorus, your voice joins with us through the ballot, and forget the limited approach of Pentheus and the US, no longer suppress the inevitable force. Lend your force to our message:

The United States Federal Government should extend visas to Dionysos.

This brings us to the third level on which the presentation works, in the search for how to approach immigration, we offer the tragic method of affirmation. This approach moves away from the individual in embracing the collective, the chorus neither proves no disproves the Resolution. There is no way to resolve such a stance without regressing back in to the trance of the Socratic will to truth. Forget truth and look to the strength of our values.

Valuing beneficence flows naturally from the position, supports the supposition of the guest-friendship model, may our speech be modelled in the Greek tradition, creating new models necessary to the task of recreating our relationship to the Dionysian other. In the worlds of Allessand Von Burg “the "robust ignorance" of ancient people prompted an imaginative effort through which they created wonderful and sublime concepts … imagination creates ("imagine a thing and at once believe it") and that poetic wisdom originates in the senses and in the ability to imagine through metaphors … the world of nations is clearly a human creation; while all nations "think they were the very first to invent the comforts of human life, and they preserve memories of their history from the beginning of the world," their time eventually … creations have logical appeal because imagination draws on our reservoir of knowledge and memories to shape new visions that become common sense, because we never really let go of what we already accept as valid .”

Our process of creation is particularly necessary with the history of immigration. For “An understanding of citizenship as separated from nationality and tied to residency, however, is revolutionary and unorthodox. We don't have the proper language to describe it logically, and we compensate for this linguistic lack with vivid images that connect what we do not know with what we are familiar with. …New visions require new metaphors they are imagined first, but they are based on what we already know.”[[file:/C:/Users/Steven/Downloads/1ac round 3.doc#_ftn4|[4]]]

The Dionysian visa is a rhetorical and ontological intervention, a necessary invention to shape the way we integrate as immigrants.
