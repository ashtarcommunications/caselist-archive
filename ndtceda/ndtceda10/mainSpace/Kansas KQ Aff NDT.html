In the height of McCarthy-era xenophobia and the “gay” panic being led J. Edgar Hoover the Senate drafted immigration legislation to protect true Americans from the constant threat of psychopathic sexual deviants.
Senator McCarran led the crusade arguing for more exclusions more protections for real American citizens.
The McCarran-Walter Act of 1952 establishes homosexuality and all manner of sexual perversion as “psychopathic personalities.” If the INS could illicit a confession of sexual deviance including homosexual acts prior to entry into the US they could revoke a visa and deport the person. The eligibility requirement was created to prohibit the entrance of gay individuals into the United States.
