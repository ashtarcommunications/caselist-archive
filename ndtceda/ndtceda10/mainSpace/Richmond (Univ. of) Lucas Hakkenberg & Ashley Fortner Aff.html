Liberty Round 1 - 1AC

1AC – Painting the Status Quo

In Tropic Orange, Bobby travels to Mexico to help a small Chinese girl—who lost her brother when traveling from China—illegally enter the U.S.. He disguises her as an ‘American’ by adorning her with U.S. commodities:
“Get her a T-shirt and some jeans and some tennis shoes,” Bobby says, “Jeans say Levi’s. Shoes say Nike. T-shirt says Malibu. That’s it. . . . We’ll drag her through the slit jus’ like any Americanos. Just like Visa cards.”
Performing American consumerism, they are baptized in the free-flow of Capital.
At this border crossing, the question of the resolution makes itself manifest. The “visa/Visa-card” trope folds—intersects! collapses!—onto itself as both a signed certificate (expressing permission to enter) and a plastic placard (signifying each being as a subject of the “law of economic necessity.”)
But as a Visa card, they are only as good as their credit limit.
This miming of the “American,” this critical inhabiting of the Visa card, enables Bobby and his companion to cross the border into the U.S., but, “Cuz is staring at her new Nikes,” Bobby notes, “‘Made in China.’ Nikes get in. But not its maker.”
This tropical collapse reveals that we no longer face a problem of negotiating a national identity with diasporic individuals—the American landscape has been completely mortgaged to global capital. “Nationality” defines a brand-name. “American consumerism” is our brand.
((Brief Pause))
In one sense, voices of people seeking an escape across a border are “just rhetoric,” but it is also rhetoric that is must become “just”—a rhetoric of justice! A message of “border crossing” can contribute to a rhetoric of radical justice in its ability to motivate thought and action about the border and in resistance to an oppressive and militarized line of demarcation. The borders and binaries we are interested in debating in this debate are also here—in fact, they begin here—and they are physical—material—even territorial, but they are also figurative, imaginary, mental, and semi-permeable. They are, above all, rhetorical.
