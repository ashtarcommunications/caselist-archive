1AC Narrative

Advocacy Statement:


Thus we present the following advocacy: Through the reading of our personal narratives we embody the Mestiza consciousness and redefine the pedagogical model through which the oppressive Borderlands is established.

Arguments embedded within the 1AC:

1) Critique of the resolution.
2) Critique of dualistic system of borders in society using Anzaldua's notions of the Borderlands and the Mestiza Consciousness.
Anzaldua (Gloria, Chicana Activist, Writer, Former Professor, Borderlands: The New Mestiza/La Frontera (Third Edition)). 2004
3) Discussion of the oppressive nature of "passing." (Taken from Sloop 2004)
Sloop (John M., Associate Dean at Vanderbilt University, Disciplining Gender: Rhetorics of Sex Identity in Contemporary U.S. Culture, p. 129). 2004
4) Discussion of the ways in which knowledge produced in the round and outside the round is important to embodying our advocacy.
5) Redefining of the pedagogical model for debate.
6) Narrative solvency (narratives are universally understood as an alternative to the rationality of the status quo, taken from
Rowland 87
Rowland (Robert C., Professor of Communication Studies, University of Kansas, Communication Monographs 54, p. 264). 1987
