Frameworkz:

The 1NC changes depending on the Affirmative, though the argument is relatively the same.
The majority of the argument is supported by Judith Butler's "Frames of War" and pieces of "Precarious Life".

We make an argument about the affirmative's ability to determine the importance of one type of visa or group of people over another. We say that the frame of the 1AC is a strategic construction used to prioritize and divide who is seen and who is not. These frames are created to protect many different things (depending on the aff).
Often times they are used to protect U.S. superiority and the U.S. as the sovereign decision maker. We read an Arundhati Roy card from "War is Peace" as an impact to this.
["People rarely win wars...isn't in the same league."]

There are two specific impacts to the argument outside of the U.S. sovereignty impact above.
1. Erasure and invisibility of violence.
2. Assimilation of migrants within the visa regime.

No cards for these impacts. Kara and I wrote the explanations of them- we use some "real-world" examples as warrants.

The alternative is to give up the frame of the 1AC and expose the hegemonic discourses that protect and deem the frame as the only way of engaging those affected in migration politics.
We use a few cards from Joanna Zylinska's "The Universal Acts and the Biopolitics and Immigration" (2004) to support this. It's easy to find, just google the title.
["Indeed, even the very...to the 'asylum' question'."]
Other than that, as I said above, most of our cards/arguments come from the two Butler books listed. We don't read too many cards.
