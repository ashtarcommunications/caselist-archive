Interpellation


The topic committee tells us: the United States Federal Government should substantially increase the number of and/or substantially expand beneficiary eligibility for its visas for one or more of the following: employment-based immigrant visas, nonimmigrant temporary worker visas, family-based visas, human trafficking-based visas.

Like the shout, “Hey you!” we all turn as if they were speaking to us. “Hey you? You mean me?”

Every day I wake to the same sound - bells – ringing, stinging, bringing… light (the) dawn, twilight, height of high noon. In bloom … soon … at sea, like the Vietnamese: boat people. Because “No man is an island, entire of itself; every man is a piece of the continent … If a clod be washed away by the sea, [America] is the less … any man's death diminishes me, because I am involved in mankind, and therefore never send to know for whom the bell tolls; it tolls for thee.”

An Indian grad, fresh out of school, applying for an H1B visa, a Mexican day laborer applies for an H2A, “I work hard! … Fornicate the judges. … I don't say 'your honor' to no judge. They don't work as hard as I do. They're not honorable!”

But that work, in one moment, is rendered irrelevant: Visa denied. Sorry, the bell wasn't tolling for you, but for her over there. Maybe we shouldn't fornicate the judges.

This hail – whether the topical hail of NDT/CEDA or the Americanizing hail of the INS – founds the individual as subject. When we respond to the hail, when we recognize our name and turn to greet our caller, we found ourselves as subject, like the migrant with a Visa, who is created as a US citizen. Yoshihko Ichida explains that:

“In any case, the conceptual figure of Malebranche's rain allows us to perceive a similarity between Schmitt's subject of decision and Althusser's subject of ideology. This ideological subject is supposedly constructed after a Pascalian logic - "Kneel down, move your lips in prayer, and you will believe" (Althusser 1971: 158) - through a 180 degree turn performed when he is "hailed," the individual becomes a subject; he does not turn because he is a subject, but becomes a subject when he turns.”

This 180 degree turn is a magical, alchemical, moment. Before the turn, we are nothing, destitute, the tired, poor, huddled masses yearning to breathe free; after, we are a subject, American, fount of creativity and autonomy, deserving of respect and protection, all because of those crucial 180 degrees.
Similarly, last June the CEDA topic committee announced this topic which interpolated us as debaters. They gave us the topic we would spend thousands of hours researching and hundreds debating over the next year. More importantly, they gave us a purpose and vision for the year ahead.

Yet this topical hail returns to us, and every round we are awoken to the same vision: Resolved! We've debated it, discarded it, affirmed it, and it's nothing, but: Resolved! We're resolved, we're interpellated, we're debaters and so we are debating, yet still: RESOLVED! We don't even need to topic committee anymore, at least not for a few more months.

“Result: caught in this quadruple system of interpellation as subjects, of subjection to the Subject, of universal recognition and of absolute guarantee, the subjects ‘work’, they ‘work by themselves’ in the vast majority of cases, with the exception of the ‘bad subjects’ who on occasion provoke the intervention of one of the detachments of the (Repressive) [Framework] Apparatus. But the vast majority of (good) subjects work all right ‘all by themselves’, i.e. by ideology (whose concrete forms are realized in the Ideological State Apparatuses). They are inserted into practices governed by the rituals of the ISAs. They ‘recognize’ the existing state of affairs ( das Bestehende), that ‘it really is true that it is so and not otherwise’, and that they must be obedient to God, to their conscience, to the [topic committee], to [framework], to the boss, to the engineer, that thou shalt ‘love thy neighbour as thyself’, etc. Their concrete, material behaviour is simply the inscription in life of the admirable words of the prayer: ‘Amen – So be it’.”

The entire year, and every debate, was decided for us by the topic committee the moment our Resolve was announced. From that moment we were debaters, from that moment we were subjects, yet Althusser explains that:

“Yes, the subjects ‘work by themselves’. The whole mystery of this effect lies in the first two moments of the quadruple system I have just discussed, or, if you prefer, in the ambiguity of the term subject. In the ordinary use of the term, subject in fact means: (1) a free subjectivity, a centre of initiatives, author of and responsible for its actions; (2) a subjected being, who submits to a higher authority, and is therefore stripped of all freedom except that of freely accepting his submission. This last note gives us the meaning of this ambiguity, which is merely a reflection of the effect which produces it: the individual is interpellated as a (free) subject in order that he shall submit freely to the commandments of the Subject, i.e. in order that he shall (freely) accept his subjection, i.e. in order that he shall make the gestures and actions of his subjection ‘all by himself’. There are no subjects except by and for their subjection. That is why they ‘work all by themselves’.”

In debate, we have one choice, debate about the topic or you can git out. Accept the topic or leave. Yet every year we have longer and longer topics, entailing more and more. We went from affirming value statements to affirming whole resolution policies to affirming an area within the topic area to being given a list of topical aff's to pick from. Now we have topics upwards of 50 words, carefully crafted to ensure the maximum of education. At all costs, we must prevent bi-directionality, limit the ground, and ensure predictable ground to the negative. We offer justifications of predictability and learning about a new topic, and with this certainty – as Foucault says – we become the bearers of our own oppression. However, Ichida says of this hailing:

“The reading of Malebranche/Schmitt leads us to change the question's direction: Is it really so important to know whether the hailing can succeed or not and under which circumstances it can succeed? Or is not already a condition for its success that it can fail, that the individual possesses every freedom to answer to the caller or not, that all is a question of free decision? Because this problem is constructed more sharply if the freedom of the individual is placed in the center of subject constitution; in short, if one allows for the individual to decide ex nihilo, preparing the theatrical device of freedom for the individual's process of becoming-subject. Not in spite but because of its freedom, the power to refuse the obedience to the caller, the individual turns around.”

A slew of critical aff's, non-topical aff's, and basically every aff we have ever read prove this to be the case. Yet every aff grapples with the same problem: How to take an active and affirmative stance towards the resolution, rather than reactive and negative, if language, morality, subjectivity, and the resolution always comes to us from elsewhere? As Althusser says, the hail offers us a unique moment, one moment, the first moment in which we are a subject – able to freely choose – and the last moment – that we are free of the regime of ideology. This is the first and last moment of freedom available to us. Yet this isn't as great a problem as it seems. Indeed, every morning I awake to those same bells. Every round I hear the same topical hail. And every round we have the choice of how we will respond. Ichida explains:

“And indeed Althusser writes "...[he] is free to obey or disobey the appeal." (Althusser 1971, 166) The freedom of the individual, the possibility that the hailing can fail, transforms the ideological initiative to an "occasion". The calling offers to the individual an occasion for this transformation: it is called and it answers by chance. To say it again in other words: the Schmittian subject of decision needs a theatrical device for its decision, which seemingly occurs ex nihilo. Just as Althusser's subject has to be hailed to become the subject. Both subjects are subject to the "occasion".”

Althusser imagines that we must answer the hail. The policeman shouts “Hey you!” and we turn. Judith Butler stepped beyond this: We may also turn away from the hail, refuse to answer. Yet this inversion remains just as much beholden to the state. Instead, we take a third option, a free option: One which, of course, decides. It turns or does not turn. But it turns freely, refusing all the offers of citizenship, democracy, topic education, fairness and ground. Because, honestly, we don't want your ground. We'll make our own ground.
