Affirmative

Text: The United States federal government should substantially increase its investment in the removal of the following federally owned dams: Ice Harbor, Little Goose, Lower Monumental, and Lower Granite.

Contention 1 is Biodiversity:

137 species rely on Snake River salmon for survival – including birds and land animals.
SOWS 09 (Save Our Wild Salmon, environmental advocacy group, 3/6/2009, “Why Restore Wild Salmon?”, Save Our Wild Salmon, http://www.wildsalmon.org/facts-and-information/faq/why-restore-wild-salmon.html)

Orca whales need the salmon – only removing the dams helps both species survive.
Hawley 10 (Steven Hawley, environmental journalist, author of Recovering a Lost River: Removing Dams, Rewilding Salmon, Revitalizing Communities, quotes Ken Balcomb, director for the Center for Whale Research on San Juan Island in Puget Sound, 2010, “The Idaho Tide,” Summer, http://www.patagonia.com/us/patagonia.go?&assetid=53761, LVS)

Solvency is reverse causal – the plan redistributes sediment which independently revitalizes riparian ecosystems.
Higgs et al 02 (Stephen Higgs of American Rivers, and edited by Elizabeth Maclin and Margaret Bowman of American Rivers and Angela Bednarek of the University of Pennsylvania. February 2002.”THE ECOLOGY OF DAM REMOVAL: A Summary of Benefits and Impacts”, http://www.michigandnr.com/PUBLICATIONS/PDFS/fishing/dams/EcologyofDamRemoval.pdf)

We should act with precaution in order to preserve as many species as possible. Biodiversity is key to promoting vital ecosystem services, reject their impact defense, it doesn’t assume the latest studies.
Science Daily 12 ("Biodiversity Critical for Maintaining Multiple 'Ecosystem Services'" Cites McGill University, August 19, **www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2011/08/110819155422.htm**, azp)

Biodiversity loss is not a discrete event but an ongoing process, as ecosystems lose richness they become more susceptible to total collapse. Reject the reductionist frame of “keystone species” – all beings are equally essential. Biodiversity is the only irreversible impact
Young 10 (Ruth Young, PhD coastal marine ecology, “Biodiversity: what it is and why it’s important”, February 9th 2010, **http://www.talkingnature.com/2010/02/biodiversity/biodiversity-what-and-why/**, azp)

Contention 2 is Speciesism:

Our relationship to salmon demonstrates our fundamental ecological assumptions
Lichatowich 1 (James, Masters in Fishery Science from Oregon State University, former Marine, Assistant Chief of Fisheries, Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife, Board Member of the Independent Scientific Advisory Board (ISAB),Salmon Without Rivers: A History Of The Pacific Salmon Crisis, pp 7-8, google books)

And---Salmon predate humans in the Pacific Northwest by millennia---we must recalibrate our decision-making away from calculated exploitation of nature toward appreciation of balance and beauty
Lichatowich 1 (James, Masters in Fishery Science from Oregon State University, former Marine, Assistant Chief of Fisheries, Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife, Board Member of the Independent Scientific Advisory Board (ISAB),Salmon Without Rivers: A History Of The Pacific Salmon Crisis, pp 1-7, google books)

Degradation of animals and nature is a part of the anthropocentric colonial machine that is US imperialism – the same machine that kills millions of humans and many times more uncounted nonhumans in its endless pursuit of profit
Boggs 2007 (Carl Boggs, Carl Boggs is the author of numerous books in the fields of contemporary social and political theory, European politics, American politics, U.S. foreign and military policy, and film studies. For two years (1999-2000) he was Chair of the Caucus for a New Political Science, a section within the American Political Science Association. In 2007 he was recipient of the Charles McCoy Career Achievement Award from the American Political Science Association, Fast Capitalism, Vol. 2, No.2, “Corporate Power, Ecological Crisis, and Animal Rights,” 2007, http://www.uta.edu/huma/agger/fastcapitalism/2_2/boggs.html, azp)

Our discussion of animal rights through the lens of the plight of the Snake River Salmon intersects with every impact – until we elevate the suffering of animals to the level of the human we will be unable to effectively address the problems of humanity – this is a takeout to all of their education claims because without addressing the issue of the belittling of animals we can never truly understand the woes of humans
Boggs 2007 (Carl Boggs, Carl Boggs is the author of numerous books in the fields of contemporary social and political theory, European politics, American politics, U.S. foreign and military policy, and film studies. For two years (1999-2000) he was Chair of the Caucus for a New Political Science, a section within the American Political Science Association. Confronting anthropocentrism in pedagogical circles is vitally important
Bell and Russel 2k (Anne C. Bell, department of education, York University, Canada, and Constance L. Russel, Associate Professor, Faculty of Education, Lakehead University, Co-Editor, Canadian Journal of Environmental Education, “Beyond Human, Beyond Words: Anthropocentrism, Critical Pedagogy, and the Poststructuralist Turn,” CANADIAN JOURNAL OF EDUCATION 25, 3 (2000):188–203, http://www.csse-scee.ca/CJE/Articles/FullText/CJE25-3/CJE25-3-bell.pdf)

Attempts to downplay violence to animals sanitize the most immoral form of disposability as embodied by the factory farm

Nietzsche

Will to Nothing

The fallacy of the affirmative is that they fail to realize that suffering and antagonism are inevitable parts of human existence. Their attempt to reorder the world within a problem-solution model binds our existence to utopian ideals and reveals their futile attempt to escape fate
Saurette 96(Paul, Prof of Political Studies @ UOttawa, “I Mistrust All Systematizers and Avoid Them: Nietzsche, Arendt, and the Crisis of the Will to Order in International Relations Theory” Millenium 25.1)


We can only hope to reduce suffering by accepting it through embrace of eternal recurrence

Kain 7– Santa Clara professor of Philosophy (Philip, Nietzsche, Eternal Recurrence, and the Horror of Existence The Nietzsche journal.)


Alternative: Vote Negative to reject the affirmative truth claims in favor of complete nihilism – only way to solve for value to life
