Evacuation

Contention 1: Duck

The national highway system was created as a mechanism for evacuation from nuclear war
Squires 9
Richard, The Interstate Sprawl System, http://www.opednews.com/articles/The-Interstate-Sprawl-Syst-by-Richard-Squires-090112-110.html
The coup de grace came from the ... as joyful as a bureaucratic picnic

Nuclear defense uses the idea of all hazards planning to create an apparent affinity between natural disasters and nuclear exchange
Clarke 99
Lee, Prof of Sociology @ Rutgers , Mission Improbable: Using Fantasy Documents To Tame Disaster, pg. 73-4
The idea of "solving problems" i... hurricanes, earthquakes, and so forth.

The apparent affinity between nuclear war and natural disasters facilitates evacuation planning, this planning is a way to pretend that we can control the uncontrollable
Clarke 99
Lee, Prof of Sociology @ Rutgers , Mission Improbable: Using Fantasy Documents To Tame Disaster, pg. 98-100
Once an affinity is created...social conflict, the topic of the next chapter.

This normalizes nuclear war and allows for the creation of artificially constructed “nuclear expertise”
Clarke 99
Lee, Prof of Sociology @ Rutgers , Mission Improbable: Using Fantasy Documents To Tame Disaster, pg. 97-8
Folding natural disasters and nuclear attack into the same category ...reliable, that it can be drawn upon

These evacuation plans legitimize nuclear war and create a system of rationality that justifies new wars, torture and terror to be unleashed on the enemy de jour
Dudely 7
Michael, Research Associate with the Institute of Urban Studies at the University of Winnipeg, Theory & Event, 10:2, Revisiting Cold War Ideology in the Secure City: Towards a Political Economy of Urbicide
20. In his 1999 book, Mission Improbable: ... foray beyond received conventional wisdom is viewed as unthinkable.¶

Thus: vote aff to increase our intellectual investment in transportation infrastructure by recognizing that nuclear evacuation plans legitimize unnecessary risks

Contention two: Cover

These plans to use freeways to evacuate are fantasy documents, they aren’t intended to be used but serve a symbolic and rhetorical purpose in order to control the frame of debate, convincing the public that the risks our leaders take in our name are acceptable, while at the same time determining who can and who can’t speak on the issue of nuclear weapons. This system results in threat construction and risks annihilation. Voting Aff ruptures current politics and enables new ways of talking about the nuclear complex
Clarke 99
Lee, Prof of Sociology @ Rutgers , Mission Improbable: Using Fantasy Documents To Tame Disaster, pg. 167-171
A short while ago I claimed that ... frankly that they can not control the uncontrollable

Nuclear weapons play a role in our everyday lives, highways are just the most obvious marker, the entirety of the national infrastructure is built around the nuclear-industrial complex, causing massive deaths world-wide
Masco 6
Joesph, Prof of Anthropology, U of Chicago, The Nuclear Borderlands, pg 24-7
The strange new intimacy of life in the nuclear age is ...has engendered new tactile experiences of everyday life in the United States, and with them, new psychosocial realities.

The role of the ballot should be to vote for the team that best challenges nuclear weapons in our everyday lives.
Now is the key time to challenge the nuclear complex that we encounter every day without realizing it. Your ballot re-writes the history of the nuclear age
Masco 6
Joesph, Prof of Anthropology, U of Chicago, The Nuclear Borderlands, pg 3-5
The “unthinkability” of the nuclear age has right ...and an anthropology of American power in the twenty-first century.

Contention 1 is the status quo sucks

To understand transportation infrastructure, we must first understand transit. To be in transit is to be made to move.
Byrd 11
Jodi A., (Chickasaw), assistant professor of American Indian studies and English at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, The Transit of Empire: Indigenous Critiques of Colonialism

This is the experience of the indigenous peoples. Transit describes the forced movement of those subjugated due to the United States' imperialist drive
Byrd 11
Jodi A., (Chickasaw), assistant professor of American Indian studies and English at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, The Transit of Empire: Indigenous Critiques of Colonialism

The history of the United States is the history of the oppressor, forcing the indigenous populations out of htier homes and out of themselves. Settler colonialism has manifested itself in a culture of sexism, homphobia, and transphobia. We must first reconclie the relation between our bodies and our histories in order to successfully spillover to our broader communities.
