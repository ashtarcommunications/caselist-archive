VC - treating people as ends

Rödl, Sebastian. Self-Consciousness, Harvard University Press, 2007.
"Suppose I walked... a unity of phases."

Further, inviolability is necessary to express the moral status of persons.

"The key idea ... determines their worth."

And, since I see myself as an end, I must extend that status to all others.

"Although Kamm's is ... to avoid and prevent."

Parfit, Derek (Philosophy Professor, Oxford University). Climbing the Mountain (Unpublished).
"According to desire-based... happiness as an end."

I contend that the possession of nuclear weapons treats people as mere means.
Churchill, Robert P. “Nuclear Arms as a Philosophical and Moral Issue.” Annals of the American Academy of Political and Social Science, Vol. 469, Nuclear Armament and Disarmament (Sep., 1983), pp. 46-57.
"Threatening civilian populations... drivers of automobiles."

Bluffs and indecision still require a conditional intention to use nukes and are impossible to practically maintain. McMahan, Jeff. “Deterrence and Deontology.” Ethics, Vol. 95, No. 3, Special Issue: Symposium on Ethics and Nuclear Deterrence (Apr. 1985), pp. 517-536.
"Suppose for the sake... such an intention."

Two constraints on self-defense: a) reciprocal, b) morally culpable. However, the possession of nuclear weapons violates both these constraints. University of Chicago Press. http://www.jstor.org/stable/2381036.
