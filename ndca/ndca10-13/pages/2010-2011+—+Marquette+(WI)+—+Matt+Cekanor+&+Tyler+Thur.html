Jeffrey T. BILE, Director of Debate at Southern Illinois University, 1987 [“When The Whole Is Greater Than The Sum Of The Parts: The Implications Of Holistic Resolutional Focus,” Contemporary Argumentation & Debate, Volume 8, Available Online at http://www.cedadebate.org/CAD/1987_bile.pdf, Accessed 10-02-2008, p. 12]

SECOND—EDUCATION: RESOLUTIONAL FOCUS PROVIDES SUPERIOR PEDAGOGICAL BENEFITS – FOCUSING ON SINGULAR EXAMPLES WEAKENS DEBATE’S EDUCATIONAL VALUE.
Jeffrey T. BILE, Director of Debate at Southern Illinois University, 1987 [“When The Whole Is Greater Than The Sum Of The Parts: The Implications Of Holistic Resolutional Focus,” Contemporary Argumentation & Debate, Volume 8, Available Online at http://www.cedadebate.org/CAD/1987_bile.pdf, Accessed 10-02-2008, p. 9]

SUBPOINT B: THE DISPLACEMENT OF RESPONSIBILITY
THE AFFIRMATIVE’S APPROACH IS A FORM OF COLLECTIVE VICTIM-BLAMING THAT STIGMATIZES ENTIRE GROUPS IN ORDER TO EXONERATE INDIVIDUAL PERPETRATORS – THEIR FLAWED METHODOLOGY DISPLACES RESPONSIBILITY AND TURNS THE CASE – THEY HAVE MISIDENTIFIED THE CAUSE OF THEIR HARMS.
Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 2-3]

SUBPOINT C: THE MOMENT OF DECISION
THE ALTERNATIVE IS TO ADOPT A DIFFERENT METHODOLOGICAL APPROACH TO VIOLENCE. INSTEAD OF VIEWING VIOLENCE AS A PRODUCT OF CIRCUMSTANCES, WE SHOULD INTERROGATE THE MOMENT OF DECISION WHEN INDIVIDUALS CHOOSE TO COMMIT VIOLENCE. THIS METHODOLOGICAL APPROACH ENABLES EFFECTIVE RESISTANCE TO THE VIOLENT IDEOLOGY OF THE AFFIRMATIVE AND IS A NECESSARY PRECONDITION TO REMEDYING THE HARMS THEY HAVE OUTLINED.
Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 5-7]

SUBPOINT D: START WITH THE “I” WORLD
FIRST – THE AFFIRMATIVE’S INFATUATION WITH INSTITUTIONAL CHANGE AT THE LEVEL OF GOVERNMENT OBSCURES OUR INDIVIDUAL RESPONSIBILITY FOR VIOLENCE – THIS ORGANIZED IRRESPONSIBILITY CONSTITUTES A WILLED REFUSAL TO MEANINGFULLY RESIST VIOLENCE. WE SHOULD BEGIN NOT WITH AN INVESTIGATION OF WHAT OTHERS SHOULD DO BUT WITH AN INVESTIGATION OF WHAT WE SHOULD DO.
Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 10-11]

Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 18-20]

Schmitt K


SUBPOINT A: FRIENDS AND ENEMIES
THE AFFIRMATIVE ECHOES THE FAMILIAR LIBERALIST COMMITMENT TO UNIVERSAL RIGHTS AND THE RULE OF LAW AND THEREBY OBSCURES THE POLITICAL REALITY OF A FRIEND/ENEMY DICHOTOMY. THIS DENIAL OF A STABLE AND PUBLIC ENEMY CREATES A VOID IN OUR OWN IDENTITY BECAUSE ARTICULATING THE DISTINCTION BETWEEN THOSE WHO ARE FRIENDS AND THOSE WHO ARE ENEMIES IS A PREREQUISITE TO DEFINING WHO WE ARE. THE AFFIRMATIVE PROJECT COLLAPSES THE POLITICAL AND UNDERMINES THE VERY FOUNDATION FOR HUMAN EXISTENCE.
S. Parvez MANZOOR, Ph.D., Islamic Author, Writer, and Researcher based in Stockholm, Sweden and Member of the Board of Advisors of the Institute of Islamic Political Thought, 2000 [“The Sovereignty of the Political: Carl Schmitt and the Nemesis of Liberalism,” Date Provided by the Internet Archive’s Wayback Machine, Available Online at http://www.algonet.se/~pmanzoor/CarlSchmitt.htm, Accessed 12-19-2005]
The totalizing thrust of Schmitt's argument is directed against liberalism, which by the postulation of a false universalism, according to him, obscures the existentially paramount nature of politics and replaces it with the struggle for purely formal notions of rights … As for liberalism's moral claim to universal humanism, Schmitt is mercilessly candid: 'The concept of humanity is an especially useful ideological instrument of imperial expansion, and in its ethical-humanitarian form it is a specific vehicle of economic imperialism.'

SUBPOINT B: GLOBAL PSYCHOSIS
THE DENIAL OF A POLITICAL ENEMY CULMINATES IN A GLOBAL PSYCHOSIS THAT MULTIPLIES LITTLE WARS AND SUSTAINS GENOCIDAL VIOLENCE. A WORLD WITHOUT A STABLE ENEMY IS INFINITELY WORST THAN A WORLD FULL OF ENEMIES – IT OPENS THE FLOODGATES TO UNPRECEDENTED LEVELS OF VIOLENCE THAT DWARF THE CONFLICTS OF THE STATUS QUO.
Kenneth REINHARD, Associate Professor of English and Comparative Literature at UCLA, 2004 [“Towards a Political-Theology of the Neighbor,” Draft of Chapter later published in Neighbor: Three Inquiries in Political Theology, Available Online at http://www.cjs.ucla.edu/Mellon/Towards_Political_Theology.pdf, Accessed 12-19-2005]
If the concept of the political is defined, as Carl Schmitt does, in terms of the Enemy/Friend opposition, the world we find ourselves in today is one from which the political may have already disappeared, or at least has mutated into some strange new shape … The rhetoric of the so-called “war on terror” is a sign of the disappearance of the traditional, localizable enemy: the terrorist does not have the stabilizing function that Schmitt associates with the enemy, but to declare war on him is to attempt to resuscitate him, to breathe life into the animus of the enemy.

SUBPOINT C: THE ALTERNATIVE
RATHER THAN ATTEMPT TO SUBJUGATE THE POLITICAL TO BENEVOLENT IDEAS LIKE PEACE, LAW, AND MORALITY, WE SHOULD EMBRACE THE FRIEND/ENEMY DICHOTOMY. THIS INTERVENTION INTO THE SPHERE OF POLITICS IS KEY TO OUR SURVIVAL.
Julien FREUND, French Intellectual and Professor of Philosophy at the University of Strasbourg, 1995 [“Schmitt’s Political Thought,” Telos: A Quarterly Journal of Critical Thought, Issue 102, Winter, Available Online via Academic Search Elite]
