Environment AC

Res refers only to nuclear weapons in existence today
1. common definition
2. greater depth b/c of more literature

We should adopt a life-centered ethic to evaluate our actions; we should endeavor to maintain the most life possible.
W/o life, no morality, so pre-req to eval "ought" claims.
We have obligations to human and non-human members of global community b/c:
1. In order for human beings to be the only beings endowed with moral status, then there must be some nontrivial property P that all humans and only humans possess, but any P that humans have is a property that some humans lack and a property that most animals have as well.
2. Rationality/autonomy/morality are not P b/c some humans not have (babies, etc)
3. We give moral rights to beings that are a subject-of-a-life.
Tom Regan Professor Emeritus NC-State Department of Philosophy The Case for Animal Rights 1983 p. 24﻿3
"Beliefs and desires; ... anyone else's interests."

The affirmative's thesis is that the creation, maintenance, and use of nuclear weapons are fundamentally destructive to human and non-human life.

A. The creation of nuclear weapons is fundamentally destructive to human and non-human life.

The waste remaining after the extraction of uranium, called mill tailings, is disastrous for the environment.

Arjun Makhijani and Scott Saleska 1 “The Production of Nuclear Weapons and Environmental Hazards” Nuclear Wastelands: A Global Guide to Nuclear Weapons Production and its Health and Environmental Effects MIT Press 2000
"Mill tailings account ... only 1000 years."


Uranium conversion is a major occupational and health hazard.
Arjun Makhijani and Scott Saleska 2
"The handling of ... as chemical wastes."

EPA "Radtown" April 2006
"Uranium mining releases ... radiation health hazards."

B. The maintenance of nuclear weapons presents continuing harm to the environment that lasts for generations.

Arjun Makhijani “A Readiness to Harm” Nuclear Wastelands: A Global Guide to Nuclear Weapons Production and its Health and Environmental Effects MIT Press 2000 p. 9
"Since World War ... of such wastes."

These harms are empirically verified.

Howard Hu and Arjun Makhijani “The Global Picture: Summary and Recommendations” Nuclear Wastelands: A Global Guide to Nuclear Weapons Production and its Health and Environmental Effects MIT Press 2000
"Highly radioactive liquid ... in other countries."

Artifacts NC

Observation 1
Res question concerns the possession of an item, not the use and/or intent to use. Use requires possession, but possession does not require use. The judge does not have jurisdiction to vote on arguments that concern use/intent. Reject AC for being extratopical.

Observation 2
Negation = not x.
Merrie Bergmann, Smith College, The Logic Book
"One might be ... of the former."

Thus, the negative is defending: It is not the case that states ought not possess nuclear weapons.

My thesis and sole contention is that morally prohibiting nuclear weapons denies human subjectivity.

A nuclear weapon is a type of technological device known as an artifact (an object made by a human being); artifacts are value-neutral.
Per Sundstrom, "Interpreting the notion that technology is value-netural" Medicine, Health Care and Philosophy Vol 1 41-45 1998
"That techniques and ... contemplate using them."

Sundstrom 2
"Focusing value neutrality ... a residual neutrality."

Analysis

Artifacts only acquire value upon their use by humans. Simple possession of an artifact imparts no value to that artifact either positive or negative, nor does the artifact have any intrinsic value. It is absurd to imbue artifacts with value independent of humans because artifacts cannot create their own value and have no subjectivity. This value-neutral viewpoint toward technology is the only one that allows for human agency.


Sundstrom 3
"The value neutral ... in its possibility."

Analysis
It is only when our decisions have effects that we willed ourselves that we can be moral actors; if artifacts posses value prior to human action, then questions of morality are unduly influenced by non-moral agents. Thus negating is the only avenue for maintaining moral agency.
