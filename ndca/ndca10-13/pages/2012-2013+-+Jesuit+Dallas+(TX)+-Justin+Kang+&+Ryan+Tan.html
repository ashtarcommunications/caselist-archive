Affirmative

Plan

After considering multiple philosophical perspectives, we offer the following plan:
Through then existing means, the United States federal government should substantially increase investment for modernization of inland waterway systems in the United States. Requisite funding provided.

1AC Outline-Grapevine

Contention 1-Inherency
Failure to understand importance of inland waterway system means inadequate funding and collapsing lock system—failure to act now risks a system that is functionally obsolete by 2020
American Society of Civil Engineers 2008 *
(Infrastructure Report Card, “Inland Waterways”, 2008, http://www.infrastructurereportcard.org/fact-sheet/inland-waterways, accessed 9/4/12, Bill)
Because of their ability to move large amounts of cargo, the nation’s inland waterways are a strategic economic and military resource… With an average rehabilitation cost of $50 million per lock, the current level allows the Corps to fully fund only two or three lock projects each year.

Additionally, current “fix when fail” policy towards system ensures delays and collapse of inland waterways
Boselovic, award winner for business and investigative reporting, 2012
(Len “THE NATION'S LOCKS AND DAMS, INCLUDING 23 IN REGION, ARE ON THE BRINK OF FAILURE, ACCORDING TO U.S. ARMY CORPS OF ENGINEERS” , Pittsburgh Post 3/18/2012 http://old.post-gazette.com/pg/12078/1217742-113.stm)
Faced with flat funding, the Corps has adopted a "fix when fail" approach to maintaining locks and dams… Because the water level rises and falls at different points along rivers, locks are used to raise and lower barges depending on the depth of the river where they are coming from and the depth of the river where they are headed.

Congress must act to increase funding—only increasing funding for modernization of inland waterways addresses problems of SQ infrastructure to improve economy and jobs. Funding for waterway modernization is inevitable – cost overruns and stop-go payment makes status quo policies collapse under themselves
Pittsburgh Post-Gazette 2012
(Editorial Board Pittsburgh Post-Gazette, “Water torture: Congress needs to act on decaying locks and dams,” March 25, 2012, http://old.post-gazette.com/pg/12085/1219052-192.stm accessed tm 9/8)
Congress bears the prime responsibility… Or must a disaster happen in order to get the nation's attention? No one wants to write an editorial that begins, "We told you so."

Contention 2—Advantages

Advantage __: It’s the Economy
New job numbers show economy on rebound but sluggish—manufacturing jobs, which had been consistently improving, slowing
Lee and Lauter, 9/7/2012
(Don and David, Los Angeles Times September 7, 2012 http://www.latimes.com/business/la-fi-jobs-20120908,0,2999579,full.story accessed tm 9/8)
WASHINGTON — Job growth slowed significantly and droves of workers dropped out of the labor market in August, increasing the odds that the Federal Reserve will step in to boost the economy and highlighting the political peril a continued sluggish recovery poses for President Obama's reelection…. And consumer spending accounts for about 70% of American economic activity.

Inland waterways system provides both an opportunity and a risk to the economy. Ongoing inland waterway breakdowns harming economic recovery and increase transportation costs
Davidson, USA Today Reporter, 12
(Paul, “USA's creaking infrastructure holds back economy,” 5/20/12, http://www.usatoday.com/money/economy/story/2012-05-20/creaking-infrastructure/55096396/1, Accessed 9/6/12, JA)
Inland waterways quietly keep the nation’s economy flowing as they transport $180 billion of coal, steel, chemicals and other goods each year …Meanwhile, seaports can’t accommodate larger container ships, slowing exports and imports. Highways are too narrow. Bridges are overtaxed.

Additionally,
Inland waterway investment prerequisite for US goods international competitiveness—failure to investment now ensure collapse of integral component of US economy, costing jobs and economic growth
Gibbs, Chairman of house committee on transportation and infrastructure, subcommittee on water resources and environment, 2012
(Bob, HOUSE COMMITTEE ON TRANSPORTATION AND INFRASTRUCTURE, SUBCOMMITTEE ON WATER RESOURCES AND ENVIRONMENT Committee Hearing, April 18 2012 Lexis accessed tm 9/8)
In this Committee hearing, we're dealing with how Reliability of Inland Waterway Systems Impacts Economic Competitiveness…. I welcome our witnesses today and I look forward to hearing from you, and at this time, I will yield to my Ranking Member, Mr. Bishop, for any comments you may have.


Specifically, we isolate two internal links to the economy that investment in the inland waterways solves

First manufacturing exports—
Manufacturing exports more significant to recovery than resolving debt issue—must improve US manufacturing position internationally
Council on Competitiveness 2011
(US Manufacturing Competitiveness Initiative, “Voice of American Labor Leaders on Manufacturing Competitiveness,” Ignite 2.0 December 2011, http://www.compete.org/images/uploads/File/PDF%20Files/Ignite_3.0_FINAL_.pdf, accessed 9/4/12, Bill)
Perspectives on America’s Economic Future Like those who participated in the previous Ignite reports, the state of the U.S. economy and the potential fallout of other global economic crises were also of significant concern for the labor leaders participating in this report… Doing so, according to the labor leaders, would eliminate many uncertainties about the future of manufacturing in America, and result in inspirational goals and objectives that incentivize manufacturers to increase investments in R&D and create jobs in the United States.

Second internal link—jobs
Beyond simple construction jobs, ripple effect through economy created through the plan
DeVol et al, 10
(Ross, chief research officer at the Milken Institute, a non-partisan, independent economic think tank, “Jobs for America”, January 2012, http://www.nam.org/~/media/58F813B0D1E643DC91E564FE4C3B3C2F.ashx, accessed 9/4/12, Bill)
Inland waterways are part of the nation’s infrastructure for moving goods, and they require federal assistance for maintainance and improvements… Assuming that the impacts of the total proposed investment occur over three years, the average annual increase would be about 22,300 jobs.

Economic decline escalates international conflicts, drawing in great powers, eroding international institutions that mitigate conflict, intensifying incentives for proliferation and terrorism which risk miscalc
Harris and Burrows 09 – PhD in European History @ Cambridge and Counselor of the US National Intelligence Council AND Member of the National Intelligence Council’s Long Range Analysis Unit
(Mathew J. and Jennifer, “Revisiting the Future: Geopolitical Effects of the Financial Crisis,” April, Washington Quarterly, https://csis.org/publication/twq-revisiting-future-geopolitical-effects-financial-crisis-april-2009, accessed tm 9/8)
Of course, the report encompasses more than economics and indeed believes the future is likely to be the result of a number of intersecting and interlocking forces…With water also becoming scarcer in Asia and the Middle East, cooperation to manage changing water resources is likely to be increasingly difficult both within and between states in a more dog-eat-dog world.


Advantage 2—Food insecurity
Failure to invest sufficiently in inland waterways system collapsing ability of US farmers to get their food to market—domestic and international
US grain exports key to world food supply and food prices
Attavanich et. al 11
(Witsanu, “The Effect of Climate Change on Transportation Flows and Inland Waterways Due to Climate-Induced Shifts in Crop Production Patterns”, July 24-26 2011 http://ageconsearch.umn.edu/bitstream/109241/2/AAEASelectedPaper_The%20Effect%20of%20Climate%20Change%20on%20Transportation%20Flows_13247.pdf, Accessed 9/6/12, JA)
Grain production plays a crucial role in response to the world’s growing demand for food, feed, and biofuels… Mississippi River and its tributaries on the Mississippi river basin are the largest inland water way system shipping grains especially corn and soybeans from the US inland to the Lower Mississippi ports for export market accounting for on average 55 and 47 percent of all US corn and soybean export, respectively during 2005-2009 (U.S. Army Corps of Engineers 2010).

Delays caused by lack of investment have direct impact on domestic and global food prices
United Nations Economic and Social Council 8
(United Nations Economic and Social Council, “ECONOMIC AND SOCIAL COMMISSION FOR ASIA AND THE PACIFIC,” TRANSPORT AND POVERTY: FROM FARM TO MARKET—EXTENDING THE REACH OF LOGISTICS, http://www.unescap.org/ttdw/ct2008/ctr_2e.pdf, Accessed 9/6/12, JA)
SUMMARY Recent increases in the price of food and concerns over its availability and access to it have focused attention on overcoming problems related to the transport of agricultural food products…. Furthermore, adequate infrastructure and access to transport services are prerequisites for the development of sustainable food trade, and requirements are becoming increasingly complex in importing countries and regions as more countries move into sophisticated trading in fresh fruit and vegetables, meat or fish at the domestic or international levels.

International food system on brink of collapse—scarcity is the new norm, US provided global food supply cushion necessary to stave off violence and conflict through the world
Brown, president of the Earth Policy Institute, 11
(Lester R.“The New Geopolitics of Food,” http://www.foreignpolicy.com/articles/2011/04/25/the_new_geopolitics_of_food, Accessed 9/6/12, JA)
In the United States, when world wheat prices rise by 75 percent, as they have over the last year, it means the difference between a $2 loaf of bread and a loaf costing maybe $2.10…With governments toppling and confidence in the world grain market shattered, the global economy could have started to unravel.

Specifically countries like China utilize imports of soybeans from the US in their food security calculations
Wong and Huang, Professorial Fellow and Research Assistant at the East Asian Institute, National University of Singapore, 2012
(China: An international Journal, Volume 10, number 1, “China’s Food Security and Its Global implications”, Project Muse)
Since China has basically attained food security at the national level, its participation in the world grain markets over the years has also been minimal, except for two particular years, 1994 and 2003…Therefore, the current level of soybean imports has not endangered China's food security — quite the reverse, it has served to buttress China's food security.

Even small disruptions in imports to China result in Chinese policy changes that contribute to huge disruptions to the entire global market
Wong and Huang, Professorial Fellow and Research Assistant at the East Asian Institute, National University of Singapore, 2012
(China: An international Journal, Volume 10, number 1, “China’s Food Security and Its Global implications”, Project Muse)
China accounts for 19.3 per cent of the world's total population, but the country has only about 9 per cent of the world's available arable land…. It is therefore in the interests of the world as a whole, in view of its sheer size, for China to rigidly adhere to its basic tenet of maintaining strong food self-sufficiency. [End Page 114]


Independently, food insecurity within China results in political instability and civil war along with famine effecting millions of Chinese; this political instability destabilize the entire region
Yee Professor of Politics and International Relations at the Hong Kong Baptist University 2002
(Herbert, 2002, The China Threat: Perceptions, Myths and Reality, Accessed 9/5/12, JA)
The fourth factor contributing to the perception of a China threat is the fear of political and economic collapse in the PRC, resulting in territorial fragmentation, civil war and waves of refugees pouring into neighbouring countries… From this perspective, a disintegrating China would also pose a threat to its neighbours and the world.

That goes nuclear, draws in major powers
Dibbs, Director Strategic and Defense Studies Centre, School of Pacific and Asian Studies, Australian National University, 2001
Paul, Naval War College Review, Vol. 54, Iss. 1, “Strategic trends: Asia at a crossroads,” Winter, Proquest, last accessed 9.4.10 [RG]
The areas of maximum danger and instability in the world today are in Asia, followed by the Middle East and parts of the former Soviet Union... Such multilateral institutions as the Association of Southeast Asian Nations and the ASEAN Regional Forum have shown themselves to be ineffective when confronted with major crises.

Contention 3- Solvency:


Only increased public investment can ensure US economic competitiveness
Scott, 12-
(ASCE New Source, “ASCE Tells Congress More Must Be Invested in Inland Waterways,” May 2012, http://www.asce.org/ascenews/shorttakes.aspx?id=25769808619, accessed 9/4/12, Bill)

Testifying on April 18 before the U.S. House Transportation and Infrastructure Committee’s Subcommittee on Water Resources and Environment, James A. Rossberg, P.E., M.ASCE, the Society’s managing director of engineering programs, said that efforts by the administration and Congress to address the growing investment deficit in waterways infrastructure have largely been ineffectual because of political considerations that give precedence to deficit reduction and tax cuts over the badly needed restoration of critical infrastructureRecovery from any event of significance would be negatively impacted by the age and deteriorating condition of the system, posing a direct threat to the American economy.”
