Affirmative

Port Dredging 1AC

Inherency

Cargo volume will quadruple by 2020 and current ports can’t handle that increase
BAF Ed 11 (Building America’s Future Education Fund, a bipartisan coalition of elected officials working to enhance American infrastructure, Falling Apart and Falling Behind, 2011 yearly report)

No federal funding for port projects, plan and federal action is the only way to solve on a large scale.
Smith 6/22 (Bruce, AP reporter, “Report: Southeast, Gulf need deeper port harbors”)

Obama approved port deepening projects but did not allocate any funds – Ergo your DA’s are non-unique and the status quo cannot solve.
Morgan and Rabin 7/19 – (Miami Herald reporter, environment reporter (Curtis and Charles, “White House Speeds Up Permits, Offers No New Cash For Port Miami Dredge Project”, The Miami Herald, July 21 2012)

Plan

The United States Federal Government should substantially increase its infrastructure investment by funding TIGER grants and investments to the deepening of the Ports of Baltimore, Charleston, Los Angeles, New York, San Francisco, Savannah, New Orleans, and Seattle to the depth of 50 feet.

Advantage 1: Jobs

Joblessness is the main cause of poverty in the status quo, and has put over 20% of children into poverty
New York Times 11 (New York Times, “Soaring Poverty Casts Spotlight on ‘Lost Decade’”, 9/13/11)

Sea Ports create immediate jobs and open job opportunities domestically and internationally
NAGLE ’11 (AAPA)

Increase in Trade directly stimulates economic growth and Job Creation.
OECD 12 (3/5/12. Organization for Economic Co-Operation and Development. Trade-induced economic growth boosts employment and wages.)

Job Creation is essential to improving the economy and fighting poverty.
UN 07 (Department of Economic and Social Affairs (DESA) Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)

Job Creation is the only way to improve an economy. No amount of municipal incentives can have an effect.
General Plan Framework 2010 (Los Angeles citywide general plan framework. “Chapter 7 Economic Development INTRODUCTION AND SUMMARY OF ISSUES”)

Job creation alleviates poverty and improves the well-being, education, and health of the poor
UN Research Institute for Social Development’10 ("Full Report - Combating Poverty and Inequality | Publications | UNRISD." United Nations Research Institute for Social Development. N.p., n.d. Web. 27 June 2012.)

Economic Growth improves quality of human life: this outweighs and turns Neg impacts
World Resources Institute 99 (“Economic growth and human development”)

Advantage 2: International Relations

Panama Canal allowing deeper ships in 2 years – US ports must adapt to stay competetive in international trade
USA Today 11 (USA Today is a world renound and reliable news source. On 2/7/11)

The Panama Canal will divert Chinese ships from the US, giving china alternate trade partners removing the US’s critical trade advantages
Lewis 10 (Leo lewis, feburay 08 2010, World business watch, The times.)

US China trade is critical to the relations and health of both countries
Shijian 12 (Zhou Shijian, Febuaryt 29 2012, Standing member of China International Trade Association; Standing member of China Society for World Trade Organization Studies; Standing member of Chinese Association for American Studies Standing member of National American Economic Association; Consultant of Beijing Municipal People's Government ; Senior Advisor to World Trade Organization Research Association; Consultant of China-EU Research Center.)

Solid US-China relations needed to maintain peace in the world
Shasha 12 (Deng, writer for the Chinese Xinhuanet News, “Proper handling of Sino-U.S. ties key to regional, world peace” 6/3/12)

International relations necessary to avoid poverty, and random violence globally.
Sheehan and Brockelhurst 06 (Helen, Mike July 29, The Independent)

Advantage 3: Agriculture

Expansion particularly effects our agricultural exports
Stallman, 2012 – President of the American Farm Bureau (Bob, “Update Our Ports or Miss the Boat”, American Farm Bureau, April 2012)

Better port access lowers prices
IDB 9 (“Logistics, Transport and Food Prices in LAC: Policy Guidance for Improving Efficiency and Reducing Costs” 7/3/9)

US has an obligation to prevent famine and is key to global food supply

More than two billion people live on less than $2 dollars a day – minimal food blips put them at risk

Ford and Benjamin, May/Junes 07, Foreign Affairs, “How Biofuels Could Starve the Poor,”)

The smallest food shortages will cause malnutrition, famine, and starvation.
Helfand, 07 (North American vice president of the International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War.(Ira, "An Assessment of the Extent of Projected Global FamineResulting From Limited, Regional Nuclear War)

Solvency

Failure to act to stop suffering and death is just as bad as causing the suffering and death—inaction guarantees the perpetuation of our harms, and such an advocacy must be rejected
Alexander 08 (Alexander, John K. Ph.D. from the University of Chicago. While his primary research interests are in the era of the American Revolution, he has special interest in the history of the media, of poverty and of crowd violence in American history generally.)


We must bring the structural nature of poverty into our ethical calculation – poverty is an example of structural violence, which is uniquely worse than behavioral violence
Gilligan 96 (James, Professor of Psychiatry at the Harvard Medical School, Director of the Center for the Study of Violence, and a member of the Academic Advisory Council of the National Campaign Against Youth Violence)

Good decision making requires you to completely reject the negative’s fixation on improbable worst-case scenarios
Rescher 83 (Nicholas, Distinguished University Professor of Philosophy at the University of Pittsburgh where he has also served as Chairman of the Philosophy Department and as Director (and currently Chairman) of the Center for Philosophy of Science. In a productive research career extending over six decades, he has established himself as a systematic philosopher)

TIGER Solves Best- has key funding and support
DOT ’12 (US Department of Transportation. 2012, no specific date)

Plan pays for itself – generates trillions in revenue annually
AAPA ’12 (American Association of Port Authorities)

The price tag is 5 billion dollars
USA Today, 12 (The report from the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers is in response to Congress f6-21-12, “Price tag to dredge Eastern ports for big ships: $5 billion”)

Federal government is only possible actor for the plan: port funding is a federal responsibility, plus state planning processes fail.
AAPA, 11 - AAPA represents 160 of the leading seaport authorities in the United States, Canada, Latin America and the Caribbean and more than 300 sustaining and associate members, firms and individuals with an interest in seaports (American Association of Port Authorities, “The U.S. Government’s Historic Role in Developing and Maintaining Landside and Waterside Connections to Seaports”, March 2011)
