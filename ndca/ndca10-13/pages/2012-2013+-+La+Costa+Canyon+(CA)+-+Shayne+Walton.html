I affirm.
US Legal**[1]** defines “universal healthcare” as:
Universal health care... and service provision.
I do not specify a specific plan to implement a universal healthcare system, but if the neg wants to me to defend a specific type of universal healthcare or the implementation of that, I can advocate that system as long as it still allows reasonable aff ground.
I value morality.
A side-constraint on all moral theories is that they must recognize the equal worth of persons.
This has two implications:
  1. Presume affirmative.
  2. The negative burden is to prove that there are morality relevant distinctions among people that justify unequal distribution of healthcare. Absent any relevant distinctions in moral worth, all people have equal desert. Steinberger:1
(Peter J. Steinberger "Desert and Justice in Rawls" Political Philosopher at Reed College Nov. 1982)
The theory of ... on moral grounds.
  1. The veil of ignorance eliminates any arbitrary distinctions among people that make impartial decision-making impossible. Freeman**[2]**
The original position ...and equal persons. [3]
2. The veil of ignorance recognizes the equal freedom of others to advance their own ends. Sachs****[4]****:
Rawls argues that ... over anyone else.
3. Purely aggregative ends-based ethics do not treat people as equal moral agents, since their worth is dependent upon their utility.
4. If morality requires us to maximize desirable ends, this just begs the question of what a desirable end is.
Thus, my burden is to show that in the original position, agents would agree to a system of universal healthcare.
My contention is that in the original position, agents would agree to a system of universal healthcare.
1. Agents in the original position would guarantee universal health care since doing otherwise arbitrarily exclude the basic liberties of sick people. Sachs 2****[5]****:

2. Health standards are based on a “natural lottery” where no one can be to blame for arbitrariness of their health problems. Sachs 3****[6]****:

3. Medical care is prerequisite to citizens’ equal rights and liberties. Sachs 4****[7]****:
In Justice as ...exercising this right.

1. First, ends-based ethics are incoherent. The dynamically unstable nature of the universe means all actions are magnified in their consequences, so predicting outcomes is impossible. Clarke[8]
