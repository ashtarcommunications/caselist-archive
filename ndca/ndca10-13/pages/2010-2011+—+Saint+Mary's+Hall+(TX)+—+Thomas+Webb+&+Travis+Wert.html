Affirmative


Contention 1: Inherency

The use of private military contractors, commonly known as PMCs, is increasing in Iraq

Salzman 2008 -- ZOE SALZMAN, Clinical Contract Attorney @ Center for Human Rights and Global Justice, J.D. Magna Cum Laude from NYU Law, “PRIVATE MILITARY CONTRACTORS AND THE TAINT OF A MERCENARY REPUTATION”, 2008, http://www.law.nyu.edu/ecm_dlv2/groups/public/@nyu_law_websitejournalsjournal_of_international_law_and_politics/documents/documents/ecm_pro_058877.pdf

Ambiguous legal status and the lack of accountability grants PMCs immunity for their actions. This is used by the pentagon to bypass jurisdiction and oversight.

Rothe and Ross (August 1, 2010 ).[ Dawn L. Rothe , Old Dominion University. College of Arts And Letters. Department of Sociology and Criminal Justice. Jeffery Ian Ross, Ph.D. a professor, writer, and consultant specializing in policing, political crime, "Private Military Contractors, Crime, and the Terrain of Unaccountability.". Justice quarterly (0741-8825), 27 (4), p. 593. /hnasser]

The issue is more pressing than ever. PMCs make every conflict escalate faster – they prompt first strikes and free market arms races.

Singer 2002 (P.W. Director of the 21st Century Defense Initiative at the Brookings Institution. Corporate Warriors: The Rise of the Privatized Military Industry and Its Ramifications for International Security International Security 26.3 186-220 TBC 6/26/10)

Thus we offer the following plan:

The United States Supreme Court should grant writ of certiorari to Saleh at al. v. Titan et al. and mandate the prohibition of private military contractors operating in the Republic of Iraq on the grounds that they violate international law under Protocol 1 of the Geneva Convention by meeting the definition of the term “mercenary” under Article 47. We’ll clarify.

Contention 2: Solvency

Despite trying to avoid association by repackaging themselves, PMCs fit the definition of mercenaries under Protocol 1 of the Geneva Convention. The courts labeling PMCs is necessary – it reveals their true nature.

Salzman 2008
Zoe Salzman, Clinical Contract Attorney, International Human Rights Clinic, Center for Human Rights and Global Justice, NYU School of Law, J.D. magna cum laude (2007) and a LL.M in International Legal Studies (2008) from New York University School of Law. “PRIVATE MILITARY CONTRACTORS AND THE TAINT OF A MERCENARY REPUTATION”, page 40, N.Y.U. J. Int’L L. & Pol. 858. Access- July 20th [KOO]

Saleh v. Titan is the perfect test case for this issue.

Center for Constitutional Rights, “Current Cases: Saleh et al v. Titan et al” Center for Constitutional Rights website, last updated July 13, 2010

Only using the Supreme Court can overrule the DC Court of Appeals’ dismissal and establish a legal precedent for the future.

Soder 2010 (Brenda, May 28, Media Relations Dir of Human Rights First, http://www.humanrightsfirst.org/media/usls/2010/alert/617/index.htm)

Mandating the prohibition of PMCs in this court case politicizes and problematizes PMCs – catalyzing demand for accountability and redetermining the necessary and the possible

Ulrich Beck, Professor for Sociology at the University of Munich, British Journal of Sociology Professor at the London School of Economics and Sciences, ’92 (“Risk Society: Towards a New Modernity”, p. 76-77)

Attempts at regulation inevitably fail because of lobbying and conflicting interests – terminating their use is key. They are not mere tools of the state – rather, they are economically driven actors who exert power over the very governments that employ them.

Anna Leander, Assoc. Prof. of Poli. Sci. and Public Management – U. of Southern Denmark, 2005 (Millennium 33.3, “The Power to Construct International Security”)

Advantage 1: Executive Power

PMC’s destroy balance of power by eliminating public and legislative oversight and falling exclusively under executive power. PMC’s are uniquely bad.

Thurer and Maclaren 2007 (DANIEL THÜRER & MALCOLM MACLAREN* Military Outsourcing as a Case Study in the Accountability and Responsibility of Power”, The Law of International Relations - Liber Amicorum Hanspeter Neuhold, p 353, http://www.ivr.uzh.ch/lstthuerer/forschung/FSNeuholdt.pdf)KM

Overexpansion of executive war powers is a slippery slope and has limitless extensions.

Lobel 2008 (Jules, Professor of Law, University of Pittsburgh Law School, Ohio State Law Journal, 69 Ohio St. L.J. 391, Lexis) jl

Overexpansion of executive powers gets modeled – causes international preemptive wars, use of catastrophic weapons, destruction of human rights, and collapses US leadership.

Sloane 2008 (Robert D, Associate Professor of Law, Boston University School of Law, 88 B.U.U. Rev. 341, Lexis) jl

Global human rights violations create conditions where extinction is inevitable

Human Rights Web, 94 (An Introduction to the Human Rights Movement Created on July 20, 1994 / Last edited on January 25, 1997, http://www.hrweb.org/intro.html)

This is key to universalize democratic peace between democracies and between nondemocratic states. None of their impact stories or takeouts assume changes in democratic structure.

Damrosch 95 (Lori Fisler, Professor of Law, Columbia University, 50 U. Miami L. Rev. 181, Lexis) jl

Advantage 2: Public sphere

Ending PMC presence is key to U.S. democracy and effective policymaking – privatized force divorces the public from political action and issues – there is no public, media, or internal governmental review. Warfare is committed on our behalf without our consent.

Blakely 2006 -- Gregg Blakely, staff writer for Peace Magazine, Political Science Honors B.A. from the University of Toronto, “Marketized Soldiering: How Private Military Companies Challenge Global Governance, Erode Accountability and Exacerbate Conflict,” Fall 2006. http://ir.lib.sfu.ca/retrieve/4187/etd2603.pdf.

PMC’s threaten emerging democracies as well- they serve commercial, not humanitarian, interests

Salzman 2008 -- ZOE SALZMAN, Clinical Contract Attorney @ Center for Human Rights and Global Justice, J.D. Magna Cum Laude from NYU Law, “PRIVATE MILITARY CONTRACTORS AND THE TAINT OF A MERCENARY REPUTATION”, 2008, http://www.law.nyu.edu/ecm_dlv2/groups/public/@nyu_law_websitejournalsjournal_of_international_law_and_politics/documents/documents/ecm_pro_058877.pdf

The inevitability of state formation means we must advocate democracy both domestically and in a broader international context—alternatives result in genocidal and catastrophic wars.

Martin Shaw 2001 Review of International Studies, The unfinished global revolution: intellectuals and the new politics of international relations, http://nationalism.org/library/science/ir/shaw/shaw-ris-2001-27-04.pdf

Circumvention of the public sphere makes war inevitable

Salzman 2008 [Zoe,New York University School of Law 2008 “Private Military Contractors And The Taint Of A Mercenary Reputation” International Law And Politics [Vol. 40:853 /hnasser] http://www.law.nyu.edu/ecm_dlv2/groups/public/@nyu_law_websitejournalsjournal_of_international_law_and_politics/documents/documents/ecm_pro_058877.pdf

And incorporation of the public sphere solves extinction.

Diamond 95, senior research fellow at the Hoover Institution (Larry, “Promoting Democracy in the 1990’s, http://www.carnegie.org/sub/pubs/deadly/dia95_01.html)
