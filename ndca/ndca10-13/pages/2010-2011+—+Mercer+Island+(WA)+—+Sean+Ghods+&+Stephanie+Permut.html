CONTENTION ONE: WHAT IS DEBATE?

High school policy debate is becoming militarized. Every year, hundreds of students attend tournaments across the country looking to discuss profoundly meaningful issues, but are lost in a perverse rhetoric of militarism. Every round, debaters engage in depictions of Nuclear Armageddon and global environmental catastrophe. The activity is a breeding ground for detached policymakers that take militarism to heart and regurgitate it through US imperialist foreign policy.
Spanos, 2002 (William, http://www.cross-x.com/vb/showthread.php?t=945110)
Dear Joe Miller … neocon policy makers is leading

Policy debate has become a laughable simulation of policymaking. Any appeals to accuracy are long buried beneath the desire to imagine as many extinction scenarios as possible.
Schweitzer, 2006 (Nick, Software Consultant in the Milwaukee area. Among various geeky pursuits, he also likes to think he’s a political pundit. “Everything Causes Nuclear War” Janary 24, 2006 http://www.nickschweitzer.net/2006/01/25/EverythingCausesNuclearWar.aspx)
I have another geeky confession to make … nuclear war damnit!

It is our intellectual responsibility to reject militarization and its depictions of violence. The discursive act of 1AC allows us to disrupt global systems of power, creating space for libratory politics.

Acting now is especially crucial. As the values of militarization continue to infect our academic spaces, we find ourselves enslaved by its vastness. Democratic activities like debate are the only check against the military’s pervasive presence
Giroux, 2008 (Henry A. “Against the Militarized Academy,” t r u t h o u t, November 20, 2008, http://www.truth-out.org/112008J)

Krepon, 2009 (Michael, Co-Founder of the Henry L. Stimson Center, a Diplomat Scholar at the University of Virginia “The Mushroom Cloud That Wasn't: Why Inflating Threats Won't Reduce Them” Foreign Affairs, May 2009 - June 2009 Pg. 2 Vol. 88 No. 3)

CONTENTION TWO: ITS TIME TO CHANGE

We are resolved that military presence should be substantially decreased from policy debate. Debate is a fluid activity that lacks a concrete set of rules. The norms of debate are ultimately determined through a currency of wins and losses. Voting Affirmative moves the activity away from militarization and mobilizes a reconsideration of our language and motives within debate.
Bjork,1993 (Rebecca, former college debater and former associate professor at the University of Utah, where she taught graduate and undergraduate courses in Communication and Women in Debate, Reflections on the Ongoing Struggle, Debater's Research Guide 1992-1993: Wake Forest University.Symposium, web.archive.org/web/20011012220529/members.aol.com/womynindebate/article3.htm)

Conventions should be done away with if they serve to reinforce an objectionable system. Challenging the norms of debate are the only means through which positive change can occur.
Skinner, 2007 (Beth, Director of Forensics at Towson University, “Rules and Laws,” 6/17/10, http://www.mail-archive.com/edebate@www.ndtceda.com/msg01716.html)
