Affirmative

Plan: United States federal government should remove nearly all counternarcotics operation from Afghanistan.

Advantage 1 – Afghanistan

First – microeconomics state that counternarcotics strategy increases value of the opium trade – that strengthens political actors adverse to security and governance – no counternarcotics policy can avoid this fate.

Caulkins et al, 10 http://www.cic.nyu.edu/Lead%20Page%20PDF/sherman_drug_trafficking.pdf, p. 7-8

And – counternarcotics redistribute gains to insurgent groups not currently reaping large drug revenues.

Caulkins et al, 10, http://www.cic.nyu.edu/Lead%20Page%20PDF/sherman_drug_trafficking.pdf, p. 5-6

And – only a risk that increased revenue shares shift towards insurgents.

Caulkins et al, 10, http://www.cic.nyu.edu/Lead%20Page%20PDF/sherman_drug_trafficking.pdf, p. 12-14

And – drug enforcement undermines anticorruption measures key to central government support.

Caulkins et al, 10, http://www.cic.nyu.edu/Lead%20Page%20PDF/sherman_drug_trafficking.pdf, p. 12-14

And – interdiction destroys strong local human intel key to success of any Afghan security strategy.

Felbab-Brown, 5, The Washington Quarterly 28.4, “Afghanistan: When Counternarcotics Undermines Counterterrorism,” muse.jhu.edu
And – ending counternarcotics guts the insurgency – Taliban loses revenue and popular support.

Somin, 6, Assistant Professor at George Mason University School of Law, “The War on Terror vs. The War on Drugs II,” http://volokh.com/posts/1157345587.shtml
And – security and governance requires reduced counternarcotics presence.

Caulkins, 10, “Drug Production and Trafficking, Counterdrug Policies, and Security Governance in Afghanistan,” NYU Center on International Cooperation, http://www.cic.nyu.edu/Lead%20Page%20PDF/sherman_drug_trafficking.pdf, p. 28
Finally – impacts – failure to defeat the Taliban emboldens global jihadists – undermines Pakistan – collapses NATO – and upsets global international relations.

Felbab-Brown,9, Shooting Up: Counterinsurgency and the War on Drugs, (the research on which this book is based received the American Political Science Association’s Harold D. Lasswell Award for the Best Dissertation in the Field of Public Policy), p. 9-10
And – US failure in Afghanistan causes global nuclear war.

Carafano, 10, senior research fellow for national security at The Heritage Foundation and directs its Allison Center for Foreign Policy Studies, “Obama must win fast in Afghanistan or risk new wars across the globe,” http://gazettextra.com/news/2010/jan/02/con-obama-must-win-fast-afghanistan-or-risk-new-wa/
And – NATO key to prevent genocide.

Robert Wexler, 5-3-2006, Congressperson, House Committee on International Relations subcommittee on Europe, “REP. WEXLER COMMENTS ON POLICIES OF U.S. TOWARDS NATO,” US Fed News
And – genocide destroys international order and leads to global WMD death.

Kenneth J. Campbell, 2001, associate professor, Political Science & IR, and director of the International Relations Program, University of Delaware, Genocide and the Global Village, p. 26-27

Advantage two – Pakistan

First – global opium production is centralized in Afghanistan – letting it remain there prevents poppy shifts to other regions.

Nadelmann, 7, "As Afghan Opium Production Goes Through the Roof, Pressure for Aerial Eradication, Increased Western Military Involvement Mounts," http://stopthedrugwar.org/chronicle/500/afghanistan_opium_production_record_eradication_military
And – counternarcotics shift production in and beyond Afghanistan.

Caulkins, 10, “Drug Production and Trafficking, Counterdrug Policies, and Security Governance in Afghanistan,” NYU Center on International Cooperation, http://www.cic.nyu.edu/Lead%20Page%20PDF/sherman_drug_trafficking.pdf, p. 3

And – opium production in Pakistan collapses the state and creates al Qaeda safe haven.

Felbab-Brown,10, "The Drug-Conflict Nexus in South Asia: Beyond Taliban Profits and Afghanistan,” Brookings Institute, http://www.brookings.edu/~/media/Files/rc/papers/2010/05_regional_counternarcotics_felbabbrown/05_regional_counternarcotics_felbabbrown.pdf
And – safe haven key to large-scale – even nuclear – attacks.

Arkedis 9, “Why Al Qaeda Wants a Safe Haven,” http://www.foreignpolicy.com/articles/2009/10/23/got_safe_haven
And – major terror attacks cause global nuclear extinction.

Morgan, 9, “World on fire: two scenarios for the destruction of civilization and possible extinction of the human race,” Futures 41.10, factiva, p. 683-693

And – perception of Pakistan collapse causes India preemptive strike and nuclear war.

Ricks, 1, WaPo staff writer, “At Pentagon: Worries Over War's Costs, Consequences,” Washington Post, lexis

And – escalates to global nuclear extinction.

Caldicott, 2, founder of Physicians for Social Responsibility, The New Nuclear Danger, p. xiii
