Affirmative

It's the same aff - some cards are different

Infrastructures both evince and reinscribe our social values. Our tendency to equate modern stability with their success means that current approaches perpetuate a strategy of control over all dimensions of experience.
Edwards, 2002
(Paul N., Professor of Information @ University of Michigan Department of History, Technology and Modernity: The Empirical Turn, Eds. Philip Brey, Arie Rip, and Andrew Feenberg, MIT Press, “Infrastructure and Modernity: Force, Time, and Social Organization in the History of Sociotechnical Systems”)

This strategy has never and will never be successful, but its failure is hidden by transportation’s attempt to reconcile time with space; at any reasonable scale of time, all infrastructure inevitably crumbles.

Edwards, 2002
(Paul N., Professor of Information @ University of Michigan Department of History, Technology and Modernity: The Empirical Turn, Eds. Philip Brey, Arie Rip, and Andrew Feenberg, MIT Press, “Infrastructure and Modernity: Force, Time, and Social Organization in the History of Sociotechnical Systems”)

Enframing infrastructure within standard risk calculus presumes the neutrality of objectivity. Ignoring our powerlessness opens us to the haunting of the unknown and makes us lash out in an attempt to reassert certainty.
Gunder and Hillier, 2009
(Michael, Senior planning lecturer in the School of Architecture and Planning @ University of Auckland, and Jean, Professor of Town and Country Planning @ School of Architecture, Planning & Landscape @ Newcastle University, Planning in Ten Words or Less: A Lacanian Entanglement with Spatial Planning, Ashgate, July 28, 2009, ISBN 0754674576, Pg. 64-66)

This refusal of other actors’ perspectives is a totalitarian ideology predicated on the denial of difference – all violence becomes inevitable and justified
Campbell, 2008
(David, Prof. of International Politics @ the University of Newcastle, Writing Security: United States Foreign Policy and the Politics of Identity, Revised Edition, University of Minnesota Press, ISBN 0-8166-3144-1, Pg. 48-51)

Moreover, our insistence upon proximate gratification is the manifestation of a violent logic of control that makes extinction inevitable. Only positive, new engagements with the Earth solve.
Joronen, 2011
(Mikko, PhD—University Teacher in Department of Geography @ University of Turku, Antipode, Vol. 43, No. 4, “Dwelling in the Sites of Finitude: Resisting the Violence of the Metaphysical Globe,” Pg. 1127-1154)

Plan: The United States federal government should substantially increase its investment in relationally planned transportation infrastructure in the United States.

Only our relational mode of planning can account for a multiplicity of experiences
Boelens, 2010
(Luuk, Professor of Regional Planning and Urban Design at the Department of Human Geography and Urban and Regional Planning @ Utrecht University, Planning Theory, Vol. 9, No. 1, “Theorizing Practice and Practising Theory: Outline for and Actor-Relational-Approach in Planning,” Pg. 28-62)

Our understanding of our infrastructures specifically determines the future of politics surrounding them - the affirmative is key to challenge the subpolitization of the systems that surround us.

Hunsinger, 2009
(Jeremy, Assistant Professor of Communication Studies @ Wilfrid Laurier University, Learning Inquiry, Vol. 3, No. 3, 2009, “Introducing learning infrastructures: invisibility, context, and governance”)

Preserving the ontological posture of unknowing is a prerequisite to actualizing meaning
Burke, 2011
(Anthony, Associate Professor of Politics and International Relations in the University of New South Wales, Angelaki: Journal of Theoretical Humanities, Vol. 16, No. 4, “Humanity After Biopolitics,” Pg. 101-114)
Shaping mobilities is necessary for the contestation of power relations or social forces
Jensen 3 (Ole B., professor of Urban Theory in the Department of Architecture and Media Technology at Aalborg University, Denmark, Making European Space: Mobility, Power and Territorial Identity, pp. 49-50)
