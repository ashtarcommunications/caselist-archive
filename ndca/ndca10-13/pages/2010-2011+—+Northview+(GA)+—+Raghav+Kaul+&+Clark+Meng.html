Iraq Aff (State)




What did the August withdrawal accomplish? Traditional military rhetoric leave us with “combat operations” – remaining US presence isn’t much different than what Bush did. The fight for independence which invasion crushed is not over – rather the way that presence has become so insidious means it has only begun
Milne in ‘10 (Seumas, columnist and editor for Guardian, “The US isn’t leaving Iraq, it’s rebranding the occupation,” August 4,
http://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/cifamerica/2010/aug/04/us-iraq-rebranding-occupation [also, lolthanks Pembroke hill] )
[For most people …has only just begun.]

Contention Two is Pre-emption
The invasion of Iraq functioned within the logic of pre-emption, a form of security whereby Americans become “rational liberators.” We force profoundly American values unto the rest of the world, antagonizing those who are different, all in the drive for geopolitical certainty
Kramer and Michalowski in ‘5 (Ronal, Prof IR @ U of Western Michigan AND Raymond, Prof Sociology @ Northern Arizona Univ, “War, Aggression and State Crime” April 05, Centre for Crime and Justice Studies)
[On the evening of 11 September …the security of Israel.’]

The logic of pre-emptive war relies on two deadly assumptions – that we know who the bad guys are, and that our conventional response can cripple any opposition to hegemony. Our deterrence utilizes spectacular murder and normalizes itself as the preferable strategy of war-making
Goh in ‘6 (Irving, research fellow @ Harvard and the National Univ. of Singapore, his articles have appeared in Cultural Politics and Theory, Culture, and Society, “Disagreeing Preemptive/ Prophylaxis: From Phillip K. Dick to Jacques Ranciere,” jstor)
[The articulation of wait …preemptive in all its forms]

Specifically, crisis representations of the east which justify our presence necessitate war and genocide – the only way to deal with the excluded other is to seek their elimination for false reasons of self-defense
Batur in ‘7 (Pinar, Director of Urban Studies and Sociology @ Vassar College, “Heart of Violence: Global Racism, War, and Genocide,” Handbook of the Sociology of Racial and Ethnic Relations )
[Albert Memmi argued that “We have no…The 21st century opened up with genocide, in Darfur]

Iraqi citizens exist in a state of exception where their deaths no longer matter due to US occupation and indiscriminate military violence – once we have designated individuals as inherently different, they can easily be murdered with impunity
(Michael, Prof Criminal Justice @ Rutgers, “Foucault in a Post-9/11 World: Excursions into Security, Territory, Population,” British Journal of Criminology, pg. 177-195, http://www.thecarceral.org/Welch%20MS%20for%20Carceral%20Notebooks%2027%20Aug%202008.pdf )
[By exploring the problems of space contained …and neglects their suffering (see Roy 2001)]

Pre-emption is the American logic of waging war to prevent it. It relies us to submit ourselves to action on impulse, constantly living in a state of fear. This state of emergency requires the state to respond militarily on our behalf to eliminate those who are different. Invocation of security is not a neutral project – rather, speaking of future threats and requiring a response determines how we act and forecloses other modes of thought
Dillon in ‘7 (Michael, Prof IR and Politics @ U of Lancaster, “Governing Terror: The State of Emergency of Biopolitical Emergence,”)
[While uncertainty has long posed …as articulated by Foucault in his 1979 lecture series The Birth of Biopolitics (Foucault, 2004)]

The idea that we can reshape the international system with our military creates a self replicating security dilemma – we are led into a damning search to eliminate whatever is considered the latest threat to western hegemony, inevitably mobilizing the populace through fear
Burke in ‘7 (Anthony, Senior Lecturer and Prof IR @ U of New South Wales, “Beyond Security, Ethics and Violence,” pg 44-49)
[Even though he could not predict it, and can hardly be blamed, Bacon's…strength will remain with the two superpowers.'49]

Managing decisions via the negative’s politics of risk are the same way in which you’re giving up your agency to the state so that they can control our actions through fear. We repudiate this position – managing politics according to the calculation of risk is the same way in which the state can map contingency and calcify value to our lives. Voting aff represents a challenge to dominant discourses of militarism
Zebrowski in ‘9 (Christopher, Prof @ Zebrowski Univ., “Governing the Network Society: A Biopolitical Critique of Resilience”, Political Perspectives Vol 3)
[It has been argued that the eradication…contemporary security climate. ]

Our 1ac is an act of resistance to our complacency to this calcifying fear. We have to challenge this mode of politics so that we can reverse the usage of consensus to identify and destroy enemies.
Dillon and Lobo-Guerrero in ‘9 (Michael, Prof Politics @ Lancaster Univ AND Luis, lecturer IR @ Kelle Univ, “The Biopolitical Imaginary of Species-being Theory Culture Society,” Lexis )
[They go two disturbing steps further…he recognized it also in the case of the hermaphrodite Herculine Barbin (Foucault, 1980)]

thus the plan: the united states federal government should eliminate through immediate withdrawal its military and police presence in the republic of iraq
we’ll clarify

Contention Three is Solvency
An affirmative ballot signifies the denial of pre-emption. We must reject pre-emptive warfare and break the consensus of speed. Democratic resistance is the most capable of generate new norms.
Goh in ‘6 (Irving, research fellow @ Harvard and the National Univ. of Singapore, his articles have appeared in Cultural Politics and Theory, Culture, and Society, “Disagreeing Preemptive/ Prophylaxis: From Phillip K. Dick to Jacques Ranciere,” jstor )
[The fact remains that the victim of the London…with the preemptive is none other than but now]

Starting by peeling open the wounds of our loss in Iraq necessarily allows for us to challenge US interventionism. Only the rejection of the logic of pre-emption can combat colonialism globally
(Larry, staff writer @ “The Revolutionary Worker,” “Oil, Power, and Empire: Iraq and the US Global Agenda, Common Courage Press )
[The NSS mentioned Iraq only once in passing. …all roads led through Baghdad.101]

Calling for a full, unconditional withdrawal directly challenges imperialism
(Arudathi, Dec. 7th, world-renowned Indian author and global justice activist, People vs. Empire, http://www.inthesetimes.com/article/1740/)
[Empire has a range of calling cards…back-breaking political mobilization.]

We’ll claim that the plan is fiated through the USfg, but voting aff is more significant than that – it embodies the judge’s commitment as an intellectual to align with movements of peace
Jones in ‘99 (Richard, Prof. IR @ Aberystwyth University, Security, Strategy, and Critical Theory, p. 155-163)
[Furthermore, the abandonment of faith in revolutionary parties…a challenge to critical security studies.]

Drones Aff (Johns Creek and Sequoyah)



First, inherency
Drones are a dominant component of US military presence in Afghanistan
[Cesar Chelala, winner of an Overseas Press Club of America award for an article on human rights, CommonDreams.org, 6/10/10, date accessed: 6/22/10, http://www.commondreams.org/view/2010/06/10-5.]

Presence has doubled and there’s no sign of let up – it’ll only get worse
Callam 10 (Andrew, candidate for a masters degree in international affairs, “Drone Wars: Armed Unmanned Aerial Vehicles,” http://www.iar-gwu.org/node/144)

There is a growing trend toward spread of UAV tech – every second we wait another country gets closer to this tech
Sherman 5 [Jason, Writer on the Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists (specifically on UAVs), The Drone Wars, Sep 1, 2005, Academic Search Premier]

Debate is necessary to shatter the veil of secrecy behind these machines
Virilio 84 (Paul, professor of phenomology at the Sorbonne, “War and Cinema: The logistics of perception)

Thus the plan: The United States Supreme Court should rule to eliminate all surveillance and assassination drones in Afghanistan on the grounds that they violate Article 51 of International Humanitarian Law under the Geneva Conventions. We’ll clarify.
Contention Two is International Law

Drone warfare undermines the stipulation of targeted killings under Article 51 of IHL under the Geneva Conventions
UN Human Rights Council 10 5/2010, p. 13, http://www2.ohchr.org/english/bodies/hrcouncil/docs/14session/A.HRC.14.24.Add6.pdf

Specifically, this warfare does not meet the definition of armed international conflict between states under humanitarian law
Rona 3 Gabor Rona, Legal Advisor in the Legal Division of the International Committee of the Red Cross, 2003, the Fletcher Forum of World Affairs vol. 27:2,, Interesting Times for International Humanitarian Law: Challenges from the “War on Terror” http://www.icrc.org/Web/eng/siteeng0.nsf/htmlall/5PWELF/$File/Rona_terror.pdf

International Law is inevitable but US engagement is key to effectiveness – claims of “sovereignty loss” are false
IEER 2 Institute for Energy and Environmental Research, (and the Lawyers Committee on Nuclear Policy, 2002 (Rule of Power or Rule of Law? An Assessment of U.S. Policies and Actions Regarding Security-Related Treaties, May, http://www.ieer.org/reports/treaties/execsumm.pdf)

And, ruling on I-law spills over to broader incorporations
Goldman 5 (Jeffrey C. Goldman, JD @ Duke, Executive Editor, Duke Law Journal. “Of Treaties And Torture: How The Supreme Court Can Restrain The Executive,” Duke Law Journal December, 2005 55 Duke L.J. 609 Waldman)

Humanized international law – far from being a western construct—is a tool of liberation of all peoples
Raskin 99[Marcus, Co-founder of the Institute for Policy Studies and Professor of Public Policy at Georgetown, “Re-framing international law for the 21st century: The International Law of Reconstruction,” 9 Transnat'l L. & Contemp. Probs. 513, lexis]

International Humanitarian Law drives human rights activism across the entire planet
Zeng 9 [ZENG Lingliang, Faculty of Law, University of Macau, Macau, China, Humanizing tendency of contemporary international law, Front. Law China 2009, 4(1): 1–30]

Humanization of international law represents the end of absolute state power—it is ushering a new era of global justice, environmental protections and human rights
Zeng 9 [ZENG Lingliang, Faculty of Law, University of Macau, Macau, China, Humanizing tendency of contemporary international law, Front. Law China 2009, 4(1): 1–30]

Freedom outweighs and turns other impacts
Copelan 99 (Rhonda Copelan, law professor, NYU, NEW YORK CITY LAW REVIEW, 1999, p. 71-2)


Broader incorporation of international law solves biodiversity loss
Glennon 90 Michael, Board of Editors @ American Journal of Intl Law, Jan., 84 A.J.I.L. 1


Biodiversity loss destroys the environment and drives multiple species into extinction – interdependence
Diner 94 Diner, David N. B.S. Recipient. Ohio State University. J.D. Recipient. College of Law. Ohio State University. LL.M. The Judge Advocate General’s School. United States Army. Judge Advocate’s

Scenario Two is Pakistan
Two internals –
First, instability
Pakistan is on the verge of collapsing due to internal instability – continued drone usage pushes the country over the brink by allowing the Taliban to expand their territory
New Statesman 10, award-winning British magazine on Current affairs, world politics, and the arts, 10 (Samira Shackle, contributing writer, “Drone attacks: what is America doing in Pakistan?”, NewStatesman, January 7th, http://www.newstatesman.com/blogs/the-staggers/2010/01/drone-attacks-pakistan-policy)



Second, relations
US-Pakistan relations are on the brink – public resentment in both countries
LODHI 9 Maleeha Lodhi, Former Ambassador of Pakistan to US, 09 (“the Future of pakistan-U.S.


Drones tank relations
Satia 9 Priya, Assistant Professor of Modern British History at Stanford University, From Colonial Air Attacks to Drones in Pakistan, Summer 2009, http://www.digitalnpq.org/archive/2009_summer/08_satia.html


Relations are key to solve the Kashmir issue – escalates to nuclear war
Riedel 8 Bruce senior fellow in the Saban Center for Middle East Policy at the Brookings Institution, 08 (“Pakistan and Terror: The Eye of the Storm”, The annals of the American academy of political and social science, July, 2008, sage journals)


That risks fast escalation and nuclear winter
NTI 10 , governed by an expert and influential Board of Directors with members from the United States, Russia, Japan, India, Pakistan, China, Jordan, Sweden, France and the United Kingdom, 10 (“Regional Nuclear War Could Devastate World Population, Report Warns “, NTI, March 16, 2010, http://gsn.nti.org/gsn/nw_20100315_4193.php)

Advantage Three is “The Military-Industrial Complex”
Drone technology makes a worldwide arms race inevitable in the status quo as a result of militarism
Weber 2010 (Jutta Weber, philosopher, STS (= science & technology studies) scholar, and media theorist., Robotic Warfare, Human Rights & the Rhetorics of Ethical Machines, http://www.gender.uu.se/filedownload.php?id=311)

U.S. drone attacks will incite international, uncontrolled drone use and risks the spread of new weapons tech
Savage 10 [Charlie, columnist for the New York Times, New York Times, “U.N. Report Highly Critical of American Drone Attacks, Warning of Use by Others”, June 6th, 2010, http://www.nytimes.com/2010/06/03/world/ 03drones.html, Academic Search Premier]

The “drone race” will result in either extinction or oppression by the very machines we currently employ
Engelhardt 9 [Tom Engelhardt, co-founder of the American Empire Project, Drone Wars: Your Future has Arrived, 4/7/09, http://www.pacificfreepress.com/news/1/3990-drone-wars-your-future-has-arrived.html.]

And, multiple other impacts – first, surveillance
Surveillance mechanisms become the ordering eyes of power – rendering people docile and reproduce social domination, while rendering people to their basic utility
Debrix 99 (Francois, “Space Quest: Surveillance, Governance, and the Panoptic Eye of the United Nations”, professor of International Relations at Florida International University)

And, this power is the creation of a docile body. This attempt to normalize justifies and necessitates extermination. This biopolitical control makes life meaningless and destruction inevitable
Foucault 78 (Michel, Prof of History of Systems of Thought at the Colege de France, The History of Sexuality – Volume One)

Second impact – distancing
Virtual warfare transmutes borders to an incorporeal plane that further disguises biopolitical diametrics
Graham 6 Stephen, Centre for the Study of Cities and Regions, Department of Geography, University of Durham, 6/4/06, “Cities and the ‘War on Terror’”, International journal of Urban and Regional research, http://www3.interscience.wiley.com/cgi-bin/fulltext/118603050/HTMLSTART


Conduction of automated warfare offers the military de-personalization of killing, allowing the target to be reduced to a statistic– this cyborg warfare nullifies the soldier and strips the target of their humanity
Masters 10 (Christina, is a Lecturer at the University of Manchester, Gianta 1/20/10, Cyborg soldiers and militarized masculinities, http://www.eurozine.com/authors/masters.html)

Only banning UAV’s International law can prevent the atrocities of post-human war
