1AC

Plan: The United States Federal Government should substantially increase its exploration and development of space by restarting the Space Shuttle Program and re-implementing missions.

In July, Congress abandoned the Space Shuttle program in favor of private spaceflight and the Space Launch System. Spaceflight is here to stay, but Congress’s failure to fund and plan dooms transition.
Cunningham and Olson 11 [Col. Walter Cunningham and Pete Olson 7/22/11, served as the NASA lunar module pilot for Apollo 7. the former ranking member for the House Science Committee, Space and Aeronautics Subcommittee. He represents the Johnson Space Center in Houston, Texas. POLITICO OPED section, http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0711/59647.html]

However, last year President BarackObama shifted NASA policy away from human spaceflight....

Contention One is Econ

Scenario 1 is growth. Retiring the Shuttle killed thousands of jobs across the country

CNBC 11 [“End of Space Shuttle Program to Have Far Reaching Impact”, Peter Suciu, http://www.cnbc.com/id/43469916/End_of_Space_Shuttle_Program_To_Have_Far_Reaching_Impact]

The final space shuttle launch on July 8 will make for huge business in Florida's Space Coast, as a million people are likely to see the shuttle take off one last time..


And, absent the shuttle, workers are jobless, homeless, and forced into poverty

Russia Today News 11 (“End of NASA shuttle program means loss of “dream jobs”; Russia Today- Russian News Station”; 7/8/11)

NASA's historic final space shuttle mission may have to wait a little longer, with bad weather threatening to delay the launch.


Scenario 2 is Private Spaceflight –

Privatization is the future of spaceflight, but status quo is too soon

Cernan 10 [Eugene, CAPTAIN AND COMMANDER OF THE APOLLO XVII, Written Testimony before the Committee of Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the United States Senate]

In this proposed budget we find several billions of dollars allotted to developing commercial human access to low Earth orbit, based upon the assumptions and claims by those competing for this exclusive contract who say that they can achieve this goal in little more than three years, and that it can be done for something less than 5 billion dollars.
Transition time is key—all other alternatives are doomed to fail
Sterner 10 (Eric R. Sterner, National Security and Aerospace Consultant in Washington D.C., Fellow at George C. Marshall Institute, Senior staff positions on the House Armed Services and Science Committees, former Associate Deputy Administrator for Policy and Planning at NASA; “Worthy of a Great Nation? NASA’s Change of Strategic Direction”; Published in the Marshall Institute Policy Outlook; April 2010; http://www.marshall.org/pdf/materials/797.pdf)

More than anything, the administration’s budget request represents a change of strategic direction, away from a focused program of exploration in which the government opens frontiers and enables the private sector to follow, towards an unstructured program intended to help tomorrow’s leaders make decisions about the future of the space program.



AND, rushing means we ignore safety standards, causing major accidents
Foust 10 [Jeff, editor and publisher of The Space Review and senior space policy analyst for Futron Corporation, a company specializing in space industry decision management solutions, “Twin Hurdles for Commercial Human Spaceflight”, May 24, http://www.thespacereview.com/article/1633/1]

Perhaps the biggest concern among skeptics of commercial crew is the ability of industry to carry out such missions safely.



The impact is the private spaceflight industry – an accident would shut down companies for good

NPR 11 [National Public Radio, Nell Greenfieldboyce, “NASA Prepares for Risks in Private Space Travel”, 1/28/11, http://www.npr.org/2011/01/28/133308080/nasa-prepares-for-risks-in-private-space-travel]

As NASA somberly marks the 25th anniversary of the space shuttle Challenger accident, the agency is looking ahead to the retirement of its aging space shuttle fleet later this year.

That means the biggest government bailout in history
Foust 10 [Jeff, editor and publisher of The Space Review and senior space policy analyst for Futron Corporation, a company specializing in space industry decision management solutions, “Twin Hurdles for Commercial Human Spaceflight”, May 24, http://www.thespacereview.com/article/1633/1]

Many are skeptical that markets are robust enough for commercial providers to develop these capabilities without significant additional NASA funding....


Bailouts substantially increase poverty
White 8 (Jerry White, write in candidate of the Socialist Equality Party in the 2008 Election, labor organizer, political activity, speaking on behalf of the Socialist Equality Party; “US government bailouts: poverty wages for auto workers, trillions for bankers”; World Socialist Web Site; 12/16/2008)

In the debate over federal assistance to the failing Detroit automakers, the clear consensus has emerged within the corporate, political and media establishment that auto workers must accept sharply lower wages and benefits as part of any industry bailout.....


Poverty is the worst form of bare life. It’s a prior question to solving any other violence.
Sachar 99(Rajindar Sachar, former Chief Justice of the Delhi High Court, member of United Nations Sub-Commission on Prevention of Discrimination and Protection of Minorities, on the counsel for the People's Union for Civil Liberties; “The worst of human rights violations”; Peace Movement Aotearoa (National Peace Organization in New Zealand); 12/8/99; http://www.converge.org.nz/pma/appov.htm)
OF THE many violations of human rights that can be listed, none is worse but less spoken of in fashionable seminars on human rights - I am referring to ``poverty'' the mother of all human rights violations...


Contention Two is Disease
The Russian Soyuz exploded in August and flights have been suspended indefinitely. No Shuttle means no backup plan
BBC 11 [British Broadcasting Corporation, 9/9/11, “Russia pins Soyuz failure to production line defect”, http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-14852534]
The Russian space agency says a rocket that failed while carrying cargo to the International Space Station on 24 August had a production line defect.

Even when the Soyuz can fly, it’s not capable of carrying critical science experiments or repairing the ISS
Abbey 11 (George W.S. Abbey, Senior Fellow in Space Policy; Baker Institute for Public Policy, Rice University; “Restore the Vision”; 7/20/11)
The space shuttle’s retirement after the mission launched on July 8, 2011, means that the station will depend entirely on the Russian Soyuz system for transporting astronauts.

BUT, ISS failure is highly probable, and only relaunching the shuttle program can prevent the loss of vital projects
Abbey 11 (George W.S. Abbey, Senior Fellow in Space Policy; Baker Institute for Public Policy, Rice University; “Restore the Vision”; 7/20/11)
In the event of damage from a fire, space debris, or a potential collision from the frequent docking of manned or unmanned commercial resupply spacecraft, the space station backup systems offer little margin of safety.


The ISS is uniquely to medical breakthroughs in curing diseases
Ruttley et. al 9 (Tara Ruttley, Associate Program Scientist for the ISS, PhD in Neuroscience; Cynthia Evans; Julia Robinson; “The Importance of the International Space Station for Life Sciences Research: Past and Future”; Published by the ISS Space Program; 2009)
One of the most exciting results reported from ISS research is the confirmation that common pathogens change and become more virulent during space flight (Wilson et al., 2007)…..

Contention 3: F/W

Our framework for evaluating the debate is that you look to probability first. This is the most productive means of accessing risks
Resher 83
(Nicholas, professor of philosophy at Pitt U. "Risk: A Philosophical Introduction to the Theory of Risk Evaluation and Management,” p.50)

And, the willingness to sacrifice the lives of their own population in the name of greater security interest constitutes bio-political control that makes war and bare life inevitable.
Giroux, 6 - Professor at McMaster University in the English and Cultural Studies Department
(Henry A., Reading Hurricane Katrina: Race, Class, and the Biopolitics of Disposability, College Literature 33.3, Project Muse)

The “any risk” logic would make all decisionmaking impossible
MESKILL 2009 (David, professor at Colorado School of Mines and PhD from Harvard, “The "One Percent Doctrine" and Environmental Faith,” Dec 9, http://davidmeskill.blogspot.com/2009/12/one-percent-doctrine-and-environmental.html)


And, neg’s extinction impact not going to happen – best models prove human extinction impossible



Discovery News 9

Discovery News, science writer Jennifer Viegas, “Human Extinction: How Could it Happen?”, 11/11/2009, http://news.discovery.com/human/human-extinction-doomsday.html


Prolif and Nuclear Weapons prevent nuclear war and escalation
Tepperman 09(Jonathan, Newsweek International's first Assistant Managing Editor, Tepperman was the Deputy Managing Editor of Foreign Affairs magazine, Newsweek, 8-28-09, http://www.newsweek.com/2009/08/28/why-obama-should-learn-to-love-the-bomb.html)

Magnitude focus imposes a tyranny of survival. Threatening the possibility of mass destruction has allowed the worst atrocities in history
Callahan, Director of International Program, PhD on philosophy in Harvard, 1985 (Daniel, The Tyranny of Survival and Other Pathologies of Civilized Life, p. 91 -93 Kim)


No war

Easterbrook, senior fellow at The New Republic, 05** [Greg, “EXPLAINING 15 YEARS OF DIMINISHING VIOLENCE — The End of War?”, http://democraticpeace.wordpress.com/2009/05/31/easterbrook-end-of-war/]
