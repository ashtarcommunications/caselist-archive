Affirmative


Post-Structuralist AIDS AC

Section 1 is ethics.

The notion of self is intertwined with the notion of the Other. The “I” is a relational being whose existence is shaped through primary interactions with an Other which are ultimately unknowable to the subject, making the self opaque, and these interactions with the Other continue to shape the “I”. Butler 1.

Judith Butler. (Ph.D. in Philosophy from Yale, Professor of Rhetoric and Comparative Literature Departments, Berkeley) “Giving an Account of Oneself”. Diacritics. John Hopkins University Press. 2001.

The Hegelian other…occasioned and disoriented.

This analysis of self is pre-ethical. It precedes an evaluation of ethics because only when we understand ourselves can we determine our moral obligations and moral identity. Without a notion of self, you cannot form, recognize, or agree to any set of principles. Butler 2.

Judith Butler. (Ph.D. in Philosophy from Yale, Professor of Rhetoric and Comparative Literature Departments, Berkeley) “Giving an Account of Oneself”. Diacritics. John Hopkins University Press. 2001.

Levinas considers the accusative inauguration… is designed to address this problem

However, a temporary or passing interaction with the Other is not sufficient—we must maintain sustained interaction and construct a narrative with them in order to understand ourselves. Butler 3.

Judith Butler. (Ph.D. in Philosophy from Yale, Professor of Rhetoric and Comparative Literature Departments, Berkeley) “Giving an Account of Oneself”. Diacritics. John Hopkins University Press. 2001.

So how do these concerns… on the basis of a transference

But, in order to maintain this relationship, we must always seek to better understand the Other, although full understanding will always be beyond reach. Butler 4.

Judith Butler. (Ph.D. in Philosophy from Yale, Professor of Rhetoric and Comparative Literature Departments, Berkeley) “Giving an Account of Oneself”. Diacritics. John Hopkins University Press. 2001.

If the identity we say we are…arranging for its death.

Section 2 is a genealogy of AIDS discourse.

First, since its origin, AIDS has been associated with homosexuality and their supposed promiscuity.

Seidman, Steven. 1988. Transfiguring Sexual Identity: AIDS & the Contemporary Construction of Homosexuality. Social Text, No. 19/20 (Autumn, 1988), pp. 187-205. Duke University Press Stable

In the heterosexual media…sexual promiscuity, disease, and death.

And, this discourse continues to paint AIDS as a righteous punishment for same sex relations, otherizing the queer community and denying them entry into our health networks.

Seidman, Steven. 1988. Transfiguring Sexual Identity: AIDS & the Contemporary Construction of Homosexuality. Social Text, No. 19/20 (Autumn, 1988), pp. 187-205. Duke University Press Stable

The AIDS discourse…heterosexual response to AIDS.

This has continued today, with queer victims suffering at the hands of insurance companies and being unable to access treatment because of societal stigma.

US Department of Health and Human Services. AIDS.gov. “Health Care Reform and HIV/AIDS”. June 2012.

On March 23, 2010…this population.

Section 3. I contend that universal health care is a necessary response to the AIDS epidemic in the US because..

1. For years, the queer community has called for universal health care

Dr. Beatrix Hoffman. “Health Care Reform and Social Movements in the United States”. American Journal of Public Health. January, 2003.

At no time…rights for all.

2. The opinion of the marginalized must be heard and the oppressed must be able to respond to their oppression as they see fit.

3. The treatment of AIDS must be transformed into a public affair. Phillip Kayal writes…

Kayal 2, Phillip M. 92 “Healing Homophobia: Volunteerism and "Sacredness" in AIDS” Journal of Religion and Health, Vol. 31, No. 2 (summer, 1992), pp. 113-128 Springer

In condemnation…self extended.

Negative

Pragmatic Sovereign NC

I negate and value morality because the resolution concerns the normative value of governmental action. However, epistemic questions must be examined before normative ones.

1. Truth has varied meanings for individuals because objects draw their significance and meaning from their practical implications, which vary from person to person. James 1.

William James. “Pragmatism: A New Name for Some Old Ways of Thinking”. 1907.

A glance at the history …be the true one.

AND, truth conceptualized as a verification of an objective reality fails to make sense due to the inability to verify every concept and object with which we interact. Instead, we have to take things as true based on their usefulness to us. James 2.

William James. “Pragmatism: A New Name for Some Old Ways of Thinking”. 1907.

Take, for instance, yonder object … all that happens.

Parrish explains how this conflict of subjective perspectives leads to perpetual disagreement and conflict within the state of nature…

Rick Parrish. “Derrida’s Economy of Violence in Hobbes’ Social Contract”. Theory & Event. Volume 7, Issue 4. 2005.

"For Hobbes truth …on individual appetites.

Parrish 2 continues, explaining how the creation of a sovereign allows for unified truth and a pragmatic solution to the conflict of desires.

Parrish 2, Rick. “Derrida’s Economy of Violence in Hobbes’ Social Contract”. Theory & Event. Volume 7, Issue 4. 2005.

All of the foregoing … the natural condition.



Thus, the necessary standard with which to measure the truth of the resolution is consistency with sovereign interpretation.

I contend that the sovereign is not obligated to provide universal health care.

1. Obligations are incoherent within a conception of absolute sovereignty.

2. Obamacare is not universal health care and so the sovereign has already decided that this is not an obligation.

Hedges 12. America's Superficial Health Care Debate Silences Single-Payer Supporters Monday, 09 April 2012 10:06 By Chris Hedges, Truthdig | News Analysis. Truth Out. http://truth-out.org/news/item/8407-the-real-health-care-debate

We have the solution…priced out of the market.

3. The only reason why people leave the state of nature is for reasons of defense and normative regulation.
