US MILITARY PRESENCE AS WELL AS THE WAY WE CHOOSE TO DISCUSS THE US MILITARY PRESENCE “POLICES” THE PRESENCE OF ASIAN IDENTITY. OUR CRITICAL EXAMINATION OF THIS YEAR’S POLICY QUESTIONS RAISES OUR CONSCIOUSNESS OF HOW WE AS ASIANS EXIST & SEE THE WORLD THIS PEDAGOGICAL PRACTICE OF DEBATE IS CALLED CONSCIENTIZATION
THIS IS HUMBLE THE POET
“Just Us”- Humble the Poet


English: You don’t know me. When you TRY to know me, you only change me. so since I am Korean, I will always be Korean.
Korean: 넌 내가 누군지 몰라. 너가 날 알라고 하면은 너가 나를 바꾸는거 뿐이야. 나는 한국사람이니까, 난 영원이 한국사람일꺼야.
Lemme ask you a question: do you know me?
When I don’t even know myself and my identity is policed?
You don’t know my situation, my life so let me summarize
Ima tell you what we feel, real talk i hope you realize.
So when i was seven years old my family moved to this land
With 60 dollars in the bank and i thought that i can
Be whoever I wanna be, they said this country was free
But no i was forced to hide my identity
They call it ESL, but they’re really taking your language
And the thing is, you believe them, you don’t know that you’re changing
The only Asian kid in school, the kid with slanted eyes
You think others are nice, but they’re really meant to lie
My parents realized, so we moved to an Asian community
In Howard County, and my new friends gave me immunity
But I hung out with too many Asians, and my parents said to stop it
Korean language? Drop it, academics? You better top it
Apparently, if you work hard you become one of the whites
That’s what we've all been doing, studying ‘til midnight
My mom sent me to school, tuna fish in my lunch bag
Yet eating rice when I’m home, and i had a hunch, that
This kind of thing we're going through, trying so hard but never gettin there
Will go on ‘til we talk about it cuz this ain’t fair
And you know it, so before this blows into another riot
My hope is for people to listen instead of forcin us to be quiet
Yo I’ve done my speed drills, and i know I didn’t like it
I hate this white form of debate, flowing ‘til your hands tighten
I do my schoolwork, get good grades yea its fine
But in here? This round is mine, this is my time
To disrupt this whiteness that’s designed to control
And make us imperial citizens, be a part of the whole
That we really are inside, but we can’t show it in this nation
So we perform to assert our identity-being “Asian.”


U.S. MILITARY OCCUPATION OF BODIES AND LAND HAS CONSEQUENCES.
US military PRESENCE creates an outflow of CULTURE. American ideas of racial hierarchies cross oceans on the backs of American soldiers changing racial lines FOREVER, forging racial inequalities, reinforcing notions of white superiority by its introduction of Hollywood movies, plastic surgery and enforced with VIOLENT military abuses that includes the rape of Korean womyn. Racial Stereotypes have travelled on the backs of Korean immigrants, the FOREVER FOREIGNERS, and the CONTRADICTIONS of American democracy has INFLAMED RESISTANCE.
MAISEL Marketing Asst for Stanford University Press 2k8
Kira-; PRESS RELEASE on a book by Nadia Kim Entitled IMPERIAL CITIZENS: Koreans and Race from Seoul to LA; June 2008; http://www.sup.org/html/book_pages/0804758875/Press%20Release.pdf





MILITARY PRESENCE BECOMES THE POLICING OF PRESENCE
KIRK 08 (visiting assc. Professor of Womyn and Gender Studies @ U of Oregon, founding member of womyn for a Genuine Security; "Gender and U.S. bases in Asia-Pacific"; FOREIGN POLICY IN FOCUS [FPIF], March 14)






The PRESENCE of minorities is POLICED by hostility and DISCRIMINATION, LOCKED between inclusion and ostracism, a position between the DOMINANT and the SUBORDINATE which is UNJUST in a nation that promises REALIZED DREAMS
The PRESENCE of minority peoples invokes stories of HOSTILITY, OVERT DISCRIMINATION and VIOLENCE as SUBSTANTIALLY DISENFRANCHISED populations are separated from channels of power by RACE, RELIGION and LANGUAGE, locked in a position between INCLUSION and OSTRACISM, DOMINANT and the SUBORDINATE that seems UNJUST in a nation-state whose IDEALS promise REALIZED DREAMS to ALL.
PEFFER Ohio University Eastern 2k7
Tony-; Editor’s Preface; JOURNAL OF ASIAN AMERICAN STUDIES (JAAS), February, Volume 10, No. 1; p. v





WE HAVE A RESPONSIBILITY AS INTELLECTUALS TO MAKE WHITENESS VISIBLE
Must locate interactions and discursive spaces that implicate race and invoke the power of whiteness and investigate intersections with class. This is our job as rhetoricians that engage in the activity of debate and our work as scholars that produce, construct and disseminate knowledge in the intellectual debate community
DR. CRENSHAW Prof of Speech Comm @ Univ. Ala. 1997
Carrie-PhD. USC; former director of debate @ Univ. of Ala.; WESTERN JOURNAL OF COMMUNICATION;
Resisting Whiteness’ Rhetorical Silence; 61(3), Summer; pp. 253-278.




“Learn Chinese”- Jin tha Emcee


It’s time to lace some REVOLUTION inside of our syllables. Along with the voices of Jin, 1TYM, and Humble the Poet, and I personally advocate US MILITARY OUT OF 대한민국 (Dae Han Min Guk) through our performance in order to stop the policing of presence within the community. We affirm this through a process of Conscientization to break down the CONSTRUCTIONS of WHITENESS around us and to serve as a PRAXIS to bring the Asian IDENTITY into the light and spit some true knowledge so we can grow epistemologically and become educated. It’s time to start makin some Changes.



DEVELOPMENT OF ASIAN AMERICAN CONSCIOUSNESS IS POSSIBLE
There is a REAL POSSIBILITY of young Asian Americans becoming CRITICALLY CONSCIOUS and POLITICALLY ACTIVE even in the post-Civil Rights climate in which political momentum has shifted to the right drowning student activism in a sea of apathy or hopelessness. Asian Americans going against the grain realize the politics of conversion where NIHILISM is countered by BELIEF, HOPE and a MEANING to struggle.
OSAJIMA professor and Director of the Race and Ethnic Studies Program @ Univ of Redlands 2k7
Keith-; REPLENISHING THE RANKS: Raising Critical Consciousness Among Asian Americans; JOURNAL OF ASIAN AMERICAN STUDIES (JAAS), February, Volume 10, No. 1; p. 64





THIS PROCESS OF CONSCIENTIZATION CAN CREATE REAL CHANGE
Conscientization allows us to NAME the world, a meaningful education that helps RECOGNIZE and UNDERSTAND the impact that societal conditions and oppression have on our lives, a constant clarification of what remains hidden within us that sees the world dynamically in the making, inspiring us to work against oppression and become active in efforts to TRANSFORM the world.
OSAJIMA professor and Director of the Race and Ethnic Studies Program @ Univ of Redlands 2k7
Keith-; REPLENISHING THE RANKS: Raising Critical Consciousness Among Asian Americans; JOURNAL OF ASIAN AMERICAN STUDIES (JAAS), February, Volume 10, No. 1; p. 67





THE 1AC SPEECH ACT IS A PERFORMATIVE TRANSFORMATION OF DEBATE INTO A PEDAGOGICAL TOOL OF CONSCIENTIZATION
Debate can be transformative. The methodology of evaluation and engagement must be one that seeks to liberate oppressed groups through the epistemological growth of the participants. In other words we must seek to transform our reality through the critical thinking skills we gain from our participation in the activity. The arguments we choose and how we validate the claims of those arguments must simultaneously shape who we can become, RAISE OUR CONSCIOUSNESS so that we may become active participants that can lead to progressive social change.
OSAJIMA professor and Director of the Race and Ethnic Studies Program @ Univ of Redlands 2k7
Keith-; REPLENISHING THE RANKS: Raising Critical Consciousness Among Asian Americans; JOURNAL OF ASIAN AMERICAN STUDIES (JAAS), February, Volume 10, No. 1; p. 74-76
CONCLUSION AND IMPLICATIONS





THE WHITE, SO-CALLED “SEARCH FOR THE BEST POLICY” IGNORES FUNDAMENTAL ISSUES BECAUSE THEY CANNOT BE WEIGHED AGAINST NORMAL “HUGE” POLICY IMPATS. THEIR WAY OF DEBATE DEVALUES INDIVIDUALS AND RELATIONS TO EACH OTHER, THE STATE, AND PUBLIC POLICIES THUS DESTROYING DEBATE, EDUCATION, AND POLICYMAKING.
Incommensurability is a barrier to the best policy option, preventing us from realizing the ideal in practice or principle. Ignoring incommensurability profoundly misconceives the value and dignity of individuals.
WARNER Prof of Law Chicago Kent School of Law 1998
Richard-; “Does Incommensurability Matter? Incommensurability and Public Policy”;
University of Pennsylvania; June; 146 U. Pa. L. Rev. 1287
