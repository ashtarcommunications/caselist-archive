New God AC


Value: Human Dignity
Kateb 1 [George. William Nelson Cromwell Professor of Politics, Emeritus; his field is political theory. “The Inner Ocean”. Individualism and democratic culture. Copyright Cornell University Press 1992.]
[Why make so much of individual personal and political rights... The most important sentiment by far is for the idea that every individual is equally a world, an infinity, a being who is irreplaceable.]

Standard: Respecting Human Dignity
Kateb 2
[In some of the pages that follow, I do make conceptual use of a state of` nature as a hypothetical condition in which no government exists as a literally or historically prepolitical or presocial condition...The protector of them at home must be condemned and resisted for violating them abroad.]

Contention 1:
Individual rights are the best way to respect human dignity because these rights are against the state;
Non citizens accused of terrorism ought have due process protections because it checks back the unrestricted power of the state.
Kateb 3
[The moral basis of this book is the belief that respect for individual...The latter rights, no matter how functional, cannot be the norm in a society devoted to individual rights.]


To maximize human dignity, the government must respect the rights of all. The bill of rights and the establishment of judicial supremacy institute the individuals supremacy over the policy of the state.
Kateb 4
[Different individuals may use or need the several rights variably, but when government refuses to respect rights, it not only makes people suffer, it injures everyone's human dignity.... I say all this to explain why the arguments I have tried to present in this book are guided by the American experience of rights, even though the theory of rights is now universal, or nearly so. ]

Kill 'em All CP

Ought is defined as “indicating moral obligation”

the negative can negate in two ways
1) I only have to clash with the nuances of the affirmative advocacy.
2) The resolution is not a comparative statement so the negative does not have the burden of proving some form of alternative solvency.

Part 1 is the counter plan text. Resolved: The United States should expand its policy of targeted killing of terrorists regardless of territorial location.

Part 2 is the justification for the Counter-advocacy
A. Fair side balance
B. Depth of Education
C. Strategic Research
D. Effective Policy Analysis
E. Intelligent Case Writing and AFF Research

Part 3 is competition/ Inherency
This policy would avoid granting any due process to terrorists; we can’t both give due process and kill every terrorist, there must be no exceptions; methodologies like due process that don’t take concrete action only increase terrorist activity.
Dershowitz, professor of law at Harvard, 2006 (Alan, “Terrorism: Confusing cause, effect,” Boston Globe, January 16, http://www.boston.com/news/globe/editorial_opinion/oped/articles/2006/01/16/terrorism_confusing_cause_effect/)
[The trouble with this ''cycle of violence" perspective is that it confuses cause and effect… Sure enough, Germany released the surviving Black September terrorists less than two months after Munich, when Palestinian terrorists ''hijacked" a Lufthansa plane].

Part 4 is Net Benefits:
Failure to condemn terrorism as pure evil invites moral relativism about everything – makes any atrocity morally justified.
Dershowitz, Felix Frankfurter Professor of Law at Harvard Law School, 2003 (Alan, The Case for Israel, Pages 193-194)
[Yet there is true evil in the world…. assassination of a single terrorist commander—is morally equivalent]

Part 5 is Solvency: Violence is necessary to solve terrorism, due process will never solve.
Ralph Peters, Retired Army officer, “In Praise of Attrition,” Parameters, Summer 2004, p. 24-32, http://www.carlisle.army.mil/usawc/Parameters/04summer/peters.htm, UK: Fisher
[Our military, and especially our Army, has come a long way. But we’re still in recovery—almost through our Cold War hangover… The truth is that even if you can’t kill yourself out of the problem, you can make the problem a great deal smaller by effective targeting. ]

Moral certainty is critical to an effective war on terrorism – lack of moral confidence breeds failure, criticism of collateral damage undermines the war on terror

Santa Zizek NC



Even if you don’t buy the offense off the nc, it is sufficient for a negative ballot to vote on defense to the ac if I prove the affirmative is false, or undesirable for some reason because



1)The negative has the burden of clash, not the burden of proof. Because the aff sets the parameters of the round, I only have to clash with the nuances of the affirmative advocacy.

2)Maintains fair ground because my opponent still retains the entirety of the resolution, proving why the US ought to extend due process protections. This best creates fairness within the debate, and without a fair debate individuals would have no reason to continue the program.

3)To insert a not in the resolution would create the contrapositive, thus reading The United States ought not extend to non-citizens accused of terrorism the same constitutional due process protections it grants to citizens, which just means I have to be textually not targeted killing.

4)The resolution is not a comparative statement so the negative does not have the burden of proving some form of alternative solvency.
