Meta-ethics AC

V-morality

Definition of ought

All morality has to recognize the construction of human identity. All choices act as a construction of human identity.
Christine Korsgaard (Harvard University). The Activity of Reason. Presidential Address – Eastern Division.
“In response, I… governed by rational standards”

The standard is respect of human worth for five reasons.

First, it is the only way to respect human identity. Korsgaard 2
“It is the conceptions… from reflective rejection”

Second, all ends based calculations are flawed, since ends are only conditional aspects relating back to the unconditional human rationality.
Immanuel Kant. Groundwork for the Metaphysics of Morals” translated by Thomas Abbott.
“The ends which… practically carried out”

Third, only deontology respects the basis of morals as being humanity. Korsgaard 3
“ask yourself what… of normative claims”

Fourth, desire-based theories fail because they are inherently circular.
Parfit. “Reasons and Motivations” Proceedings of the Aristotelian Society, Supplementary Volumes, Vol. 71, (1997), pp. 99-146   Stable URL: http://www.jstor.org/stable/4106956
“According to desire-based… happiness as an end”

Fifth, moral rules only exist if they apply to all actors.
Michael E. Bratman (Stanford University). The Sources of Normativity. International Phenomenological Society. Sep., 1998. JSTOR.
“In her fourth lecture… so Korsgaard claims”

I contend that the possession of nuclear weapons does not respect humans as ends. They are weapons of imperial control.

Michael Hardt and Antonio Negri. Empire. Harvard University Press. 2000.
“Imperial control operates… the power of life”

The imperial insecurity causes rogue states.
Stephen Walt (professor of international affairs at Harvard University's John F. Kennedy School of Government), The Coming Crisis: Nuclear Proliferation, U.S. interests, and world order- ed. Victor A. Utgoff 2000, Pg 196
“It also discounts… state less dangerous”

Sovereignty NC

V - morality

I value morality as ought prescribes a moral obligation. All rights claims are based in the community because all human rights claims are grounded in social interaction. Humans are not capable of moral rationality outside of the community.

The libertarian perspective… enhanced or diminished (for details, see Etzioni 1968a, 1988).

Thus, the community of individuals is the only source of moral authority.

The moral process that the community allows reveals itself in the declarations of the state.

Brad R. Roth [Associate Professor of Political Science and Law, Wayne State University. J.D., Harvard Law School, 1987; LL.M., Columbia Law School, 1992; Ph.D., University of California, Berkeley, 1996.] COMMENTARY: THE ENDURING SIGNIFICANCE OF STATE SOVEREIGNTY. Florida Law Review December, 2004. 56 Fla. L. Rev. 1017 Lexis

Sovereignty takes on… be democratic about.

Thus, the affirmative burden is to prove that the non-possession of nuclear weapons is a requirement of state sovereignty. The community decision making of the state is the statement of morality through the citizens’ deliberation in the community.

I contend that the possession of nuclear weapons is a statement of sovereignty.
