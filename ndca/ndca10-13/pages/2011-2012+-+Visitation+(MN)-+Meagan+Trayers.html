Framework
Justice... defined as giving each due, requires treating interests of persons as equals

Peter Singer - * In TOM REGAN & PETER SINGER (eds.), Animal Rights and Human Obligations. 1989,

"It is an implication of this principle of equality... how can it entitle humans to exploit nonhumans?"

Limiting rights to only humans is incoherent/arbitrary

Gray, Richard. "Animals Can Tell Right from Wrong - Telegraph." Telegraph.co.uk - Telegraph Online, Daily Telegraph and Sunday Telegraph - Telegraph. Telegraph Media Company, 23 May 2009. Web. 17 Aug. 2011. <http://www.telegraph.co.uk/earth/wildlife/5373379/Animals- can-tell-right-from-wrong.html>.

" Scientists studying animal behavior believe.... different species of animals appear to have an innate sense of fairness, display empathy and help other animals that are in distress."

Gray 2
"Whales have been found to have spindle cells..... complex emotional judgments such as empathy may have evolved considerably earlier in history than previously thought and could be widespread in the animal kingdom."


The only way to avoid irreconcilable rights conflicts is by making rights weighable. This doesn't violate people because even if we use them as means they are still part of a greater end.


Rakowski, Eric. Taking and Saving Lives” Columbia Law Review, Vol. 93, No. 5, (Jun., 1993), pp. 1063-1156, http://www.jstor.org/stable/1122960]

"In determining what rights people have.... not as independently valuable moral counters."


Standard is maximizing good.

Cafos are defined by the EPA as:

"Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations (CAFOs) - What Is a CAFO? | Region 7 | US EPA." US Environmental Protection Agency. 2002. Web. 22 Aug. 2011. <http://www.epa.gov/region7/water/cafo/index.htm>.

"agricultural operations where animals are kept and raised in confined situations"

Cafos are increasing now

Gurian-Sherman, Doug. senior scientist in the Union of Concerned Scientists (UCS) Food and Environment Program. "CAFOs Uncovered." Union of Concerned Scientists: Citizens and Scientists for Environmental Studies. Union of Concerned Scientists USA, Apr. 2008. Web. 21 Aug. 2011. <http://www.ucsusa.org/assets/documents/food_and_agriculture/cafos-uncovered.pdf>.

"The increasing size of animal farms and geographic concentration of CAFOs... trend is facilitated by a wide array of subsidies both direct and indirect paid for by the public."


CAFOs Abuse Animals
Mench, J. A, H. James, E. A. Pajor, and P.B. Thompson, 2008. The Welfare of Animals in Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations. In Report to the Pew Commission on Industrial Farm Animal Production. Pew Commission on Industrial Farm Animal Production, Washington, D.C.

"The scientific assessment of welfare involves evaluating the welfare of both individual animals and groups...without pain relief, and stress and mortality during transport."
