1AC - La Costa

Ever heard of Nevada Union before? Hint: it’s not in Nevada, though its understandable that everyone just assumes that it is. After all, we are just some hillbilly backwater, so there’s no need to get into uber- specific stuff like what state we are from.

Moreover, there’s no need to remember, I suppose, that Nevada County predates the state of Nevada by a good ten years, that our town is where the gold rush began. It hardly bears notice that it is a vibrant place full of art and culture. Forget our Pulitzer prize winning poets, world-famous musicians, international film festivals, and stunning natural beauty because we don’t have a shopping mall. Our rural identity means that we will always have to confront the assumption that we are just dumb hicks.

This is personal for us. Our rural community, like so many others, faces a shrinking population and therefore shrinking tax base. With state funds wrapped up in securing the urban prison-industrial complex, our school was forced to lay off a dozen teachers including our debate coach. This is the dominant trend formed by ruralism. Any kind of curricular enrichment is cut first. Turns out, there’s some evidence that we aren’t alone.
Ruralism leads to separate but (not) equal education, particularly with regard to the ability to get transportation to extracurricular activities like debate. We have to drive nearly everywhere we go. We have no school debate budget. The only reason we have a coach is because our parents pay his salary. The amount of tournaments we attend is severely limited because we just don’t have the transportation to get there. We sit through 14 hour car rides to vegas just get to a bid tournament, and we can’t go to many because we’re limited to regional tournaments, which, as seems hella obvious by now, really aint much.
