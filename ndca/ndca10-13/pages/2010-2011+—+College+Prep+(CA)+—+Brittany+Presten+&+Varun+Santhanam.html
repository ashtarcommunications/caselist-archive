1AC - Opaque Sexual War

Contention One is the Opaque Sexual War

Actions by the United States and South Korea to further prostitution in the status quo prove any actions in the status quo are purely cosmetic.
Sang-Hun 9 (Choe, January 7, http://www.nytimes.com/2009/01/08/world/asia/08korea.html?pagewanted=1&_r=1, “Ex-Prostitutes Say South Korea and U.S. Enabled Sex Trade Near Bases”, New York Times)

South Korea has railed for years against …. but the government’s and the U.S. military’s.”

And the USFG isn’t leaving anytime soon – South Korea wants our presence for military exercises – all their evidence doesn’t take into account recent shelling
VOA 12/1 (US, South Korea End Joint Maneuvers, Discuss Future Exercises, Voice of America,http://www.voanews.com/english/news/asia/US-South-Korea-End-Naval-Exercises--111104399.html)

As the U.S. and South Korean joint naval exercise wraps up, … United Nations for the Security Council to respond to Pyongyang's attacks or its new nuclear activities.

After the great liberation of Korea from Japanese imperialism cold war tactics split the nation into to lands distanced from each other in terms of political super power alignment based of off lines of longitude and latitude. The north went to the soviets and the south to the Americans-but only one got the benefit of a constant occupation of an alien military. In its quest for global dominance and red deterrence the United States built up a number of bases and military facilities in South Korea. Along with this build camp another increase, not in arms but in the objectified tools of sexual pleasure for the bodies on the ground.
Soh 2008 [Sarah Chunghee, Professor of Anthropology at San Francisco State University, “Military prostitution and women’s sexual labour in Japan and Korea,” The comfort Women, 49-52]
“Camp town ‘comfort women’” in Korea
After its liberation from Japanese rule in August 1945, … The Yun KuM-I murder case is one of the very that resulted in the criminal serving his sentence in a Korean prison.

And, military bases and troops in South Korea inherently result in rape and other forms of violence against sex workers.
Kirk 2008 (Gwyn, Prof Women’s Studies @ Antioch & Hamilton Universities & Founder of East Asia-US-Puerto Rico Women's Network Against Militarism, “Gender and U.S. Bases in Asia-Pacific”, March 14, http://www.fpif.org/articles/gender_and_us_bases_in_asia-pacific)

Violence against women is pervasive at U.S. bases in the region and in prevailing military culture and training…... by legal, political, economic, and social structures.

This strategy of military stationing on foreign territories is the foundation of a pervasive militarism that results in racism, sexism, sexual violence, rape, and environmental destruction.
Kirk 2008 (Gwyn, Prof Women’s Studies @ Antioch & Hamilton Universities & Founder of East Asia-US-Puerto Rico Women's Network Against Militarism, “Gender and U.S. Bases in Asia-Pacific”, March 14, http://www.fpif.org/articles/gender_and_us_bases_in_asia-pacific)

The power dynamics of militarism in the Asia-Pacific region rely on dominance and subordination…… – violence against women and girls, violation of local people’s self-determination, and abuse and contamination of the environment – that reinforce gender stereotypes.

1AC - Misery Business


Contention Two is the Misery Business

Prostitution and brothels are integral to US military bases in Korea, culminating in sexual exploitation
Enriquez 1999 (Jean, Director of the Coalition Against the Trafficking of Women in the Asia Pacific, November,
“Filipinas in Prostitution around U.S. Military Bases in Korea: A Recurring Nightmare”, http://www.catw-ap.org/resources/speeches-papers/filipinas-in-prostitution-around-us-military-bases-in-korea-a-recurring-nightmare/)

The grave labour situation that migrant women find themselves in, compounded by …… This is obviously in collusion with local and foreign businesses that make profits from the “entertainment industry,’ and local governments that similarly earn from the lucrative R & R business.

South Korea is a central hub and transit point for sex trafficking in the broader Asia-Pacific region
Statemaster Encyclopedia 2006 (“Encyclopedia > Prostitution in South Korea”, http://www.statemaster.com/encyclopedia/Prostitution-in-South-Korea)

Prostitution in South Korea is a large illegal industry. The Ministry of Gender and Family Equality estimated that it comprises over 4% of South Korea's GDP, with revenue exceeding $22 billion. Prostitution ranges from streetwalkers in red light districts to expensive "room salons" for private parties. ….. a number of them claim to have been tricked or forced.

Prostitution is inseparable from sex trafficking. Trafficking causes physical, psychological, and emotional abuse
Enriquez 1999 (Jean, Director of the Coalition Against the Trafficking of Women in the Asia Pacific, November,
“Filipinas in Prostitution around U.S. Military Bases in Korea: A Recurring Nightmare”, http://www.catw-ap.org/resources/speeches-papers/filipinas-in-prostitution-around-us-military-bases-in-korea-a-recurring-nightmare/)

CATW asserts that trafficking in women is inseparable with the issue of prostitution ….. In Korea, the Philippines and elsewhere, the women are viewed as commodities to be bought, and being Asians, they are certainly perceived as less than human.

And, violent militarized prostitution affects minors as well – this corrupting practice must be ended to provide any real justice for the countless under-aged girls who have been violated.
Chang 01 (Emily, J.D. Candidate at the Notre Dame Law School, “Engagement Abroad: Enlisting Men, U.S. Military Policy and the Sex Industry”, Notre Dame Journal of Law, Ethics, and Public Policy, Lexis Nexis)

Lita began her "career" as a prostitute working for a bar, called The Penthouse, in Olongapo. ….. The U.S. government did not give any compensation for the wrongs the U.S. soldiers committed."

Sex workers forced into military prostitution are rejected and stigmatized by their families and country – justifies their abuse.
Kirk and Francis 00 (Gwyn and Carolyn Bowen, both are founding members of the East Asia-US Women’s Network Against Militarism, “Redefining Security: Women Challenge U.S. Military Policy and Practice in East Asia”, Berkeley Women’s Law Journal)

These women who, forced by poverty and lack of meaningful alternatives, …... For many people in the host communities, military prostitution is a national disgrace.

1AC - Frames of War

Contention 3 is Frames of War

Prostitution is fueled exclusively by US military presence – withdrawing solves.
Dujisin 09 [Zoltan Dujisin, Jul 7, 2009, “Prostitution Thrives with U.S. Military Presence”, IPS]

With the presence of U.S. soldiers, flesh trade is flourishing ….. he says. "After entering the prostitution business they can’t get out."

And status quo doesn’t solve; traditional frames of war silence the wars against the oppressed occurring in the status quo. The status quo doesn’t recognize the root cause- only the plan action solves.
Hynes 2004 [H. Patricia, “On the battlefield of women’s bodies: An overview of the harm of war to women,” Women’s Studies International Forum 27 (2004) 431– 445, www.elsevier.com/locate/wsif]

The first question is significant because if we define war solely as direct conflict employing weapons ….. This distancing ignores the upfront trauma and impacts of war.

Prostitutes in South Korea are symbols of the US-South Korean military relationship-the only way to allow for a new conceptualization of South Korean society is the abolishment of US presence in the region
Moon 97, [Katharine H.S., Department of Political Science and Chair of Asian Studies @ Wellesley College, “Sex Among Allies: Military Prostitution in U.S./Korea Relations”, 1997, Columbia University Press, p.56]

I think there is yet another, unspoken, reason why these women have been forced out of Korean consciousness for nearly half a decade: …. the liberation of the kijich’on woman from the sexual domination of the GI.

The evolution of ethical systems is built upon centuries of reasoned insight and personal experiences that reveal which actions are better than others, …. misunderstanding from inspiring endless fighting and slaughtering of human beings.

The threats of huge, contrived impacts are exaggerated depictions of imagination that paper over reality – we must evaluate the systemic impacts of the aff first.
