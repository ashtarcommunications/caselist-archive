Things CP


The CP TEXT: States ought possess nuclear things


It solves:

Debating specific nuclear systems doesn’t matter, as long as they’re referred to as “nuclear weapons.” The only way to get at the root of nuclear danger is to re-frame the nuclear debate through language. Their deployment of the words “nuclear weapons” ensures the continuance of the violence that causes nuclear danger in the first place.



Brien Hallett, University of Hawaii Matsunaga Institute for Peace, and Ralph Summy, Founder of Peace Studies and professor of political science, University of Queensland, April 2003, Peace and Change, Vol. 28 no. 2, p. 238



“Thus the …prudential regulations.”4’



By using the terms of the opposition, the ENTIRE anti-nuclear movement is effectively disabled. Only by changing language can the movement force the debate onto their own terms, putting proponents of nuclear things on the defensive.



Brien Hallett, University of Hawaii Matsunaga Institute for Peace, and Ralph Summy, Founder of Peace Studies and professor of political science, University of Queensland, April 2003, Peace and Change, Vol. 28 no. 2, p. 236-37



“Before concluding … the entire antinuclear movement.”



Using the traditional nuclear language ensures that the movement plays the role of the loyal opposition – it may be heard, but any time a decision has to be made, decision-makers will reject the movement.

Brien Hallett, University of Hawaii Matsunaga Institute for Peace, and Ralph Summy, Founder of Peace Studies and professor of political science, University of Queensland, April 2003, Peace and Change, Vol. 28 no. 2, p. 238
