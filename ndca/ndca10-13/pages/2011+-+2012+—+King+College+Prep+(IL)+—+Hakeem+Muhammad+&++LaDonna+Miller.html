Debate reifies internal colonialism that privileges white participants. The image of the successful national level debater is signified by a white male economically privileged body and all others are judged relative to this norm. Reid-Brinkley, 08 (Assistant Professor PhD and Debate Coach at University of Pittsburgh "THE HARSH REALITIES OF “ACTING BLACK”: HOW AFRICAN-AMERICAN POLICY DEBATERS NEGOTIATE REPRESENTATION THROUGH RACIAL PERFORMANCE AND STYLE"






Traditional knowledge production within the debate community privileges those with institutional and economic power while excluding those internal colonies. The three tier process which includes organic intellectual, academic intellectuals, and personal experiences within debate makes alternative forms of knowledge production legitimate and checks the homogenizing function of 'expert' discourse.Shanara Reid, 08, [Dr. Shanara Reid-Brinkley, "THE HARSH REALITIES OF “ACTING BLACK”: HOW AFRICAN-AMERICAN POLICY DEBATERS NEGOTIATE REPRESENTATION THROUGH RACIAL PERFORMANCE AND STYLE" pages 81-83]




Advocacy

We refuse to work within the bounds of this state-centered resolution and embrace the debate community as a site to expose the workings of white supremacy in our community. We advocate that theresolution is a metaphorfor oppressed groups to move beyond the mesosphere or limits that white supremacy has placed upon them, grow and demolish the traditional, policed system of education in the debate space. We must break through the mesosphere, fly past the sky, and create a new space free of white supremacy so we can create real social change.





The Role of the ballot


The role of the ballot is to affirm the team that performatively and methodologically carves out a space for the voices of those who have experienced oppression and been silenced by a society that has different priorities. We have to challenge white supremacist structure right here in this community so we can get the education and momentum to challenge internal colonialism within our own community.

Our peformative pedagogy is a counter hegemonic aesthetic that expresses our black culture. THIS is key to dismantle colonialism. No matter how many framework arguments you run, you cannot kill OUR SPIRIT TO BE FREE!
Warren and Fassett, 2004
