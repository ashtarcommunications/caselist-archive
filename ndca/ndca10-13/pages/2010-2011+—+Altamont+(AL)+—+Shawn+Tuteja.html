Insecure nuclear weapons-ready material in Russia poses a large threat to Russian national security, and has since the end of the Cold War. Howard Baker and Lloyd Cutler write, myweb.clemson.edu/~maloney/download/.../pdfs/seab_draft_report1.pdf. A Report Card on the Department of Energy's Nonproliferation Programs with Russia. January 10, 2001




And the relationship between Russia and Chechnya is strained in the status quo. Valery Dzutsev in ’10 writes,politicom.moldova.org/.../russianchechen-frictions-resurface-with-mass- ethnic-clashes-in-krasnodar-211140-eng.htm July 25, 2010. former coordinator for the Institute for War and Peace Reporting




Everyone agrees that continued conflict between Russia and Chechnya is inevitable. Attacks from the Chechen rebels are literally happening every day. Gregory White and Richard Boudreaux write this August, Chechen Rebels Dig In, Suggesting Rift in Insurgency Georgian Daily. http://georgiandaily.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=19697&Itemid=132



Therefore, the plan: The President of the United States, in consultation with Congress and in cooperation with the Russian Federation, should formulate a strategic plan to neutralize all nuclear weapons-usable material located in Russia. I reserve the right clarify specific implementation – if the negative wishes, they can call the specific strategy for disarmament. It’s way legit and we do indeed have a solvency advocate. Please call it.




Chechen rebels could get a nuclear weapon – they have the tactical skill to pull it off. Graham Allison writes,elfercenter.ksg.harvard.edu "Nuclear Terrorism: How Serious a Threat to Russia" September/October 2004. Director of Belfer Center for Science.



And, there are multiple scenarios for nuclear escalation. Chechen separatists can use Russia’s nukes to make a dirty bomb. Simon Saradzhyan One writes,Russia: Grasping the Reality of Nuclear Terror. Sept 2006. http://www.jstor.org/stable/25097839 (Research at Harvard Kennedy Center).

State ownership of nuclear weapons requires that state to have some sort of agency responsible for maintaining, testing, and using nuclear technologies, i.e. a military.
