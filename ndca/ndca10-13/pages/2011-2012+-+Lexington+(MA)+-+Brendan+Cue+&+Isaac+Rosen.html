Plan Text:

The United States federal government should develop and demonstrate inflatable solar power satellites in non geostationary orbit.

1AC Warming

Contention 1 is Warming:
It’s real and anthropogenic – immediate emission reductions key to prevent climate disruption
Somerville 11 (Professor of Oceanography @ UCSD Richard Somerville, Distinguished Professor Emeritus and Research Professor at Scripps Institution of Oceanography at the University of California, San Diego, Coordinating Lead Author in Working Group I for the 2007 Fourth Assessment Report of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, 3-8-2011, “CLIMATE SCIENCE AND EPA'S GREENHOUSE GAS REGULATIONS,” CQ Congressional Testimony, Lexis)

"In early 2007, ... reaching near zero well within this century."

Warming is human induced -- prefer our scientific assessments over single scientists or fringe theories
Alley 10 (Professor of Geoscience @ Penn State Richard, authored over 200 refereed scientific papers, which are "highly cited" according to a prominent indexing service, erved with distinguished national and international teams on major scientific assessment bodies, 11-17-2010, “CLIMATE CHANGE SCIENCE; COMMITTEE: HOUSE SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY; SUBCOMMITTEE: ENERGY AND ENVIRONMENT,” CQ Congressional Testimony, Lexis)

"Background on Climate Change and Global Warming. Scientific assessments such as those of the ... and that sufficiently large warming has removed them entirely."

SPS key -- it’s the only form of alternative energy that can relieve fossil fuel dependence – optimum efficiency and feasibility
Hsu 10 (Dr. Feng Hsu, Sr. Vice President of Systems Engineering & Risk Management Space Energy Group, 2010, Harnessing the Sun: Embarking on Humanity's Next Giant Leap, http://spacejournal.ohio.edu/issue16/hsu.html)

"We must set priorities and choose wisely. Within the next 30 years... there are business models for overcoming these issues."

Plan solves – initiates a new industrial revolution and revamps the global energy base
Hsu 10 (Feng, PhD and Former Head of NASA Goddard Center’s Risk Management and Former Research Engineer @ Brookhaven National Laboratory, ??/??/10 “Harnessing the Sun: Embarking on Humanity's Next Giant Leap,” http://spacejournal.ohio.edu/issue16/hsu.html)

"Abstract It has become increasingly evident ...start to do something in a significant way that has a chance to make a difference."

Independently – perception of SPS development prompts international cooperation on warming
Medin ’10 (Kristen,- Chief Industrial Designer, NewSpace DesignLabs, Winter http://spacejournal.ohio.edu/issue16/medin.html)

"Space-based solar power (SBSP) is an emerging industry ...We can focus on addressing the important issues of tomorrow with increased global cooperation."

Warming is the ONLY existential threat
Deibel 7 (Terry, Professor of IR at National War College, 200, “Foreign Affairs Strategy: Logic for American Statecraft” pg 387-389)

"Finally, there is one major existential threat to American security ...It is a threat not only to the security and prosperity to the United States, but potentially to the continued existence of life on this planet."
Warming amplifies ALLconflict – causes political instability and food, water, energy, and trade conflict
Kirpup 11 (James, Political correspondent for the Telegraph citing Energy Secretary of the UK, Huhne. Chris Huhne: Climate change threatens UK security. July 7, 2011. http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/8622945/Chris-Huhne-Climate-change-threatens-UK-security.html)

"The Energy Secretary predicted that UK ...It threatens to rip out the foundations on which our security rests.” "


1AC Aerospace

Contention 2 is Aerospace:
Aerospace atrophy now – foreign competitors are threatening U.S. leadership in innovation and technology
Albaugh 11 (Jim Albaugh, President and CEO of Boeing Commercial Airplanes, 4/27/11, “Keeping America’s Lead in Aerospace””, http://www.aia-aerospace.org/assets/speech_042711_albaugh.pdf | AK)

"The second threat to...is based on 21st century composites and innovations."
Only federal government investment into groundbreaking space development ensures private sector investment and long-term sustainability of the aerospace sector
Mankins 9 (Former NASA Scientist, managed numerous advanced space technology programs during his 25 years at NASA Headquarters and the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, and is widely known as an expert in space solar power
John, “To boldly go: the urgent need for a revitalized investment in spa ce technology,” 5-18-2009, http://www.thespacereview.com/article/1377/1)

"Unfortunately, the US investment in advanced research ...We need to reconsider what makes an ambitious space program worth a substantial investment of public dollars—and consider again the historical and future importance of advancing space technology and developing truly new and valuable space capabilities for the public, the nation, and the world."


Only a federal investment into SPS invigorates US aerospace leadership
NSSO 7(National Security Space Office, Report to the Director, 10-10-2007, “Space-Based Solar Power As an Opportunity for Strategic Security; Phase 0 Architecture Feasibility Study,” NSS, http://www.nss.org/settlement/ssp/library/final-sbsp-interim-assessment-release-01.pdf)

"FINDING: The SBSP Study Group found that SBSP offers a path to address...the United States must overcome the obstacles that jeopardize its ability to sustain leadership in space.” An SBSP program would be a powerful expression of this imperative."


Scenario 1 is Hegemony
It’s on the brink – rising China and challengers – American military capabilities key to maintain leadership
Kagan 11 (Robert Sr., a fellow at Brookings, 1/24/11, “The Price of Power: The benefits of U.S. defense spending far outweigh the costs,”)

"Today the international situation is also one of high risk....Those who recommend doing the same with less are only proposing a policy of insufficiency, where the United States makes commitments it cannot meet except at high risk of failure."
A collapsing aerospace sector takes down key sectors of US hegemony
Walker et al 2(Chairman of the USAIRobert Walker, et cal, Chair of the Commission on the Future of the United States Aerospace Industry Commissioners, 2002, “Final Report of the Commission on the Future of the United States Aerospace Industry Commissioners,” http://www.trade.gov/td/aerospace/aerospacecommission/AeroCommissionFinalReport.pdf)

"Defending our nation against its enemies in... the superior technologies and capabilities essential to the on-going transformation of the armed forces, thus maintaining our position as the world’s preeminent military power."
Loss of hegemony results in global nuclear war
Kagan, 7 - senior fellow at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace (Robert, “End of Dreams, Return of History”, 7/19, http://www.realclearpolitics.com/articles/2007/07/end_of_dreams_return_of_histor.html)

"This is a good thing, and it should continue to be a primary goal of American foreign policy to perpetuate this relatively benign international configuration of power. ...Difficult as it may be to extend American predominance into the future, no one should imagine that a reduction of American power or a retraction of American influence and global involvement will provide an easier path."



Second – Economic Leadership
Aerospace competitiveness drives economic growth – purely private sector approaches guarantee failure
Hernnstadt 8 (Director of Trade and Globalization @ IAMAW Owen, director of the Trade and Globalization Department, International Association of Machinists and Aerospace Workers, “Offsets and the lack of a comprehensive U.S. policy,” Economic Policy Institute, http://www.sharedprosperity.org/bp201.html)

"Aerospace is an especially important industry fora nation's economic and physical ...While the U.S. government continues a hands-off approach to this market-distorting scheme, other countries are giving their companies significant backing based on well-developed industrial policies."

Collapse causes WW3 and global transition wars---turns all their impacts
Panzner 8 (Michael J. Panzner, faculty at the New York Institute of Finance, 25-year veteran of the global stock, bond, and currency markets who has worked in New York and London for HSBC, Soros Funds, ABN Amro, Dresdner Bank, and JPMorgan Chase, 2008, “Financial Armageddon: Protect Your Future from Economic Collapse”, Revised and Updated Edition, p. 136-138)

"Many will wonder whether the United States might renege on some of its financial obligations or even declare an outright default on its once AAA securities....Many will interpret stepped-up confl ictsbetween Muslims and Western societies as the beginnings of a new world war."


Economic benefits happen before any component is ever in space
SEC 8(Space Enterprise Council, 2008, NSS, http://www.nss.org/settlement/ssp/library/2008-SECSpaceBasedSolarPowerWhitePaper.pdf)

"SBSP is unusual among renewable energy options because it might satisfy a...In this and related applications, SBSP could emerge as an enhancement for, rather than a competitor with, terrestrial solar power generation."



1AC Solvency

Contention 3 is Solvency

Plan solves – only major government investment and demonstration jumpstarts private sector involvement
IAA 11(International Academy of Astronautics, Academy that brings together the world's foremost experts in the disciplines of astronautics on a regular basis to recognize the accomplishments of their peers, and explores and discuss cutting-edge issues in space research and technology, 4-2011, “The First International Assessment of Space Solar Power: Opportunities, Issues and Potential Pathways Forward,” Green Energy From Space Solar Power, http://iaaweb.org/iaa/Scientific%20Activity/Study%20Groups/SG%20Commission%203/sg311/sg311finalreport.pdf)

"Finding 1: Fundamentally new energy technologies clearly appear to be needed ...and demonstrations."
No solvency deficits – new inflatable architecture overcomes all tech and launch cost barriers
Brown 9 (Trevor, contributor to the Air and Space Power Journal and author focused on political, economic, and military strategy for the medium of space and holds a BA from Indiana University and an MSc from Nanyang Technological University, 6/1/09, “SSP: A Spherical Architecture”, http://www.thespacereview.com/article/1383/1)

"Accordingly, SSP has been criticized for ...in the medium of space."

It’s cost competitive with traditional energy sources – three warrants – military interest, global warming, and carbon taxes
Lemonick 9 (Michael D., currently a senior writer at Climate Central and former science and environmental Senior Writer for Time Magazine and Professor at Princeton University, 8/31/9, “Solar Power from Space: Moving Beyond Science Fiction”, http://e360.yale.edu/mobile/feature.msp?id=2184)

"Despite the enormous promise of solar power, the... SBSP will immediately become more competitive economically."

Microwaves are safe and efficient – comparatively less dangerous than sunlight
Garretson 9 (Peter A., US-based Airpower & Spacepower Strategist and Visiting Fellow at the Indian Institute for Defense Studies and Analysis, 5/22/09, “Space as the Source of Our Future Energy”, http://www.idsa.in/idsastrategiccomments/spaceasthesourceofourfutureenergy_pgarretson_220509)
"Existing communications satellites use a similar process... and is non-ionizing and not dangerous like UV."

Non-geostationary orbit is key - makes SPS cost competitive and avoids your disads
Jones 10 (Royce, Space Technology Entrepeneur, BA in Construction Management, Computer Programming, and Computer Aided Design, Former Marine Corps NCO, Expert on SPS, " Alternative Orbits: A New Space Solar Power Reference Design," Online Journal of Space Communication, Winter, http://spacejournal.ohio.edu/issue16/jones.html, EMM)

"This paper offers a new space solar power reference design based ...substantially reduce SSP satellite mass."
