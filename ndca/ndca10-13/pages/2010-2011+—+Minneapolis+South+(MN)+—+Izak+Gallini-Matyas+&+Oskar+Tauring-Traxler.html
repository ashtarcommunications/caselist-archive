Affirmative


Militarism Aff


Contention One is Significance:

Military is an inherently violent and destructive institution
PR Watch, August 16-23, 2010 edition, http://www.prwatch.org/node/9331

Thus, we demand the United States federal government remove all military presence from the following countries; Iraq, Afghanistan, South Korea, Kuwait, Japan, and Turkey. We reserve the right to clarify.

Contention Two is the Give me Liberty or Give me Death:

Advantage 1 is Society
A. The military is our society. It dominates our economy, our entertainment, even our very logic and thinking
Fogarty, Brian E., chair and professor of Sociology at College of Saint Catherine, 2000, (War, Peace, and the Social Order, Page 102-103)
B. Local actions like the plan are key to stopping militarism in our society
Fogarty, Brian E., chair and professor of Sociology at College of Saint Catherine, 2000, (War, Peace, and the Social Order, Page 213)
C. And, small changes solve
Fogarty, Brian E., chair and professor of Sociology at College of Saint Catherine, 2000, (War, Peace, and the Social Order, Page 198-199)
D. Faith in violence only recreates violence. Stopping violence is a D rule, because it is the root cause of all harms
MLK, Dr Martin Luther King, Jr., The Trumpet Of Conscience, 1967, p. 70-71

Advantage 2 is Kill to Save

A. We must reject the Western illusion that we can save humanity by destroying part of it. This mindset justifies the most violent atrocities in history
Boaventura de Santos, Prof at Univ of Coimbra, April 2003, “Collective Suicide?”, Bad Subjects, Issue # 63
B. Faith in the idea that we can kill to save only worsens the cycle of violence. Rejecting this idea is an act of nonviolence and recognition of our role as individuals able to make judgment.
Stephen Wink, Associate at Cleary, Gottlieb, Steen & Hamilton, and Walter Wink, Prof of Biblical Interpretation, Auburn Theological Seminary; Saint Louis University Law Journal, Winter, 1993-1994


Advantage 3 is Militarism

A. Militarism creates a world in which violence is celebrated; makes war inevitable.
Bachevich, professor of International Relations at Boston University, 2005 (Andrew, The new American Militarism, page 22-23)
B. Militarism is the root cause of environmental degradation.
Simon Doolittle, works on the program for development, Peacebuilding, and the environment at the Political Economy Research Institute at the University of Massachusetts, Spring 2003, "Ten reasons why militarism is bad for the environment"
C. Biodiversity is key to the globe
Irish Times 02 (7/27, LN)

Advantage Four is Security:

A. Language constitutes reality: cold-war rhetoric spurred the violently exclusionary identity of a national security state hell bent on constructing threats to serve political purposes of maintaining power. Our critical self examination of identity creations is crucial to breaking the self fulfilling cycle of national security.
Grondin 2004 (David Grondin, Masters in Political Science and Ph.D. Candidate – University of Ottawa, 2004, (“(Re)Writing the ‘National Security State,’ Center for United States Studies, p. 12-17, MT)

B. The reality created by security imposes deeply embedded hatred for the enemy in all of us which enables violent acts to occur under the guise of less than human enemies. Our enemies are not real, rather creations of our own mind that are sustained by group mentalities that political leaders prey on. This orientation to the world ensures a globe of violence that will inevitably kill us all. We are not innately violent, we can overcome violence.
Mack 1988 (John E., M.D. an American psychiatrist, writer, and professor at Harvard Medical School. He was a Pulitzer Prize-winning biographer. “The Enemy System” 1988.http://www.johnemackinstitute.org/passport/enemysystem.html, MT)

C. Security is an illusion that has forgotten it is an illusion. Recognizing this and abandoning its logic emancipates us and opens up alternative spaces in the future that can social domination.
Neocleous 2008 (Mark is a Professor at Brunel University, Critique of Political Economy; Head of Department of Politics & History, he joined Brunel University in the Department of Government in 1994. Since then he has published numerous books and articles. His most recent work has been towards the development of a critique of security. “CRITIQUE OF SECURITY” 2008. Pg. 185-186, MT)

D. Representations form the basis of what is and what is not thinkable in policy making. Neg arguments should be evaluated on the basis of how it represents foreign policy. Our representation of the world will always create a better outcome than the neg.
Jourde 2006 (Cedric, Ph.D., Political Science, University of Wisconsin-Madison, Madison, 2002 * M.A., Political Science, University of Wisconsin-Madison, Madison, 1996 * B.Sc., Political Science, Université de Montréal, Montréal, 1995 Hegemony or Empire?: The redefinition of US Power under George W Bush Ed. David and Grondin p. 182-183 2006, MT)

Observation Four, Framework: The Art of Love


FIRST)
We must abandon the notion that ends can justify means in our attempts to achieve peace. Peace is not only an end, but also the only means to that end.
MLK, Dr Martin Luther King, Jr., The Trumpet Of Conscience, 1967, p. 70-71

“Now let me say, secondly, that… means cannot bring about constructive ends.”

SECOND)
Conceptions of war as an event outside of daily life lend themselves to crisis-based politics. This crisis-based politics prohibits the investigation of questions central to understanding and resisting violence. A move away from these conceptions opens up space for more nuanced investigations of violence and pave the way for additional strategies of resistance.

Chris Cuomo, assistant professor of philosophy and women's studies at the University of Cincinnati, Hypatia, Fall 1996, v11 n4 p30(16)
