The E-Lusion - Narrative for 1AC/1NC
It’s called the prologue, the preface, the beginning, my narrative, the reason why I spit, I flow, and rhyme. Its starts at, the beginning, the prologue, my narrative, when I wake up in the morning and realize that our youth is losing their culture and pride, They have become color blind to the real world, and as I stand here I see it with my own eyes. The brutality of it all as equivalent to when I walk out the door of my house only to see another black body brutalized by law enforcement and to hear the sounds of gunshots blasting like the speed reading of a policy debate round. No communication, no education. The gentrification of our voice like the gentrification of our hoods. It’s like the debate community is pushing us out, pushing my voice out and where do I go from here. Become homeless because I was forced out. Forced to reside within the Brownfield’s of the debate community because our voice was not on their right field, get it a bright field! We are not here to throw up stacks and bring turf wars. Traditional vs. Nontraditional. Is it Bloods vs. Crips, yet reality vs. fiat? Imprisoned like the Panoptic borders of a nation built on the back bones of immigrants forced to obtain the permanent residency to the debate community, under the rain of emotional stimulants prescribed to the 9 month pregnant woman due to give birth to our style our culture Hip-Hop, within the unhealthy corporate oppressive market co- modified environment. It is the unhealthy factors of the debate community like military recruiters; they recruit to be the future of our country, a future void of morality. Like father like son, like teacher like student, like the state like administration. If this community mirrors the political pollution, like the board of ED, the people will not be engaged. We are asleep, hit with a home run to the head as we forget our identity, while the wind takes out brain away and we become vegetables to the state. Domestically abused by the aggressive nature of this activity, within its elites mindset. Evicting us out for our lack of resources under the impression that our school is yes pink…. Pink Like the panther mysteriously lacking critical pedagogy to keep us engaged, and so they drop. Dropped in a disruptive vibe. Becoming airheads, in a world of realistic but unrealistic ideologies. Profiled like the face books of this society let alone our racial ethnicity. Forgetting what’s moral, forgetting what’s right. My narrative, the beginning, the reason why I rhyme. This is the United States of amnesia.

Hip-Hop has become a global language. Perspectives of color are essential for the revolution. Unless debate changes to accommodate voices of oppressed, Elitism will continue to rule,
