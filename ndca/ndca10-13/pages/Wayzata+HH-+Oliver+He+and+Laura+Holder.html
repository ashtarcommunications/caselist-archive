CONTENTION ONE: INHERENCY

THE UNITED STATES REMAINS COMMITTED TO MAINTAINING AN EXPANDED MILITARY PRESENCE ON OKINAWA DESPITE GROWING TENSIONS AND POLITICAL CLIMATE
Peter Ennis, “Okinawa: Why the Nakaima win spells trouble,” 11/29/10, Dispatch Japan http://www.dispatchjapan.com/blog/2010/11/okinawa-why-the-nakaima-win-spells-trouble.html

THUS THE PLAN:

THE UNITED STATES FEDERAL GOVERNMENT SHOULD WITHDRAW ALL THIRD MARINE EXPEDITIONARY FORCE TROOPS FROM THE OKINAWA PREFECTURE OF JAPAN EXCEPT THE 31ST MARINE EXPEDITIONARY UNIT AND MARINE WING LIAISON OF KADENA.

CONTENTION TWO: BASES

FIRST, TRYING TO HOLD ONTO OUR MARINE PRESENCE CAUSES A POLITICAL BACKLASH THAT ENSURES WE LOSE OUR ALLIANCE AND ALL OF OUR BASES – SACRIFICING OKINAWA SAVES THE TROOP PROJECTION REGIME
O’Hanlon, Director of Research & Senior Fellow in Foreign Policy at Brookings, Director of Research for the 21st Centry Defense Initiative, 01 (Michael, “Defense Policy Choices for the Bush Administration”, ed. Mike Mochizuki)

SECOND, FAILURE TO MOVE THE MARINES WILL ROCK THE ALLIANCE AND IGNITE OPPOSITION TO OUR OTHER BASES IN JAPAN – THE PLAN IS THE BEST WAY TO SHORE UP OUR DEFENSE CAPABILITIES
Ennis, former member of the Council on Foreign Relations task forces regarding Japan, former Research Fellow at Dartmouth’s Dickey Center for International Understanding, long-time analyst of U.S.-Japan relations, U.S. Correspondent for Keekly Tokyo Keizai, founder of The Oriental Economist, 8-18-10 (Peter, “Gates and Japan,” Dispatch Japan, http://www.dispatchjapan.com/blog/2010/08/gates-and-japan.html)

SCENARIO 1: YOKOSUKA

1. STATUS QUO KILLS YOKOSUKA NAVAL DEPLOYMENT – ONLY THE PLAN ALLOWS NAVAL PROJECTION
Auslin, Director of Japan Studies at the American Enterprise Institute & former History Professor & Senior Research Fellow at the MacMilan Center for Interantation & Area Studes at Yale, 6-16-10 (Micahel, “The Real Futenma Fallout,” Wall Street Journal Asia,http://www.aei.org/article/102196)

2. EVERY CARRIER IS KEY—LOSS OF OUR CARRIERS WOULD DESTROY OUR GLOBAL NAVAL DOMINANCE
Allen, former commander US Atlantic Fleet and former president Association of Naval Aviation, 01
Richard Allen, retired from active duty in 1996 as commander, Naval Air Force, U.S. Atlantic Fleet, and is the immediate past president of the Association of Naval Aviation., “Aircraft carriers and sea-based air power: Smaller is not better and numbers do count!,” Sea Power, June 2001

3. CARRIER-BASED DETERRENCE MAINTAINS WORLD PEACE - IT PREVENTS INEVITABLE BRUSHFIRES FROM BECOMING GLOBAL CONFLAGRATIONS
Admiral Johnson, Chief of Naval Operations, & General Krulak, Commandant of the U.S. Marine Corps, 97 (, “Forward Presence is Essential to American Interests,,” http://www.milnet.com/pentagon/navy/fwdpresn.htm)


4. PERCEPTION OF NAVAL DETERRENCE IS KEY TO PREVENT CHINESE AGGRESSION OVER TAIWAN
Ross ‘2 (Robert S., Prof of Pol Sci @ Boston College and Associate of John King Fairbank Center for East Asian Studies @Harvard University, “Navigating the Taiwan Strait”, http://belfercenter.ksg.harvard.edu/files/ross_v27n2.pdf)

5. CHINESE AGGRESSION TO TAKE TAIWAN MEANS ESCALATION INTO NUCLEAR WAR
Strait Times 2k (The Straits Times (Singapore), “No One Gains In War Over Taiwan”, June 25, 2000, L/N)

SCENARIO 2: KADENA

1. KADENA IS A STRATEGIC NECESSITY – IT’S KEY TO AIR POWER AND ESSENTIAL FOR QUICK ACCESS TO THE REGIONAL HOTSPOTS
Axe 2k7 (David, David Axe is a military correspondent living in Washington, D.C. Since 2005 he has reported from Iraq, Lebanon, East Timor, Afghanistan and Somalia. He is a regular contributor to Wired, The Washington Times, C-SPAN and BBC Radio, among many other outlets “Fortress Hubs,” Defense Technology International, pg nexisghs-ef)

2. SPEED IS ESSENTIAL – AIR POWER MUST BE QUICK TO STOP CRISIS ESCALATION IN SOUTHEAST ASIA AND THE SOUTH CHINA SEA
Tellis Et Al ’98 (Ashley, Ashley J., Senior Political Scientist at RAND specializing in South Asian Security, Chung Min Lee, James Mulvenon, Courtney Purrington, and Michael D. Swaine, pg online @ http://www.rand.org/pubs/monograph_reports/MR897/MR897.chap3.pdf ghs-ef)


3. AND, THE SOUTH CHINA SEA IS A CRITICAL FLASHPOINT – MISCALCULATION AND LACK OF COMMUNICATION MAKES THE RISK OF ESCALATION HIGH
The Global Post 6/27 (“South China Sea: The Coming War?,” pg online @ http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/china/100625/south-china-sea-paracel-spratly-islands-military ghs-ef)

CONTENTION 3: ALLIANCE

NEWLY ELECTED OKINAWA GOVERNOR AND THE ANTI-BASE MINDED DEMOCRATS ENSURE THAT ABSENT POLITICAL RESOLUTION, THE ALLIANCE COLLAPSES IN 6 MONTHS

Peter Ennis, “Okinawa: Why the Nakaima Win Spells Trouble,” 11/29/10, Dispatch Japan http://www.dispatchjapan.com/blog/2010/11/okinawa-why-the-nakaima-win-spells-trouble.html


COOPERATION WITH JAPAN IS CRITICAL TO SOLVE THE NORTH KOREA ISSUE
Rapp, Lieutenant Col. With a PH.D in IR from Stanford, 2004 (William E., “Paths Diverging? Accessed online at http://www.carlisle.army.mil/ssi/pdffiles/PUB367.pdf)


FAILURE OF NEGOTIATIONS ON NORTH KOREA WILL MASSIVE CONFLICT
Brad Glosserman, Director of Research at Pacific Forum CSIS, 11-4-2003, US-China: the next alliance?, http://www.glocom.org/debates/20031104_gloss_us/


KOREAN WAR CAUSES EXTINCTION
Chol, ’02 (Kim Myong, The Agreed Framework is Brain Dead, http://www.nautilus.org/fora/security/0212A_Chol.html)

CONTENTION 4: DEFENSE INTEGRATION

1. DESPITE A JAPANESE PLEDGE TO ENGAGE IN MISSILE DEFENSE DEVELOPMENTS, BUDGET SHORTAGES HAVE FORCED CUTS – JAPAN HAS BEEN FORCED TO FREEZE MISSILE DEFENSE FUNDING AND DELAY LAUNCHING SYSTEMS
AFP 12/19/09, (“Japan To Halt New Missile Defence Spending: Media,” pg nexis)


2. THE PLAN CREATES THE PERFECT COMPROMISE – REMOVING THE MARINES FROM OKINAWA WOULD SOLVE THE ALLIANCE AND FREE UP FUNDS FOR JAPAN TO COMMIT TO MISSILE DEFENSE
O’Hanlon ’98, (Michael, “How to Be a Cheap Hawk: The 1999 and 2009 Defense Budgets,” pg 82-83)


3. BMD HAS A DETERRENT EFFECT
Pfaltzgraff Et. Al. ‘9 -- Robert, President, Ph.D. in PoliSci, Institute for Foreign Policy Analysis, Prof @ National Defense College, “Boost-Phase Missile Defense: Present Challenges, Future Prospects”, 4-3, The George Marshall Institute, http://www.marshall.org/pdf/materials/653.pdf.

4. THAT CHECKS A GLOBAL NUCLEAR ARMS RACE
Stensrud ‘9 – Jamie, Prolific Author, Cites Multiple Studies, “Fears Over North Korean
Ballistic Missile Capability,” http://yowusa.com/war/2000/war-2000-09a/1.shtml.

5. EXTINCTION
Doyle ‘9 (Clare, Committee for a Workers' International, Nuclear sabre-rattling, http://socialistworld.net/eng/2009/06/0701.html)


6. MISSILE DEFENSE IS THE KEY INTEGRATION ELEMENT – WILL ENSURE U.S.-JAPANESE DEFENSE INTEGRATION, PLANNING AND INTEROPERABILITY
Blumenthal 05 (Dan, Dan Blumenthal is a resident fellow at AEI, “The Revival of the U.S.-Japanese Alliance,” pg online @http://www.aei.org/outlook/22027)


7. CHANGING U.S.-JAPANESE DEFENSE COOP TO INCLUDE A CROSS-FLOW OF DUAL USE HIGH TECHNOLOGY SOLVES THE ALLIANCE, U.S. INNOVATION, MANUFACTURING, AND ECONOMY
Bloch 97, (Erich, National Research Council, Maximizing U.S. Interests in Science and Technology Relations with Japan, Committee on Japan Framework Statement and Report of the Competitiveness Task Force, National Research Council)

8. U.S. RATE OF INNOVATION, TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENT AND R&D MUST KEEP PACE TO DEVELOP PRODUCTS TECHNOLOGY AND SOLUTIONS TO SOLVE COLLAPSE OF THE GLOBAL BIOSPHERE
Barker 2k, (BRENT is manager of corporate communications, having earlier served as manager of strategic and executive communications and for12 years as the Journal's editor-in-chief, Technology and the Quest for Sustainability, EPRI Journal, pg nexis)

10. US-JAPAN BMD COOPERATION IS CRITICAL TO RELATIONS AND ALLIANCE
Rubinstein ‘7 -- Gregg A. Rubinstein, 9/5, Official of the U.S. Departments of State and Defense, Consultant on U.S.-Japan Defense Programs, “US-Japan Missile Defense Cooperation: Current Status, Future Prospects” http://www.japanconsidered.com/OccasionalPapers/Rubinstein%20USJA%20BMD%20article%20090507.pdf.


CONTENTION 5: SOLVENCY

WHAT THE U.S. SHOULD DO IS SUBSTANTIALLY REDUCE THE NUMBER OF MARINES IN JAPAN – IT PRESERVES THE ENTIRETY OF OUR DETERRENCE AND IMPROVES SECURITY TIES IN THE REGION
O’Hanlon, Director of Research & Senior Fellow in Foreign Policy at Brookings, Director of Research for the 21st Centry Defense Initiative, 97 (Michael, “Restructuring U.S. Forces & Bases In Japan,” Toward a True Allicane: Restructuring U.S.-Japan Security Relations, ed. Mike Mochizuki)
