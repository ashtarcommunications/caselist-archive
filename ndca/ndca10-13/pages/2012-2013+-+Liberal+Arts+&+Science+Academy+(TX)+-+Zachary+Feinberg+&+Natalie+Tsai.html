1AC #1


Observation 1: The Status Quo

Despite 84% of the population living in metropolitan areas, federal support for mass transit infrastructure is declining
Regan 7-12 (Ed Regan, 6-7-12, CDM Smith, “Ground Zero: America’s Cities Need New Infrastructure and Transit Funding”, http://cdmsmith.com/en-US/Insights/Funding-Future-Mobility/Exit-3A-Ground-Zero.aspx, 7-12-12)


This is part of a broader neoliberal re-structuring of the urban economy. Decreasing federal support forces localities to generate revenue through increased privatization of public goods.

Brenner & Theodore 02 (Neil, Department of Sociology and Metropolitan Studies Program, New York University and Nik, Urban Planning and Policy Program and Center for Urban Economic Development, University of Illinois at Chicago, Cities and the Geographies of “Actually Existing Neoliberalism”, 2002, http://www.urbaneconomy.org/sites/default/files/Theodore_

citiesandactuallyexistingneoliberalism.pdf)


Public transportation is a lynchpin of this neoliberal strategy –privatized infrastructure exacerbates fragmentation and inequality.

Farmer 10(Stephanie Department of Sociology, Roosevelt University, “Uneven public transportation development in neoliberalizing Chicago, USA,” Environment and Planning A, volume 43, pages 1154 ^ 1172)


This continued transfer of transportation from the public to the private is a contraction of the democratic public sphere in the name of expanding private capital

Harvey 8(David, September-October, PhD Cambridge 1962; Distg Prof, “The Right to the City,” New Left Review, Volume: 53, http://www.newleftreview.org/II/53/david-harvey-the-right-to-the-city)


Neoliberal control of transportation systems exacerbates social inequalities, liquidates democratic practices, and reduces culture and education to indexes of profitability.

Giroux 4(Henry A., Global TV Network Chair in Communications at McMaster University in Canada, author of The Abandoned Generation: Democracy Beyond the Culture of Fear, “Public Pedagogy and the Politics of Neo-liberalism: making the political more pedagogical,” Policy Futures in Education, Volume: 2, p. 495 and 496, JS)


Being reduced to profitability requires devaluation and dehumanization destroying value to life

Dillon 99

(Michael Dillon, University of Lancaster, “Another Justice” Political Theory Vol. 27, No. 2, Aprill 1999, JSTOR)


Neoliberalism requires a logic whereby whole populations are sacrificed as collateral damage to the expansion of markets.

Santos ‘03 (Boaventura de Sousa, director of the Center for Social Studies at the University of Coimbra, EUROZINE, COLLECTIVE SUICIDE OR GLOBALIZATION FROM BELOW, http://www.eurozine.com/article/2003-03-26-santos-en.html)


Neoliberalism is a self-destructing system that destroys its working class - we have to end it now

Pramono 2 (Siswo, School of Social Sciences, The Australian National University, Canberra, http://www.fatihun.edu.tr/~jesr/Neoliberalism.pdf, pg. 121-122)


Thus the Plan:


The United States federal government should fully fund public development and control of fare-free public transit in all metropolitan areas in the United States.

Observation 2: Solvency


Free mass transit is the key —rejecting commodification of public goods creates genuine political dialogues and offers concrete visions for how to transform material conditions of oppression for the poor.

Schein 11(Rebecca, assistant professor at the University of California, Santa Cruz, “Free Transit and Social Movement Infrastructure: Assessing the Political Potential of Toronto’s Nascent Free Transit Campaign,” Alternate Routes: A Journal of Critical Social Research, Volume: 22, p. 115)


It’s the combination of struggles to define how resources are used, outline the impacts of the order and work to project an alternative vision. The judge’s rejection of neoliberalism in this round is key.

Kotz 3 (David M., Professor of Economics at the University of Massachusetts – Amherst, Socialism and Global Neoliberal Capitalism, Economics Department and Political Economy Research Institute at the University of Massachusetts – Amherst, pg. 6-7, http://people.umass.edu/dmkotz/Soc_and_Global_NL_Kism_03_03.pdf, 2003, JKE)


Free mass transit is the starting point --plan builds coalitions and allows for other projects to expand in a proactive movement with a new ontology

Schein ‘11 (Assistant Prof Human Rights –The Institute of Interdisciplinary Studies at Carleton 2011, Rebecca. "Free Transit and Social Movement Infrastructure: Assessing thePolitical Potential of Toronto’s Nascent Free Transit Campaign", Alternate Routes volume http://www.alternateroutes.ca/index.php/ar/article/view/14421)


Ontology first - challenging the metaphysical assumptions of the relationship between humans and economic policy is key

Oksala ‘11(Johanna, Senior Research Fellow in the Academy of Finland research project is Philosophy and Politics in Feminist Theory at the University of Helsinki, November 3rd, “Violence and Neoliberal Governmentality,” Constellations, Volume: 18, Pages 482 and 483)


Federal action is key - local and private action leads to social polarization

Barney Warf, Association of American Geographers, Mar.,2003(Splintering Urbanism: Networked Infrastructures, Technological Mobilities, and the Urban Condition by Stephen Graham; Simon Marvin Review, Annals of the Association of American Geographers, Vol. 93, No. 1, pp. 246-247)
