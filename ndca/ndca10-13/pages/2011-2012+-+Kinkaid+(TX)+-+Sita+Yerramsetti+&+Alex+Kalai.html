Affirmative

Plan: The United States Federal Government should substantially increase the development of nanotechnology in space beyond the Earth’s mesosphere.

Contention 1 is poverty


A. The US economy is still fragile
Roubini Jan. 16 2012 [Nouriel. Don’t Believe the Good News
Consumers are income-challenged, wealth-challenged, and debt-constrained. That doesn’t bode well for 2012. Roubini He anticipated the collapse of the United States housing market and the worldwide recession which started in 2008. He teaches at New York University's Stern School of Business. http://www.slate.com/articles/news_and_politics/project_syndicate/2012/01/u_s_economic_growth_will_be_anemic_in_2012.html]


B. The recession substantially increased global poverty.
Global Economic Assessment by Austrilian Federal Government 2010 [October 12. AusAid’s response to the global recession. http://www.ausaid.gov.au/makediff/gec.cfm]


C. Poverty causes violence that is worse than a perpetual nuclear war.

Abu-Jamal 98 [Mumia. Sept. 19. Incarcerated social activist and author who has written books and articles since being placed on death row. http://www.mumia.nl/TCCDMAJ/quietdv.htm]

D. Large government investment in nanotech is key to status quo economic recovery.
Tour 2011 [James M. April 14. Tour has a Ph.D. from the Richard E. Smalley Institute for Nanoscale Science and Technology at Rice University. Pdf before House Science, Space and Technology Subcommittee on Research and Science Education.]


E. The U.S. economy is key to the global economy.

Werner 10 [Erica. November 10. Erica Werner is a reporter for the Associated Press. http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2010/nov/10/obama-strong-us-economy-key-global-recovery/]




Contention 2 is STEM.

A. Plan revamps STEM education.
Murday et al 9 [Dr. James S. Murday - Office of Research Advancement
University of Southern California, Sponsored by the National Science Foundation, Dr. Mihail Roco - Senior Advisor for Nanotechnology @ the National Science Foundation, Dr. Mary Poats - Engineering Education and Human Resource Development, Division of Engineering Education and Centers @ the National Science Foundation, “ Partnership for Nanotechnology Education,” 4/28, pdf, ZR]

B. Federal research and development is critical to motivation and collaboration.
Nelson 7 [George D. Nelson - Director in Science, Mathematics and Technology Education Western Washington University, Congressional Testimony, “EDUCATORS' VIEWS ON SCIENCE EDUCATION,” lexis, ZR]

C. STEM is key to long run economic growth-jobs.
Koebler 12[Jason. January 18. Jason is an author on STEM and education for the U.S. news and is also an author for the Washingtonian. http://www.usnews.com/news/blogs/stem-education/2012/01/18/innovateeducate-launches-stem-e-magazine]

D. Economic collapse causes global war and increased terrorism-empirics prove.
Mead 9 [Walter Russell. February 4. Walter Russell Mead is the Henry A. Kissinger Senior Fellow in U.S. Foreign Policy at the Council on Foreign Relations and the author of God and Gold: Britain, America and the Making of the Modern World. http://www.tnr.com/article/only-makes-you-stronger-0.]




Contention 3 is Warming.
A. Warming is real and is anthropogenic.
Borenstein 11[Seth. Borenstein is a Science writer for the Associated Press.http://news.yahoo.com/biggest-jump-ever-seen-global-warming-gases-183955211.html

B. Nano solves warming – green tech, carbon clean up, and pollution monitoring.

Peterson and Heller 7 [Christine Peterson, co- founder and President of Foresight Institute, serves on the Advisory Board of the International Council on Nanotechnology and the Editorial Advisory Board of NASA's Nanotech Briefs, and served on California's Blue Ribbon Task Force on Nanotechnology. Jacob Heller, Policy associate for the Foresight Nanotech Institute. A Truman Scholar, he received his B.A. in politics and economics from Pitzer College. “Nanotech’s Promise: Overcoming Humanity’s More Pressing Challenges” in “Nanoethics: The Ethical and Social Implications of Nanotechnology” Copyright 2007, BJM]


C. Warming is the biggest terminal impact---leads to nuclear war, terrorism, famine, and global instability.
Harris 2004 [Paul. February 22. Harris is a reporter for the British Guardian newspaper. http://www.guardian.co.uk/environment/2004/feb/22/usnews.theobserver.]


D. Warming independently causes extinction

Sify 10 [Sydney newspaper citing Ove Hoegh-Guldberg, professor at University of Queensland and Director of the Global Change Institute, and John Bruno, associate professor of Marine Science at UNC Sify News, “Could unbridled climate changes lead to human extinction?”, http://www.sify.com/news/could-unbridled-climate-changes-lead-to-human-extinction-news-international-kgtrOhdaahc.html, WEA]



E. U.S. involvement is key to combating warming.

Environmental America 9 [December 3. Environment America is a federation of state-based, citizen-funded environmental advocacy organizations. America on the Move: State Leadership in the Fight Against Global Warming and What it Means for the World.]
