Baudrillard says that humanity is in space looking in on a hollow and meaningless pseudo-Earth. All words and ideas lose meaning in a mass of signifiers with only other signifiers as their signified, a model of a model that distances people from reality. While the simulacra and simulation theories may be accurate, reject the nihilism of Baudrillard, embracing instead a Deleuzian alternative approach to cultural analysis.

Put away Baudrillard—his ideal is still comprised of simulacra. Instead embrace the revolutionary potential behind the process of engaging a multiplicity instead of straining for singularity and to reclaim the possibility of individual agency from Baudrillard’s passivity. Humans have a potential to make meaningful change. Capitalism itself is giving the opportunity to change; it is deterritorializing both spaces and identities, giving space for the rhizome and schizo. Acting now prevents the reterritorialization coming later and destroys capitalism’s control over our lives.

How does all of this apply to our present ... becoming realer than real in a monstrous contagion of our own making.

Capitalism is the root of all impacts – it controls the military by creating the desire for war to deposit surplus capital and it engineers genocide to check the human surplus. Cap good warrants assume an apolitical cap but the good things cap does are to cover up its negative effects.

Four reasons why cap comes first:
1. No impact to death—humans are atoms arranged one way and death just rearranges them—it’s a new form of becoming

2. Body counts eliminate questions of suffering. America never stopped slavery, just exported it to sweatshops; never ended fascism just called it patriotism. Util justifies the worst forms of bare life by repressing our desires in the name of life as solely continuing to breathe.

3. There is no honor or beauty in human life - death is forgotten fast as another worker takes one’s place. Everything wishes for death under capitalism because there is nothing to live for but another routine day at the factory. Capitalism produces carbon copies of life used to power the mills.
Deleuze and Guattari 1972 To reject the personal pronouns, Anti-Oedipus, 330-39, Murray
But it seems that things are becoming very obscure, for what is this distinction between the experience ... following behind and being swallowed up-a question of regime.


4. This affirmation and its impact framework begin with an evaluation of desires and instincts. Absent this investigation, change is doomed to failure as desire is tainted by the flaws of past politics. Desire is strangled by the formations of the status quo, turning life against itself and preventing the existence of immaculate joy
Deleuze and Guattari 1972 To reject the personal pronouns (Anti-Oedipus 345-349)
Libidinal investment does not bear upon the regime of the social syntheses,... social machines back to the elementary forces of desire that form them.
