Anderson 1ac

Plan: The United States federal government should remove its B-61 gravity bombs from the Republic of Turkey.


Contention 1 is Inherency

U.S. has TNWs in Turkey with no use. Weapons have been reduced in the past but 90 still exist. Theory of deterrence does not apply
Alexandra Bell is the research assistant for Nuclear Policy at the Center for American Progress. Bell received a master's degree in International Affairs from the New School and a bachelor's degree in Peace, War and Defense and U.S. History from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Her graduate work focused on nonproliferation and security issues in the Middle East. 11-23-2009 [The status of U.S. nuclear weapons in Turkey, http://www.thebulletin.org/-web-edition/features/the-status-of-us-nuclear-weapons-turkey] VP



Contentions 2 is Terrorism

Terrorist have a bull’s-eye for TNWs in Turkey
Aslıhan Tümer is a disarmament campaigner for Greenpeace Turkey, 6-8-2006 [“NATO’s Nuclear Sharing and Incirlik” International Network of Engineers and Scientists Against Proliferation, http://www.inesap.org/sites/default/files/inesap_old/bulletin26/art11.htm]

Nuclear terrorism is likely and causes global nuclear war
Patrick Speice is a JD Canidate February 2006 Lexis

Even an unsuccessful attack triggers extinction

Mohamed Sid-Ahmed is a political analyst, August 26-September 1st, 2004 [Al-Ahram Weekly http://weekly.ahram.org.eg/2004/705/op5.htm]


Contention 3 is Russia

U.S.-Russia Relations on the brink

Mark Knoller is an award-winning White House Correspondent for CBS News, 7-6-2009 [U.S.-Russia Relations Improved, but Not Perfect, CBS News, http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-503544_162-5137927-503544.html] VP

TNW removal key to improve Russia relations
Hans Kristensen is director of the Nuclear Information Project at the Federation of American Scientists.March 2006, Federation of American Scientists, "U.S. Nuclear Weapons in Europe: Current Posture and Policy Implications," http://www.allacademic.com//meta/p_mla_apa_research_ citation/1/0/0/9/1/pages100915/p100915-1.php

A strong NATO-Russia Council is key to key to preventing arctic territorial disputes
Alexander Lisken is a Staff Writer for Ria Novosti, 5-27-2009[“U.S. analyst says NATO-Russia Council ideal for Arctic talks”, Russia Newspaper, http://en.rian.ru/world/20090527/155103313.html] VP

Left unchecked, these disputes lead to a US-Russia war
Barry Zellen is a Security Innovator, 8-23-2007[“The Polar Show Down: As the Arctic’s Ice begins to Melt, a new race for its undersea resources begins” Ria Novosti-Russia Newspaper http://securityinnovator.com/index.php?articleID=12387&sectionID=43] VP

US-Russia war is the only extinction threat
Nick Bostrom is a philosophy professor at Oxford 2002 [“Existential Risks Analyzing Human Extinction Scenarios and Related Hazards” http://www.nickbostrom.com/existential/risks.html] VP


Russia will use bioweapons
Christopher Ruddy is an American conservative journalist. 3-15-1999 [Russia and China Prepare for War -->http://archive.newsmax.com/articles/?a=1999/3/14/193145] VP

Bioweapons cause extinction and outweigh nuclear war
Richard Ochs is a Project Management Principal Leader at CSC 6-9-2002 [“Biological weapons must be abolished immediately,” http://www.freefromterror.net/other_articles/abolish.htm] VP


Contention 4 is Proliferation

Scenario 1 is NPT

NPT credibility is on the brink
Grahm Allison is the Director at belfer Center for Science and International Affairs January and February 2010 ["Nuclear Disorder: Surveying Atomic Threats" http://belfercenter.ksg.harvard.edu/publication/19819/nuclear_disorder.html] VP

TNW removal strengthens NPT
John Avery is an Associate Professor of Theoretical Chemistry at the University of Copenhagen 1-22-2010 [The ways is open for a nuclear weapon free northern Europe http://www.active-nonviolency.org/?p=196] VP


NPT collapse leads to proliferation which would go nuclear
Harold Muller is the Director of the Peace Research Institure Franfurt in Germany and a professor of IR at Frankfurt University 2008 [The Future of Nuclear Weapons in an Interdependent World pg 63-75] VP


Scenario 2 is Iran

-sanctions won’t solve
The Daily Telegraph6-27-10 [“Iran could have nuclear weapons by 2012, CIA Chief warns”, http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/middleeast/iran/7857627/Iran-could-have-nuclear-weapons-by-2012-CIA-chief-warns.html] VP

TNWs must be removed from Turkey to restore credibility

Mustafa Kibaroglu is a professor and vice chair of the inter-relations department at Bilikent University 6-7-2010 [Reassessing the Role of U.S. Nuclear Weapons in Turkey”, Arms Control Association,
http://www.armscontrol.org/act/2010_06/Kibaroglu] VP


Turkish credibility key to prevent Iran proliferation
Alon Ben-Meir is a Senior Fellow at the Center for Global Affairs in New York University and teaches courses on the Middle East and international negations, Spring 2009 [“A Nuclear Iran is not an Option: A New Negotiating Strategy” http://www.unc.edu/depts/diplomat/item/2009/0103/comm/benmeir_iran.html] VP

Iranian proliferation causes a Middle East arms race – risking nuclear war
St. Louis Post-Dispatch, 2006 Philip Dine, “Behind the nuclear curtain, Iran lurks While attention is focused on North Korea, Iran could be more menacing”, 10-20, L/N


Contention 5 is Solvency

Unilateral TNW removal jump-starts US-Russian relations
Brian G. Polser Air Force Major, Master's in National Security Affairs from the U.S. Naval Postgraduate School September 2004 [“Theater Nuclear Weapons in Europe: The Contemporary Debate,” http://www.dtic.mil/cgi-bin/GetTRDoc?AD=ADA427697&Location=U2&doc=GetTRDoc.pdf] VP

Russia will have a positive reaction if the U.S. removes TNWs
David Cortright is a peace scholar and Andrea Gabbitas works in the political science field 2003 [Tactical Nuclear Weapons: Emergent Threats in an Evolving Security Environment]

Removing TNWs unilaterally would stop proliferation
Bob van der Zwaan is a senior scientist at the Energy research Centre of the Netherlands January 2009 [Nuclear Weapons in Europe: Time for Disarmament?, http://www.pugwash.org/reports/nw/time_for_disarmament.htm] VP

Now is key the NPT conference is in 3 months
Graham Allison is a Director at Belfer Center for Science and Int’l Affairs, January 2010 [“Nuclear Disorder: Surveying Atomic Threats” http://belfercenter.ksg.harvard.edu/publication/19819/nuclear_disorder.html]

Removing TNWs removes the primary justification for Iranian proliferation restoring Turkish diplomacy
Mustafa Kibaroglu teaches courses on arms control and disarmament in the Department of International Relations at Bilkent University in Ankara, Turkey and was a Joint Research Fellow at the Harvard University Belfer Center's Project on Managing the Atom/Science, Technology, and Public Policy Program and International Security Program, November/December 2007 [A Turkish Nuclear Turnaround, http://www.mustafakibaroglu.com/sitebuildercontent/sitebuilderfiles/Kibaroglu-Bulletin-USnukesTurkey-NovDev2007.pdf]

Withdrawing TNWs from Turkey would play a key role in non-proliferation
Rolf Mützenich is a member of the Parliamentarians for Nuclear Non-proliferation and Disarmament, Patrick Vankrunkelsven is a Politian and Sergei Kolesnikov is a Deputy Chair, Committee for Public Health, Russian Parliament,. Co-President of IPPNW, Member of PNND Council, 2008 [“Time to Remove Tactical Nuclear Weapons from Europe?”]
