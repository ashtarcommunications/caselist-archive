Negative

Security Kritik

The aff frames the world and its people as objects and tools to be policed and used as needed. This labels certain populations as expendable. Instead of making us safer it naturalizes a mode of politics that ensures absolute war and extermination.
Burke, Senior Lecturer in the School of Politics and Int. Relations at the Univ. of New South Wales, Sydney, in ʻ7 [Anthony, Beyond Security, Ethics and Violence, 190-210]


The aff is constructing threats in order to coerce order and control. They are treating human life as an animal existence, a living that just happens to be. The aff’s strategical debate is a world of calculations where ethics are nonexistent.
Burke , Senior Lecturer in the School of Politics and Int. Relations at the Univ. of New South Wales, Sydney, in ʻ7 [Anthony, Beyond Security, Ethics and Violence, 196-7]



Extinction is inevitable due to environmental damage, climate change, and depletion of resources.
Wiener 5 (Jonathan B., Perkins Professor of Law, Environmental Policy, and Public Policy at Duke University, and a University Fellow of Resources for the Future, “Book Review: Catastrophe: Risk and Response; Collapse: How societies choose to fail or succeed”, Journal of Public Analysis and Management, Autumn, Volume 24, Issue 4, pp. 885-9)



And, there is no link turn. Reliance on the forms of knowledge that produce the security dilemma inevitably replicates its
most violent tendencies in one-way or another.
Chow, Prof. of Modern Culture and Media at Brown University, in 06 [Rey, The Age of the World Target, pg. 39-41]



The affirmative will argue that they resolve a particular instance of international insecurity; however, the causes of wars run deeper – particular events and threats flow on a deeper bed-rock of strategic reason and sovereignty. These dual ontologies render conflict unavoidable and necessary since all life becomes usable for geostrategic ends.
Burke , senior lecturer at University of New South Whales, 07 [Anthony, “Ontologies of War: Violence, Existence and Reason”, theory & Event, 10, 2]


The impetus toward security is premised on an ontological framework that commits itself to order and control not only of conflict, but of life itself. This drive to order anything and everything guarantees that the mass graves and nuclear holocausts of the 20th century will be repeated.
Burke, senior lecturer at University of New South Whales, 07 [Anthony, “Ontologies of War: Violence, Existence and Reason”, theory & Event, 10, 2]


Were they to succeed, the measures of security championed by the affirmative would manage to protect life only by suppressing some profoundly valuable part of it. Difference, Uncertainty, and Change are important parts of what makes life beautiful. Security is a hostile effort to eradicate these paradoxes in favor of knowledge and predictability. This results in resentment toward a domesticated life hardly worth living.
Der Derian, Professor of IR and political science at the University of Massachusetts at Amherst, in ʻ93 [James, , “The Value of Security: Hobbes, Marx, Nietzsche and Baudrillard”, The Political Subject of Violence, ed.David Campbell and Michael Dillon, Page 101-105]


Our alternative is to renounce the affirmativeʼs violent and futile search for security in favor of affirming an insecure life. Precisely the defining characteristic of human being is its vulnerability to Otherness. Rather than responding to this with a fear that calcifies into a defensive disposition against difference, we should celebrate our condition in order to seek outan ethical politics of work on the Self. This model of responsibility is the only ontologically appropriate response to the dangers that we inevitably face, whereas the administrative politics of the affirmative damn us to race from one threatening scenario to another, militarizing all ofbeing in a fruitless search for sovereignty and certainty.
Dillon, professor of politics and international relations at University of Lancaster, in ʻ96 [Michael, The Politics of Security: Toward a Political Science of Continental Philosophy, pg. 137-143]


Our alternative refuses security in favor of a work on the Self which celebrates difference and questions international relationsʼ impetus toward sovereignty and sameness. Such a move is capable or radically resituating politics from the ground up and or creating ethical relationships with an insecure world.
Burke, Senior Lecturer in the School of Politics and Int. Relations at the Univ of New South Wales, Sydney, in ʻ7 [Anthony, Beyond Security, Ethics and Violence, 52-3]
