Whitey On the Moon


Walking under the crisp, bright lights of downtown Omaha was something I always cherished as a hip, young rebel who never really left the comfort of my suburban wonderland.

The smell of cheap alcohol eating through the air and my innocence was something that I knew I would treasure and watching a few folks wear the heart on their sleeves as they played blues and folk music for a few bucks was like a picture-perfect Kodak moment that I would never tell myself existed anywhere besides the wonderful world of this corrupted hipster nation of the Old Market, the Dundee area and Omaha itself.

The trendy retro nature of everything, of book stores and coffee shops was just another piece of the puzzle of this unknown that I was strolling through to capture a new perspective of my life and the world surrounding it.

And who wouldn't want this perspective? – I was bored with my luxuries and fed up even more with the responsibilities that came along with them.

This place was my escape, my Narnia, my Amurrica.

If I had encountered something ugly I would just turn my head towards this hidden gem, and I would caress its beauty with my shining eyes and the lights would bounce off of my luscious red hair and every previous thought of boredom or frustration would exist no more, and I could explore the privilege of imagination without limitation.

This place was my escape, Nirvana, without the dead Kurt Cobain – my glowing, mystical moon.

But every moon has a dark side.

Walking under the crisp, bright lights of downtown Omaha was doubled by the aroma of cheap alcohol used as a means to escape one's depressing life – a weapon of intoxication that pushed the drinker closer to the edge with each drink.

The bleeding hearts that played music for those of us anxiously waiting to be impressed and thrilled wanted those couple of bucks for food for that night because they didn't have the means of getting by in any other manner.

And outside of those closed book stores and coffee shops were people sleeping, and they should be grateful that the store owners would grant them even that much.

This other side of the world that I was unknowingly passing through was invisible to me as an outsider because I was too busy taking in the bright, lit side of this moon to bring myself to look towards its unlit side that was always there but never really seemed important to me.


I was privilege.

I was a man watching the news who cared more about the weather than the child who died the night before.

I was George Bush flying over Katrina cheesing at the camera.

I was Manifest Destiny.

I was Resolved: The United States federal government should substantially increase its exploration and/or development of space beyond the Earth's mesosphere.

After walking endlessly through the world of poverty, rat bites and a system that turned its back on him, 1960's bluesologist Gil Scott Heron became convinced that the system he was told he could always rely on was more willing to focus on “more important matters” rather than acknowledge those who live within the cracks of such systems.

And the post cold war space race would only confirm such a profound thought as the system had embodied a privileged ideology that would enable itself to become distanced from lesser attractive things in life in order to focus on the interesting nature of the untouched mystery that existed beyond the Earth's mesosphere.

The resolution is itself a result of such privilege – a call for us to play the same politicians that sapped funds from the working class to build rocket ships, from FEMA to fund the war on terror.

By surrendering our political and personal agency to a brand of politics that historically and necessarily ignores and pushes away those who are oppressed, we embody the identity of the Whitey that condones the presence of the violence that it creates or sustains in order to focus on sexier distractions.

Mumia Abu Jamal explains the harsh realities of being on the other side of this privilege...of being pushed to the bottom of political and social institutions...of being invisible...when he said:
Mumia Abu-Jamal 1998[“A QUIET AND DEADLY VIOLENCE,” 9/19/98, http://www.mumia.nl/TCCDMAJ/quietdv.htm]
It has often ... throughout the world." [Gillitgan, p. 196]




Therefore, this debate is about who best performatively and methodologically challenges structural violence.

The reasons for why society and politics is unable to effectively call into question systems of oppression is because we are never willing to escape the privileged nature of our politics that we have become so comfortable and satisfied with so that we never have to investigate how we contribute to and sustain such forms of oppression.

We watch a sad movie about poverty and that becomes our one good deed for the day so poverty goes to the back of our minds

We go to war with terrorism but in doing so, we bomb civilians and take money from FEMA

We go to space to stay at the top of the world but in doing so we take money from the people who don't really matter to us.

Discontinuing these forms of oppression that remain unseen requires that we escape the privilege ideologies in our political and social interpretations of the world.

We need to stop worrying so much about how we need to sustain the system and understand what happens when the system turns its back on those who become disposable when then the system comes face to face with something that matters more

We must investigate the unlit side of our debate community and the resolution because doing so allows us to better understand the ways our practices replicate privilege.

We must open ourselves and our politics to the voices of those who are overlooked and invisible to us in order to reverse the very logic of privilege that justifies masking over and ignoring structural violence.

Brent R.Henze2000 “Who Says Who Says?: The Epistemological Grounds for Agency in Liberatory Political Projects.” Found in Reclaiming Identity: Realist theory and the predicament of postmodernism. Edited by Paula Moya and Michael Hames-Garcia

Though I argue ... effect of power.

Beneath the Neon

DING! FIRST STOP! LAS VEGAS!
Burke 08, (Adam, Writer for NPR) December 4, 2008 “Sucked into the tunnels underneath Las Vegas” http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=97800190


MUCH LIKE THE NEON LIGHTS OF LAS VEGAS, THE FLICKERING STARS ABOVE HAVE BECOME A TOURIST TRAP, BLINDING US FROM THE OPPRESSIVE REALITY SOCIETY’S MOST DISADVANTAGED MEMBERS ENDURE. MUMIA ABU JAMAL CALLED THIS INTO QUESTION WHEN HE SAID.
Mumia Abu-Jamal 1998 [“A QUIET AND DEADLY VIOLENCE,” 9/19/98, http://www.mumia.nl/TCCDMAJ/quietdv.htm]

IT IS IN THIS NEON HYPNOSIS THAT WE FIND THE AFFIRMATIVE, TOO CAUGHT UP IN REACHING FOR THE SKIES TO CARE ABOUT THE PEOPLE UNDERGROUND. TO ADDRESS THIS OPPRESSION WE MUST FIRST RECOGNIZE THAT THE GOVERNMENT IS HERMETICALLY TRAPPED IN ITS OWN IDEALS, AND CONTINUALLY SACRIFICES THE OTHER IN THE NAME OF ITS GLORIOUS NEON LIGHTS.
Wadiwel in 02 Dinesh Joesph, “Cows and Sovereignty: Biopower and Animal Life” Borderlands E-Journal Vol.1 #2)


POLITICS, OR DEBATE TEAMS, THAT CHOOSE TO IGNORE THE REALITY OF OPPRESSION ONLY SERVE TO CREATE AND RE-CREATE THAT OPPRESSION, AND FAIL TO CREATE ANY REAL CHANGE.
Paulo Friere, Pedagogy of the Oppressed, Chapter 1, educator and director of the Department of Cultural Extension of Recife University,1970


OUR ALTERNATIVE IS TO PUSH DOWN; TO BEGIN POLITICS FROM THE STANDPOINT OF THE OPPRESSED. THESE POLITICS ARE ABLE TO COMBAT OPPRESSION THROUGH A KNOWLEDGE BASED STANDPOINT FROM THOSE WHO HAVE NOTHING ELSE TO GIVE. THIS KNOWLEDGE IS KEY TO BREAK DOWN THE OPPRESSIVE POWER STRUCTURES THAT GOVERN US.
Brent R.Henze2000“Who Says Who Says?: The Epistemological Grounds for Agency in Liberatory Political Projects.” Found in Reclaiming Identity: Realist theory and the predicament of postmodernism. Edited by Paula Moya and Michael Hames-Garcia



F* Cyborgs

the cyborg myth has failed. what began as a potentially liberating schema has fallen on deaf ears. the cyborg myth has not proven intrinsically liberating, infact popular appropriation of the cyborg mythology turns the project on its head. in place of genderless-boundriless entity, the cyborg has become a hyper-sexualized creature that reinforces rather than destroys identity limits. in the face of the loss of the human identity to hybrid technology, the powers that be reinforce other static notions of identity. this turns the case.

Cohen Shabot ’06 (Sara, “Grotesque Bodies: A Response to Disembodied Cyborgs.” Journal of Gender Studies, Vol. 15, No. 3, November. P. 223-235)



furthermore, despite the integration of the technological into the human, the myth of the cyborg does not affirm our humanness. instead, the technological attempts to perfect the body, making the cyborg a being who desires a god-like status and rejects the corporeal body. we will offer the grotesque body as an alternative to this rejction.

Cohen Shabot ’06 (Sara, “Grotesque Bodies: A Response to Disembodied Cyborgs.” Journal of Gender Studies, Vol. 15, No. 3, November. P. 223-235)



and, good intentions of the cyborg project are irrelevant. the flight from the body and reliance on strict binaries dooms the project to failure. the embodied grotesque offers a hybrid notion of the subject that achieves the ends of the cyborg project without the flight away from the body which is at the very core of our deconstruction of the subject.

Cohen Shabot ’06 (Sara, “Grotesque Bodies: A Response to Disembodied Cyborgs.” Journal of Gender Studies, Vol. 15, No. 3, November. P. 223-235)


the excesses inherent in the human body prevent the philosophical from integrating the grotesque into the schema of the same, guaranteeing that our project cannot be coopted. furthermore, the return to the body as the center of the deconstruct of the subject opens up the space necessary for a reimagination of our connection to technology. this means the grotesque will always be a prerequisite to their solvency.

Cohen Shabot ’06 (Sara, “Grotesque Bodies: A Response to Disembodied Cyborgs.” Journal of Gender Studies, Vol. 15, No. 3, November. P. 223-235)



Frontier



THEIR UNDERSTANDING OF OUTER SPACE AS AN ARENA FOR WESTERN EXPANSION AND UNDERSTANDING OF THE WORLD IS THE VERY SOCIO-POLITICAL FRONTIERISM THAT WE FIND SEALED WITHIN CURRENT US SPACE POLICY AND ITS EFFORTS TO RECREATE ITSELF INTO A NEW WORLD ORDER. IN ORDER TO BETTER UNDERSTAND SUCH A EUROCENTRIC DIALECTIC, WE BEGIN A DESTRUCTIVE CRITICISM OF “THE FRONTIER” AND THE VIOLENT ONTOLOGY FROM WHICH IT DERIVES.

Young 87
(M. Jane, Professor of American Studies and Regents Lecturer, both at the University of New Mexico, “Parables of the Space Age-The Ideological Basis of Space Exploration,” Western Folklore, October of 1987,DA:6/25/11, CP)

THE IMPERIAL PROCESS OF EXPLORATION AND DEVELOPMENT, OF PERPETUATING THE VIOLENT WESTERN IDENTITY INTO THE STARRY ABYSS IS NOTHING MORE THAN CULTURAL, SOCIAL, AND POLITICAL WARFARE MADE INVISIBLE THROUGH MYTHS OF PROGRESSION, CIVILIZATION AND MODERNIZATION. THIS MOVE TOWARDS A COMPLETE REIGN OF EXCEPTIONALISM GUARANTEES A FORM OF ERROR-REPLICATION THAT JUSTIFIES THE DEATH OF DIFFERENCE.

Slotkin 92
(Richard Slotkin a cultural critic and historian. He is the Olin Professor of English and American Studies at Wesleyan University in Middletown, CT, and in 2010 was elected a member of the Academy of Arts and Sciences wrote award winning trilogy, Regeneration Through Violence, The Fatal Environment, and Gunfighter Nation , 1992, Gunfighter Nation, The Myth of the Frontier in 20th century America, pg 112-114 E169.12 .S57 1992, DA: 6/24/11, CP)



Thus our alternative is to reject the American frontier myth – Only then can a destabilization of the ideological violence that is Western ontology exist as we open national identity to a state of fluidity necessary for genuine freedom.

Clifford, ‘1
[Michael, Associate Professor of Philosophy at Mississippi State University, Political Genealogy After Foucault: Savage Identities, p. 134-137]



This debate is all about methodology – There is no opportunity for a permutation because the 1AC has already done harm. Its advancement of the frontier myth has constructed a universal narrative of violence that has subjected bodies to nationalism, imperialism and epistemological violence. A debate about methods allows us to determine the most education and productive forum for politics because it not just what you have done but what you have justified. Questioning history is critical to challenge exclusion and imperialism – this evidence is specific to high school students and colonial nation state histories
Trofanenko, ‘5
[Brenda, Professor in the Department of Curriculum and Instruction at the University of Illinois, The Social Studies, Sept/Oct]





Gnosticism


the JUDEO-CHRISTIAN Religion- specifically the book of Genesis- has become the source for understanding humanity’s relation to nature in the Western world. Adam claimed dominion over nature through a process of naming based on the unimpeachable authority of God. Our project of post-sustainability is to find a counter book to this dominant discourse.

Stoekl 2007 (Allan, Professor of French and Comparative Literature at Penn State University. “Bataille’s Peak: Energy, Religion, and Postsustainability.” P. 151-152)

Now one could certainly argue that White’s...... still offer us some hope: St. Francis of Assisi.

Unfortunately, current humanist modes of environmental ethics fail to provide this counter-book. Rather, these ethics have rewritten the book in a way that maintains the trace of absolute divinity. Humanity In effect replaces God. This humanist construction of religion represents the worst of both worlds; the Environment is bent to the will of humanity, while the motives underlying this action remain unassailable.

Stoekl 2007 (Allan, Professor of French and Comparative Literature at Penn State University. “Bataille’s Peak: Energy, Religion, and Postsustainability.” P. 158-160)

However, again we have the problem of bad faith. Religion.... God’s image. The sacred is divine, not human. And so on.


This desire to save the planet reduces all energy/resources to the role of work. This is the logic of the standing reserve. This austere cult of sustainability ignores the accursed share of energy that resists this utter classification. Only a mode of expenditure that puts us in touch with this cursed energy and thus loss can liberate us from the slavery of work. The unintended consequence of such expenditure is the salvation of the planet from ecological destruction.

Stoekl 2007 (Allan, Professor of French and Comparative Literature at Penn State University. “Bataille’s Peak: Energy, Religion, and Postsustainability.” P. 141-143)

If we read Bataille from a Heideggerian perspective, we can therefore propose... the movement of celestial bodies, the agony of God.


Our mode of expenditure predicated on cursed objects/energy disinvests and reinvests the standing reserve- returning it from the world of work- tearing us from our projects and placing us into a community of those who expend on an intimate level. It is time to accept our madness.

Stoekl 2007 (Allan, Professor of French and Comparative Literature at Penn State University. “Bataille’s Peak: Energy, Religion, and Postsustainability.” P. 148-149)

If there is to be a postsustainable world, it will open itself as the....recycling of a few bits of refuse, “I am that madman” (OC, 7: 179; AS, 197, note 22).

Our attachment to cursed energy/matter reveals the nature of our counter-book- one not based on the unimpeachable authority of God, but one rather based in the moment when God realizes his own insufficiency and thus dies a humored atheist.

Stoekl 2007 (Allan, Professor of French and Comparative Literature at Penn State University. “Bataille’s Peak: Energy, Religion, and Postsustainability.” P. 20-23)

The intellect, then- science- excretes and devours, and this process... the death of the king, the leader, the most high).=

In order to communicate this fall of God we come to the public square- a place racked with the same kind of authority present in the status quo's relationship to nature, the very authority we have come here to topple- and in our madness declare out loud "THE DEATH OF GOD."

Bataille 1985 (Georges. Edited and translated by Allan Stoekl. “Visions of Excess.” Written from 1927-1939. P. 213-214)

A “mystery” cannot be posited in the empty region of spirit, where only ..... he will belong to a higher history than all history hitherto.’”
