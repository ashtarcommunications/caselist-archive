PLAN: THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE SHOULD DEMONSTRATE AND DEPLOY SPACE-BASED SOLAR POWER IN TERMS OF SPACE BASED MISSILE DEFENSE.

ADVANTAGE ONE

There are three specific scenarios of rogue proliferation that are on the brink in the status quo – missile defense is key to deter all three:

First, North Korea – current missile defense systems are not enough
Klinger 11 – senior research fellow for Northeast Asia at the Heritage Foundation (Bruce, “North Korea's Imminent Threat”,1/12/11/http://www.heritage.org/research/commentary/2011/01/north-koreas-imminent-threat)


Second, Iran is itching to use cruise missiles – breaking that bubble risks global nuclear instability
Burgess 10 – Lieutenant General and General of the DIA (Ronald L., “IRAN’S MILITARY POWER”,4/14/10, http://www.foreignpolicy.com/files/fp_uploaded_documents/100414_FINAL%20DIA_SFR_to_SASC_Iran_Hearing_20100413.pdf, Date accessed 9/10/11,)


Finally, China – nationalism is creating a multipolar power vaccum in East Asia – this is the biggest internal link to war
Kaplan 11 - is senior fellow at the Center for a New American Security
(Robert, The South China Sea Is the Future of Conflict, Sept/Oct 2011, http://www.foreignpolicy.com/articles/2011/08/15/the_south_china_sea_is_the_future_of_conflict?page=0,3, DateAccessed:9/10/11, )


Ballistic missiles are in this season – they boost power projection capabilities and make distance attacks possible
Missile Defense Agency 11(“THE THREAT”, 4/19/11, http://www.mda.mil/system/threat.html)


And we can’t defend against them now – current cuts to space systems mean we can’t intercept attacks
Holmes, Carafano, and Spring 9 (Kim, PhD - is Vice President for Foreign and Defense Policy Studies and Director of the Kathryn and Shelby Cullom Davis Institute for International Studies, James, PhD - is Assistant Director of the Davis Institute and Senior Research Fellow for National Security and Homeland Security in its Douglas and Sarah Allison Center for Foreign Policy Studies, Baker, is a F. M. Kirby Research Fellow in National Security Policy in the Allison Center at The Heritage Foundation, “What Americans Need to Know About Missile Defense: We're Not There Yet”, 6/30/9 http://www.heritage.org/research/reports/2009/06/what-americans-need-to-know-about-missile-defense-were-not-there-yet, Date accessed:9/10/11, )


Ballistic missile threats from North Korea and Iran risk global nuclear war – terrestrial interceptors are inefficient and take forever to build
Obering 7 - Director of the United States Missile Defense Agency
(Lt. Gen. Henry A., “European Missile Defense: The View From The Pentagon”,October 2007, http://www.armscontrol.org/act/2007_10/Obering.asp%2523bio, Date accessed,9/10/11,)


And, China will definitely take advantage of that weakness
Blumenthal et al 11 - Resident Fellow at the American Enterprise Institute (Dan, “ASIAN ALLIANCES IN THE 21st CENTURY”,Summer 2011, http://project2049.net/documents/Asian_Alliances_21st_Century.pdf,Date Accessed:9/10/11,)


US defense failure in that scenario causes China to bombard Taiwan
Blumenthal et al 11 - Resident Fellow at the American Enterprise Institute (Dan, “ASIAN ALLIANCES IN THE 21st CENTURY”,Summer 2011, http://project2049.net/documents/Asian_Alliances_21st_Century.pdf,Date Accessed:9/10/11,)


SBSP is key to anti-ballistic missile lasers – solves international security, proliferation, and any risk of wars
Dietzler 7 – Former contributor for National Space Society (David, “WHY SPACE?”,2007, http://www.moonminer.com/WHY_SPACE.html,)

And, it powers itself
Ramos 2k – Major USAF (“SOLAR POWER CONSTELLATIONS IMPLICATIONS FOR THE UNITED STATES AIR FORCE”, April 2000, http://www.dtic.mil/cgi-bin/GetTRDoc?AD=ADA394928&Location=U2&doc=GetTRDoc.pdf)


SBSP creates a global bistatic radar system
Johnson 9 – the Head of the High Energy Space Environment Branch in the Naval Research Laboratory (W. Neil, “Space-based Solar Power: Possible Defense Applications and Opportunities for NRL Contributions”,10/23/09, http://www.dtic.mil/cgi-bin/GetTRDoc?Location=U2&doc=GetTRDoc.pdf&AD=ADA513123)



Bistatic radar is key to detecting cruise missiles
Tanks 2K – Contributor for The Institute for Foreign Policy Analysis
(David, “Assessing the Cruise Missile Puzzle:How great a defense challenge?”, http://www.missilethreat.com/repository/doclib/20001000-IFPA-cruisemissilepuzzle.pdf,DateAccessed 9/11/11)



ADVANTAGE TWO


China’s current anti-space agenda destroys US pacific deterrence.
Cheang 11 – Fellow @ Heritage Foundation
(Dean, “China’s Space Program: A Growing Factor in U.S. Security Planning”,8/16/11, http://www.heritage.org/Research/Reports/2011/08/Chinas-Space-Program-A-Growing-Factor-in-US-Security-Planning,)




And without a credible US deterrence in the region it risks collapse of global hegemony, rapid Japan remilitarization, Taiwan conflict and instability in India-Pakistan and Korea
Goh 8 – Lecturer in International Relations in the Department of Politics and International Relations at the Univ of Oxford (Evelyn, International Relations of the Asia-Pacific, “Hierarchy and the role of the United States in the East Asian security order,” 2008 8(3):353-377, Oxford Journals Database)




SSP allows the U.S. to maintain space control – it can harden satellites, make them more survivable and reconstitute lost assets rapidly
Ramos 2K – US Air Force Major, Thesis submitted for the AIR COMMAND AND STAFF COLL MAXWELL Air Force Base (Kim, “Solar Power Constellations: Implications for the United States Air Force,” April, http://handle.dtic.mil/100.2/ADA394928)



And, satellite hardening and reconstitution capabilities are the only thing that can mitigate chinese space inroads.
Kueter 07 - is president of the George C. Marshall Institute, a nonprofit think tank dedicated to science and technology in public policy (Jeff, New Atlantis, “China's Space Ambitions -- And Ours,” Spring, lexis)




ADVANTAGE THREE

NASA is committing us into a nuclear-only deflection system
Chapman 7 [Clark R., Senior Scientist, Southwest Research Institute Department of Space studies, 5/2/07, “Critique of "2006 Near-Earth Object Survey and Deflection Study: Final Report", http://www.b612foundation.org/b612/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/NASA-CritChap.pdf


Nuclear deflection fails – guarantees a bigger asteroid impact
O’Neill, 8
[Ian, PhD Solar Physics @ University of Wales - Space Producer for Discovery News, Nov. 27, 2008, “ Apollo Astronaut Highlights Threat of Asteroid Impact,” http://www.astroengine.com/2008/11/apollo-astronaut-highlights-threat-of-asteroid-strike/]


Asteroid impacts are inevitable - it’s the only scenario for extinction. You have a moral obligation to prevent asteroids – without it it’s just like playing Russian roulette.
Globus, Senior Research Associate at NASA, 3 (Al – Senior Research Associate at SJSU/NASA Ames Research Center, BA in Information Science, Editor-in-Chief of NSS Space Settlement Journal, Recipient of the NASA Software of the Year Award, multiple NASA Group Achievement Awards, NASA Public Service Medal, and the NSS Space Pioneer Award, Orbital Space Colonies, Windstorm Creative Full Spectrum Reference Books, “Why Build Orbital Space Colonies?,” __http://space.alglobus.net/Basics/where.html__, mrs)We do not endorse gendered language


SSP solves asteroids
Hempsella, 2006(Mark, professor at University of Bristol, “Space power as a response to global catastrophes,” Acta Astronautica, Volume 59, Issue 7, October 2006, Pages 524-530, EBSCO host)

SSP inevitable – US development is key to hegemony
Stone 11 [Christopher, space policy analyst and strategist, 10/3/11, “National Space Strategy: Proactive or Reactive?”, http://www.thespacereview.com/article/1938/1, Stransky]


SSP pilot projects can be up and running within one year
Costa 10 - sociobiologist whose unique expertise is to spot and explain emerging trends in relationship to human evolution, global markets, and new technologies. Demonstration projects make the DoD the best actor to incentivize other sectors to commit to SBSP
Rouge et al 7 - Director, National Security Space Office (Joseph, “Space‐Based Solar Power As an Opportunity for Strategic Security”,10/10/07, http://www.nss.org/settlement/ssp/library/final-sbsp-interim-assessment-release-01.pdf,DateAccessed:9/11/11),)
