VP: Morality
Utilitarianism is the only moral theory that can guide governments.
Gary Woller (Professor of Public Management, Brigham Young University). “A Forum on the Role of Environmental Ethics in Restructuring Environmental Policy and Law for the Next Century.” Policy Currents 7.2 (June 1997): p. 10-11.
“Moreover, virtually all public policies… basis for public policy in a democracy.”
VC: Max. HR (prioritize life before other rights)
One: Terrorists
Nations will give terrorists nuclear weapons
Krieger, David. What Happens If...? Terrorists, Revolutionaries, and Nuclear Weapons; Annals of the American Academy of Political and Social Science, Vol. 430, Nuclear Proliferation: Prospects, Problems, and Proposals (Mar., 1977), pp. 44-57; Research political scientist, former Director of the IR Center at San Francisco State University.
“We can imagine… result in international war.”
Theft is relatively easy
Wilson, Valerine. “A world without nuclear weapons; Ex CIA agent Valerine Plame Wilson says we need to make it real”; NY daily news; former CIA agent, specializing in nuclear counter proliferation.
“We know that terrorist groups… ensure that terrorists do not get the bomb.”
Impact: Ira Helfand, MD (Chief of Emergency Medical Section of Cooley Dickinson Hospital in MA), Lachlan Forrow, MD (Associate Professor of Medicine at Harvard University), Jaya Tiwari (Research Director at Center for Global Health and Security), Projected Casualties From a Terrorist Nuclear Explosion in a Large Urban Area, Clinical Medicine and Health Research, January 11, 2002.
“A 12.5 kiloton nuclear explosion… mechanical and thermal injuries.”
Two: Irrational leaders
James Stegenga, Nuclear Deterrence: Bankrupt Ideology, Policy Sciences, Vol. 16, No. 2, pp. 127-145, 1983.
“According to the ideology of nuclear deterrence… generally erodes rationality.”

Warning errors
Bruce Berkowitz, The Journal of Conflict Resolution, March 1985.
“The sheer magnitude… mass firing of weapons.”
Empirical examples of almost-accidents
John Leslie, “The End of the World: The Science and Ethics of Human Extinction,” Routledge, April 1996.
“Nuclear training exercises can be… where it was programmed to explode.”
2 reasons why accidents are likely
Scott Sagan, “The Perils of Proliferation; Organizational Theory, Deterrence Theory, and the Spread of Nuclear Weapons.” International Security, Spring 1994.
“Emergent nuclear powers lack… while sitting on the workbench.”
“The opaque nature of nuclear proliferation… will inhibit safety design efforts.”
Impact for 2/3: Morton Mintz, Two Minutes to Launch, The American Prospect, February 2001.
“The bitter disputes over national… a nuclear war by checklist, by rote.”
