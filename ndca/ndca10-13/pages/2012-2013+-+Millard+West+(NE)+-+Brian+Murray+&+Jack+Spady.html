1AC- NIB- (Made Some Changes--Fremont)

Observation 1 – Lebron Stinson

We begin this round by presenting the story of Lebron Stinson. Stinson lives in Chattanooga, Tennessee and is currently unemployed. He has realized something that many unemployed people across the nation know, the jobs are in one place, and he is in another. Without a car or proper access to public transportation, Stinson can’t get to those jobs. This article was written by Peter Goodman July 11th, 2012.
(Unemployment Problem Includes Public Transportation That Separates Poor From Jobs, July 11th, 2012, http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/07/11/unemployment-problem-public-transportation_n_1660344.html)
CHATTANOOGA, Tenn. -- In the two months since he lost his job driving a delivery truck for a door company…. had transport, I’d be back at work by now. I know this."

Thus the plan:


The United States federal government should substantially increase its transportation infrastructure investment in the United States by creating the National Infrastructure Bank solely for the purpose of public transportation.
