Rhizomes-Gonzaga

Observation 1 is Desire:
Since the Cold War, conceptions of space have been founded on the heternormative discourses of exploration and domination. These conceptions have remained deeply ingrained in society thus 'outer space' have become a societal expression. Griffin 9
(Penny, Senior Lecturer - Convenor, MA International Relations, ‘The Spaces Between Us: The Gendered Politics of Outer Space’, in Bormann, N. and Sheehan, M. (eds), Securing Outer Space. London and New York: Routledge, pp.59-75.)

Heteronomativity can only exist when there is a desire for it to be in place. These types of desire takes place on the individual level and is called Fascism. This fascist tendency is a self-denying desire that seeks to whether through incremental repression or mass slaughter and butchery as it enters the authoritarian sphere.
Seem ‘83
[Mark, Intro to AO xvii]

We criticize institutions that drive desire, those that seek to expand centralized power at the expense of free spaces. Our affirmation is of a larger struggle against State Fascism. We are against divisions and affirm a politics of continuation – constantly seeking new political outlets and investigating their potential. Our affirmation cannot be tied down or controlled – we move beyond the binary of beginnings and ends and explore the vast middle ground of infinite possibilities
Conley in 2006(Verena Andermatt, professor of literature at Harvard, “Borderlines; Deleuze and the Contemporary World, 95-100)

The SQUO has turned the State into a War Machine. Everything is a battlefield: war is being waged on culture, politics and the individuals being oppressed. War is an immanent thing, everywhere and nowhere at the same time, a war on all society. Fear has turned us all into cannibals, desiring our own domination and servitude. This is Daniel Bell in 2007Daniel M., Associate Professor of Theological Ethics, Lutheran Theological Southern Seminary, Journal for Cultural and Religious Theory, JCRT 8.2 SPRING 2007 55,d http://www.jcrt.org/archives/08.2/)
We take a behind the scenes look at what space is. We explore space by investigating not only the discourse that is responsible for our current conception of space, but how that conception is formed. We chose to develop a new way of relating to Space, heteronormativity, and the Fascism responsible for it all. An aff ballot supports for a new mode of resistance that removes its self from the cycle of fear in favor of one based off a rhizomatic model.

Our movement does not require grandiose movements or charismatic leaders, it requires only a single strike from the norm. The potential for change already exists, all that is needed is a line of flight – one action – in order to awaken this power.
Ian Pindar and Paul Sutton, 2000 (Translators Introduction to The Three Ecologies. Pindar reviews regularly for the Guardian, the Times Literary Supplement and the London Review of Books: Sutton is an Ecosophist and has a PhD in ecology) pp. 9-11


Our affirmative is supposed to learn from and play with the content and ethos of their criticism, to combine the function and form of their political strategy with our own, creating an entirely new machine.
Brian Massumi, 1983(Political philosopher and social theorist, with a PhD in French literature from Yale; A Thousand Plateaus, Introduction)

Observation 2 is Debate:
The debate space is key – imperial control has manifested itself into the sphere of communication – cutting off politics before it can be developed. Interjecting the affirmative politics of rhizomatic movements into the ether debate is necessary to challenge state sovereignty and reclaim agency
Hardt and Negri in 2k (Terrorists, awesome dudes, profs at places of respectable respectedness; Empire)

We should use the debate space as a beginning to struggle against fascism. This struggle is creative; it’s unique and outside the box. Our affirmative breaths joy, passion and experimentation back into the staleness of the debate space.
Foucault 72 [Anti-Oedipus pg. xiii-iv]


Debate is only a collection of shared understandings and explicit or implicit rules for interaction. Wins and losses are our currency, and we should use this place, space, and time – the alternative is forfeiting the only power we have. We may not be able to immediately enact federal policy, but we can change the community that we participate in. Rebecca Bjork, former college debater, coach, and professor in ‘93 (former college debater and former associate professor at the University of Utah, where she taught graduate and undergraduate courses in Communication and Women in Debate, Reflections on the Ongoing Struggle, Debater's Research Guide 1992-1993: Wake Forest University.Symposium, web.archive.org/web/20011012220529/members.aol.com/womynindebate/article3.htm)




Negative

Death

A - - Death is a rendezvous, not an objective destiny. Meeting death is a chance encounter that cannot be controlled, because accidents, death, and other events. The more we try to escape fate, the more we seem to run to it.
Baudrillard explains in 2001 (Seduction, 2001, p. 72-74)


B. Death is a fantasy – our biology means we cannot truly “die” or cease to be, rather we enter a new form of becoming. Even if we are all wiped from the face of the earth in a hailstorm of nuclear bombs the process of life goes on even at the micro level. There is no unique warrant for why the biological construct of human deserves a place higher than the construct of atoms. This desire to be secure from death is the most personal level of microfascism that negates our ability to live life to its fullest and breeds ressentiment.

Deleuze and Guattari 72 [AO, 330-39]


B - - Our interpretation is that 1.the aff should have to prove that death is something that can be avoided, unlike life and 2. The aff has to prove there is a warrant for death being an impact and a reason why human life is better than micro life. If they can prove this, the aff can weigh death as an impat, but if not you vote neg on presumption



AND,



“Should” means that there is a practical reason for the United States federal government to do something.

WordNet in ‘97Princeton University, 1.6

C - - because they fail to distinguish life from death, the affirmative doesn’t provide a justification for plan passage, and you vote negative on presumption.
