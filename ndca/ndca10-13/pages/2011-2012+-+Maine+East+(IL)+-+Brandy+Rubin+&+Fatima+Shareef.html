The status quo cannot overcome the attitudinal, structural, or existing barriers to US-Chinese cooperation in space.

Martina, Reuters, in ‘11
[Michael, “China astronaut calls for U.S. cooperation”, Reuters, April 29]

current attitudes for china-u.s cooperation are based on flawed, lingering cold war ideology—makes conflict inevitable creates the perception of space race

Johnson-Freesea, Naval War College, Newport, USA, 06
[Joan, “A New US-Sino Space Relationship: Moving Toward Cooperation”, Astropolitics, Vol 4 No 1, August]

Sadeha, Astroconsulting International, USA, 10

Grondin 07 David Grondin Lecturer School of Political Studies Faculty of Social Sciences University of Ottawa The US Religion of Technology in the Weaponization of Outer Space A Case for Technological Atheism and Resisting Space WarPaper to be presented at ISA Convention 2007

Kellner 2k8 - Graduate School of Education and Information Studies, George F. Kneller Chair in the Philosophy of Education, UCLA(Douglas. "Preface The Ideology of HIgh-Tech/Postmodern War vs. the Reality of Messy Wars." http://gseis.ucla.edu/faculty/kellner/essays/2008_Kellner_MessyWarPreface_ver29052008FINAL.pdf)


Space militarization is not inevitable
1. it’s a social construct
2. foreign weaponization is contingent upon U.S. action
Grondin, 6 – Assistant Professor, School of Political Studies, University of Ottawa (David, The (Power) Politics of Space: The U.S. Astropolitical Discourse of Global Dominance in the War on Terror)


[ ] Space militarization is only “inevitable” because we keep militarizing space. Stepping back from it would allow arms control to lift the security dilemma and avoid an arms race

Zhang 2011 - Director of the Center for Asia Pacific Studies at Lingnan University [Baohui The Security Dilemma in the U.S.-China Military Space Relationship The Prospects for Arms Control ASIAN SURVEY 51:2 JSTOR Accessed July 12]



Despite political opposition—provision doesn’t end bilateral scientific cooperation

Foust 2011(Jeff Foust, is the editor and publisher of The Space Review. He also operates theSpacetoday.net web site and the Space Politics and Personal Spaceflight) (“What’s the future of US-China cooperation in space?”) (May 5th) (http://www.spacepolitics.com/2011/05/05/whats-the-future-of-us-china-cooperation-in-space/) (RICHARD M)



The Chinese ASAT test was a response to the Bush administration’s argument that there is no need for space cooperation because there is no issue in space. China has made multiple efforts to cooperate with the US on space issues and has been repeatedly shut down. Now is the key time to re-orient our thinking about the Chinese space threat, before military buildup becomes a self-fulfilling threat.

Hitchens, 07 (Jan 22, Director of the Center for Defense Information, Analysis: Chinese Anti-Satellite Weapons Test in Space is Provocative and Irresponsible, http://www.cdi.org/program/document.cfm?DocumentID=3800&from_page=../index.cfm).


China’s pursuits in space have always been scientific and peaceful – even though it has created space weapons it has also repeatedly pushed to restrict military application of the tech
Kulacki, Gregory ’10 “Potential for Cooperation with China” December 5th, 2010 - Carnegie Endowment for International Peace http://www.ucsusa.org/assets/documents/nwgs/cooperation-with-china-on-space-gkulacki.pdf


Currently China wants to cooperate with the US over space—it’s just a matter of implementation

De Selding 2011(Peter de Selding, , 4/14/11, “Chinese Government Official Urges U.S.-Chinese Space Cooperation” http://www.spacenews.com/civil/110414-chinese-official-space-cooperation.html ACC 7/31/11)
