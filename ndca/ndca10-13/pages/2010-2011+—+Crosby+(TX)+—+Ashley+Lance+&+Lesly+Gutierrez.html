Affirmative

Plan: The United States federal government should end its military cooperation in the Standard Missle-3 Cooperative Development Project.


Contention one – inherency

The Standard Missile-3 Project is the latest phase of US-Japan joint missile defense development – it’s objective is to develop and deploy the block 2 interceptor
pike, director @ globalsecurity.org/former director of Space Policy, Cyberstrategy, Military Analysis, Nuclear Resource and Intelligence Resource projects @ Federation of American Scientists for over 20 years, 10 (jim, RIM-161 SM-3 Japan Maritime Self Defense Force Deployment, 7/11, http://www.globalsecurity.org/space/systems/sm3-japan.htm



Contention two – topicality

MILITARY PRESENCE IS DEFINED AS US MILITARY ASSETS ENGAGED IN ROUTINE, NON-COMBAT ACTIVITIES – SPECIFICALLY INCLUDES INTEROPERABILITY EFFORTS, MILITARY TRAINING OF FOREIGN PERSONNEL, AND CO-PRODUCTION OF MILITARY EQUIPMENT
Thomason, senior analyst in strategy, forces and resources division @ institute for defense analysEs/phd in ir @ northwestern, 02 (james s., Transforming US Overseas Military Presence: Evidence and Options for DoD Volume I: Main Report, ida paper p-3707, http://www.comw.org/qdr/fulltext/0207thomason.pdf)

the sm-3 project is a substantial MILITARY presence operation – it includes interoperability, MILITARY training, and co-PRODUCTION OF the block 2 interceptor
Department of defense, 10 (Ballistic missile defense review, feb,
http://www.defense.gov/bmdr/docs/BMDR%20as%20of%2026JAN10%200630_for%20web.pdf

the sm-3 project maintains a comprehensive military presence in japan
koplow, jd candidate @ Georgetown law, 05 (Justin l., NOTE: Assessing The Creation Of A Duty Under International Customary Law Whereby The United States of America Would Be Obligated To Defend A Foreign State Against The Catastrophic But Localized Damage Of An Asteroid Impact, Georgetown International Environmental Law Review, winter)

THE SM-3 COOPERATIVE DEVELOPMENT PROJECT IS RECEIVING SUBSTANTIAL FUNDING IN THE 2011 BUDGET
PR Newswire, 2/4/10 (Missile Defense Makes Significant Financial Gains Under Obama)


CONTENTION THREE – HARMS

BLOCK 2 INTERCEPTORS ARE THE LYNCHPIN TO ALL OUR SCENARIOS – THEIR BURNOUT VELOCITY IS GREATER THAN THE BLOCK 1 PROVIDING ICBM INTERCEPT CAPABILITY TRIGGERING US PRESSURE FOR COOPERATIVE DEFENSE

THE BLOCK 2 IS SCHEDULED FOR DEPLOYMENT IN LATE 2011 – MEANS NOW IS THE KEY TIME TO STOP PARTICIPATION
TOKI, Research Associate @ THE JAMES MARTIN CENTER FOR NONPROLIFERATION STUDIES/FORMER Peace Fellow @ the Union of Concerned Scientists where she researchED National Missile Defense, 09 (MASAKO, MISSILE DEFENSE IN JAPAN, JAN 16, http://www.thebulletin.org/print/web-edition/features/missile-defense-japan)

ONLY THE BLOCK 2 TRIGGERS OUR IMPACTS – IT HAS ICBM INTERCEPT RANGE, BLOCK 1 DOESN’T
HICKS, PROGRAM DIRECTOR @ AEGIS MISSILE DEFENSE/REAR ADMIRAL @ US NAVY/ Deputy Director for Combat Systems and Weapons in the Surface Warfare Directorate @ Office of the Chief of Naval Operations/requirements and programmatic action officer in support of the Joint Requirements Oversight Council, 07 (ALAN B., AEGIS BALLISTIC MISSILE DEFENSE SYSTEM – STATUS AND UPGRADES, WASHINGTON ROUNDTABLE ON SCIENCE & PUBLIC POLICY, NOV 28, http://www.marshall.org/pdf/materials/573.pdf)

scenario ONE – china

BLOCK 2 DEPLOYMENT THREATENS CHINA’S NUCLEAR DETERRENT – ONLY BANNING THE SM-3 PROJECT IN FAVOR OF A LIMITED THEATER MISSILE DEFENSE MODEL CAN ENSURE STRATEGIC STABILITY
Johnson-Freese and Nichols, 10 (Joan – PROF and Chair of the National Security Decision Making Department @ US Naval War College AND Thomas – PROF of National Security Affairs @ US Naval War College/fellow in the International Security Program and the Project on Managing the Atom at the John F. Kennedy School @ Harvard UNIV, Space, Stability and Nuclear Strategy: Rethinking Missile Defense, http://www.chinasecurity.us/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=470

BLOCK 2 underminING OF CHINA’S DETERRENT sparks arms racing and makes pre-emptive nuclear warfighting inevitable
Dae, contributor @ solidarity for peace and reunification of korea, 10 (ko young, U.S. Military Strategy on the Korean Peninsula and Missile Defense in Northeast Asia, may 9,
http://www.spark946.org/bugsboard/index.php?BBS=eng_1&action=viewForm&uid=67&page=1

Even if block 2 fails – China still fears US coercive ability
Chase, prof of Strategy and Policy @ us Naval War College, 10 (Michael, The U.S.-China Strategic Security Relationship and the Nuclear Posture Review Report, 4/29, http://www.trumanproject.org/posts/2010/05/us-china-strategic-security-relationship-and-nuclear-posture-review-report

ICBM intercept is the lynchpin – block 2 triggers Chinese nuclear modernization and development of multiple independently targetable re-entry vehicles – uniquely increases the destructive toll of our nuclear war scenario
Hughes, Principal Research Fellow and Acting Co-Director at the Centre for the Study of Globalisation and Regionalisation @ UNIV of Warwick, 06 (Christopher w., Ballistic Missile Defence and US-Japan and US-UK Alliances Compared, garnet working paper, December, http://www.garnet-eu.org/fileadmin/documents/working_papers/1106.pdf

China war leads to extinction
ching, career journalist of 25 years covering taiwan-china relations, 2k (cheong, No one gains in war over Taiwan, will taiwan break away: the rise of taiwanese nationalism, originally published on 6/25/2k, p. 5-7

Block 2 allowS increased potential anti-satellite functionality – even if we don’t use it, OTHER NATIONS will be forced to develop offensive asat capabilities
Weeden, technical advisor @ Secure World Foundation/former US Air Force officer with a background in space surveillance and ICBM operations, 07 (Brian, The space security implications of missile defense, the Space Review, 9/28, http://www.thespacereview.com/article/1474/1
