The sexiest category... or subtle forms.

As depicted in John Carpenters horror movie “The Thing” Confrontation with the infinitely changing extraterrestrial invokes an extreme horror upon the self which can only be overcome by becoming the very thing which we fear.

Continued fear of the alien will only serve to reinforce the structures of heirarchal subordination which is the basis for all suffering, however, becoming the alien and accepting that it, and ourselves, are part of a larger chaotic world allows us to access the semiotic chora, or “good abjection” which propels all productive cultural activity and informs all valuable life experiences.
White 93 (Eric White is an associate professor of English at university of Colorado at Boulder, with A PhD in rhetoric at University of Cal Berkely,The Erotics of Becoming: Xenogenesis and "The Thing", Source: Science Fiction Studies, Vol. 20, No. 3 (Nov., 1993), pp. 394-408 Published by: SF-TH IncStable URL: http://www.jstor.org/stable/4240279 .Accessed: 07/08/2011 22:23

After I had... as "becoming other."

My partner Eric and I affirm that we should become extra-terrestrial.

This becoming is not a method by which to imitate aliens or to swap identities for a new ones, rather what we advocate is an open state of being which allows us to tear down molar identities, these are the fixed structures which are at the root of all exclusionary logic.

In opposition to the fixed molar identities, the becoming which we advocate creates molecular identities. Since these molecular identities follow no set path, they open up space to a universe of voices which can oppose hierarchal structures such as the human-animal binary
Vint 05 (Sherryl, Assistant Professor of English at Brock University, Science Fiction Studies, Vol. 32, No. 2, Becoming Other: Animals, Kinship and Butler’s “Clay’s Ark”, July 2005)

Thus the reality of becoming doesn’t merely tear down a single molar structure, by opening us up to a universe of molecular identities, a becoming creates a line of flight.

In opposition to the fixed structures created by molar identities, a line of flight has no set course or path, and thus enables us to explore an infinite number of ways to be.
Deleuze and Guattari 80 (French Sorcerers, A Thousand Plateaus: Capitalism and schizophrenia, Becoming-Intense, Becoming-Animal, Becoming-Imperceptible ..., Pg. 241-243)

ALT TEXT: Our alternative is to engage in a politics of rhizomatics.

To us the focus of this debate round has been desire. Rooting from the smallest level within all things desires forms the world around us, the way we think, the way we feel, and the way we act.

Through the different planes in this speech we’ve encountered a number of ways in which desire can either be freed or controlled. There is a distinction between types of planes- The smooth ones in which desire moves freely in all directions and the striated ones, ones in which desire is controlled and organized in a certain way by institutions who wish to control it.

In order to ensure desire can operate freely we would like to engage you in a politics of rhizomatics. Always moving in opposition to striated space, the rhizome smooths out space, freeing desire from the fascist control imposed upon it.
