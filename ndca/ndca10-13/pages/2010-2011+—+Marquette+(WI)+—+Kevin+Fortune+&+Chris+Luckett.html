Contention Two: The Environment


First— Okinawa’s Biodiversity is as rich as Warren Buffet— Unique characteristics in its history makes Okinawa the Galapagos of the East
Izawa Masako, Associate professor in the Faculty of Science at the University of Ryukyus (Japan), 2008
[“Okinawa the Galapagos of the East”, January 26th, 2008, available online at http://www.virtualginza.com/okinawa.htm?galap.htm, accessed September 4th, 2010Thur]

OKINAWA-ken (prefecture) has a total land area of 2,265 km2 and is made up of around 160 different islands.
… We are entrusted with the mission of protecting this invaluable legacy for future generations

Second— U.S. will move to Nago— Only question now is what type of base will be built
Japan Real Time, section of the Wall Street Journal focusing on Japan and their ties to the U.S., 2010
[“A Small Step Forward for Okinawa Base”, Yuka Hayashi is a staff writer for the Wall Street Journal, Wall Street Journal, August 31st, 2010, available online at http://blogs.wsj.com/japanrealtime/2010/08/31/a-small-step-forward-for-okinawa-base/, accessed September 4, 2010 Thur ]

A few months ago, the U.S.-Japan alliance was rocked by the prospect that the new Tokyo government might try to kick the Marines off Okinawa.
… This dashed the hope that the Futenma saga will be sealed before President Barack Obama’s visit to Japan in early November.

Third— The plan to move to Nago will destroy environment— deforests 30 hectarces, kills Dugong, poisons water, and reclaims land from sea
Asia-Pacific Journal, Peer-Reviewed, open-source journal on the forces shaping the Asia-Pacific region, 2010
[“Inside the Sit-ins Against U.S. Military Expansion in Okinawa”, Written by Kikuno Yumiko Editor of “U-Yu-Yu,” a community journal, originally published in the Asia-Pacific Journal, Published on History News Network, February 22nd, 2010, accessed August 25, 2010, available online at http://hnn.us/articles/124089.htmlThur]

Henoko suffering more from new Japan-U.S. agreement

… Mr. Toyama said, “We have been told that the new U.S. base is necessary for our security, but this plan will threaten that very security because it will ruin even our drinking water.”

Fourth— Plan is key to recognize the importance of biodiversity— ignoring long-term for short term leads to environmental crisis
International Union for Conservation of Nature, world’s first global environmental organization over 1,000 governmental and NGO members and 11,000 volunteer scientists from 160 countries, Funded by governments, bilateral and multilateral agencies, foundations, member organizations and corporations, 2010
[“Why We Need Biodiversity”, May 3rd, 2010, available online at http://www.iucn.org/iyb/about/biodiversity/, accessed August 10, 2010Thur]

The benefits that we gain from biodiversity go far beyond the mere provision of raw materials.
… This can result in the degradation of many services, such as fisheries, water supply, and protection against natural hazards, seriously affecting people’s well-being.

Fifth— Bases destroy Okinawa’s enviornment in a laundry list of direct and indirect ways
Jonathan S. Taylor, Associate Professor and Graduate Advisor at the Center for Remote Sensing of the Environment California State University Fullerton, 2007
[“Anti-Military and Environmental Groups in Okinawa, Japan”, Jonathan Taylor, Published in P.P. Karan (ed), Grassroots Environmental Movements in Japan and the United States, forthcoming, University of Kentucky Press, available online at www.uky.edu/.../Anti-Military%20and%20Environmental%20Movements%20in%20Okinawa.pdf, accessed September 4, 2010Thur]


U.S. Bases It goes without saying that military bases produced environmental degradation of various kinds
…. Thus the main catalyst for environmental grassroots movements in Japan has largely been missing from Okinawa.
Finally— tolerating the destruction of this ecosystem saps us of our humanity—it causes endless violence
[Murray Bookchin, co-founder of the Institute of Social Ecology, 1987 ("An Appeal For Social and Psychological Sanity," The Modern Crisis, Published by Black Rose Books Ltd., ISBN 0920057624, p. 106-108 SDI]

Industrially and technologically, we are moving at an ever-accelerating pace toward a yawning chasm with our eyes completely blindfolded.
…. A humanity disempowered of its capacity to change a misbegotten "civilization," ultimately divested of its power to resist, reflects a natural world disempowered of its capacity to reproduce a green and living world.

Contention Three: Sexual Violence
First— Military bases abroad foster the machismo and militarism that lead to sexual violence justified by military authorities— Women are literally shipped in for soldiers’ pleasure
Transnational Institute, a global network of scholar activists focusing on social justice issues, international reputation for remaining non-sectarian, 2007
[“Sex Crimes and Prostitution”, Transnational institute, March 1st 2007, available online at http://www.tni.org/archives/act/16374, accessed August 22nd, 2010Thur]

The heady mix of machismo and militarism that pervades US army bases generally means trouble for relations with local women.
…In its continued condoning of the use of large-scale prostitution and its refusal to take responsibility for the safety of women around its bases, the US military's attitudes continue to facilitate the use of women as objects in this way.

Second— This structural, sexual violence is particualrily true in the case of Okinawa— concentration in Japan, past conflict, and history— Withdrawal key to solve
Transnational Institute, a global network of scholar activists focusing on social justice issues, international reputation for remaining non-sectarian, citing Suzuyo Takasato of the Okinawa Women Against Military Violence, 2007
[“Sex Crimes and Prostitution”, Transnational institute, March 1st 2007, available online at http://www.tni.org/archives/act/16374, accessed August 22nd, 2010Thur]

Okinawa Seventy-five per cent of the US bases in Japan are concentrated on Okinawa, a tiny island occupying just 0.6 per cent of the country’s land area.
… It was the victory of the power of hope: believing in life, peace and co-existence.

Finally—this sexual violence is equivalent to gendered warfare—it causes incalculable suffering and affects entire communities.
Chowdhury, works in a domestic abuse help office 2
[Rumna, “Kadic v Karadzic – rape as a crime against women as a class” 20 Laq & Ineq J 91]

Feminist scholars have long recognized sexual violence as a tool used by men to manipulate and control women through fear.
… Having considered the "public" effects of "private" sexual violence, it is clear that rape is a problem that deserves much more attention than it has traditionally received.

Contention Four: A Reality Check
The negative will attempt to convince you that the plan is undesirable by appealing to far-fetched scenarios of global destruction – we ask that you remain skeptical of their convoluted internal link chains.

FIRST—“there’s always a risk” is not a reason to vote negative – low probability internal link chains should not be used as an excuse to reject the plan.
Dale A. Herbeck, Professor of Communication and Director of the Fulton Debating Society at Boston College, and John P. Katsulas, Debate Coach at Boston College, 1992
[“The Use and Abuse of Risk Analysis in Policy Debate,” Paper Presented at the 78th Annual Meeting of the Speech Communication Association (Chicago, IL), October 29th-November 1st, Available Online via ERIC Number ED354559, p. 10-12 BATMAN]

First, and foremost, we need to realize that some risks are so trivial that they are simply not meaningful.

… Just as we ignore these risks in our own lives, we should be willing to ignore minimal risks in debates.

Second—rejecting low probability internal link chains is key to rational policymaking—accumulated experience proves that appeals to the possibility of catastrophic causal chains should not influence our decision-making.
Sven Ove Hansson, Professor in the Department of Philosophy and the History of Technology at the Royal Institute of Technology (Stockholm, Sweden), 2005
[“The Epistemology of Technological Risk,” Techné: Research in Philosophy and Technology, Volume 9, Number 2, Winter, Available Online at http://scholar.lib.vt.edu/ejournals/SPT/v9n2/hansson.html, Accessed 10-08-2008 BATMAN]

However, it would not be feasible to take such possibilities into account in all decisions that we make.
…. The same applies to technological, economic, social, and political systems, although our knowledge about their resilience towards various disturbances has not been sufficiently systematized.

Third—disregard the negative’s pleas for you to “judge normally” and reject their absurd internal link chains – improving risk calculation is key to meaningful education in debate.
Dale A. Herbeck, Professor of Communication and Director of the Fulton Debating Society at Boston College, and John P. Katsulas, Debate Coach at Boston College, 1992
[“The Use and Abuse of Risk Analysis in Policy Debate,” Paper Presented at the 78th Annual Meeting of the Speech Communication Association (Chicago, IL), October 29th-November 1st, Available Online via ERIC Number ED354559, p. 14 BATMAN]

It is sometimes argued that debate is a laboratory for testing argumentation
…If we understand this tool, we will be well-armed in our battle with the bogeymen of our age.21

And— Their scenarios of conflicts rising to thermonuclear war aniliating the planet are absurd. In the past decade all kinds of major war have shown a major decline
Mueller, John. Professor of political sciences and dance at Ohio State University, Woody Hayes Chair of National Security Studies at the Mershon Center for International Security Studies 09
[ “War has almost ceased to exist: An assesment” Political Science Quarterly Volume 124 Number 2]

No matter how defined, then, there has been a most notable decline in the frequency of wars over the last years.
…Indeed, if civil war becomes (or remains) as uncommon as the international variety, war could be on the verge of ceasing to exist as a substantial phenomenon

And— Assigning inaccurate weight or risk to war collapses international relations – leading to increased escalation
Fettweiss., Dr. Christopher J., Professor of National Security Decision Making at the Naval War College, 3
[ “Revisiting Mackinder and Angell: The Obsolence Of Great Power Geopolitics”, Comparative Strategy, Volume 22, April]

All areas of human endeavor—religion and philosophy, economics and social mores— adapt and evolve, except, we are told, international politics.
…. It is hard to see how good policy can arise from a foundation of flawed theories and assumptions.

Finally— No extinction—fallout, ozone, fires, and nuclear winter are all wrong
MARTIN 1982 (Dr Brian Martin is a physicist whose research interests include stratospheric modelling. He is a research associate in the Dept. of Mathematics, Faculty of Science, Australian National University, Journal of Peace Research, No 4, http://www.uow.edu.au/arts/sts/bmartin/pubs/82jpr.html)

(a) Global fallout. The main effect of long-term fallout would be to increase the rate of cancer and genetic defects by a small percentage.
… The rate of impact of such climatic change is likely to be sufficiently slow - decades, or years in some cases - for the avoidance of the death of a substantial portion of the world's population through climatic change.

Violence K


SUBPOINT A: GUNS DON’T KILL PEOPLE, PEOPLE KILL PEOPLE
THE ASSUMPTION THAT CIRCUMSTANCES CAUSE VIOLENCE IS MISGUIDED – EVERY VIOLENT ACT REQUIRES AN INDIVIDUAL TO CHOOSE TO COMMIT VIOLENCE.
Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 1-2]
Violence is a ubiquitous reality in our society … The circumstances identified may differ according to the politics of the explainers, but the method of explanation remains the same.

SUBPOINT B: THE DISPLACEMENT OF RESPONSIBILITY
THE AFFIRMATIVE’S APPROACH IS A FORM OF COLLECTIVE VICTIM-BLAMING THAT STIGMATIZES ENTIRE GROUPS IN ORDER TO EXONERATE INDIVIDUAL PERPETRATORS – THEIR FLAWED METHODOLOGY DISPLACES RESPONSIBILITY AND TURNS THE CASE – THEY HAVE MISIDENTIFIED THE CAUSE OF THEIR HARMS.
Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 2-3]
While consideration of mitigating circumstances has its rightful place in a court of law trying (and defending) an offender, this does not automatically make it an adequate or sufficient practice for political analysis … It is a version of collective victim-blaming, of stigmatizing entire social strata as potential hotbeds of violence, which rests on and perpetuates the mainstream division of society into so-called marginal groups — the classic clienteles of social work and care politics (and of police repression) — and an implied ‘centre’ to which all the speakers, explainers, researchers and carers themselves belong, and which we are to assume to be a zone of non-violence.

SUBPOINT C: THE MOMENT OF DECISION
THE ALTERNATIVE IS TO ADOPT A DIFFERENT METHODOLOGICAL APPROACH TO VIOLENCE. INSTEAD OF VIEWING VIOLENCE AS A PRODUCT OF CIRCUMSTANCES, WE SHOULD INTERROGATE THE MOMENT OF DECISION WHEN INDIVIDUALS CHOOSE TO COMMIT VIOLENCE. THIS METHODOLOGICAL APPROACH ENABLES EFFECTIVE RESISTANCE TO THE VIOLENT IDEOLOGY OF THE AFFIRMATIVE AND IS A NECESSARY PRECONDITION TO REMEDYING THE HARMS THEY HAVE OUTLINED.
Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 5-7]
A politics aiming at a change in people’s behaviour would require political work that is very much more cumbersome and very much less promising of success than is the use of state power and social control … Ideology, however, is not just made by others; we are all of us subjects of ideology — as the producers of our own thinking and as the recipients of other people’s discourse — unless we resist such ideological structures of thought and discourse in a continual critique of ideology itself.

SUBPOINT D: START WITH THE “I” WORLD
THE AFFIRMATIVE’S INFATUATION WITH INSTITUTIONAL CHANGE AT THE LEVEL OF GOVERNMENT OBSCURES OUR INDIVIDUAL RESPONSIBILITY FOR VIOLENCE – THIS ORGANIZED IRRESPONSIBILITY CONSTITUTES A WILLED REFUSAL TO MEANINGFULLY RESIST VIOLENCE. WE SHOULD BEGIN NOT WITH AN INVESTIGATION OF WHAT OTHERS SHOULD DO BUT WITH AN INVESTIGATION OF WHAT WE SHOULD DO.
Susanne KAPPELER, Lecturer in English at the University of East Anglia (UK) and freelance writer, 1995 ["Violence and the will to violence," The Will To Violence: The Politics of Personal Behaviour, Published by Polity Press (Cambridge, UK), ISBN 0745613047, p. 10-11]
‘We are the war’ does not mean that the responsibility for a war is shared collectively and diffusely by an entire society — which would be equivalent to exonerating warlords and politicians and profiteers or, as Ulrich Beck says, upholding the notion of ‘collective irresponsibility’, where people are no longer held responsible for their actions, and where the conception of universal responsibility becomes the equivalent of a universal acquittal.6 … We share in the responsibility for this war and its violence in the way we let them grow inside us, that is, in the way we shape ‘our feelings, our relationships, our values’ according to the structures and the values of war and violence.
