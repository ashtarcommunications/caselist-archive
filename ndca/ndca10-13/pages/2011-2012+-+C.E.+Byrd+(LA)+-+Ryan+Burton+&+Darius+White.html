Heidegger

Thiele ’95 (Leslie, Professor of Political Science at the University of Florida, Timely Meditations: Martin Heidegger and Postmodern Politics, pg 201-203)



The desire to experience the cosmos is nothing more than a symptom of technicity. Attempts to measure and control the universe constantly encounter the limits of modernity’s attempt to grasp being. Technological thinking becomes our only frame for thinking and humanity is reduced to nothing more than standing reserve.
Soccio ’09 (Douglas, Writes textbooks for philosophy at Wadsworths Cengage Learning and was professor of philosophy at Shasta College, Archetypes of Wisdom: An Introduction to Philosophy, pg 514-516)



The loss of being leads to ontological damnation, an existential meaninglessness that is more destructive than any other impact.
Zimmerman 93 (Michael E., Professor of Philosophy at University of Tulane, Contesting Earth's Future: Radical Ecology and Postmodernity, Pg. 119-120. 1993)


Thus the alternative is to rethink and reject the ontology of the 1AC

Speaking Evil


We disagree with the aff on the status of good and evil – they say good and evil are moral or polemic terms, descriptive of two qualitatively different acts. We say that such claim are naïve. Evil is a real part of our existence but not the will to exterminate. That desire is of the objective force of good, the will to sameness, the view of the revisionist, and the condemning and whitewashing of history. The attempt to eradicate the evils of the world is responsible for the most atrocious acts of history. The aff will say we’ll go extinct; such claims are only expressive of the psychosis and fantasy of the collective ‘good’. This is simply a nihilistic denial of the most intense form of reality.
Baudrillard and L’yvonnet in 04 [Jean, dead french dude, Interview with Francois L’yvonnet, Fragments, p. 105-111]
Our alternative isn’t one action to advocate but a strategy or process whereby we can come to an understanding with evil. We’ll call this strategy “speaking evil” – not an act one and done. But a way of thinking and action, even in our attempts to overcome some forms of violence, that always respects that evil is an inevitable force in our world. Their death threats are just a mirror of the suicidal violence of the good. Within the debate round we can let a theater of cruelty be splayed across the screen of our bodies in order to fully play out and make a drama of the perverse. Only by positioning ourselves as our own agon can we begin to understand the convulsion of the world order. This is the intelligence of evil.
Baudrillard in 05 [Jean, dead french dude, The Intelligence of Evil, p. 159-164]

Allegory of the Squirell

*The 1NC for the Allegory is NEVER the same, but it's usually something like this*
1. When problems overwhelm, us and sadness smothers us, where do we find the will and the courage to continue? Well, the answer may come in the caring voice of a friend, a chance encounter with a book, or from a personal faith. For Jane thelp came from her faith, but it also from a squirrel. Shortly after her divorce, Janet lost her father, then she lost her job. She had mounting money problems. But Janet not only survived, she worked her way out of despondency and now she says, life is good again. How could this happen? She told me that late one Autumn day when she was at her lowest she watched a squirrel storing up nuts for the winter, one at a time he would take them to the nest. And she thought, if that squirrel can take care of himself with the harsh winter coming along, then so can I. Once I broke my problems into small pieces I was able to carry them, just like those acorns, one at a time. – That was in a White Stripes song (Little Acorns)

Deleuze and Guattari

The affirmatives fixation with events outside of our control contributes to a culture of guilt feeding which turns life against itself. Your death is inevitable and the actions of a federal government you have no agency over will not save you. The alternative to the 1ACs approach to politics is to affirm a rhizomatic politics which invigorates personal agency.
Deleuze and Guattari 1972, Anti-Oedipus, 330-39, Murray

It’s not the destination that matters, but the journey. It’s not about where you go but how you get there.” I Don’t have time to reference that quote. Also; “One day we reach our goal, and now point with pride to the long travels we undertook to reach it. In fact, we were not even aware of traveling. But we got so far because we fancied at every point that we were at home.” Nietzsche in the Gay Science.

Politics is not about the endpoint but the process. We are adrift amidst an ocean of political options. We can sail between one of two poles: the paranoic pole or the schizophrenic. The paranoiac pole is marked by its demand for purpose, teleogical end, the security of self and state, and the containment of revolutionary energy. The Schizophrenic pole lies as it’s opposite, marked by its willingness to experiment, its freeness disconnected from pre-determined conclusion.

We propose they are headed towards the paranoiac pole. Their plan and goals and ends and organizations are all too paranoid.

The benefit to voting negative is the ability to willingly lose control. The wind will take you where you need to go. Slide.
Deleuze and Guattari 1972 (Anti-Oedipus)
