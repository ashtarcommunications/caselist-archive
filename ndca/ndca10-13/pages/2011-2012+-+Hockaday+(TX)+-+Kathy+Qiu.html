V: Morality

Only contractarianism accounts for individual interests. Gauthier, David P. Morals by Agreement. Oxford: Clarendon, 1986. Print. “Moral principles are…in their affairs.” GAUTHIER:

Only contractarianism avoids circular claims of morality. Gauthier, David P. Morals by Agreement. Oxford: Clarendon, 1986. Print. “A contractarian theory...morally neutral, base.”

C: consistency with rational contractarianism.

First, our reasons for our own actions are individualized. Gauthier, David P. Morals by Agreement. Oxford: Clarendon, 1986. Print. “Scanlon has shifted…agree with her.”

Second, contractarianism links to universalizability better than the AC.

Third, even if reasons ought to be universal the only way they can act as an authoritative guide to action is if they align with individual interest.

I contend that assisting people in need is inconsistent with rational contractarianism.

First, it would never be in the rational self interest of individuals to assist people in need.

Second, assisting people in need impedes upon the ability of individuals to pursue their interests. Timothy Chappell, [Professor of Philosophy at the Open University Ethics Centre], “Integrity and Demandingness”, Ethic Theory Moral Prac (2007) 10:255–265. “Roughly, William’ own…dispositions and commitments.”
