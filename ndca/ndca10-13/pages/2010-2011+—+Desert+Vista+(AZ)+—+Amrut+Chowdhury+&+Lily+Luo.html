Conention One is Iran Proliferation…

Iran is pursing nuclear weapons
Ben-Meir 2009 [Alon Senior Fellow at the Center for Global Affairs in New York University “Nuclear Iran is Not an Option: A New Negotiating Strategy to Prevent Iran from Developing Nuclear Weapons” The Whitehead Journal of Diplomacy and International Relations Spring 2009]

We’ll Isolate two scenarios:

First, A nuclear Iran will cause an Israeli pre-emptive strike
Chan 2010 [Jonathan Undergraduate at the University of Tornonto “A Precarious Pursuit: Why Iran Should Abandon its Quest for Nuclear Weapons” Journal of Near and Middle Eastern Studies http://www.utoronto.ca/nmc/JNMES_2010.pdf#page=86]

And Iran-Israeli conflict draws in great powers – ensures extinction
Ivashov, 07 (Leonid Ivashov, analyst at the Strategic Culture Foundation, 4/21/2007, "Iran: the Threat of a Nuclear War," http://www.megachip.info/modules.php?name=Sections&op=viewarticle&artid=3871)

Second Iranian proliferation sparks regional proliferation
Wimbush and Ford 1/14 [ S. Enders Senior Vice President at Hudson Institute and Christopher Senior Fellow and director of the Center for Technology and Global Security at Hudson Institute “Perspectives on a Nuclear Iran” The Hudson Institute 2010 http://www.hudson.org/index.cfm?fuseaction=publication_details&id=6698 ]

Arms race in the Middle East causes global nuclear war – strategic, cultural and political factors.
Cimbala 8 [Stephen Cimbala, March 2008. Professor of Political Science at Penn State University. “Anticipatory Attacks: Nuclear Crisis Stability in Future Asia,” Comparative Strategy 27.2, p 113-132.]


Turkey favors the plan and wants a denuclearized Iran – They will engage in negotiations
Barkey 2009 [Henri Visiting scholar in the Carnegie Middle East Program and the Bernard L. and Bertha F. Cohen Professor at Lehigh University “TURKEY.’S PERSPECTIVES ON NUCLEAR WEAPONS AND DISARMAMENT”, http://www.carnegieendowment.org/files/Nuclear_Turkey.pdf]

And Turkey is key to bridging the East and West
Ben-Meir 2009 (Alon Senior Fellow at the Center for Global Affairs in New York University “Nuclear Iran is Not an Option: A New Negotiating Strategy to Prevent Iran from Developing Nuclear Weapons”, The Whitehead Journal of Diplomacy and International Relations Spring 2009)

However removing TNW’s is key to gaining Iran’s trust – only way to curb Iran Prolif
Kibaroglu 2010 [Mustafa Professor and Vice Chair of the International Relations department at Bilkent University, “Reassessing the Role of U.S. Nuclear Weapons in Turkey”, Arms Control Today pg 12, June 2010//dml]


Contention 2 is Relations…

U.S. Turkey relations low - Lack of Contingency plans, joint cooperation and the use of Incirlik
Flanagan and Brannen 2009 [Stephen J senior vice president of the CSIS, Samuel J fellow for the CSIS International Security Program “Turkey’s Evolving Dynamics: Strategic Choices for U.S.-Turkey Relations” Center for Strategic and International Studies http://www.ncci-library.org/jspui/bitstream/123456789/207/1/CSIS%20-%20Turkeys%20Evolving%20Dynamics%20(2009).pdf]

us-turkish relations are key to stability in the caucusus and central asia
Baran 2003 [Zeyno, director at the Senior Fellow and the Director of the Center for Eurasian Policy at The Hudson Institute in Washington The National Interest, “The U.S.-Turkey Partnership: Looking to the Future” http://nationalinterest.org/article/the-us-turkey-partnership-looking-to-the-future-2379]

Conflict in Caucasus causes worldwide war
Blank 2000 (Stephen, professor at the Strategic Studies Institute of the U.S. Army War College. American Grand Strategy and the Transcaspian Region, “U.S. Military Engagement with Transcaucasia and Central Asia,” World Affairs) MJ

But Removal of TNWs would harbor a shift towards contingency planning and joint cooperation
Herzog 9/20 [ Stephen independent security policy analyst, arms control consultant to the Federation of American Scientists and contributor to Foreign Policy In Focus. "The End of Tactical Nukes in Europe?" Washington, DC: Foreign Policy In Focus, September 20, 2010 http://www.fpif.org/articles/the_end_of_tactical_nukes_in_europe]
