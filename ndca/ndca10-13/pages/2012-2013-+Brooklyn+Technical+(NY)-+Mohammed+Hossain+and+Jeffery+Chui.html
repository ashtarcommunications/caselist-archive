Spoken Word subject to change...
Anthony Paul Farley No Date (Albany Law Professor, "The Station")
We strive for progress, to expand faster and bigger, yet the shadows casted by the highways and bridges casts veil of ontological violence that condemns and marginalizes minorities in the status quo into the sidelines of invisibility. We live in a society where predestination isn't just some part of a religion but an invisible disposition that permeates our society on multiple abstracts. Our social location and our literal location determines where we go and when we get there. We are confronted with the resolution question of transportation infrastructure, the "common sense" notion of this nation's infrastructure is as the committee of infrastructure puts it, "To create jobs, boost the economy, and improve the wellbeing of its citizens" . The same sort of transportation infrastructure that segregated blacks from whites, the same transportation infrastructure that forced the minority populations of New Orleans into the basin deathtrap, the same transportation infrastructure that displaced underprivileged communities while catering to the ones who could afford to buy it and I don't just mean with money. Ignorance is not bliss but murder, where forgetting becomes the same as condemning. The duty that we have as we are confronted with this year's resolution is not to simply accept the bleached history of transportation infrastructure as a stepping stone for genocidal progress but to excavate what it meant to invest in transportation infrastructure.
-John Henry Song-
NELSON Assc. Prof of History @ the College of William and Mary 2k6
Scott Reynolds-; Steel Drivin’ Man –JOHN HENRY- The untold story of an American Legend; p40.

Slavery was never ended in a sentence. Through the policy process we illegalized slavery but internalized it. the C&O railroad was not built on legitimate labor, but on the back of exploitation and the libidinal notion of Manifest Destiny which was satisfied through the fungibility of the black body as well as the perverse augmentation of the narrative of John Henry. The slave absent of ontology always ready to be used and disposed, and at the same time, there is a perverse desire to dominate Blacks farther than a material or political relation. John Henry’s narratives of sufferance and death are propagated and celebrated around the country illustrate both these concepts so Americans can sing and dance to a song that was sung while building the endoskeleton of America.
Hartman, Saidiya V. Scenes of Subjection. New York, Oxford: Oxford University, 1997. Print.


Thus, Jeffrey and I endorse an affirmation of the topic through an interrogation history through the novel of John Henry.

THE QUESTIONS OF WHY KATRINA WAS ALLOWED TO HAPPEN, WHY RESIDENTIAL SEGREGATION EXISTS, AND WHY A NORMATIVE INVESTMENT IN TRANSPORTATION INFRASTRUCTURE JUST DOESN'T CUT IT CAN ONLY BE ANSWERED THROUGH A RADICAL GENEOLOGY OF THE HISTORICITY OF THE RESOLUTION.

Toward a More Global Sense of the Nation

The debate community is created from a plethora of identities and narratives such as John Henry’s. The way that we integrate ourselves within the community is specific to the individual, but an abandonment of these narratives makes debate irrelevant to many debaters through social distancing causing the decline and eventual extinction of the debate community. Furthermore, an abandonment of these narratives continues an erasure of historicity which continues the destruction and fungibility of narratives such as John Henry’s.
Valdivia-Sutherland, Professor at Butte Community College, 98
