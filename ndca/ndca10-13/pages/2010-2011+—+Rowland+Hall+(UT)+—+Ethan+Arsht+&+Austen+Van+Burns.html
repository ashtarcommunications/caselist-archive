Affirmative

Afghanistan Plan -


Plan: The United States federal government should substantially reduce its counterinsurgency presence in Afghanistan.

OR

Plan: The Department of Defense should substantially reduce its counterinsurgency presence in Afghanistan.


Hegemony Advantage


First, we’re not ahead – Counterinsurgency is impossible in Afghanistan.

Failure is inevitable in the status quo – Corruption, lack of resources, ethnic tensions, and geography make the strategy ineffective.

Previously, Dorronsoro was a professor of political science at the Sorbonne, Paris and the Institute of Political Studies of Rennes (Gilles, “The Case for Negotiations,” In These Times, 5/24, http://www.carnegieendowment.org/publications/index.cfm?fa=view&id=40863)

The coalition's strategy in Afghanistan is at an impasse … recognize the impossibility of a military solution.


The Afghan COIN model is disastrous for US hegemony – We will control 2 internals

b. Morale & Readiness

Afghanistan is a quagmire –presence eviscerates American morale.

Kuhner, 9 - the president of the Edmund Burke Institute for American Renewal (Jeffrey, Washington Times, “Obama’s quagmire; US should look to its own interests,” 9/7, Lexis Academic)

America is losing the war in Afghanistan … Afghans into the evil, welcoming arms of extremists

Loss of primacy guarantees world wars.

Kagan 07 - senior fellow at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace (Robert, “End of Dreams, Return of History”, 7/19, http://www.realclearpolitics.com/articles/2007/07/end_of_dreams_return_of_histor.html)

Finally, there is the United States itself. As a matter of national policy … influence and global involvement will provide an easier path


The impact is extinction

Even if they win that a world without hegemony is good, they still lose – getting there would mean massive transition wars across the globe

Brzezinski 5 (Zbigniew National Security Advisor in the Carter Administration, Professor of Foreign Policy at Johns Hopkins University, “The Choice: Global Domination or Global Leadership”, p. 2-4)

History is a record of change, a reminder that nothing endures … enlist others in a common effort to shape a secure international environment.


Less is more – shifting to a counterterrorism strategy maintains public support allows for a smaller, but permanent presence in the region.

Stewart, ‘9 - Ryan Family Professor of the Practice of Human Rights and Director of the Carr Center for Human Rights Policy, studied at Oxford and served briefly in the British army before working in the diplomatic service in Indonesia and as British representative to Montenegro (9/16/09, Rory, “The Future of Afghanistan,” http://www.hks.harvard.edu/news-events/news/testimonies/rory-stewart-on-afghanistan)

The best Afghan policy would be to reduce the number of foreign troops … In Afghanistan in the long-term, less will be more.

The war in Afghanistan is at a crossroads… watching them very closely.


This usurpation of grand strategy by the military brass will have devastating consequences—military dominance of foreign policy results in tunnel vision, conflict escalation, and adventurist wars that drag the U.S. into perpetual quagmires.

Hillman Aff

PLAN: The United States federal government should eliminate its military presence in Afghanistan.

Observation One: A Failure of Imagination
1. Hegemony –
The American military portrays itself as the “Prince of Peace.” To quote a few leading hegemony authors, American unipolarity “deters hostile rivals;” it “projects power;” “it preserve order by acting as a global policeman;” and it “creates a balance of power in world politics that ensures stability.” This goal for primacy is the final crusade for peace itself.
Hillman in 2004 (James, retired Director of the Jung Institute, “A Terrible Love of War”, The Penguin Press, ISBN 1-59420-011-4, pg 84)
As latecomers (twenty- five centuries or more) … battalions go abroad "waging peace."

2. Military Presence –
It’s an integral part of this war for peace and is rooted in the definition of the phrase.
Thomason et al 2002 (IDA Paper P-3707, “Transforming US Overseas Military Presence: Evidence and Options for DoD Volume I: Main Report” This paper has been prepared by the Institute for Defense Analyses (IDA) in partial fulfillment of a task being performed for the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense . The task, entitled “Effects-Based Assessments of US Presence and Deployment Patterns,” is being conducted to help the DoD identify evidence of the effects that actual and potential alternative US overseas military presence postures and activities have or may have in promoting key US defense and national security strategy goals. James S. Thomason, Senior Analyst, Strategy, Forces and Resources Division, Institute for Defense Analyses EDUCATION Ph.D., International Relations, Northwestern University (1978) B.A., Government, Harvard College (1969) , http://www.comw.org/qdr/fulltext/0207thomason.pdf)
WHAT IS OVERSEAS MILITARY PRESENCE? … should call for them.2

And, our occupation of foreign countries is a failed attempt to manage and fix global problems. In short, our aversion to war is actually producing it.
Thomas Moore in 1990 (Thomas Moore is the author of the bestselling book Care of the Soul and fifteen other books. He has Ph. D. in religion from Syracuse University and has won several awards for his work, including an honorary doctorate from Lesley University. The essential James Hillman: a blue fire, Routledge; 1 edition, 1-3)
Because of its family ties to medicine, … in our symptoms is our soul.

3. Sanitation –
Our presence in Afghanistan is the logical extreme of this aversion. Drones sanitize war and remove operators from the massive amounts of damage done
Payne, ‘9 (Michael, American Foreign Policy Specialist for Online Journal and the Information Clearing House, “Deadly Drones: Immoral Weapons of Civilian Destruction,” October 23, 2009, http://www.opednews.com/articles/Deadly-drones-immoral-wea-by-michael-payne-091021-444.html)
I call the drones being used in Afghanistan … what America has become?
And, Counterinsurgency attempts to win the hearts of minds of Afghan civilians and our soldiers are made to see themselves as heroes crusading against the evil Taliban. This sanitized understanding of war brainwashes us, the civil soldiers, so we forget what war really is – war in the name of peace is the ultimate psychological delusion.
Hillman 04 (James, retired Director of the Jung Institute, “A Terrible Love of War”, The Penguin Press, ISBN 1-59420-011-4, pgs 90-92)
Why dwell on this archaic …the mind would be enough.

4. Obama
Declared the anti-war president, Obama was awarded the Nobel Peace prize for his vision of disarmament and love. Yet, every year, more drone strikes happen under his administration than the entire Bush presidency. This amplification of violence in Afghanistan is the underbelly of our pursuit of peace.
Hillman 04 (James, retired Director of the Jung Institute, “A Terrible Love of War”, The Penguin Press, ISBN 1-59420-011-4, pgs 30-31)
The worst of war is that it ends … declared for a sword.

5. Afghanistan –
We will conclude with a narrative describing the violence that takes place in Afghanistan every day.
Jaffry, ’10 (Abdul-Majid, “Afghanistan War: A Saga of Lopsided Death and Destruction, May 26, 2010, NWO Observer,
http://nwoobserver.wordpress.com/2010/05/27/afghanistan-war-a-saga-of-lopsided-death-and-destruction/
Today, history of another … with swords and lances.


Observation Two – The Role of the Ballot

The psyche exists and comes first – it shapes our understanding of the world – everything depends on it. The resolution should only be evaluated on its psychological qualities.
Jung 06 (Carl G., renowned scholar of psychoanalysis and founder of the Jung Institute, The Undiscovered Self, New American Library, New York, 81-83)
For more than fifty years we have known… can happen to everyone.

Refusing to operate on the terrain of the psyche ensures a loss of soul. This state of dehumanization destroys any meaning to life and risks death.
Hillman 1990 (James, has written dozens of critically acclaimed books, received his PhD from the University of Zurich, and is a retired Director of the Jung Institute, “A Blue Fire”, a blue fire, Routledge; 1 edition p. 17-18)-mikee
Anthropologists describe a condition among "primitive" … in search of a soul."

Our 1AC is solvent – Military presence is the primary psychological task that shapes all of our notions about society. Unless we come to terms with the war in each of us, our desire for war will lash out and push us closer and closer to annihilation.
Hillman 04 (James, retired Director of the Jung Institute, “A Terrible Love of War”, The Penguin Press, ISBN 1-59420-011-4, pgs 1-3)
One sentence in one scene from … is the purpose of this book.

The negative will attempt to map out the topic and psychologically attempt to silence the soul within the topic by trying to empiricize and literalize it, and looking at implementation. In contrast we offer the plan of archetypal psychology: to explore our deep psychological commitments and affirm our most terrorizing images of the world, namely the topic of military presence.
