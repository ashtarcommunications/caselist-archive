Observation 2 is Anthropocentric Sovereignty

Our most basic understanding of humanity is derived from an anthropological machine which constructs understandings of humanity against understandings of animality. These understandings of animality serve as both constitutive outside and inside which produces and maintains a hierarchal categorization of humanity. The very act of definition necessarily submits a third phantom category of life – bare life, which by its own subjugation connects and sustains the categories of human and animal. Only through an understanding of this construction can we hope to stop this form of violence by the sovereign. [Gendered Language modifications made]

Observation 3 is Jamming the Anthropological Machine:

The conceptualization of the UFO presents a triple threat to the legitimacy of anthropocentric sovereignty—physical, ontological, and metaphysical. It is for this reason that modern governmentality chooses to actively ignore the UFO instead of interrogate it. The ability of sovereignty to deny the UFO as worthy of scientific inquiry is precisely the foundation upon which the legitimacy and coherence of anthropocentric sovereignty is based upon.

Wendt and Duvall 2008 (Alexander Wendt and Raymond Duvall, Duvall is a professor of political science at U. of Minnesota, Wendt is a constructivist IR Professor at Ohio State U., June 11 2008, Political Theory, Vol. 36, No. 4, “Sovereignty and the UFO,” http://ptx.sagepub.com/content/36/4/607)
