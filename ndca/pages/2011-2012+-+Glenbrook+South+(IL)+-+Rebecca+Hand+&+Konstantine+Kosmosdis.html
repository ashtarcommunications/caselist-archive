Affirmative

1AC: SSA


Text: The United States federal government should substantially increase its civil space situational awareness by creating a Space Guard.

Contention 1: Crisis Stability
US space assets are currently vulnerable-multiple different adversaries with different military doctrines invite attack
Rendleman, 10 - Colonel, U.S. Air Force (Retired), (James, Astropolitics, 8:220–255, 2010, “A Strategy for Space Assurance,” Ebsco Political Science)

"The 11 January 2007 test of a Chinese...robust enough to counter now 21st Century adversary attack capabilities."

Only increasing SSA can reduce vulnerability – failure guarantees instability
Bruce W. MacDonald Assistant director for national security at the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (1995-1999), Senior director for science and technology on the National Security Council (1995-1999) BSE and MA, Princeton, Aerospace Engineering, Rocket Propulsion and Public and International Affairs 2009

"Our space assets are exposed and fragile...more frequent space collisions and growing instability in space."

Increasing vulnerability creates first strike incentives that make escalation more probable
MacDonald, 11 - Senior Director, Nonproliferation and Arms Control Program, U.S. Institute of Peace (Bruce, CQ Congressional Testimony, “MILITARY AND CIVIL SPACE PROGRAMS IN CHINA”, 5/11, lexis)

"One characteristic of too many wars...space instability seems high and likely to grow."

This will cause crises to go nuclear
Belois and Garwin et. al. 2004 Bruce M. DeBlois is Directorof Systems Integration at British Aerospace, formerly Adjunct Senior Fellow for Science and Technology at the Council on Foreign Relations Richard L. Garwin is IBM Fellow Emeritus, Watson Research Center of IBM formerly Senior Fellow for Science and Technology at CFR Scott Kempis a Fulbright Fellow to the European Union and research staff at the Program on Science and Global Security at Princeton University, CFR. Jeremy C. Marwell is a Furman Scholar at New York University International Security, Vol. 29, No. 2 (Fall 2004), pp. 50-84

"Techniques for denying an adversary's satellite reconnaissance...US and Soviet reconnaissance satellites and their overflight rights."

The world is committed to measures to improving space stability – a lack of US commitment is the only obstacle
Meyer 2011 – Paul Meyer, Ambassador to the Office of the United Nations and Ambassador for Disarmament in Geneva, representing Canada, April 4, 2011, “Diplomatic Options for Reinforcing Outer Space Security,” http://www.unidir.ch/pdf/conferences/pdf-conf1042.pdf

"One of the striking features of the outer space...what the judge said about pornography - the US will know it when it sees it."

The US military isn’t trusted by international actors – only a Space Guard provides the balance of civil and military involvement necessary to foster SSA data sharing
Bennett 10 – James C. Bennett, President and Chairman of the Board of Internet Transactions Transnational, Inc.; co-founded Space Enterprise Consultants, the first consulting firm devoted entirely to commercial space development; member of the 1983 White House Task Force on Space Commercialization; Adjunct Senior Fellow of the Hudson Institute; Director of the Foresight Institute; member of the Board of Advisors for the National Space Society; October 2010, “The Coast Guard Model: A Third Organizational Option For International SSA Data Sharing and Other US Space Responsibilities,” George Washington University Space Policy Institute, http://www.gwu.edu/~spi/assets/docs/CoastGuardModel-GWUpaper.pdf, p. 16-17

"An organizational home for non-military SSA data functions...discussion and study of this concept is merited."

Contention 2: Space debris

First, All Qualified experts agree – Orbital debris is at the tipping point – failure to control it collapses our space assets
The Telegraph 11 (Heidi Blake, staffwriter, “Space so full of junk that a satellite collision could destroy communications on Earth,” February, pg online @ http://www.telegraph.co.uk/science/space/8295546/Space-so-full-of-junk-that-a-satellite-collision-could-destroy-communications-on-Earth.html) np

"The volume of abandoned rockets...switch the television to watch the World Cup next month in Johannesburg."

Current SSA fails, 3 reasons – lack of Southern hemisphere coverage, outdated technology, and lack of data sharing
Weeden and Kelso 9 [Brian Weeden, Technical Advisor for Secure World Foundation and former US Air Force officer, T.S. Kelso, Senior Research Astrodynamicist at the Center for Space Standards and Innovation, 2009, “Analysis Of The Technical Feasibility Of Building An International Civil Space Situational Awareness System,” http://swfound.org/media/1719/iac-09_bw.pdf]

"The main drawback of the US military's SSA capabilities...potentially losing track of an object."

Situational Awareness is at a CRITICAL Stage-a lack of a new dedicated civilian organization means current efforts at space debris removal will fail
Bennett 10 (James, C. Research Associate @ George Washington University’s Space Policy Institute, “The Coast Guard Model:A Third Organizational Option For InternationalSSA Data Sharing and Other US Space Responsibilities,” George Washington University, Space Policy Institute, pg online @ http://www.gwu.edu/~spi/assets/docs/CoastGuardModel-GWUpaper.pdf) np

"Recent events have underscored the reality...distrustful of the USAF as a collaborative partner."

Acknowledging the existent of space debris would cause a spillover to removal measures
David 11 [ Leonard David: Space.com, How to Clean Up Space Junk: Darpa’s Orbital Catcher’s Mitt, 5-13-2011, http://www.space.com/11657-space-junk-orbital-debris-cleanup-darpa.html]

"Although space is not an ecosystem...cause the future growth in the debris population."

Reliable space traffic management systems would prevent a cascade effect – security limitations means the US military can’t perform this task
Weeden 9 [Brian Weeden, Technical Advisor for Secure World Foundation and former US Air Force officer, March 2009, “Space Sustainability: To Preserve And Protect...,” Satellite Magazine, http://www.satmagazine.com/cgi-bin/display_article.cgi?number=1415465455]

"In the meantime, space actors must...ensure that no collisions happen."

Without effective space debris control, US space assets which are crucial to US hegemony are put at risk
Marshall, 8 - NASA Ames Research Center (Will, Astropolitics, 6:154–199, “REDUCING THE VULNERABILITY OF SPACE ASSETS: A MULTITIERED MICROSATELLITE CONSTELLATION ARCHITECTURE,” Ebsco Political Science)

"Space assets are one of the most critical...topic that could benefit from further research."

This could result in global nuclear conflicts in every region of the world
KAGAN, 7 (Robert, senior fellow at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace (Robert, “End of Dreams, Return of History”, 7/19, http://www.realclearpolitics.com/articles/2007/07/end_of_dreams_return_of_histor.html)

"This is a good thing, and it should...global involvement will provide and easier path."

Contention 3: Solvency

The plan establishes a Space Guard built on Civilian SSA – that spills-over to shift functions from other agencies

Bennett 11 (James C. Bennett, Research Associate @ George Washington University’s Space Policy Institute, the author of The Anglosphere Challenge, has been involved in space entrepreneurship, consulting, and advocacy for more than three decades “Proposing a ‘Coast Guard’ for Space,” pg online @ http://www.thenewatlantis.com/publications/proposing-a-coast-guard-for-space) np

"New responsibilities. In addition to these...to alleviate its chronic span-of-control issues."
