Nietzsche Aff


The Modern Age Has Destroyed Every Claim To A Metaphysical Basis For Truth. The Truths Of The World, Both Moral And Factual, Are All Constructed And Deconstructed By History. Only That Without A History Can Be Defined And As Everything Has A History Nothing Can Be Defined. Before Any Meaning Can Be Given To The Resolution Or To Space Or Any Combination ThereIn We Must First Acknowledge The Meaninglessness Which Will Underpin Any Investigation.
Ian Johnston. “There’s Nothing Nietzsche Couldn’t Teach Ya About the Raising of the Wrist: A Lecture in Liberal Studies.” Google It. 1999.

However, If You Think The Loss Of A Priori Meaning Is Sad Or Depressing, You Are Not Thinking Hard Enough. The Only Sane Response To A World Devoid Of Meaning Is To Look At It Joyfully Like An Artist’s Canvas, A Blank Slate For Creation. What’s More We Must Dedicate Ourselves Fully To This Creation, To Create The World And Ourselves In The Way We Find Most Affirming. To Do Anything Less Is To Deny Our Will To Power, And By Extension Our Very Humanity.
Ian Johnston. “There’s Nothing Nietzsche Couldn’t Teach Ya About the Raising of the Wrist: A Lecture in Liberal Studies.” Google It. 1999.


This Struggle Between The Establishment And Deconstruction Of A Universally Anchored Meaning Is Most Profoundly Exemplified Through Questions Of Earths Place In The Universe. Space Exploration Challenges The Notion That Earth Holds A Privileged Place In The Universe. Such A Challenge Cuts At The Heart Of The Idea Of Metaphysical Truth By Reducing The Earthly Plain Upon Which All Knowledge Is Based To An Absurd Cosmic Accident.
Niel Turnbull, ‘6, Senior Lecturer in Philosophy and Social Theory and Nottingham Trent U, “The Ontological Consequence of Copernicus: Global Being in the Planetary World”, Theory, Culture & Society Vol. 23(1))

And Once Again We Think This Is Wonderful. If Earth Holds No Privilege, And Can Never Serve As A Metaphysical Anchor, The Universe Is Ours For Creating
Niel Turnbull, ‘6, Senior Lecturer in Philosophy and Social Theory and Nottingham Trent U, “The Ontological Consequence of Copernicus: Global Being in the Planetary World”, Theory, Culture & Society Vol. 23(1))

Now Clearly We Will Not Defend That Everyone In The USFG Suddenly Wakes Up And Agrees With US. We Know That’s Not The Normal Mode Of Affirmation And That’s Fine. In A World Of Radical Self Creation There Is No Reason The Game Of Debate Should Be Excluded. We Think Debate, Like The Universe, Is Ours For Defining.
Ian Johnston. “There’s Nothing Nietzsche Couldn’t Teach Ya About the Raising of the Wrist: A Lecture in Liberal Studies.” Google It. 1999.

Our Mode Of Affirmation Is Ours Alone, We Do Not Expect That Any Will Copy Us, To Do So Would Miss The Point Entirely. We Would Never Presume To Dictate The Content Of Anyone’s Response To The Topic, We Only Insist You As The Judge Do The Same. This Also Answers That Stupid Argument Where People Say We Link To Ourselves
Frederick M. Dolan. “SYMPOSIUM: Nietzsche and Legal Theory (Part 1): Nietzsche’s Gnosis of Law” 24 Cardozo L. Rev. 757. January. 2003

In The End, This Will Outweigh All There Flustered Cries Of “You Cheat”. You Must Refuse Their Calls To Police Debate. You Have No Way Of Determining How To Measure Good Debate From Bad, Such A Determination Would Require The Ability To Look Beyond Your Own Cultural And Historical Frame And Determine What All Participants Should Get Out Of Every Debate. Such A Move Is Impossible And You Shouldn’t Try. Instead You Should Defend The Only Thing You Can Know Has Value, And That Is The Creation Of Openness Within Which We Can All Affirm Ourselves Through Our Subjective Approach To The Topic.
Ian Johnston. “There’s Nothing Nietzsche Couldn’t Teach Ya About the Raising of the Wrist: A Lecture in Liberal Studies.” Google It. 1999.

We should not decide in advance what educational goals this activity should fulfill. To presume to know what educational value debate has for every debater guarantees oppressive hierarchies of knowledge
Charles Bingham 98, The goal of language the language of goals: Nietzsche’s concern with rhetoric and its educational implications, Educational Theory, 00132004, Spring98, Vol. 48, Issue 2

There are two different ways of thinking about rules. We can recognize them as product of our own subjectivity at which point the Aff is a prerequisite or see them as handed down from an unquestionable authority at which point we violently exclude any difference at all
.Ian Johnston, Prof at Vancouver Island University , “There’s Nothing Nietzsche Couldn’t Teach Ya About the Raising of the Wrist: A Lecture in Liberal Studies.” Google It. 1999.

Your consensus cards are wrong: Debate is only possible with respect for different approaches.
Bleiker 98 asst. prof. of International Studies at Pusan National University (Roland, “Retracing and redrawing the boundaries of events: Postmodern interferences with international theory”, Alternatives, Oct-Dec 1998, Vol. 23, Issue 4)

Thus Marie And I Advocate The Creation Of A Voluntary Female Separatist Space Colony

Contention 2 Is Separatism
Patriarchy as a mode of social relations has pervaded every aspect of social life for the majority of people for the majority of history. Escaping the hegemony of patriarchy will require nothing short of a radical rethinking of social relation.

The omnipresence of patriarchy has resulted in literally countless acts of gendered violence that extend beyond traditional female victims to endanger the entire world.
Jennie Ruby. Male-Pattern Violence. She is a feminist writer and works at multiple feminist magazines. http://www.offourbacks.org/malepat.htm. No Date Given (NDG).

For centuries the antagonism between women and the patriarchal systems that seek to define them and their possibilities has resulted in sporadic struggles and gains, but the overall system of partiarchy remains well intact. The consistent problems with these struggles has been that they take as a starting point the notion that a feminist politics must be enacted within a larger male society. This is false.Only through true and deliberate separatism can a feminist politics capable of challenging patriarchy emerge. Anything short of this radical set guarantees concession and cooption.
Julia Penelope. The Mystery of Lesbians: III From Lesbian Ethics Vol 1. No 3, 1985. She is a radical lesbian separatist. http://www.feminist-reprise.org/docs/penelope3-1.htm

We will isolate two independent warrants for why a politics of separatism is key to fostering a true challenge to patriarchy. First, as long as a feminist politics is placed within a patriarchial society that society will be able to exert social pressure on any feminist movement, diluting and coopting it.
Jeffner Allen, 1989, The Thinking muse: feminism and modern French philosophy, Indiana Press
Second. Separatism allows space for all of male society to be named as an enemy in the fight against patriarchy. This is only possible when feminism is unburdened by the necessity of existing in the society it criticizes.
Julia Penelope. The Mystery of Lesbians: III From Lesbian Ethics Vol 1. No 3, 1985. She is a radical lesbian separatist. http://www.feminist-reprise.org/docs/penelope3-1.htm

History proves that separatist politics can spill over to integrated society, reducing the hold of patriarchy for everyone.
Margaret Sloan-Hunter. 1976. Making Separatist Connections: The Issue is Woman Identification. She is a radical black lesbian feminist, and a writer on feminist issues. http://www.feminist-reprise.org/docs/Sloan-Hunter.htm

Contention Three Is Framework
The politics of true separatism begins not with the act of separating, but with the affirmation that we as individuals have the power to separate. Patriarchy maintains power rigidly defining where power lies, and thus patriarchal society will always attempt to deny this power to individuals. The appropriate response it to ignore the denial and affirm, even in the face of possibility, that we a individuals have the ability to separate.

Marilyn Frye, from Politics of Reality: essays in feminist theory (Crossing Press, 1983). She is a writer, and a feminist. “Lesbian Feminism and the Gay Rights Movement: Another View of Male Supremacy, Another Separatism” http://www.feminist-reprise.org/docs/fryegayrights.htm.



Negative

The “N” K


The AFF positions themselves as standing between humanity and the chaos and suffering of the world. They are not simply being ethical, but are taking the radically modern position that the world can be tamed and ordered. In juxtaposing the chaotic real world with an idealized ordered world the AFF necessitates resentment both for a life that can never reach the ideal world and for the other who must be scapegoated to explain the failure of world order.
Saurette, 96 – PhD in Political Theory at John Hopkins University – 1996(Paul, “’I Mistrust all Systematizers and Avoid them’: Nietzsche, Arendt and the Crsiis of the Will to Order in International Relations Theory”, Millennium: Journal of International Studies, Vol. 25, No. 1, Page 1-28)

Any life affirming politics must begin from the fact that suffering is an inevitable element of existence. The fact that the Aff’s first instinct is to seek out an instance of violence, suffering, or death and attempt to alleviate it demonstrates the misguided nature of their politics and baselessness of their claims to moral obligation.
Kain 7 (Phillip J., Professor of Philosophy at Santa Clara University "Nietzsche and the Horror of Existence", The Journal of Nietzsche Studies, Issue 33, Spring 2007

The AFFs approach to the chaos of existence marks themselves as continually responsible for moving humanity towards this ideal world. This notion of responsibility can only result in unmitigated guilt for the inevitable failure to bring the world under control. This makes life unlivable as we continually try and control an uncontrollable world at the expense of our own self-affirmation and humanity.
Friedrich Nietzsche, On the Genealogy of Morals, 1887

This approach also has consequences for the rest of the world. The desire to rid the world of chaos and suffering justifies the continuous attempts at universal control that justifies the worst forums of western imperialism by compulsively labeling all forums of political difference as threat.
Der Derian 95 (James, Professor of Political Science at Brown University, “The Value of Security: Hobbes, Marx, Nietzsche, and Baudrillard,” Chapter Two in On Security by Ronnie D. Lipschutz)

The idea of a moral calculus which determines the highest good of humanity is neither natural nor objective. Rather it is a false belief rooted in the Eurocentric distinction between civilized and savage life. In the same way human Christendom needed to see its foil in figure of the subhuman new world savage, the affirmative’s calculus guarantees arbitrary scapegoating to justify its claim to moral purity
Rasch 2003 (William, Prof of Germanic Studies @ Indiana University, "Human Rights as Geopolitics: Carl Schmitt and the Legal Form of American Supremacy", Cultured Critique 54)


Your notions of prediction are a knee jerk reaction to the horror of an orderless universe. As we look into the abyss we are overcome with the desire to order it and to make it familiar by inventing laws, patterns, and causality. These laws however, cannot respond to the complexity and unknowability of the universe and if they could we would lack the cognitive capacity to grasp them. As such predictions represent the ultimate drive to control and master a universe which we have no hope of controlling.

Kain 1983(Philip, Professor of Philosophy at Santa Clara University, Skepticism, and Eternal Recurrence, Canadian Journal of Philosophy, Vol. 13, No. 3)


Our Alterative Is To Vote Negative To Willfully Forget The Harms Of The 1AC. This Act Of Forgetting Frees Us To Radically Focus On Our Own Acton And Self Creation And By Extension To Exist In The World Absent The Guilt And Drive To Control Which Underscore’s The Politics Of The AFF.
Zupancic 2003 (Alenka, Researcher at the Institute of philosophy @ U of Ljubljana, The Shortest Shadow: Nietzsche's Philosophy of the Two, p. 56-60

This ability to self-create is the strongest internal link to a valuable life. Do not be swayed by the Aff’s appeal to slave morality, there is no basis for such claims, there is only the drive of the individual to live and create oneself in a way that is true to one’s own most individuality. Our individualistic focus is the opposite of the AFFs desire to control others and the universe.
Ian Johnston, Prof at Vancouver Island University , “There’s Nothing Nietzsche Couldn’t Teach Ya About the Raising of the Wrist: A Lecture in Liberal Studies.” Google It. 1999.
