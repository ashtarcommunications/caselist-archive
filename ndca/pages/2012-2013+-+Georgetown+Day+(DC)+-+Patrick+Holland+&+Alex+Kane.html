Affirmative

1AC (Greenhill)

Contention 1 is Exclusion--


Investment decisions for transportation infrastructure have systematically removed disabled populations from society by denying them of their mobility.
AAPD 12 (The American Association of People With Disabilities, “Equity in Transportation for People with Disabilities”, May 23, http://www.infrastructureusa.org/equity-in-transportation-for-people-with-disabilities/)
Transportation and mobility play key roles in the struggle for civil rights ..... a lack of requirements has meant very uneven progress.

And current transportation infrastructure reinforces the medical model of disability and result in forced social exclusion.
Audirac 08 (Ivonne Audirac, Florida State University, 5/16/2008 “Accessing Transit as Universal Design”, Journal of Planning Literature 2008, Sage Journals, http://jpl.sagepub.com/content/23/1/4.full.pdf+html)
Although influential transportation planners believe ..... Sullivan 2000; Grieco 2003; Hine and Mitchell 2001; TCRP 1999).


And this medical model is the root cause of conflict.
Elden 02 (Stuart Elden, politics at University of Warwick, 2/29/2002 “The War of Races and the Constitution of the State: Foucault's «Il faut défendre la société» and the Politics of Calculation,” Boundary, http://boundary2.dukejournals.org/content/29/1/125.full.pdf)
The reverse side is the power to allow death. State racism......able to wage so many wars, causing so many men to be killed. (VS, 180; WK, 136)

And ableism within status quo transportation infrastructure perpetuates broader exclusion of all groups – Justifies eugenics and genocide.
Bagenstos 2000 (Sam Bagenstos, Professor at the University of Michigan, Ex-Harvard Law Prof, 2000 “Subordination, Stigma, and Disability,” Virginia Law Review, Vol. 86, No. 3, p.437-45)
Erving Goffman’s notion of stigma is a useful tool.. directed at people with disabilities have effectively marked that group as a “dependent caste.” 1 9 0

And this type of everyday violence must be prioritized in your calculations. It creates the conditions that psychologically cause the worst atrocities.
Scheper-Hughes and Bourgois 04 (Prof of Anthropology @ Cal-Berkely; Prof of Anthropology @ UPenn, and Nancy and Philippe, Introduction: Making Sense of Violence, in Violence in War and Peace, pg. 19-22)
This large and at first sight “messy” Part VII is.....including the house gun and gated communities; and reversed feelings of victimization).

1AC – Plan Text

Thus we advocate: The United States federal government should substantially increase its transportation infrastructure accessibility investment.

And contention 2 is solvency--

Vote aff to endorse inclusion and reject the exclusionary status quo – bringing abelism into policy debates is an ethical imperative. What we say here is the litmus test for subjugation across the globe.
Bérubé 03 (Michael Bérubé, Paterno Family Professor in Literature at Pennsylvania State University, 2003“Citizenship and Disability”, Spring, http://www.dissentmagazine.org/article/?article=506)
It is striking, nonetheless, that so few leftists ...... and for the good of democracy, which is to say, for the good of all of us.

And Universal Design deconstructs the medical model through infrastructure investment.
Sullivan 11 (Kathryn Sullivan Franklin W. Olin College of Engineering, 5/2/2011, “The Prevalence of the Medical Model of Disability in Society”, Olin College)
Many designers are now beginning to acknowledgethe social model of disability... but rather as unique individuals who have a lot to offer to society.



And Universal Design is feasible – models prove.
Audirac 08 (Ivonne Audirac, Florida State University, 5/16/2008,“Accessing Transit as Universal Design”, Journal of Planning Literature 2008, Sage Journals, http://jpl.sagepub.com/content/23/1/4.full.pdf+html)
UD applied to transit is an inclusionary strategy that seeks to redress .... Thus, although they are a form of inclusive design, they are not UD solutions to micro-level transit accessibility.


As the privileged, we have a moral duty to prevent injustices from befalling the marginalized—it is impossible for us to disengage from a system riddled with inequalities, and we must try to fix it as much as possible
Tan 97 -(Kok-Chor, “Kantian Ethics and Global Justice,” Social Theory & Practice, Spring97, Vol. 23 Issue 1, p53, 21p)
There is another reason that Kantian justice in practice calls for positive duty .... In any case, some positive duty is in order.


And federal action is key to raising national consciousness.
Drimmer 93 (Jonathan Drimmer, Editor, UCLA Law Review, UCLA 6/1993 “CRIPPLES, OVERCOMERS, AND CIVIL RIGHTS: TRACING THE EVOLUTION OF FEDERAL LEGISLATION AND SOCIAL POLICY FOR PEOPLE WITH DISABILITIES” 40 UCLA L. Rev. 1341, lexis)
Society resonates with the message that people with disabilities..... society will continue to view people with disabilities as inherently inferior.

And action sends an international signal to end the stigmatization of the disabled.
Stanton 96 (John F., JD Candidate at Georgetown, “Immigration Laws from a Disability Perspective: Where We Were, Where We Are, Where We Should Be”, 10 Geo. Immigr. L.J. 441, Lexis)
3. A Proposal for the INA to Adopt the Americans with Disabilities Act's "Reasonable Accommodation" .... that people with disabilities can be productive members of society and not a class to be shunned and avoided.

And contention 3 is no war--

The appeal to utilitarianism directly ensures the continued and abuse of the disabled globally.
Light 07 (Richard, Research and Publications Director at Disability Awareness in Action, “A Real Horror Story: the Abuse of Disabled People’s Human Rights”, July 1, 2007, http://www.disabilityworld.org/04-05_03/violence/horrorstory.shtml)
There is, however, a horror which has remained almost invisible:....... if only because the alternative would seem to be even greater social stresses than those that already exist.

And the prior question is whether the plan on its-face affirms a worthy ethical principle. To void this obligation in the name of future consequences fails to establish any coherent moral framework and makes decisions impossible.
Finnis 80 (John, Professor of Law and Legal Philosophy at Oxford, Natural Law and Natural Rights, 120-122)
To choose an act which in itself simply (or primarily) damages a basic good is thereby to engage...... Act so that you treat humanity, whether in your own person or in that of another, always as an end and never as a means only'.12

And attempting to start from a position of preventing large-scale violence blinds us and makes war and violence inevitable
Cuomo 96 (activist, artist, and Professor of Philosophy and Women's Studies at the University of Cincinnati. She is the author of The Philosopher Queen: Feminist Essays on War, Love, and Knowledge-- (Chris, “War Is Not Just an Event: Reflections on the Significance of Everyday Violence!” hypatia, 1996, Proquest)
Ethical approaches that do not attend to the ways in which warfare and military practices are woven into the very fabric of life .......These approaches also leave unquestioned tendencies to suspend or distort moral judgement in the face of what appears to be the inevitability of war and militarism.
