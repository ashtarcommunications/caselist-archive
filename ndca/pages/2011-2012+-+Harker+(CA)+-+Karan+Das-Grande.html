Desi NC


I negate.

The neg burden is to show that it is morally impermissible for victims of repeated domestic abuse to use deliberate deadly force.

All definitions are from Legal-Dictionary hosted by The Free Dictionary.

The Value is Morality as implied by the resolution.

Regardless of the moral system we employ, it must reject all violence. There are 3 warrants:
First, violence is immoral because it destroys agency. Allen

“Comparitive Philosophy and Religion in Times of Terror.” Douglas Allen, 2006

Gandhi’s approach is…… and work toward reconciliation.

Second, morality is about respecting worth, however if we can say that something that disrespects worth is still moral, morality loses its meaning in informing us what is right and what is wrong.

Third, morality is not black and white. The idea that something is either moral or immoral, and that there is always a moral option that you must take is flawed.

Thus, the necessary and sufficient standard is rejecting all violence. This means the burden on both debaters is to show that their side of the resolution rejects all violence while the other side doesn't.

My thesis and sole contention is that the aff does not reject all violence, but rather deems it morally permissible. Affirming allows the victim to use deadly force, which is defined as

“An amount of………..to another person.”

This is supercharged by the fact that this violence is deliberate, which is defined as

"Willful; purposeful; determined……… to be made.”

Thus, because the aff legitimizes violence, which violates the standard, vote neg.
