AC (Round 6) vs. Loyola BF


Added one contention:

Contention 4: Framework

A) Our framework is that you evaluate the affirmative against a competitive policy option or the status quo.

Prefer our framework because:

1. Plan focus – we allow for a stable locus for links and comparison of alternatives. Their framework makes confusion and judge intervention inevitable.

2. Ground – they access a massive amount of K frameworks, links, and impacts. They can leverage framework to moot the 1AC. We can never predict what we will have to compare the plan to. Even if we get ground, it’s bad and unpredictable.

3. Topic education – their framework encourages generic Ks that get rehashed every year. We change the topic to learn about new things.

Extinction outweighs – no coping mechanisms, no experience, no trial-and-error, future generations
Bostrom 2 (Nick Professor of Philosophy and Global Studies at Yale.. www.transhumanist.com/volume9/risks.html.)JFS

The alternative is dangerous. Abandoning state politics cedes it to the elites – causing war, slavery, and authoritarianism
Boggs 2K (CAROL BOGGS, PF POLITICAL SCIENCE – SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA, 00, THE END OF POLITICS, 250-1

To create real change, the use of political institutions is essential. Though we may have the best intentions to create change and make a difference, there is no way that we can actually do anything without a foot in the political world.
Cook 92 (Anthony, Associate Professor, Georgetown Law, New England LR, Spring, 26 New Eng.L. Rev. 751)

NC


V: Justice

VC: States Rights

Contention 1: State Implementation

The states solve Universal Health Care better - Universal health care implemented at the state level ensures better health policy because it ensures interstate variation is taken into account, and ensures better monitoring and revision of policies.
Briffault 92 [Richard, “Federalism and Health Care Reform: Is Half a Loaf Really Worse than None?” Hastings Constitutional Law Quarterly, Vol 21:611, 1992]

State reform expands access to universal health care by being able to address the needs of specific communities.
Smedley et al 08 ["Report Examines State Health Care Reforms That Target Minorities, Immigrants, Non-English Speaking Residents", Brian Smedley, Ph.D., Beatrice Alvarez, Rea Panares, M.H.S., Cheryl Fish-Parcham, M.S.W., and Sara Adland. The Commonwealth Fund, April 25 2008]

The CP solves for affordability since states can continue existing programs that have already worked, and prevents high pricing due to uncertainty.
Weil 12 [Alan, M.P.P., J.D. “The Value of Federalism in Defining Essential Health Benefits.” New England Journal of Medicine 366;8. February 23, 2012.http://www.nejm.org/doi/pdf/10.1056/NEJMp1200693 AW.]

Contention 2: Federalism

A Subpoint is the Status Quo: States have control of health policy now – the Supreme Court’s ruling sets a strong precedent in favor of federalism.
Davenport 12 [“Federalism’s Silver Lining in the Health Care Decision.” Forbes, 6/29/12.]

B. Link: Federal control of health care, traditionally a state issue, tramples states’ rights and destroys federalism. Health Care is a key issue – federal control here spills over and destroys the very essence of federalism. Federalization of traditional state, such as health care policy, snowballs into greater federal control of policies everywhere
Briffault 92 [Richard, “Federalism and Health Care Reform: Is Half a Loaf Really Worse than None?” Hastings Constitutional Law Quarterly, Vol 21:611, 1992] AT. “Nevertheless, reliance on …worse than none?”

C. Subpoint is the Impact – the impact here is twofold

First - Federalism solves human rights – this is the only way to administer justice
Calabresi 95 (Steven G. Calabresi, professor of law at Northwestern University)

Second - US Federalism prevents tyranny.
Siefel 08 Associate Professor of Law and Political Science at Duke University School of Law (Neil S, “INTERNATIONAL DELEGATIONS AND THE VALUES OF FEDERALISM”, Law and Contemporary Problems, Winter, http://scholarship.law.duke.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=2321&context=faculty_scholarship)

Tyranny must be rejected in EVERY INSTANCE
Petro 74 (Sylvester Petro, Wake Forest Professor, Toledo Law Review, 1974)
