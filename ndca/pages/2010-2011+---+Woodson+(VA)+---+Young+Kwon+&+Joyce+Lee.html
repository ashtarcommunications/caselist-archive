Affirmative


Contention 1: Inherency


Private contractors are the defining feature of the US military presence in Iraq- yet they are allowed to operate in a culture of impunity, engaging in torture, abuse and wonton violence that threatens the entire Iraqi mission

Human Rights First, 6-18-2010
[Human Rights First Written Testimony for The Commission On Wartime Contracting In Iraq And Afghanistan; Hearing On “Are Private Security Contractors Performing Inherently Governmental Functions,” http://www.humanrightsfirst.info/pdf/HRF_Testimony_CWC-06-18-10.pdf, accessed 6-27-10, mss]


PMCs are an inherent part of the USFG’s military presence in Iraq- AND they will be massively increased as general troops withdraw- this amplifies problems with accountability and effectiveness of the US military in Iraq

Davidson, Washington Post staff writer, 6-22-2010
[Joe, "Defining 'inherently governmental' and role of contractors in war," Washington Post, l/n, accessed 6-30-2010, mss]


PLAN: The Supreme Court of the United States should ban the use of privatized military for tactical military operations in Iraq and Afghanistan on the ground that it violates Section 3108 of the Anti-Pinkerton Act.

Advantage 1: Military Professionalism


A. PMC destroys military professionalism. Two internal links –

1. Loss of military functions – Traditional core military roles are taken away and assigned to the contractors.

Kohn, Chief of Air Force History for the USAF, 2009
[Richard, a professor of history at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, “Tarnished Brass: Is the U.S. Military Profession in Decline?” http://www.worldaffairsjournal.org/articles/2009-Spring/full-Kohn.html Accessed Date: 10/6/10] Y2K


2. Corrupting image – PMC severely damages image of armed forces and military leadership.

Schreier and Caprini, a B.A. in international relations from the Graduate Institute of International Studies (HEI) and Senior Fellow at the Geneva Centre for the Democratic Control of Armed Forces (DCAF), March, 2005
[Fred, a M.A.L.D. from the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy, Medford, USA. Marina, a doctoral candidate in the Department of War Studies, King’s College, University of London. “Privatising Security: Law, Practice and Governance of Private Military and Security Companies” GENEVA CENTRE FOR THE DEMOCRATIC CONTROL OF ARMED FORCES (DCAF) http://www.smallarmssurvey.org/files/portal/issueareas/security/security_pdf/2005_Schreier_Caparini.pdf Accessed Date: 10/8/10] Y2K

B. Military professionalism is crucial for objective civilian control and good civil-military relations

Owens, an Adjunct Fellow of the Ashbrook Center, July 2003
[Mackubin Thomas, “Civilian Rumsfeld: Overseeing the Military Editorial” http://www.ashbrook.org/publicat/oped/owens/03/rumsfeld.html Accessed Date: 10/6/2010] Y2K

C. This is modeled worldwide and incorporated by other countries.

Holmes, an adjunct professor at Georgetown University's School of Foreign Service., July, 1997
[H. Allen, a former career diplomat who served as the U.S. ambassador to Portugal, as an assistant secretary of state and as an assistant secretary of defense. “Agency Civil-Military Relations in a Democracy” Electronic Journals of the U.S. Information http://italy.usembassy.gov/pdf/ej/ijde0797.pdf Accessed Date: 10/8/10] Y2K

D. Civilian control is necessary to suppress military institutions. Without control, military can wage violence to sacrifice democratic values.

Kohn, Chief of Air Force History for the USAF, 1997
[Richard, “An Essay on CIVILIAN CONTROL of the MILITARY” University of North Carolina http://www.unc.edu/depts/diplomat/AD_Issues/amdipl_3/kohn.html Accessed Date: 10/6/10] Y2K

E. Democracy solves extinction.

Diamond, Senior Fellow at Hoover Institution, 1995
[Larry “Promoting Democracy in the 1990s”, December, http://www.carnegie.org//sub/pubs/deadly/dia95_01.html]

F. Maintaining good CMR solves your DA by improving effectiveness of military, intelligence, and police forces. The result is stabilized and legitimized government.

Bruneau and Matei, Program Manager for Latin America and a Research Associate at the Center for Civil–Military Relations, December 2008
[Thomas and Florina, Naval Postgraduate School, Monterey, California, “Democratization, Vol.15, No.5”, http://iis-db.stanford.edu/evnts/5541/Bruneau_final_file.pdf Accessed Date: 10/12/10] Y2K

Advantage 2: Iraqi Stability


PMCs destroy stability in Iraq- 3 reasons


1. Moderates- US PMCs reckless violence drives the insurgency and collapses the mission. Prefer our evidence- it’s from a former PMC commander who is blowing the whistle

Geddes, SAS veteran and British PMC commander, 3-27-2006
[John, "Never mind the rebels - the most dangerous gunmen in Iraq are American mercenaries," Daily Mail, Abridged extract from Highway To Hell by John Geddes, l/n, accessed 6-27-2010, mss]

PMCs excessive violence collapse moderate Iraq support for the US, fuels the insurgency and guarantees backlash and failure

Ross, 9-27-2007
[Brian, "Report: Private Military Contractors Hurt War Effort," ABC News, http://blogs.abcnews.com/theblotter/2007/09/report-private-.html, accessed 6-28-10, mss]

AND- The political strategy is key, not the military strategy- moderates are key to marginalize the insurgency

Rodman, Assistant Secretary of Defense, 3-14-2005
[Peter, Federal News Service, "PANEL ONE OF A HEARING OF THE NATIONAL SECURITY, EMERGING THREATS, AND INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS SUBCOMMITTEE OF THE HOUSE COMMITTEE ON GOVERNMENT REFORM," l/n, accessed 6-30-10, mss]

2. Civilians - PMCs undermine distinctions between combatants and civilians- jeopardizing the role other civilians play

Human Rights First, 6-18-2010
[Human Rights First Written Testimony for The Commission On Wartime Contracting In Iraq And Afghanistan; Hearing On “Are Private Security Contractors Performing Inherently Governmental Functions,” http://www.humanrightsfirst.info/pdf/HRF_Testimony_CWC-06-18-10.pdf, accessed 6-27-10, mss]

AND- Civilians workers are key to stability- they play vital roles

Clinton, Secretary of State, 4-30-2009
[Hillary, Targeted News Service, "Clinton Praises Partnership Between State, Defense," l/n, accessed 6-30-2010, mss]

﻿

Empowering moderates solves their alt causes- ensures Iraqi unity

State Department, 1-6-2007
[U.S. State Department Documents and Publications, "Rice Announces Israeli, Palestinian Talks on Mideast Road Map," l/n, accessed 6-30-10, mss]

3. Self-interest- PMCs presence makes stability impossible- they have a vested interest in prolonging and exacerbating conflict for profit

Salzman, J.D. New York University School of Law, Spring 2008
[Zoe, New York University Journal of International Law and Politics, "Private Military Contractors And The Taint Of A Mercenary Reputation," 40 N.Y.U. J. Int'l L. & Pol. 853, l/n, accessed 6-28-10, mss]

AND- Iraq is on the brink of instability- collapse spills-over to the Middle East and South Asia and dooms counter-terrorism

UPI, 6-25-2010
[United Press International, "General: Middle East goes as Iraq goes," http://www.upi.com/Top_News/Special/2010/06/25/General-Middle-East-goes-as-Iraq-goes/UPI-24361277491860/, accessed 6-28-2010, mss]

South Asian conflict is the most likely scenario for nuclear war

Fai, Kashmiri-American Council Executive Director, 2001
[Ghulam Nabi, “The most dangerous place,” July 8, Washington Times, LN]

AND- Iraq instability escalates quickly- draws in Russia and China into nuclear World War III

Corsi, Ph.D from Harvard in political science, 1-8-2007
[Jerome, "War with Iran is imminent," http://www.worldnetdaily.com/news/article.asp?ARTICLE_ID=53669﻿

Contention 3: Solvency


The plan solves the worst aspects of PMCs without removing their necessary functions- Section 3108 ruling by the courts against PMCs labels them as mercenaries- robust political debate after the plan ensures accountability and oversight, checking anti-democratic power

Press, J.D., Northwestern University School of Law, September 2008
[Joshua, Northwestern University Law Review Colloquy, "Article: Crying Havoc Over The Outsourcing Of Soldiers And Democracy's Slipping Grip On The Dogs Of War," 103 Nw. U. L. Rev. Colloquy 109, l/n, accessed 6-28-10, mss]

﻿

Labeling PMCs as mercenaries solves- tarnishes their reputation, keeping them in the public eye and establishing accountability

Salzman, J.D. New York University School of Law, Spring 2008
[Zoe, New York University Journal of International Law and Politics, "Private Military Contractors And The Taint Of A Mercenary Reputation," 40 N.Y.U. J. Int'l L. & Pol. 853, l/n, accessed 6-28-10, mss]

﻿

Even if they win a no solvency argument, the plan opens the door to future regulation that solves

Salzman, J.D. New York University School of Law, Spring 2008
[Zoe, New York University Journal of International Law and Politics, "Private Military Contractors And The Taint Of A Mercenary Reputation," 40 N.Y.U. J. Int'l L. & Pol. 853, l/n, accessed 6-28-10, mss]

There is a legal precedent – Court’s narrow ruling of Section 3108 in Weinberger v. Equifax can be applied to ban PMCs.

Press, J.D., Northwestern University School of Law, September 2008
[Joshua, Northwestern University Law Review Colloquy, "Article: Crying Havoc Over The Outsourcing Of Soldiers And Democracy's Slipping Grip On The Dogs Of War," 103 Nw. U. L. Rev. Colloquy 109, Accessed Date: 10/19/10] Y2K
