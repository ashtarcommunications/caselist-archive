AFFIRMATIVE

contention one is the paper tiger

the global war on terror is only the united states latest ruse to justify the expansion of its empire far from creating order and security this agenda promise the violent subjugation of the middle east to americas imperaial prefroagtive.

achin vanaik professor of international relations and global politics ant delhi university "selling us wars" pp. 13- 14 2007

similarly us presence in east asia is an attempt at establishing a foothold in the region in order to shore up competitiveness with china neither south kores nor japan benefit from us presence in fact in korea especially it raises the likelihood of war because the us seems prepared to take an active military stance in the region

workers world (a socialist party that fights on all issues that face the working class and oppressed peoples. behind washington's new military strategy in Korea http://www.workers.org/2006/world/korea-0119/) 2006

and so mcuh for the audacity of hope - obamas foreign policy is not significantly different from bush's both believe in the role of america to violently police international relations and impose its imperial agenda on the world

workers canguard november 2008 (no. 925 " obama: commander-in-chief of racist u.s. imperalism" http://www.class-struggle.com/2008/12/obama-commander-in-chief-of-racist-us.html )


finally dont believe the negs lies not only is US hegemony unsustainable but it requires constant wars to maintain the illusion that capitalism and imperalism are peaceful

chalmers johnson (is an american author and professor of the university of california, sandiego he served in the korean war, was a consultant for the cia from 1967-1973 and led the center of chinease studies at the iniversity of california, berkeley for years he is also president and co-founder of the japan policy research institute, 3 good reasons to liquidate our empire, motherjones.com http://motherjones.com/politics/2009/07/three-good-reasons-liquidate-our-empre?page=1) july 30, 2009

contention2 is imperial encounters:

the logic of imperalism relies upon biopolitical control as a tool of sovereign power to maintain global order. this order wields exclusive authority over life and death which leads to inevitable nuclear annihilation and genocide

Hardt and Negri (hardt is an american literary theorist and political philosopher perhaps best know for empire written with antonia negri and published in 2000. negri is an italian marxist sociologist and political philosopher best known for his co-authorship of empire and secondairly for his work on spinoza, negri became a political philosophy professor in his hometown university negri founded the potere operaio (worker power) group in 1969 and was a leading member of autonomia operla multitude war and democracy in the age of empire) 2004


there is no hope of winning a disad agaisnt our case because we control all terminal impact uniqueness imperalism sows the seeds of its own destruction other nations will inevitably challange us power unending war is the inevitable result the greatest hope is the rising tide of revolt from below

foster co-editor of monthly review, professor of sociology at the university of oregon, 2K3 [john, "the new age of imperalism" monthly review 55.3]

the neoliberal rationality of imperalism culminates in collective suicide as more and more if the population is designated disposable for the sake of the very narrowly defined "greater good"

santos 3 (boaventura de sousa leading portugese social theorist and the director of the center for social studies at the university of coimbra bad subjects issue 63 "collective suicide?", april 2001 http://bad.eserver.corg/issues/2003/63/santos.html)



the function of us policy presence is exclusively to smooth out the contradictions in the imperialist system whole populations are sacraficed to ensure the smooth flow of capital it is imperative that the system constantly challenged from below

antonia negri, philosopher, insurgent, political prisoner, 2008 "empire and war" by empire and beyond


Therefor, we demand that the united states federal government withdraw its military and police presence in all areas designated by the resolution

contention three is insurgency

only the demand for a complete withdrawal is an appropriate response to genocidal us foreign policy. any plea for a kinder more just imperalism plays into the hand of American exceptionalism and legitimizes violent us hegemony

mike ely, founder kosama project, demand complete and immediate withdrawal from afghanisatan 2002 http://recolutionaryfrontlines.wordpress.com/2010/03/26/demand-complete-and-immediate-withdrawal-from-afghanistan/

similary we must demand the unconditional withdrawal of us troops in korea and japan us imperalism has dominated east asia since the end of wwII annihilating hundresds of thousands of japanese lives, and millions of korean lives

international communist league ("defend north kores agaisnt u.s. imperalism!" workers vanguard no. 960 http://www.icl-florg/english/ww/960/northkores.html) june 4, 2010

the affirmative is an instance of militant resistance we identify with anti- imperial insurgency the 1ac is propaganda intended to express the resistance to us imperalism and to mobilize others agaisnt it as well only this kind of militancy can create the counterpower that is capable of challenging the imperial ambitions of the us

hardt and negri, insurgetns and social theorist in '2K [michael, and antonio, empire pg.411-413



endorse the plan as flashpoint to ignite a global anti-imperalist movement coalescing around the demand for troop withdrawal this movement unites insurgents and resistance forces globally under the banner of challenging imperalism

steve sherman "the empire of bases and the american anti-war movement" march 10th 2010 http://dissidentvoice.org/2010/03/the-empire-of-bases-and-the-american-anti-war-movement/

Moreover, apocalyptic discourse is not directed at preserving life but instead preserving the dominant position of government officials and an insecure middle class. Empirically the interests of minority women have been subordinated to some national security threat or another
Smith ‘7 (Anna Marie, Prof. Gov’t @ Cornell U, “Welfare Reform and Sexual Regulation,” Cambridge University Press, p. 15) vp
