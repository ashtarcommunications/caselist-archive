Nietzsche's Metaphysics AC (TOC Prelims and Doubles vs Evanston EB)


Theory must be clarified in cx.
RVI goes aff.
Obligation is aff ground.
CPs don't negate.

Next, meta-ethics. The metaphysical view is dumb. We need to critically reassess morality based on an ontologically natural form of existence. Miyasaki****[1]****:

Indeed, not interpreting the world from a radical ontology of sense experience is self-negating. Miyasaki 2:

And, any transcendental metaphysical truth forgoes contingent historical and phenomenological facts about a person, creating moral nihilism. Warren****[1]****:

Thus, since metaphysical concepts are indemonstrable and prone to nihilism, morality has to be situated in an agent’s natural experience of the world.

Next, ethics. First, the will to power solves back nihilism by framing morality in terms of our essential practices in life and critically reassessing traditional philosophical problems. Warren 2:

And, the will to power is an essential element of our desire to experience unified agency. We unify our natural experience by exercising the power of our volition. Warren 3:

Indeed, the physical nature of the world is overcoming. Time passes, plants grow, and the cosmos change, an ongoing process of the will to power. Soner****[1]****:

Thus, the world is always becoming. Attempting to deny becoming is circular, because that would assert an already static truth which relies upon the assumption that things do not become.

And, traditional conceptions of ontology and agency are meaningless; there is no definite ‘being,’ just a constant state of becoming. Our ontology is overcoming. Soner 2:

And, constant becoming destroys our knowledge of the world and ourselves – epistemically, we can only know our own constant struggle to overcome. Knowing is itself an act of overcoming. Soner 3:

Finally, if we don’t embrace the will to power, we’re confined to unending pain. The good needs to be situated in overcoming because ends are inescapably fleeting. Katsafanas****[1]****:

Finally, any ethical assertion collapses into the will to power – even denying it. Aydin****[1]****:

Killing is consistent.

2. Dependency. Downs**[1]**:

Fear AC

Skepticism affirms. Naturalism is the only way to prevent skepticism.
A. Naturalism solves infinite regress of moral justification. Richards:
B. Ethical judgments are inextricably tied with evolutionary forces. To not accept an ethic that advanced our interests would mean that ethic wouldn’t survive evolutionary processes and be exterminated. Street:
C. Since morality is a guide for action it has to motivate action or else it would be worthless. If people contradict moral principles out of fear or evolutionary instincts, then morality makes no sense. Katsafanas:
D. There is no foundation for morality because it is a biological illusion. Thus, either there is no morality, or we embrace our evolutionary instincts. Ruse:

Epistemology comes first.
A. Naturalism is only way to avoid epistemic impossibility. If morality isn’t founded in the natural world, then it can’t influence the natural world. Papineau:
B. Mental states are only influenced by physical forces. Nothing is known a priori because nothing can be completely a priori. The only way we can account for how we behave and what we know is through a naturalist perspective. Papineau 2:
C. The only reason we believe things to be true is because they are burned into our psychology by past generations. Morality cannot reflect absolute truth, but rather, evolutionary success. Ruse 2:

Thus, the standard is maximizing potential life forms.

The contention is that fear is an evolutionary good which renders the use of deadly force permissible.
A. Fear is an evolutionary good and a biological requirement.
1. The fear module of the brain is structurally distinct and independent from the seat of rational decision making. For 4 reasons we cannot rationally decide to ignore our fear. Ohman:
2. Fear-governed behavior is central to our evolutionary development. Ohman 2:
B. Fear is the causation behind the use of deadly force.
1. The fear experience by victims of domestic abuse is psychologically debilitating and impairs rational judgment. Swinford:
2. The fear module of the brain is selectively sensitive to repeated forms of violence. Ohman 3:
3. Only by killing the abuser can the victim escape their fear. Swinford 2:

Pacifism NC

Epistemology comes first.
A. Pacifism is the only way to accurately understand our metaphysical position in relation to moral truth. Violence excludes the experience of others and closes our mind to the world. Grimsrud:
B. We can never verify the intentions of others and humans are incapable of knowing the moral truth of a situation. Therefore, we may never purport to know when harming someone is a morally good action, so harm is impermissible. Moellendorf:
C. Willing harm subjectively contradicts our objective notions of the good. Nagel:

You negate because A. the actor in the resolution is using deadly force which is an action which at least risks causing harm, and B. the act is deliberate, which means that an actor intends to harm somebody.

Thomas Nagel, The View From Nowhere, Oxford University Press, 1986.
Darryl Moellendorf, Cosmopolitan Justice, Westview Press, 2002.
