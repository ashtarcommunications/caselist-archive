Affirmative

Plan: The United States Federal Government should withdraw all of the United States Federal Government’s military and police presence from Iraq


Contention 1: Inherency
Troops will remain long after withdrawal. There will be 58 permanent bases, the same model used by the British Empire to colonize Iraq.
Margolis 09 (Eric Margolis, Journalist, March 2 2009, Toronto Sun, WK)

There is empirical evidence that indicates that there will be a permanent military occupation of Iraq.
Hassan 08 (Salah Hassan, “Never-Ending Occupations” CR: The New Centennial Review, Volume 8, Number 1, Spring 2008)

Advantage 1: Security

After 9/11, the United States began to spread democracy through violence and terror. This project of colonization is inextricable from the continuation of the war – absent withdrawal, the initial legacy will continue.
Kramer and Michalowski 05 (Ronal C. Kramer, professor university of western Michigan, and Raymond J. Michalowski, professor northern Arizona university. “War, Aggression and State Crime” April 05, Centre for Crime and Justice Studies)

The continued framing of Iraq as a security threat creates a nexus through which it is impossible to approach it as anything other than an object in need of development and westernization.
Bilgin, 2008. Pinar Bilgin (Department of International Relations, Bilkent University), Thinking Past Western IR? Third World Quarterly. http://dx.doi.org/10.1080/01436590701726392

This otherization of external threats provides the State with a mandate to violently eradicate any and all opposition.
Pinar Bilgin, @ Bilkent Univ, ‘4 [International Relations 18.1, “Whose ‘Middle East’? Geopolitical Inventions and Practices of Security,” p. 28]

Through this break with the security discourse surrounding Iraq, the affirmative internally deconstructs contemporary Imperialism.
Welch, 2008 (Michael Welch, September 26, 2008. Ordering Iraq: Reﬂections on Power, Discourse, & Neocolonialism)


Advantage 2: Orientalism

The stereotypical perception of Iraq and the Middle East as unstable and inferior served as the justification for the Iraq War.
Spencer 6 (Robert, NYT Bestselling author on Islam, http://media.web.britannica.com/ebsco/pdf/23/23648989.pdf, date accessed: 7/8/2010) AJK

American Orientalist views ensure colonialism of Iraq through systemic military presence necessary to promote “assimilation” to a “civilized culture”.
Said 78 (Edward, “Orientalism”, professor of comparative literature at Columbia, p 120, date accessed: 7/7/2010) AJK

Orientalist policy is inherently dehumanizing
Said 3 (Edward, Prof. of English/Comparative Lit., Columbia U., “Preface.” Orientalism. p. xxvii)
AND dehumanization externalizes itself into violence.
Hooks and Mosher 05 [Gregory and Clayton, June 05, Washington University a Soros Senior Justice Fellow Professor & Chair Ph.D., Wisconsin, 1985, Associate Professor of Liberal Arts and Sociology @ WSU, Outrages Against Personal Dignity: Rationalizing Abuse and Torture in the War on Terror, 01.25.2011, (http://web.ebscohost.com/ehost/pdfviewer/pdfviewer?hid=21&sid=5a7c18bf-f04c-4188-8c36-d525299fddce%40sessionmgr13&vid=9)gdi-tjc]

Contention 2: Solvency

The Current view of international security is based on civilized/uncivilized binaries. We assume Iraq is a breeding ground for conflict. The affirmative resists colonialism by examining the historical contexts of US occupation.Tarak Barkawi, Prof. in Interenational Security @ Univ. of Cambridge, Mark Laffey, Prof. in International Politics @ Univ. of London, ‘6 [
Review of International Studies 32, “The postcolonial moment in security studies,” p. 344-6]

False Neoconservative Orientalist depictions of the Middle East were justification for invading Iraq. Lack of thorough examination of political intentions recreates colonization of the Middle East.
El-Affendi 03 [Abdelwahab, University of Westminster, October 20, Daily Star ( Lebanon ), Orientalism is alive and well ­ in Iraq, (http://www.campus-watch.org/article/id/830)gdi-tjc]


Comes First. Questioning current problem/solution dichotomies is necessary to break down colonialism
Diana Brydon, University of Western Ontario, ‘6 [Postcolonial Test 2.1, “Is There a Politics of Postcoloniality?” http://journals.sfu.ca/pocol/index.php/pct/article/viewArticle/508/175]
