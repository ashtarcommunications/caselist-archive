CAPITALISM AFF


The lack of animal rights is intricately tied with the corporate power structure – animal exploitation is tightly linked to global corporate governance.
Boggs ’07 (Carl, Prof of Social Science @ National University, Adjunct Professor @ Antioch University, on the editorial board of numerous journals, recipient of the Charles McCoy Career Achievement Award from the American Political Science Association, Corporate Power, Ecological Crisis, and Animal Rights, Fast Capitalism 2.2, http://www.uta.edu/huma/agger/fastcapitalism/2_2/boggs.html, 2007, AC)

The struggle for...the modern crisis.

The very logic of capitalism requires exploitation and destruction of the environment.
Boggs 2

Spurred by unfettered...toward population reduction.


The exploitation of non-human animals is an inherent part of the corporate power structure – it’s the root cause of speciesism. Furthermore corporate power structures are the root cause of all other impacts – I control the internal links.
Boggs 3

Such far-reaching critiques...fast food outlets.


Capitalism requires the exploitation of animals – without animal rights and non-exploitation, it will continue to flourish.

Best ’10 (Stephen, Ph.D, Associate Prof of Phil @ University of Texas, El Paso, Cofounder of the Institute for Critical Animal Studies, Dr. Steven Best on “The Left, Capitalism, and Animal Rights,” http://portland.indymedia.org/en/2010/08/401411.shtml, 2010, AC)

It’s try or die right now – without a complete break with current, corporate based politics, the environment and the planet will die – even minimal gains are enough to begin to resist the corporate structure.

The role of the ballot is to use the ballot as a tool to reject capitalism – you as a judge have an ethico-political obligation to reject capitalism and the suffering and oppression of fellow beings that it causes. You use the ballot as a tool to recognize the plight of those oppressed by the capitalist structure – that’s your first obligation.

Agribusiness and capitalism causes global warming and will destroy the environment – it’s happening right now.

Agribusiness and destruction of the environment causes resource wars – it’s the most likely internal link to extinction.
Klare ’06 (Michael, Ph.D, Professor of Peace and World Security Studies @ Hampshire Univ, The Coming Resource Wars, Alternet, http://www.alternet.org/story/33243/the_coming_resource_wars, 2006, AC)

It's official: the...global climate change.


The alt text is: embrace animal rights to prevent agribusiness from flourishing and to jam the corporate machine –this entails is a recognization of animal rights. This is not a retreat from politics – it doesn’t acknowledge the inviolability of animals at every single juncture, it just means we shouldn’t exploit, torture and kill them. And the alt solves – the capitalist machine is both dependent on our willingness to serve it and its exploitation of the natural environment. Animals rights and a refusal to go along with capitalism is the key to breaking it down.

ANIMAL RIGHTS K


Any attempt to grant animals rights will only reinforce the oppressive aspect of humanity – rights discourse leaves the human-animal binary and traditional power structures in place, fails to question the historical hierarchies necessary in the first place, and does not address inequalities in other areas, like resource distribution.
Oliver ’08 (Kelly, W. Alton Jones Prof/Chair of Philosophy with appointments in African-American and Diaspora studies, Film Studies, and Women’s and Gender studies @ Vanderbuilt University, What is Wrong With (Animal) Rights?, J. Of Speculative Philosophy 22:3, 2008, Project Muse, AC)

If we have...remain in place.


Rights as entitlements require calculation between human and animal rights and reinforce the binary of animal and human because they are contingent on the subject they are given to.
Oliver ’10 (Kelly, W. Alton Jones Prof/Chair of Philosophy with appointments in African-American and Diaspora studies, Film Studies, and Women’s and Gender studies @ Vanderbuilt University, Animal Ethics: Towards an Ethics of Responsivness, Research in Phenomenology Vo. 40, 2010, 267-280, AC)



In other words,...(like human rights) are based.


The root cause of all violence against humans and animals is the man/animal binary – it justifies treating others as animals, not worthy of human respect. Furthermore, this binary must be resolved before anything else – unless we drastically change our relationship with animals, we will continue to exploit, oppress, and commit violence against others.
Oliver ’10 (Kelly, W. Alton Jones Prof/Chair of Philosophy with appointments in African-American and Diaspora studies, Film Studies, and Women’s and Gender studies @ Vanderbuilt University, Animal Ethics: Towards an Ethics of Responsivness, Research in Phenomenology Vo. 40, 2010, 267-280, AC)

In the face...people like animals.


The alternative is to embrace an ethic of responsiveness – this means that we acknowledge that we are dependent on animals for our human status, yet we should not exploit that status.

Oliver ’10 (Kelly, W. Alton Jones Prof/Chair of Philosophy with appointments in African-American and Diaspora studies, Film Studies, and Women’s and Gender studies @ Vanderbuilt University, Animal Ethics: Towards an Ethics of Responsivness, Research in Phenomenology Vo. 40, 2010, 267-280, AC)

In this era...this collective planet.

The alt solves – recognizing that both animals and humans can suffer and respond allows us to begin to break down the man/animal binary.
Oliver ’08 (Kelly, W. Alton Jones Prof/Chair of Philosophy with appointments in African-American and Diaspora studies, Film Studies, and Women’s and Gender studies @ Vanderbuilt University, What is Wrong With (Animal) Rights?, J. Of Speculative Philosophy 22:3, 2008, Project Muse, AC)
