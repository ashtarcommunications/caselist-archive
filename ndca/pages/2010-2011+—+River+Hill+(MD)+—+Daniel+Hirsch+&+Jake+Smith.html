South Korea Aff-Big Bronx


The United States federal government should implement a phased withdrawal of its ground troops in the Republic of Korea

The sinking of the South Korea’s ship makes conflict inevitable – retaliation will spark an escalatory war and failure to respond will only cause more North Korean provocations.
Bandow, 10 – senior fellow at the Cato Institute and former special assistant to Reagan (4/18/10, Doug, “Let the Koreans Take Care of the Koreas,” http://www.huffingtonpost.com/doug-bandow/let-the-koreans-take-care_b_542141.html, JMP)

The U.S. response to bolster deterrence will just increase provocations and make miscalculation more likely

On the…danger point


Withdrawing ground troops solves – stops North Korea from probing U.S. weakness to draw our forces into a wider conflict. Air and naval installations will maintain power projection capabilities.
Stanton, 10 – U.S. Army Judge Advocate in Korea from 98-02 and practicing attorney in Washington, D.C. (4/12/10, Joshua, The New Ledger, “It's Time for the U.S. Army to Leave Korea,” http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2010/04/11/opinion/main6386737.shtml, JMP)

“First Among Equals,” http://www.nationalinterest.org/Article.aspx?id=20570, JMP)

Withdrawal solves accidental war with China, stops friction with Japan and yields a nuclear free peninsula

Possible Futures of a Confrontation between China, Taiwan and the United States of America”, http://www.lamp-method.org/eCommons/Hunkovic.pdf
