Contention 4 - Real Talk

Okay, shenanigans aside, the 1AC is a speech act that functions as a means of exposing the ways in which transportation policies in the United States have been historically associated with perpetuation of systemic exclusion and displacement, contributing the construction of “racial spaces” where the black body is swept under the rug in favor of mobilizing Whiteness. Though slavery may have ended in name, its operational logic is still manifested within the status quo and permeates virtually every facet of our lives—socially, economically, or discursively. Rejecting—or worse, ignoring—the Ontology of Blackness justifies genocide, social exclusion, and structural violence

By ironically affirming the resolution, Sophie and I utilize the 1AC as a means of opening debate as a space to challenge status quo institutions and their foundations. It’s in the fact that when you heard the plan text, you were initially shocked at the notion of the USfg building highways over black people—when in actuality it’s been going on for centuries, albeit not in so literal a sense—that we expose the ideological flaws contingent with the state, civil society, and how we engage them respectively.

The affirmative advocacy is to engage in an unflinching structural analysis of the ontological position of Blackness—the very possibility of ethics and freedom resides in a rejection of the state and civil society. Resisting the lure of anti-blackness through a genealogy of history’s constitutive void is the starting point for imagining a new world. Burn America down.
