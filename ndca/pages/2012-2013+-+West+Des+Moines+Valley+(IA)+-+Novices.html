West Des Moines Valley Debate

Novice – High Speed Rail 1AC

Contention 1 is Inherency

The United States Federal Government is not currently funding high speed rail projects. Nussbaum, Paul, 7/12/12, “High speed rail executives from around the world are meeting in Philadelphia this week,” Philadelphia Inquirer, http://articles.philly.com/2012-07-12/news/32633398_1_high-speed-rail-high-speed-rail-high-speed-trains

Thus the plan: The USFG should substantially increase its investment in transportation infrastructure by constructing and/or upgrading track to accommodate a high-speed rail system connecting major population centers in the United States.

Contention 2 is Advantages:

The First Advantage is Clean Energy

High speed rail can be powered by electricity rather than fossil fuels. Tutton, ‘11. Mark Tutton, “How Green is High-Speed Rail,” CNN, November 19, 2011. http://www.cnn.com/2011/11/18/world/how-green-is-hsr/index.html

Mark Tutton is a technology columnist for CNN.

And this results in decreased U.S. dependence on fossil fuels. Tutton, ‘11. Mark Tutton, “How Green is High-Speed Rail,” CNN, November 19, 2011. http://www.cnn.com/2011/11/18/world/how-green-is-hsr/index.html

And This Has Two Primary Impacts

A. It allows for the sort of urgent action that is required of us as we reach a tipping point on climate change. Romm, March 30, 2012. Joe Romm, “Civilization is at risk absent ‘urgent and large-scale action’ warns planetary summit,” Think Progress, March 30, 2012. http://thinkprogress.org/romm/2012/03/30/455377/civilization-at-risk-absent-urgent-and-large-scale-action/

AND Reducing Auto Traffic is Critical to Slowing Global Warming. West ‘12. (Larry, 20-year professional writer and editor who has written many articles about environmental issues for leading newspapers, magazines and online publications, “U.S. Autos Account for Half of Global Warming Linked to Cars Worldwide,” http://environment.about.com/od/globalwarming/a/autoemissions.htm)

AND HSR would help renewable energy “go viral” nationwide. Longshore ‘10. (Samantha Longshore, Certification Analyst at Transwestern, " Putting the Brakes on High-Speed Rail" 2010, http://www.worldgreen.org/home/wg-feature-articles/4601-putting-the-brakes-on-high-speed-rail.html)


AND US Investment in green transportation also makes an international climate agreement more likely. Burwell, ’10, David, “Transportation: The Leading Cause of Global Warming,” The Carnegie Endowment, April 15, 2010, http://carnegieendowment.org/2010/04/15/transportation-leading-cause-of-global-warming/2fr2 David Burwell is director of the Energy and Climate Program at the Carnegie Endowment. His work at Carnegie focuses on the intersection between energy, transportation and climate issues, and policies and practice reforms to reduce global dependence on fossil fuels.


AND Unless we reduce emissions, extinction is inevitable. Mazo ‘10. PhD in Paleoclimatology from UCLA. Jeffrey Mazo, Managing Editor, Survival and Research Fellow for Environmental Security and Science Policy at the International Institute for Strategic Studies in London, 3-2010, “Climate Conflict: How global warming threatens security and what to do about it,” pg. 122

AND This isn’t just about warming, humans need oxygen to live. Tatchell ‘08. (Quoting Professors, Peter, 13 August 2008, “The oxygen crisis”, http://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/2008/aug/13/carbonemissions.climatechange)

B. The Second Impact of Reduced Dependence on Fossil Fuels Is Increased National Security & Decreased Funding for Terrorism. Brown, et al. ‘11. Emily Brown, Brian Asingia, and Maddie Press, “The Governance of Technology: Energy”, Fall 2010. http://sites.lafayette.edu/egrs251-fa11-energy/contexts/historical/. Brown, Press and Asingia make up the Energy Project Team at Lafayette College.

AND Terrorism IS the Most Significant Threat to US National Security. Kouri ‘11. Jim Kouri, “Lone Wolf Terrorist Threat Increasing, Say Experts,” Accuracy in Media, June 24, 2011. http://www.aim.org/guest-column/lone-wolf-terrorist-threat-increasing-say-security-experts/. Jim Kouri is currently fifth vice-president of the National Association of Chiefs of Police and he's a staff writer for the New Media Alliance (thenma.org).

AND Terrorists will not hesitate to use nuclear weapons against the US. Doyle ‘09. James E., Nuclear Nonprolif Division at Los Alamos for over a decade, worked on nuclear projects with Russian nuclear weapons institute, senior policy analyst at Science Applications International Corporation, PhD in Int’l Security Studies from U Virginia, “Eyes on the Prize: A Strategy for Enhancing Global Security,” in “Abolishing Nuclear Weapons: A Debate,” Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, http://www.carnegieendowment.org/files/abolishing_nuclear_weapons_debate.pdf Herm

AND this snowballs into a global nuclear exchange. Ayson, ’10. Robert Ayson, Professor of Strategic Studies and Director of the Centre for Strategic Studies: New Zealand at the Victoria University of Wellington, 2010 (“After a Terrorist Nuclear Attack: Envisaging Catalytic Effects,” Studies in Conflict & Terrorism, Volume 33, Issue 7, July, Available Online to Subscribing Institutions via InformaWorld)

AND a Great Power nuclear exchange equals extinction. Bostrom ‘02. (Nick, Professor, Faculty of Philosophy at Oxford University, March, Journal of Evolution and Technology, Vol. 9, No. 1, http://www.nickbostrom.com/existential/risks.html, 7/8/12 MDRJ)

AND, Independently, the Plan also helps protect against a Bioterror attack. Warrick and Kornblut, Joby and Ann, ’10, “U.S. is unprepared for major bioterrorism attack, commission finds,” Washington Post, January 27, 2010, http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/01/26/AR2010012601265_pf.html


AND Bioterror is the most likely extinction scenario. Matheny, ’07, Jason, Department of Health Policy and Management, Bloomberg School of Public Health, Johns Hopkins University “Reducing the Risk of Human Extinction,” Risk Analysis, 2007. http://www.upmc-biosecurity.org/website/resources/publications/2007/2007-10-15-reducingrisk.html

The Second Advantage of High-Speed Rail is Economics

High-speed rail projects are the best way to spur economic growth and federal involvement is key. Transit News ‘11. Transit News, “Federal Investment in High Speed Rail could Spur 1.3 million jobs,” American Public Transportation System, April 6, 2011. http://www.apta.com/mediacenter/pressreleases/2011/Pages/110406_HSR_Business.aspx

AND Infrastructure Spending is Uniquely Key to US Economic Recovery Due to Multiplier Effect. Copeland, et. al ‘11. Claudia Copeland, Linda Levine and William Mallett, “The Role of Public Works Infrastructure in Economic Recovery,” Congressional Research Service, September 21, 2011. http://www.fas.org/sgp/crs/misc/R42018.pdf. Claudia Copeland is a Specialist in Resources and Environmental Policy, Linda Levine is a Specialist in Labor Economics and William Mallett is a Specialist in Transportation Policy.

AND We Also Help Grow the Economy By Reducing Otherwise Inevitable Congestion. Staley ‘07. (Sam, economic development policy analyst for the New York Times, November 25, 2007, “A Congested Economy”, http://www.nytimes.com/2007/11/25/opinion/nyregionopinions/25CIstaley.html?pagewanted=all)

AND U.S. Economic collapse causes extinction. Kerpen ‘08. Phil, National Review Online, October 29, Don't Turn Panic Into Depression, http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2008/10/29/opinion/main4555821.shtml


AND U.S. Market growth key to preventing multiple extinction scenarios. Reisman, ‘08. George Reisman, Pepperdine Univ. Prof. Emeritus of Economics, 8. “A Word To Environmentalists,” February 19, http://archive.mises.org/7800/a-word-to-environmentalists/, ACC. 6-26-12, JT



Contention 3 is Solvency



Funding high-speed rail links directly to a reduction in oil consumption and CO2 emissions. Schwartz, ‘08. Sam Schwartz, President and CEO, Sam Schwartz Engineering (SSE), a multi-disciplinary consulting firm specializing in traffic and transportation engineering, 2008, “Breaking the Logjam: Environmental Reform for the New Congress and Administration: Panel V: Urban Issues: A Comprehensive Transportation Policy for the 21st Century: A Case Study of Congestion Pricing in New York City,” New York University Environmental Law Journal, 2008, 17 N.Y.U. Envtl. L.J. 580


AND HSR Infrastructure is the most essential element to reducing emissions. Prum, ‘12. Darren A. Prum, director at the center for urban infrastructure, 2012, “High Speed Rail in America: An Evaluation of the Regulatory, Real Property, and Environmental Obstacles a Project will Encounter,“ North Carolina Journal of Law & Technology



AND Federal investment in HSR will lead to long term economic growth in all sectors. Williams ‘11. (Mantil, American Public Transportation Association's Dir Advocacy Communications, April 6, 2011, “Federal Investment in High-Speed Rail Could Spur 1.3 Million Jobs”, http://www.apta.com/mediacenter/pressreleases/2011/Pages/110406_HSR_Business.aspx)
