I negate.
Observations:
  1. The United States refers to all three branches of the U.S. federal government working in unison.
  2. Ought indicates a normative obligation; thus, the resolution asks a normative policy question.
  3. The resolution is truth-testing, which means you affirm by proving the resolution true and negate if I prove any part of the resolution untrue.
  4. A non-citizen is a person neither born in nor naturalized to the United States. Non-citizens cannot vote, do not pay taxes, and are not members of the body politic.

The value is Justice. Justice is defined as giving each their due.

[1] Hobbes explains, in the state of nature

every man is Enemy...danger of violent death.

Hobbes 2 explains,

a man be willing...men in the condition of Warre.



My sole contention is that the United States has no obligation to offer due process protections to non-citizens accused of terrorism because they were never parties to the covenant.

Non-citizens are not parties to the social contract. They never renounced their right in the state of nature to dominate others nor submitted to be governed by the United States. As a result they have no claim against the United States for due process protections.

You negate because there is no obligation to extend due process protections to non-citizens.

[1] Hobbes, Thomas. Leviathan- Revised Edition, eds. A.P. Martinich and Brian Battiste. Peterborough, ON: Broadview Press, 2010.
