Self-Sacrifice NC


Value: morality

First, the purpose of morality is self-preservation. Moral norms evolve out of a need to enable species continuation. Paradigmatic examples observable in mammals include incest taboos and systems of reciprocity which evolve to ensure species survival. Simply, it is wrong to kill because it negates the other’s ability to survive. Even if theorizing fills in further details, justifying acts contrary to self-preservation justifies nihilism because it negates the purpose of morality. Thus, self-preservation is metaethically the central feature of moral statecraft.
