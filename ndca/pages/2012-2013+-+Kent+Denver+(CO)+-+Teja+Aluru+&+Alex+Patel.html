Advantage Two is Mexico

The Panama Canal expansion will dramatically shift global trade routes through North America - Obama’s new port dredging program is already adjusting ports for the new traffic and should’ve triggered your generic links, but rail expansion is the missing piece in accommodating for the new shipments
Schwartz 8/21 [John Schwartz, National Legal Correspondent for the New York Times. “U.S. Ports Expand, With an Eye on Panama.” The New York Times. August 21, 2012. Lexis.] AP
This sense that the new set of locks … ''the railroads are not going to sit idly by'' and let the water route undercut their business.

Expanded rail capacity is key to the success of the new wave of freight – it’s the dominant mode upon which both trucking and water freight rely – that’s independently key to global trade
Horsley 11 [John Horsley, Executive Director, American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials. “Freight-Rail Bottom Line Report.” Cambridge Systematics. 4/28/11 (Last Modified)] AP
Freight-rail service provides a critical link in the nation’s intermodal freight transportation system, …account for 80 percent of rail-borne international trade by value.

Rail capacity expansions are key to keeping up with new trade from the Panama expansion – absent improvements Mexico won’t be able to ship products throughout North America
Blank 09 [Stephen Blank is the Ross Distinguished Visiting Professor of Canada-U.S. Business and Economic Relations, Western Washington University, and Co-Chair, North American Transportation Competitiveness Research Council. “Freight Transportation Infrastructure in North America: Getting Value for (Billions Of) Dollars.” Ivey Business Journal. February 2009.] AP
With regard to rail, a study of U.S. rail capacity commissioned …must we assume that best transportation planners can do is to enshrine Chicago as the black hole of freight transportation for the next 2 or 3 decades?

Rail is unique – the Mexican shipping industry relies on U.S. rails
Cassidy 8/23 [William B. Cassity, Senior Editor, Journal of Commerce. “Shippers Report Tightening Truck Capacity in Mexico.” The Journal of Commerce. August 23, 2011. http://www.joc.com/trucking/shippers-report-tightening-truck-capacity-mexico.] AP
U.S. shippers importing goods from Mexico … increasing 17.1 percent year-over-year to $29.1 billion.

Freight’s key to the Mexican economy

The bilateral economic relationship with Mexico is of key interest to the United States … After the significant decrease in trade in 2009 that resulted from the global economic downturn, U.S.-Mexico trade increased considerably in 2010 and 2011.

Solvency

Put away your disads – private railroads will increase investment in maintaining capacity but will fall short in expansion because of internal financial restraints – additional investment is key
Hamberger 08[Ed Hamberger, President, Association of American Railroads. “Statement of Ed Hamberger, President and CEO , Association of American Railroads.” Committee on House Transportation and Infrastructure Subcommittee on Railroads, Pipelines, and Hazardous Materials. April 23, 2008. ProQuest Congressional.] AP
As described above, the railroads are diligently doing everything they believe to be prudent to maintain and expand their capacity to provide service … In such cases, those investment decisions should be based on projected returns that will most favor the long-tern sustainability of the rail network.

A Rail Trust Fund solves investment shortfalls – spurs other funding from states, localities, and private companies
Lipinksi 02 [William Lipinksy, D. Rep, Illinois’ 5th and 3rd district. “Letter to Norman Y. Mineta, Secretary, Department of Transportation.” October 17, 2002.] AP
As you know from your tenure as a Member of Congress on the House Transportation and Infrastructure Committee and now as Secretary of Transportation, capacity constraints and congestion abound within our national freight rail system… It is in the national interest for that to change.

A rail trust fund would catalyze public and private sector projects – systematic and consistent funding is key
Fritelli 03 [John F. Frittelli, Transportation Analyst, Resources, Science and Industry Division, CRS. “Intermodal Rail Freight: A Role for Federal Funding?” March 31, 2003.] AP
The railroads are the only transportation mode … Federal funds could serve as a catalyst for getting projects underway.
