Observation 1 is Inherency:

1.) Freight rail capacity will have to rise by 90% by 2035, additionally many rails are already congested
Economist 7/22/10 (no author, http://www.economist.com/node/16636101)

2.) Amtrak ridership expected to set more records as congestion worsens, gas prices rise, and the population grows; new investment is needed to keep freight on the tracks
Amtrak 4/11/12 (news release, no individual author, http://www.amtrak.com/ccurl/85/16/ATK-12-030%20Amtrak%20on%20Pace%20for%20Ridership%20Record%20in%20FY%202012.pdf)


Thus the Plan:

Plan text: The U.S. Federal Government should substantially increase transportation infrastructure by funding Transportation Investment Generating Economic Recovery (TIGER) grants to create seperate railroad tracks for Amtrak and Acela passenger trains along the Northwest corridor, Empire Corridor, and Chicago Detroit line, in order to reduce freight rail congestion.






Advantage 1 is The Economy:

1.) Outdated rail infrastructure costs 200 billion a year through congestion and raises prices across the board
Davidson 5/20/12 (Paul) Writer for USA today http://www.usatoday.com/money/economy/story/2012-05-20/creaking-infrastructure/55096396/1


Scenario 1 is Job creation:

A.) Unemployment is at a record high and damages the economy
Zumbrun and Chen 9/10/11 (Business week, http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2011-09-29/bernanke-says-u-s-facing-national-crisis-as-high-unemployment-persists.html)

B.) For every 1% reduction in unemployment, there is a 2% growth in GDP
Mayer 6/5/12 (New York Times, http://www.netplaces.com/economics/where-did-my-job-go/why-unemployment-is-bad.htm)


C.) Studies have proven that rising unemployment rates are linked to rising crime rates- declining wages increase the payoff of criminal activity.
Grabmeier 4/10/10 (From an article in The Review of Economics and Statistics,http://researchnews.osu.edu/archive//crimwage.htm)

D.) Long and short term unemployment causes serious mental and physical health issues, but can be solved by employment.
Weaver 10, (February 24. Rheyanne Weaver, PHD, Doctorate of Psychology and journalism from University of Arizona. http://www.empowher.com/mental-health/content/mental-physical-health-effects-long-term-unemployment)

E.) Unemployment causes poverty and is exacerbated by the U.S’ current economic crisis, but can be solved by creation of employment opportunities by the government.
Economy Watch 10/4/10 (http://www.economywatch.com/unemployment/poverty.html, Economy watch is a world economy newspaper.)


F.) Construction of railroads stimulates short and long term job growth more efficiently than capital investment
ARRA 10 (2/9/10, from an ARRA study: what we learned from the stimulus. http://www.smartgrowthamerica.org/resources/what-we-learned-from-the-stimulus/ (American recovery and reinvestment act of 2009)


G.) Freight supports millions of jobs
NRLC 08 (data from 08, no date for article, http://www.raillaborfacts.org/status-and-key-issues/freight-rail’s-economic-impact/)



Scenario 2 is Trade:

A.) Freight rail investment needs to happen now, the expansion of the Panama Canal in 2014 requires better inland rails.
The Economist 7/22/10 (No author, http://www.economist.com/node/16636101)


B.) Exports are key to economic growth and have been responsible for half of economic growth since the Great Recession.
Fisher 4/18/12 (from Forbes, http://www.forbes.com/sites/greggfisher/2012/04/18/the-rise-of-us-exports/)



C.) Rail transports one third of all U.S. exports, including 30% of our grain harvest
NRLC 08 (data from 08, no date for article, http://www.raillaborfacts.org/status-and-key-issues/freight-rail’s-economic-impact/)


D.) U.S. is world’s largest exporter of corn and soybeans, and the third largest of wheat.
Simpson, 7/12/12 (Steven Simpson, CSA, from investapedia article: Top agricultural producing countries.)


E.) Corn is a staple food for sub-Saharan Africa.
Kunzemann 14/4/10 (Thilo Kunzemann, 10 crops that feed the world from Allianz knowledge, http://knowledge.allianz.com/health/food_water/?709/food-crops)

F.) U.S. agricultural exports to Sub-Saharan Africa account for 8.7% of their imports.
International Trade Administration 2009 (International Trade Administration, 2009, “U.S.-African Trade Profile,” http://www.agoa.gov/resources/US_African_Trade_Profile_2009.pdf)


G.) Millions of Sub-Saharan Africans are undernourished; we cannot let the starvation get any worse.
Smith 2012 (David Smith, May 15 2012, The Guardian, “Sub-Saharan Africa can only grow if it solves hunger crisis- UNDP”, http://www.guardian.co.uk/global-development/2012/may/15/sub-saharan-africa-hunger-undp)

H.) Famine is a voter

LaFollette 03 [Hugh, Cole Chair in Ethics University of South Florida

St. Petersburg, "World Hunger" Blackwell Companion to Applied Ethics,

ed. Ray Frey and Christopher Heath Wellman, Blackwell 2003,

http://www.stpt.usf.edu/hhl/papers/World.Hunger.htm]








Scenario 3 is Efficiency:

A.) Running one passenger train prevents 6 freight trains from operating in that corridor. Investment is necessary now because capacity will have to increase by 90% by 2035
Economist 7/22/10 (no author, http://www.economist.com/node/16636101)


B.) Increase in rail efficiency reduces road congestion, and thus air pollution and pulmonary health risks in New York and the world
EDF 3/30/08 (Environmental Defense Fund, Article: Freight rail investment would reduce congestion and pollution, http://www.edf.org/news/freight-rail-investment-would-reduce-traffic-and-air-pollution)


C.) Air pollution kills 3.3 million people each year, and is caused by the use of fossil fuels. Investment on the federal level is necessary to make an impact.
WHO 12/11 (World Health Organization, Air quality and Health, http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs313/en/)





Observation 2 is Solvency

1.) Good decision-making requires you to completely reject the negative’s fixation on improbable worst-case scenarios
Rescher 83 [Nicholas, Distinguished University Professor of Philosophy at the University of Pittsburgh where he has also served as Chairman of the Philosophy Department and as Director (and currently Chairman) of the Center for Philosophy of Science. In a productive research career extending over six decades, he has established himself as a systematic philosopher: A Philosophical Introduction to the Theory of Risk Evaluation and Management, Pg 50]


2.) Failure to act to stop suffering and death is just as bad as causing the suffering and death—inaction guarantees the perpetuation of our harms, and such an advocacy must be rejected.
Alexander 08 Alexander, John K. “Eliminating the Harm We Cause: A Defense of Singer and Agent-Causation Responsibility.” Philosophy in the Contemporary World 15, no. 1 (2008): 11-21.

3.) Ethics should be grounded in direct experience—pain, starvation and consciousness should be our moral guidelines.
Phelps 2009 Norm, animal rights activist and author of The Longest Struggle: Animal Advocacy from Pythagoras to PETA, “The Quest for a Boundless Ethic: A Reassessment of Albert Schweitzer” Journal for Critical Animal Studies, VII.1



4.) TIGER grants are politically popular in all levels of government, especially in economically tough times.
Holeywell 6/22/12 (Ryan Holeywell, staff writer for Governing, article: Feds announce latest TIGER grants for transportation, transit projects. http://www.governing.com/blogs/fedwatch/USDOT-Announces-Latest-Round-of-TIGER-Grant-Winners.html)
