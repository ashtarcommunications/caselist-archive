Negative - State Checks

Framework

I negate and value Just Governance. All governments have a direct obligation to further the interests of its citizens as:
  1. 1. At the point of the creation of the state, the citizens cede their rights to absolute freedom in a state of nature in exchange for a sovereign that holds the interests of its citizens above its self-interest…..analytic….

And, the only way to maintain that a government protects its citizens~’ interests is through a system of constitutional checks on power. Mueller[1] in ~’96:

The specter has been raised that a government once created will turn upon its creators
AND
who will someday have the authority to enforce it, abide by it.

In addition, this framework uniquely applies to the United States:
  1. 1. The system of checks and balances asserted in the USA is an independent reason to prefer the NC framework…..specific to US, etc…

Thus, the standard is Establishing Functional Checks on State Power, accomplished by furthering the interests of citizens. Further prefer this standard as allowing the government unrestricted control leads to oppressive, controlling practices on the citizens with no chance of stopping…..

Contention

I contend that overriding the digital privacy of citizens in the name of national security allows the government unchecked power.

First, government surveillance programs spy on citizens without consent; allows unchecked state power. Hussain[2] in ~’13:
In 2010, the Washington Post~’s landmark report "[[Top Secret America-http://projects.washingtonpost.com/top-secret-america/]]" - an
AND
government with a level of information about its citizens unprecedented in human history.

By advancing their own conception of "national security", the state denies avenue for democratic discussion and forecloses the possibility of reform – negating solves this back by giving citizens and avenue to protest and demand change, the internet.

Second, surveillance leads to the ~’chilling effect~’ and limits the expression of civil liberties. Richards[3] in 12:
At the level of theory, I will explain when surveillance is particularly dangerous,
AND
prosecuted or ¶ blackmailed for wrongdoing unrelated to the purpose of the surveillance.

This is definitionally unchecked state power – the influence of the state limits the individual’s authority to criticize the state and instills a paralyzing fear in the population. Richards proves this is the root cause of coercion from the state.

Finally, the "take first, check later" mentality of the government violates the fundamental right to probable cause that all US citizens have. By forgoing this check, the aff allows the government the ability to label anything as a threat to national security without justification and violate rights. Smith[4] in ~’11:
Law enforcement efforts to obtain cell phone location data and Internet records have prompted privacy
AND
should be the same standard that the desk calendar was 10 years ago."

And, I always outweigh the aff on probability – blanket surveillance in the aff world violates the rights of everybody before knowing whether there is a threat of terrorism in the first place, but the aff only gets a low probability of catching a terrorist.
