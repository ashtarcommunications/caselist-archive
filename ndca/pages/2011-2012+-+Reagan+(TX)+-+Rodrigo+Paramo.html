Affirmative

I affirm, “Resolved: Justice requires the recognition of animal rights.”

I value justice.

Wise defines animal rights in 96 [Steven M., practices animal protection law and is a professor of “Animal Rights Law” a the Vermont Law School, Legal rights forNonhuman Animals: The Case for Chimpanzees and Bonobos, pg 1 http://heinonline.org/HOL/LandingPage?collection=journals&handle=hein.journals/anim2&div=14&id=&page=]
““Animal rights” … they assume the shape of law.”
Animal rights deserve the same protection as human rights. Animals have value and thus justice demands that they deserve respect that can only be given through recognition of their rights.
Wenz 92 [Peter.Emeritus Professor of Philosophy at the University of Illinois at Springfield, University Scholar of the University of Illinois, and Adjunct Professor of Medical Humanities at the Southern Illinois University School of Medicine. Environmental Justice]
“The principle that we found implicit in our ordinary moral judgments is that …. All subjects-of-a-life, both human and nonhuman, have these rights as part of their right not to be harmed.”
And recognition of animal rights doesn’t mean that we would never be able to violate their rights, or that we have to stop all meat production, its instead a question of our methodology while we carry out these acts.
Friend 2k8 [Catherine. M.S. in Economics and a B.A. in Economics and Spanish. The Compassionate Carnivore pp. 255]
“Selling meat … raised is the moral issue.”

It is permissible to kill animals for the sake of human survival.
Wenz 92 [Peter.Emeritus Professor of Philosophy at the University of Illinois at Springfield, University Scholar of the University of Illinois, and Adjunct Professor of Medical Humanities at the Southern Illinois University School of Medicine. Environmental Justice]
“Regan argues also that in cases … an individual for the good of others. “
1 Is the Plan Text: the United States Federal Government should stop subsidizing factory farms (CAFO’s) and outlaw contract growing. I reserve the right to clarify.

2 Is Justification: I only defend this plan text. All disadvantages must be specific to this plan text. This is theoretically legitimate because,


a) narrowing the scope of evaluation prevents advocacy shifts.
b) it is impossible to prove any statement completely, categorically, and 100% true;

c) It’s good for precise argumentation

d) Its better than just having the same debate over and over again, if we don’t parametricize the resolution then every round is just debating the same broad concept of justice but never getting any depth in the education;

3. Is Inherency:
The government subsidizes 9,900 CAFOs.
The Ethicurean, 2008. (Elanor, “Bucking the CAFO tax: A plea for conscientious objection”, April 24 2008, The Ethicurean, http://www.ethicurean.com/2008/04/24/buck_the_cafo_tax/.)

“Here’s a number to knock you out of that mid-day stupor… “the figure would undoubtedly be much higher.””
4. is Harms: Factory farming has made it impossible to operate an independent family farm that respects animal rights.


Humane Society 2k8 [Humane Society of the United States. “The True Cost of Animal Agribusiness for Rural Communities, Public Health, Families, Farmers, the Environment, and Animals”, http://www.humanesociety.org/assets/pdfs/farm/hsus-factory-farming-in-america-the-true-cost-of-animal-agribusiness.pdf]

“Factory farms have made it increasingly difficult, ….involves some of the world‟s most powerful and concentrated industries.””
CAFO’s destroy animal rights.
Mason and Finelli 2006[Jim, Mary. Farmed Animal Advocates In Defense of Animals: the Second Wave]
“Factory methods and equipment…. to be turned into companion-animal food and feed supplements, to incinerators, or to landfills.”


Advantage 1 is Global Warming:
Factory farming contributes more greenhouse gases to global warming than the entire transportation industry.
Woods 2k8 [Cathleen. Founder of Vegan-Nutritionista.com, http://www.vegan-nutritionista.com/causes-of-global-warming.html]

“The problem is, it's not widely publicized that …This destruction of the natural ecosystem is a huge cause of global warming.”

Methane destroys the environment 100 times faster than carbon.

Pearce 2k7 [Fred. Journalist for WWF, the UN Environment Programme, the Red Cross, UNESCO, the World Bank, the European Environment Agency, and the UK Environment Agency. Reed Business Information, 2007. With Speed and Violence. pp 77-78]
Now our most feared global Armageddon is climate change. …. But we have to change tense here. For "would create," read "is creating."[RP]

Methane driven global warming triggers 4 extinction scenarios.
Lynas 2k8 [Mark. Journalist, Environmental Activist. Six Degrees: Our Future On a Hotter Planet pp. 255-259]
“Worse was still to come. … before anything like pre-extinction levels of biodiversity returned.”
255-259
5. is Solvency: Organic Livestock farming prevents the manure buildup that kills the environment significantly lowers the methane production of meat-consumption and eliminates the violations of animal rights.
Wainana 2k10[Bernard. Livestock Consultant, PROFARMS CONSULTANTS “Livestock And Climate Change;What are the true facts?” March 8, 2010 http://my.opera.com/PROFARMS/blog/show.dml/8598901]
Meat production, especially beef, … climate warming and animal cruelty.


Negative

I negate.


Animal rights is defined by Wise in 96 [Steven M., practices animal protection law and is a professor of “Animal Rights Law” a the Vermont Law School, Legal rights forNonhuman Animals: The Case for Chimpanzees and Bonobos, pg 1 http://heinonline.org/HOL/LandingPage?collection=journals&handle=hein.journals/anim2&div=14&id=&page=]
“Animal rights” is a term… the shape of law.”

The standard for the round is recognizing the sameness between man and animal; The anthropological machine distinguishes between man and animal by allowing humanity to suspend its animality to move past this part of this nature.
The affirmative fails to recognize that not only is there an animality within man, but there is also an inherent humanity within the animal; This refusal perpetuates the binary of the human and the inhuman.
Wadiwel 2K2 [Dinesh. “Cows and Sovereignty: Biopower and Animal Life” Borderlands E-Journal. animal by any other name vol 1 no 2 http://www.borderlands.net.au/vol1no2_2002/wadiwel_cows.html]
“The life of cattle… the vivisector’s knife”


The affirmative’s distinction between the man and animal is a perpetuation of the anthropological machine; This machine is what creates the divide between human and subhuman.
