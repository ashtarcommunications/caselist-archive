Contention two – Ultimate Evil


Space has become a forum for capitalist exploitation. The new venue for the struggle against capitalism is now space.

Barton BEEBE Law @ NYU ’99 “Law's Empire and the Final Frontier: Legalizing the Future in the Early Corpus Juris Spatialis” Yale Law Journal 108 (7) p. 1758-1763

Commenting on the outpouring of writing on space law in the wake of Sputnik, ....? Law owns the universe.

Making empires in space subjects us to the same slavery to capital as on earth, the fear of the that the commons collapse allows economic and technological inequalities subjected less developed states to be slaves to the technologically superior states.

Kathryn Milun, Anthropology/Sociology @ Minnesota Duluth 2010 The Political Uncommons: the cross-cultural logic of the global commons p. 141-145

In 1999the United Nations General Assembly voted to reaffirm the Outer Space Treaty ... nineteenth-century imperialist sense.


Allowing this evil greed to expand into space risks the worst extinction scenarios possible, allowing capitalism into space outweighs any possible impact you can read, it would turn mankind into a standing reserve and would justify anything to further our development

Josée JOHNSTON, Associate Professor of Sociology at the University of Toronto, 2003
Capitalism, Nature, Socialism, “Who Cares about the Commons?” December 2003, [Stolarski]

Even when the specific language of enclosure of the commons ... discern any problem.

The fear of the tragedy of the commons sets us up to failure. This tragedy is not possible in space as most resources in space are not finite;
Harlan Cleveland, president of World Academy of Art and Science, professor emeritus of Hubert H. In the commons environments, but instead being wasted, intensifying preexisting inequities and providing a whole set of new occasions for international conflict."


Respecting space as the heritage of the commons allows us to establish a new anti imperial discourse, Voting Affirmative allows us to shape our space policy into a mode of multilateral cooperation

Kathryn Milun, Anthropology/Sociology @ Minnesota Duluth 2010 The Political Uncommons: the cross-cultural logic of the global commons p. 147-149

For his final argument in favor of further privatization of outer space....... spectrum, the World Wide Web, and the biodiversity of the earth—was the distance we had to go from Western notions of property to find a jus novum in which to ground a new economic order based in a right to subsistence (“the needs of life”) with wealth generated from the assets of globally common property.

Contention three – The Great War


In the status quo space militarization is inevitable, the United States values its capital in space as too precious to expend meaning weapons will be built to defend them, only by equalizing our assets in space with other countries can we truly demilitarize space.
Dickens and Ormrod 7 –[Peter Dickens and James Ormrod, Affiliated Lecturer in the Faculty of Social and Political Sciences at the University of Cambridge and Visiting Professor of Sociology, University of Essex and Lecturer in Sociology at the University of Brighton Cosmic Society: Towards a sociology of the universe, pg 94-95]


The United States government is by far the dominant military force ... it is a strategy that is being spread throughout the cosmos.

The current policy of militarizing space creates thousands of wars that do not need to be fought. Extending our militaristic domination into space destroys our only chance of defeating a deadly astropolitics

MacDonald 07, Fraser MacDonald, Professor of Anthropology, Geography, and Environmental Studies at Melbourne University in Australia, “Anti-Astropolitik – outer space and the orbit of geography,” Progress in Human Geography, 2007, Pages 592-615.

Two things should now be clear. First, outer space is no longer remote from our everyday lives;.....o all’ sits awkwardly with the current suspension of the rule of law in Guantanamo Bay as well as in various other ‘spaces of exception’ (see Gregory, 2004; Agamben, 2005).


Put away your realism cards. Realism cannot explain why most superpowers choose to respect space as a global commons. Realism does not apply to space policies

M. J. Peterson, 97 – M.J. Peterson, Spring 1997, International Organization, Vol. 51, No. 2 (Spring, 1997), pp. 245-27
Realist theories of international relations, with their focus on rational actors perceiving interests and calculating how well they can do in the prevailing distribution of capability, easily anticipate that the states most able to pursue space activity would have the greatest influence on writing outer space law...... of cooperative or competitive behavior among states and other actors.

Critically Affirming International Law is the only way that critical theory can become successful and actually challenge powerful norms

Aceves Law Associate Professor, California Western School of Law 2001
William J., “Critical Jurisprudence and International Legal Scholarship: A Study of Equitable Distribution,” Columbia Journal of Transnational Law, 39 Colum. J. Transnat'l L. 299, LexisNexis


While critical jurisprudence has focused primarily on the domestic legal system, it is worthwhile to consider its relevance and application to other legal systems. .... a distinction without value. n77
