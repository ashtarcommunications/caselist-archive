Dedevelopment


The Current System Dominates the Individual. It is a Monster That Consumes and Wastes Everything in its Path.
Trainer ‘85 (Abandon Affluence!)

Our Current Economy inevitably creates gross inequality and waste on the national and international levels.
Trainer ’85 (Abandon Affluence!, pg. 226)

These Inequalities Cause Permanent Repression and Exploitation of the World’s Poor.
Trainer ’85 (Abandon Affluence!, pg. 121)

PLAN: THE UNITED STATES FEDERAL GOVERNMENT SHALL WITHDRAW ALL MILITARY AND POLICE PRESENCE IN SOUTH KOREA, TURKEY, AFGHANISTAN, IRAQ, JAPAN AND KUWAIT.

U.S. reneging on its commitments and withdrawing troops makes power projection difficult. Economic security, military capability and commitments to allies are impossible in a world of isolationism or off shore balancing.
Thayer 06(Bradley, Associate Professor in Missouri State University, In Defense of Primacy, http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m2751/is_86/ai_n27065796/pg_2/), citing Barry R. Posen is Professor of Political Science in the Security Studies Program at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (“American Defense Policy”)

PERRY, VP and director of studies of IFPA, JAMES L. Hegemony is key to the global economy – free trade and globalization.

Deconstruction


In the status quo, Western culture disrupts the connection of how we understand ourselves in relation to our physical being and corporeality. This culture then creates a singular bastardization of our being and our understanding of identity, which rightfully exist on both a physical and metaphysical level.
Anna-Teresa Tymieniecka, Angela Ales Bello, Phenomenology: Foundations - Dynamics - Life - Engagements (Analecta Husserliana, Vol. 80), 2003

The controlling social structures work to define our single identities. By ‘accepting’ the control of the prevailing social structures, we rely on it to define our being for us, becoming ontologically dependant on them through their use of normative generalizations of how we should be.

John Wilson, Director Farmington Trust Research Unit, Oxford, CONCEPTS OF INDOCTRINATION, I.A. Snook, editor, 1972, p.102

Since objective values can never be proven, the normative statements social structures impose are never justified.
Alasdair MacIntyre, After Virtue, 1981. Page 20.

This lack of proof turns all people into resources that simply fuel the creation of further “objective” claims. This reduces people into usable objects and justifies all atrocity.
Joseph Kockelmans, Penn State philosopher, HEIDEGGER AND SCIENCE, 1985, p.251-2.

This use of normative statements destroys the ability to affirm any single value as true, as a true statement must be confirmed by multiple perspectives.
Jon Simons, professor of political philosophy and feminist theory @ the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, Foucault and the Political, 1995, p. 20

We thus propose the plan: the United States federal government will revoke the citizenship all of the soldiers in Iraq, Afghanistan, South Korea, Turkey, Japan and Kuwait.

This would have an intrinsic effect, an effect we will view through the lens of The Soldier. Disconnected from the social structures that legitimized Its understanding of being, The Soldier has no justification for Its being. The Soldier will seek a new justification for Its being, one that can only be provided by the here-and-now. Becoming the Overman allows for an understanding of being based on Its relationships with the world in which it resides.
Eugen Fink, 2003, Nietzsche’s Philosophy

Only through this state of constantly understanding one’s relationship with everything else can there be true value: it is this self-ownership of ones ideals that gives us the ability to take a just or moral action.
Murray Rothbard, Professor of Economics, Polytechnic Institute of New York, FREEDOM AND VIRTUE, George Carey, ed., 1984, p.92-3

We purport that the role of the ballot in this round would be an affirmation that such social structures turn us into resources towards ends that are never able to be justified, as The Soldier is. A vote for the affirmative would represent the need to break free from those structures. However, a vote for the negative would always represent a complacence with those social structures and the harms of the status quo.

It is through deconstructive acts that we can successfully dismantle knowledge we once considered self-evident. This analysis of ideas we took for granted or passively accepted allows us to achieve a better understanding of the foundations of knowledge they’re built upon.
Christopher Norris, Professor of the History of Ideas, University of Wales, DERRIDA, 1987, p.18-19.

It is because of our discourse that we are able to deconstruct the social structures and the normative statements they impose, as language creates our understanding of a subject.
Honi Fern Haber, Professor of Philosophy, University of Colorado, Denver, BEYOND POSTMODERN POLITICS, 1994, p.11.

Lastly, our being will always be affected by this understanding. This means that our discursive deconstruction affects our understanding of being: our reinterpretation of ontology is what allows us to reach self-ownership through affirmation.
Bleiker, 00 (Roland, Ph.D. visiting research and teaching affiliations at Harvard, Cambridge, Humboldt, Tampere, Yonsei and Pusan National University as well as the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology and the Institute of Social Studies in The Hague, Popular Dissent , Human Agency and Global Politics, Cambridge University Press)

Deconstruction


In this round, the affirmative has taken it upon themselves to define the relationships between multiple beings, stating that those relationships have a singular significance that benefits the points they have made. They have assumed that theirs is the only truth of those relationships and that those truths justify their course of action.

This kind of definition of relationships is what is inherent with the status quo culture, making us ontologically dependant upon them for our understanding of being and justifying the status quo’s harms.
John Wilson, Director Farmington Trust Research Unit, Oxford, CONCEPTS OF INDOCTRINATION, I.A. Snook, editor, 1972, p.102

This passive acceptance places us within a singular mode of being that separates us from the physical world. This disrupts the connection of how we understand ourselves in relation to our physical being and corporeality. This creates a singular bastardization of our being and understanding of identity, which rightfully exists on both the physical and metaphysical.
Anna-Teresa Tymieniecka, Angela Ales Bello, Phenomenology: Foundations - Dynamics - Life - Engagements (Analecta Husserliana, Vol. 80), 2003

The social structures they use that reinforce singular relationships attempt to create objective claims and normative statements, which can never be proven or justified.
Alasdair MacIntyre, After Virtue, 1981. Page 20.

The affirmative’s constant attempt at creating normative statements will result in the use of humans as resources to further power of the controlling social structures, justifying all atrocity in the name of “the truth”. This turns all solvency of the affirmative as they cause that which they seek to abolish.
Joseph Kockelmans, Penn State philosopher, HEIDEGGER AND SCIENCE, 1985, p.251-2.

A true statement can only be true if it is proven true from multiple perspectives. Since the affirmative social structures only ever allow a single view to be formed and controlled, we can never prove that their statements about relationships or values can ever be true.
Jon Simons, professor of political philosophy and feminist theory @ the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, Foucault and the Political, 1995, p. 20

To remove that which controls each person is to allow each person a right to access themselves. The affirmative never provides a way for each person to surpass themselves, to become the Overman. The negative removes the agent that justifies each person’s being, so ontologies are not how they should be for the agent, but how they should be for any given individual person.
Murray Rothbard, Professor of Economics, Polytechnic Institute of New York, FREEDOM AND VIRTUE, George Carey, ed., 1984, p.92-3

The Alternative is that by rejecting the affirmative, we are able to surpass the social structures that define our being and we are able to define our own being; we can finally self-realize. By doing this, we solve for the harms of the affirmative by allowing each person to make their own decisions with legitimate justifications, never justifying their actions for them.
Eugen Fink, 2003, Nietzsche’s Philosophy

By affirming the intellectual as a means to reaching justified conclusions, we can understand why a statement is true and not simply how it functions as the affirmative attempts to do in their definition of relationships. This ultimately creates better action by the participants of relationships.
Biswas December 2007, (Shampa, Professor of Politics at Whitman College, “Empire and Global Public Intellectuals: Reading Edward Said as an International Relations Theorist,” Millennium: Journal of International Studies, Vol. 36, No. 1, p. 124



Only by deconstructing the meaning and ontology of Being can we truly see the ontological relationships of each person. If we do not attempt to reach the “bottom”, we will never understand how Being is to exist in terms of the physical and ideal, rather than the singularity proposed by the affirmative.
Christopher Norris, Professor of the History of Ideas, University of Wales, DERRIDA, 1987, p.18-19.

It is because of our discourse that we are able to deconstruct the social structures and the normative statements they impose, as language creates our understanding of a subject. At the same time, this is a unique reason to reject any attempt by the affirmative to permutate: their representations will always co-opt ours, and they cannot escape their representation in the 1AC without the rejection of the plan.
Honi Fern Haber, Professor of Philosophy, University of Colorado, Denver, BEYOND POSTMODERN POLITICS, 1994, p.11.

Multiple understandings of Being are only available through discourse. By denying the negative’s attempt at discourse, ontologies can never actually be accessed or changed, which is essential to understanding and to solving for the harms of the affirmative. Discourse reinstates the identity of the individual as a space for discussion of what ‘is’.
Bleiker, 00 (Roland, Ph.D. visiting research and teaching affiliations at Harvard, Cambridge, Humboldt, Tampere, Yonsei and Pusan National University as well as the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology and the Institute of Social Studies in The Hague, Popular Dissent , Human Agency and Global Politics, Cambridge University Press)
