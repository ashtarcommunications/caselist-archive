Lacan 1AC- Valley

Plan: The United States Federal Government should withdraw all of its military and police presence from the topically designated regions.

Our sole contention is The Fantasy

﻿
A false narrative of progress is used to legitimate our occupation in places like Afghanistan, where our strategy is doomed to failure
Ahmed 07 (Faiz, PhD, “Afghanistan's Reconstruction, FIVE Years Later: Narratives of Progress, Marginalized Realities, and the Politics of Law in a Transitional Islamic Republic”, Gonzaga Journal of International Law 10 Gonz. J. Int’l L. 269, Autumn 2007)
"When considering the disturbing trends...the dominant reconstruction actors take."

US interventions abroad render the targeted populations as Homo Sacer—disposable persons
Zizek 02 (Slavoj, senior researcher at the Institute of Sociology, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia, visiting professor at Columbia University, Princeton, The New School, “Are we in a war? Do we have an enemy?”, London Review of Books, 5/23/02, http://www.lrb.co.uk/v24/n10/zize2410.htm)
"The distinction between criminals and...dropping bombs or food parcels."


Our military presence abroad functions as an empty reach toward a utopian impossibility. The claim that such policies represent progress on the path to achieving the impossible is used to sell us a destructive ideology.
Daly 04 (Glyn, political theorist, has taught at Northampton, Essex, and Manchester, Introduction, “Conversations with Zizek,” 2004, pp.12-13)
"The central issue is one...ideological supplement in today's realpolitik"

This Mythology of Progress is flawed and dangerous. When we see the United States as the City on a Hill that all other nations should act more like, it justifies military intervention against all of those we believe have not “progressed” enough. This has a few implications:
First, it fails inevitably because of a lack of understanding
Second, it breeds more enemies through resentment
Third, it leads to national politics premised on violent, self-righteous militarism.
Crowe 07 (Lore, “the ‘Fuzzy dream”: Discourse, historical myths, and Militarized (in)security – interrogating dangerous myths of Afghanistan and the ‘West’”, http://archive.sgir.eu/uploads/Crowe-loricrowe.pdf)

"Discourses of Danger Several problematic...means of control and coercion.92"

This militarism leads to extinction
Reardon 93 (Betty, a UN consultant, Women and Peace—Feminist Visions of Global Security, p. 21-25) [elipses in the original]
"The very weapons we have...peace and authentic global security."


Our fixation on controlling scenarios for catastrophe is symptomatic of the Freudian death drive in which we desire to bring death upon ourselves
Fischer 2000 (Mark, Economist and Associate Policy Director at FRB of Atlanta, Flatline Constructs: Gothic Materialism and Cybernetic Theory-Fiction, http://www.cinestatic.com/trans-mat/Fischer/FC2s11.htm)

"Baudrillard: “The car is not...no goal to head towards."

The War on Terror constructs a state of endlessly imminent catastrophe; living in this state renders life valueless
Zizek 03 (Slavoj, senior researcher at the Institute of Sociology, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia, visiting professor at Columbia University, Princeton, The New School, “The Puppet and the Dwarf: The Perverse Core of Christianity,” pp.164-165)

"One should thus invert the...the permanent threat of catastrophe."

Humanitarian action takes place within the hegemonic ideological coordinates, ensuring that our constant engagement changes nothing. Withdrawing into pure theory without action is the only way to transform social consciousness and effect real change
Zizek 02 (Slavoj, senior researcher at the Institute of Sociology, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia, visiting professor at Columbia University, Princeton, The New School, “Revolution at the Gates,” pp.169-171)
"In these circumstances, we should...political and state apparatuses work."

Our act of withdrawal, of wiping the slate clean, is a vital prerequisite to any New Beginning
Zizek 99 (Slavoj, senior researcher at the Institute of Sociology, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia, visiting professor at Columbia University, Princeton, The New School, “The Ticklish Subject,” pp. 153-154)

"The crucial thing here is...a newly emerged Master-Signifier."


Our masters’ project is presented as a means of progressing us toward the fantasy; our withdrawal from this project is a traversal of the fantasy. Once we recognize the fantasy does not exist, we may finally resist the symbolic structures that produce violence
McGowan 04 (Todd, teaches critical theory and film in the English department at the University of Vermont, “Lacan and Contemporary Film,” Ed. McGowan and Kunkle, pp. 155-169)
"Traversing the fantasy doesn't allow...and authors a radical break."


Voting affirmative repudiates the Western prioritization of a secure world. Returning the gift of security opens a space so that politics may be otherwise.
Neocleous, prof. of critique of political economy @ Brunei University, 2008 p. 185-186 (Mark, Critique of Security)
"The only way out of...brave enough to return the gift.143"

When focusing on obvious signals of violence such as terror and international conflict, we can’t perceive or address its systemic underlying causes
Zizek 08 (Slavoj, senior researcher at the Institute of Sociology, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia, visiting professor at Columbia University, Princeton, The New School; “Violence,” 2008, pp.1-4)
"If there is a unifying thesis...of respect towards its victims."

The call to act against subjective violence is merely a distraction from violence’s true systemic roots—the SOS call must be resisted in favor of a genuine analysis of violence
Zizek 08 (Slavoj, senior researcher at the Institute of Sociology, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia, visiting professor at Columbia University, Princeton, The New School; “Violence,” 2008, pp.10-11)
"Opposing all forms of violence...most visible of the three."


The War on Terror’s purpose is to construct one Enemy that justifies our ideological aims; Israel proves our refusal to engage in this project can elevate the victims of our military strategy from Homo Sacer to Neighbors
Zizek 02 (Slavoj, senior researcher at the Institute of Sociology, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia, visiting professor at Columbia University, Princeton, The New School, “Are we in a war? Do we have an enemy?”, London Review of Books, 5/23/02, http://www.lrb.co.uk/v24/n10/zize2410.htm)
"There is a lesson to...among critics of Israeli politics."

Contention Three is The Thrown Project, and What It's Really All About

We’ve just presented qualified authors indicating military overstretch is killing hegemony and our hegemony isn’t in danger; soft power both preserves and destroys hegemony; hegemony prevents and creates war. To our contradictory and equally valid deterministic theories, we could add that nuclear war causes and prevents extinction; that human extinction is good and bad for life more broadly; that life is desirable and undesirable. This illustrates the subjectivity and fundamental unknowability of reality and value judgments—rather than hand-picking some deterministic theory in bad faith and pretending we chose the only logical action, we freely choose the plan because Andrew and I feel that removing our oppressive forces from the topically designated regions would enable others to be freer than they are now. Our action’s ultimate results can’t be predicted or objectively evaluated as good or bad; what matters is that it’s a deliberative and freely chosen act.

AND, we must make our choices alone. No abstract system of morality can guide us through the particulars of concrete situations, a priori values don't exist, and any sign of how to act is open to our subjective interpretation. In choosing which experts' advice we'll seek, we predetermine what kind of advice we want to get
Sartre 45 (Jean-Paul, philosopher, critic, playwright, novelist, Nobel laureate, "existentialism is a humanism," 10-29-1945; English translation by Yale University, 2007; pp.30-34)
"To give you an example...be. Such abandonment entails anguish."


We freely choose what we are to be; we may have certain dispositions but it is our acts that define us—to deny this is to resign our entire essence to be some arbitrarily predetermined thing or another and reject our capacity for change
Sartre 45 (Jean-Paul, philosopher, critic, playwright, novelist, Nobel laureate, "existentialism is a humanism," 10-29-1945; English translation by Yale University, 2007; pp.38-39)
"In light of all this...one way or the other."

Since we’re defined by our actions, our morality must be one of action and commitment. Our realization of ourselves is the only foundation for absolute truth; everything outside of self-awareness is merely what’s probable
Sartre 45 (Jean-Paul, philosopher, critic, playwright, novelist, Nobel laureate, "existentialism is a humanism," 10-29-1945; English translation by Yale University, 2007; pp.39-40)
"We have now, I think...need only seize it directly."

The only thing we can judge is whether actions affirm freedom. Freedom is the foundation of all values—there are no a priori values and they are always too vague to apply to specific situations. To promote a deterministic theory of what might happen or claim our existence is necessary is bad faith, concealing ourselves from our freedom. We must reject bad faith above all else
Sartre 45 (Jean-Paul, philosopher, critic, playwright, novelist, Nobel laureate, "existentialism is a humanism," 10-29-1945; English translation by Yale University, 2007; pp.47-50)
"Nevertheless we can pass judgment...in the name of freedom." 

We can't predict or control what others will do; we must act without hope and only concern ourselves with our own commitment to action
Sartre 45 (Jean-Paul, philosopher, critic, playwright, novelist, Nobel laureate, "existentialism is a humanism," 10-29-1945; English translation by Yale University, 2007; pp.34-36)
"As for "despair," it has...I cannot count on anything."


Our personal choices imply prescriptive value judgments that concern everyone; therefore, each of our choices must be weighed as not just for us but all of humanity—Andrew and I embrace this by choosing a global action for the federal government
Sartre 45 (Jean-Paul, philosopher, critic, playwright, novelist, Nobel laureate, "existentialism is a humanism," 10-29-1945; English translation by Yale University, 2007; pp.23-25)
"The word "subjectivism" has two...attributing a universal value to lies."

Negative

Nietzsche


A. Link - The affirmative resembles the Socratic Will to Order positioning itself as the arbiter of stability and control over the world. It continuously searches to eliminate all suffering in the world to create perfection. This search for the “Real World” abandons the present reality creating ressentiment for life as they constantly act to heal the wound of existence.
Saurette, 96 – PhD in Political Theory at John Hopkins University – 1996(Paul, “’I Mistrust all Systematizers and Avoid them’: Nietzsche, Arendt and the Crsiis of the Will to Order in International Relations Theory”, Millennium: Journal of International Studies, Vol. 25, No. 1, Page 1-28)
"According to Nietzsche, the philosophical...fundamental Philosophical principle of modernity."


B. Impacts
1. The desire for mastery and control the affirmative replicates only creates ressentiment for our current existence. Such hatred and search for a new life makes life laughable and makes extinction desirable.
Nietzsche, 86 – German Philosopher – 1886(Friedrich, “Beyond Good and Evil”, Page 342)
"225. Whether it be hedonism, pessimism...the elevations of humanity hitherto?"


2. The Socratic delusion creates mastery, control, and violence as a justification for achieving the end goal of the “Real World”
Saurette, 96 – PhD in Political Theory at John Hopkins University – 1996(Paul, “’I Mistrust all Systematizers and Avoid them’: Nietzsche, Arendt and the Crisis of the Will to Order in International Relations Theory”, Millennium: Journal of International Studies, Vol. 25, No. 1, Page 1-28)

"The dichotomisation of the ideal...neither factual nor beyond critique."


3. Attempts to secure our experience of existence produces paranoiac fear. This produces life as a project where we struggle to perfect it through security. This fuels a ressentiment that negates life.
Der Derian, 95 - Political Science Professor at the University of Massachusetts – 1995(James, “On Security” Page 29)

"The will to power, the...of being protected by a god.”


C. Alternative – Take the risk of exposing yourself to violent impacts and abandon the desire for control. Refuse to engage the 1AC as way of embracing the unknowable aspects of life. This risks danger but your mortality is a certainty. The alternative is the only way to bring meaning to life by living life eventfully through a rolling of the dice.
Deleuze. 83 – French Philosopher – 1983(Gilles, “Nietzsche and Philosophy”, Page 25-27)
"The game has two moments...of the fatally obtained number."
