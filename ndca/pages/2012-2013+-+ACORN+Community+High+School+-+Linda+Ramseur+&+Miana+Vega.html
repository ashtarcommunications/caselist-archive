Affirmative

Ableism Aff
Contention One: Immobility

Investment decisions for transportation infrastructure have systematically removed disabled populations from society by denying them of their mobility.
AAPD, 2012 (The American Association of People With Disabilities, "Equity in Transportation for People with Disabilities", May 23, http://www.infrastructueusa.org/equity-in-transportation-for-people-with-disabilities/)

Transportation and mobility play key roles in the struggle for civil rights..... a lack of requirements has meant very uneven progress.

And - These seemingly innocent investment decisions render differently-abled people prisoner to a transportation infrastructure system that forces immobility upon them. Disabled people are confined to their own space and the only way out is through a transportation system that was designed and is maintained without consideration of their perspective. This is not unintentional, rather, fixed-routes and times are regulated to allow society to exist free from contact with these individuals
Langan, 2001 (Celeste, Associated Professor of English at the of California Berkeley, "Mobility Disability", Publc Culture, Vol. 13,No. 3, Fall, Project Muse)

Plan - the United States Federal Government should substantially increase its transportation infrastructure accessibility investment

Contention 2: Mobility

Our current understanding of disability within transportation policy makes it such that the disable prespective is always relegated to the periphery. Traditional assumptions about transportation blame individuals for their lack of accessability and consider them ultimately responsible for their own mobility. Only repolitizing transportation infrastructure can shift responsibility to the institution that maintain exclusion and can open space to expose our complicity in ableist structures of knowledge
Koch, 2008 (Tom, Adjunct Professor of Medical Ethics at the University of British Columbia, "Spaced Out in the City: the Wrinkled World of Transit for Those With Limited Mobility", Disablity Studies Quarterly, Vol. 28 No. 2, Spring, http://dsq-sds-org/article/view/94/94)
It is strikinging, nonetheless, that so few leftist have understood disability to these terms..........we believe the need to equal the playing field to assure equal access to all will be not simpily a political ideal but a social goal in transit planning.

Ableism is a pre requisite to all oppression
wolbring '08 (Gregor Associate Professor Community Rehabilitation and Disablity Studies, Past President of Canadian Disability Studies Association and member of the board of the society for disable studies (USA) the politics of abelism, June 2008)
Abelism is an umbrella ism for other isms such as racism, sexism ..... ABECS could be used as the abelism equivilent to the NBICS S&T convergence

As an American policy intellectual you are morally obligated to support domestic disability protections
Olberheltzer, 2001 (Jerry, Professor at University of Norte Dame, "Equity of Attitude", May 7, http//www.nd.edu/frswrite/issues/2001-2002/Philbin-shtml)
As a Compassionate society, American has a moral obligation to help the disabled ....In turn, those whom the government helps will be less reliant upon these subsidy programs, and the same time, be productive, full members of society.

The societal concept of disability sets the basis for discrimination and violence against other minorities
Baynton, 2001 (Douglas C., Associate Professor at the University of Iowa, "Disability and the Justification of Inequality in American History", http://courses.washington.edu/intro2ds/Readings/Baynton.pdf)
Disability has functioned historically to justify inequality for disabled people themselves, but it has also done so for women and minority groups....disability has functioned for all such groups as a sign of and justification for inferiority.

And – As students participating in policy debates we have an obligation to put disability at the center of our discussion because what we debate about here says a lot about human conduct on a larger scale – this is not a meta-theoretical quibble but central to change the way that disability is represented and conceived

Bérubé, 2003 (Michael, Paterno Family Professor in Literature at Pennsylvania State University, “Citizenship and Disability”, Spring, http://www.dissentmagazine.org/article/?article=506)
It is striking, nonetheless, that so few leftists have understood disability in these terms....
citizens with disabilities participate in the life of the United States as political and moral equals with their non-disable peers

Solvency

Financial investment is key to resolve transportation barriers and ensure accessibility for all

Fittante, 2011 (Steven, Executive Director of Middlesex County Area Transit, “Promoting Broader Access to Public Transportation for America’s Older Adults and People with Disabilities”, Hearing before the Senate Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs Subcommittee, Congressional Documents and Publications, June 29)
I am here today to focus on the need for new transit operating models for increasing mobility for the growing senior population, particularly in areas with limited pubic transportation.....Thank you for the opportunity to raise these issues as you tackle the challenges of providing federal support for these critical services.


Inclusion programs can act as a catalyst that changes the entire socio-economic system of disability – the call for the plan serves as the springboard for sweeping changes in the way that society looks at disability

Metts, 2006 (Robert, Professor of Economics at the University of Nevada, http://www.ecomod.org/files/papers/181.pdf)

This paper has developed a theory of the connections between poverty and disability that suggests that societal factors such as prejudice and discrimination are more significant in the fight against poverty and disability than so called economic factors.....
In such a situation, in which people who experience impairments no longer have to face unnecessarily inhospitable environments, disability itself can be abolished.



The incremental progress made by the plan will spark a broader movement in support of disability rights

Kanter et al, 2003 (Arlene, Professor of Law at Syracuse University, September 9, http://www.ncd.gov/newsroom/publications/2003/pdf/foreign03.pdf)

The development of the full recommendations outlined in this report may be incremental.....people with disabilities that could drive the long-term development of a much larger movement in both human rights and economic development.


Simply putting in access for disabled people isn’t enough to solve, our aff is a call for change in practices and structures.


Martha McCluskey, Professor, William J. Magavern Faculty Scholar, 1988, “Rethinking Equality and Difference: Disability Discrimination in Public Transportation,” Yale Journal of Law, http://www.jstor.org/stable/pdfplus/796517.pdf, CP

In contrast to both the race and sex contexts, most courts and commentators perceive that the real physical differences related to disability are frequently relevant to the ability to function in society.....undue administrative and financial burdens" or that would require a "fundamental alteration in the nature of [a] program.
