Debate Space 1AC

This years high school policy debate resolution is The United States federal government should substantially increase its exploration and/or development of space beyond the Earths mesosphere. This topic provides us with many research incentives for teams in the area of policy making and the science of space, but it ignores the larger pedagogical implications that we find in debate as an activity. To bring a revolution to the resolution, we believe that we the debaters must substantially increase our exploration and development of our consciousness in the debate space and epistemologically grow beyond the barriers that police our voice; the mesosphere. We advocate that the space that the resolution refers to is the wrong one; rather we advocate that debate is a space where pedagogy formulates our understanding of the world in relation to our social locations as members of an oppressed community.

Whiteness has invaded the debate space and turned it into a dehumanizing, assimilating world that continues to shape future generations
Ricky Lee Allen 2004 [professor at the University of New Mexicos College of Education,Whiteness and Critical Pedagogy, Educational Philosophy and Theory, Vol. 36, No. 2, 2004]
