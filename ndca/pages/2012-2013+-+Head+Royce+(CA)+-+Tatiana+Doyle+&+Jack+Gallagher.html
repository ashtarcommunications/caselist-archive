Affirmative

The Plan: The United States federal government should substantially increase its intelligent transportation systems investment in the United States


Contention one is Economic Leadership:

First inherency,

Your links are non-unique – huge transportation funding packages are inevitable but they won’t include Intelligent Transportation Systems.
Ezell 2010(Stephen Ezell, senior analyst with the information technology and innovation foundation (itif), Explaining International IT Application Leadership: Intelligent Transportation Systems, http://www.itif.org/files/2010-1-27-ITS_Leadership.pdf, azp)

Next, the advantage –

The domestic economy is in its death throes – collapsing labor force and dwindling stimulus are bleeding the economy dry.
Nutting, 9/7(Rex Nutting, holds a master's degree in economics from The American University. MarketWatch's global commentary editor, based in Washington. Previously he was Washington bureau chief, responsible for economic and political coverage, “Economy stuck in low gear; time for Fed to act”, Market Watch, 9/7/12, http://articles.marketwatch.com/2012-09-07/commentary/33662654_1_job-growth-jobs-report-new-jobs, RM)

There are several scenarios for economic instability, the plan solves each –

Scenario one is Growth:


The US is locked in a war for talent; the plan creates the conditions for the attraction and retention of skilled labor.
Keeling and Mooney 2011 (Mary Keeling and Gerard M. Mooney, Dr. Mary Keeling is a Manager of the IBM Institute for Business Value Center for Economic Analysis. She holds a first-class honors degree in Economics and Anthropology and an M.A. in Economics and Finance from NUI Maynooth and was awarded a Ph.D. by Trinity College Dublin in 1998. Gerard M. Mooney is currently General Manager, Global Smarter Cities. Gerry serves as a member of the Board of Directors of the Intelligent Transportation Society of America He holds an MBA from Yale University, an MS in Accounting from Georgetown University and a BA in Philosophy from Mount Saint Mary’s College, “Transportation and economic development Why smarter transport is good for jobs and growth”, IBM Institute for Business Value, 2011, http://www-935.ibm.com/services/us/gbs/thoughtleadership/ibv-smarter-transport.html, RM)


Growth controls the internal link to everything – absent a positive trajectory the economy is doomed to fester in the wake of partisan politics.
Swanson 2011 (Bret Swanson, President of Entropy Economics—a research firm focused on technology and the global economy, President of Entropy Capital—a venture firm that invests in early-stage technology companies, Visiting Fellow at Digital Society, Fellow at the National Chamber Foundation, former Senior Fellow and Director of the Center for Global Innovation at The Progress & Freedom Foundation, 2011, “The Growth Imperative,” Forbes, May 27th, Available Online at http://www.forbes.com/sites/bretswanson/2011/05/27/the-growth-imperative/, Accessed 08-29-2012, azp)


Scenario two is Congestion:

Congestion is the biggest proximate cause of economic instability – ITS improves traffic flow and eliminates the need to build new roads.
Ezell 2010 (Stephen Ezell, senior analyst with the information technology and innovation foundation (itif), Explaining International IT Application Leadership: Intelligent Transportation Systems, http://www.itif.org/files/2010-1-27-ITS_Leadership.pdf, azp)


Additionally, absent the plan’s networking approach traffic accidents will create irreversible bottlenecks that supercharge the internal link.
Keeling and Mooney 2011 (Mary Keeling and Gerard M. Mooney, Dr. Mary Keeling is a Manager of the IBM Institute for Business Value Center for Economic Analysis. She holds a first-class honors degree in Economics and Anthropology and an M.A. in Economics and Finance from NUI Maynooth and was awarded a Ph.D. by Trinity College Dublin in 1998. Gerard M. Mooney is currently General Manager, Global Smarter Cities. Gerry serves as a member of the Board of Directors of the Intelligent Transportation Society of America He holds an MBA from Yale University, an MS in Accounting from Georgetown University and a BA in Philosophy from Mount Saint Mary’s College, “Transportation and economic development Why smarter transport is good for jobs and growth”, IBM Institute for Business Value, 2011, http://www-935.ibm.com/services/us/gbs/thoughtleadership/ibv-smarter-transport.html, RM)


Independently, unchecked congestion leads international manufacturers to secede from US markets.
Weisbrod and Fitzroy 2011(Glen Weisbrod has served Chair of the National Academies' TRB Committee on Transportation and Economic Development, and he previously served on the Board of Directors of the Council for Urban Economic Development. Mr. Weisbrod was also formerly Sr. Vice President of Cambridge Systematics, Inc. and Director of the Boston office of HBRS and Hagler Bailly Consulting. The author of over 30 published articles, he holds a MS in Engineering and MCP in Planning from M.I.T., and BA in Economics from Brandeis University. Stephen Fitzroy has over 30 years of experience in providing economic consulting services and program management for private and public sector clientèle. His areas of expertise include economic analysis applications for transportation planning, long range forecasting, housing, land use, and economic development. Currently Dr. Fitzroy chairs the Transportation Research Board's (TRB) Joint Subcommittee on the Economics of Pricing, and serves on the Transportation Economics, Congestion Pricing and Environmental Justice in Transportation committees.“Traffic Congestion Effects on Supply Chains: Accounting for Behavioral Elements in Planning and Economic Impact Models,” “Supply Chain Management – New Perspectives, 8/29/11 http://cdn.intechopen.com/pdfs/18515/InTech-Traffic_congestion_effects_on_supply_chains_accounting_for_behavioral_elements_in_planning_and_economic_impact_models.pdf3.7 Business location, azp)


And, these economic feedbacks are compounded – the loss of a credible model for US capitalism is catastrophic. The foreclosure of US economic balancing, and consequent power vacuum would empower a global regime of Chinese mercantilism. The impact is the destruction of interdependence, multilateralism, and a swift backslide into protectionism.
Posen 2009 (Adam, deputy director and senior fellow of the Peterson Institute for International Economics “Economic leadership beyond the crisis”, http://www.foresightproject.net/publications/readers/article.asp?p=4299, RM)


And, the plan is key now. China is thriving – they are insulated from the Eurozone crisis and are experiencing untold growth.
Hughes and Garibian 9/7 (Krista Hughes and Pablo Garibian, Hughes and Garibian are Chief Economics Correspondents for Reuters, “China fund sees economy meeting growth goal,” 9/7/12, Reuters, http://www.reuters.com/article/2012/09/08/us-funds-sovereign-china-idUSBRE88615D20120908, RM)


Credibility is the only measure of uniqueness – loss of economic resources is a slippery slope to anti-globalization and mercantilism.
Garten 2009 (Jeffrey, professor at the Yale School of Management and held economic- and foreign-policy posts in the Nixon, Ford, Carter and Clinton administrations , “The Dangers of Turning Inward”, 3/5, Wall Street Journal, http://www.business.illinois.edu/aguilera/Teaching/WSJ09_Dangers_of_Turning_Inward.pdf, RM)


A mercantilist transition will collapse the economy, intensify global hotspots, give rise to biological and nuclear terrorism, and guarantee resource and great power wars—these will escalate and cause extinction.
Panzner 2008 (Michael, faculty at the New York Institute of Finance, 25-year veteran of the global stock, bond, and currency markets who has worked in New York and London for HSBC, Soros Funds, ABN Amro, Dresdner Bank, and JPMorgan Chase, “Financial Armageddon: Protect Your Future from Economic Collapse”, Revised and Updated Edition, p. 136-138, googlebooks, RM)


Advantage two is Computational Science:

The plan is the ultimate testbed for innovation in computational science.
Winter et al. 2010 (Stephan Winter: The University of Melbourne, Australia, Monika Sester: Leibniz University Hannover, Germany, Ouri Wolfson: University of Illinois, Chicago, USA, Glenn Geers: National ICT Australia, Sydney, Australia, ACM SIGMOD Record, Volume 39 Issue 3, September 2010 , Pages 27-32, http://www.cs.uic.edu/~boxu/mp2p/39-135-1-SM.pdf, RM)


New innovations in computational science are critical now – the plan’s bottom up approach is vital to creating a data extraction backbone that can synthesize information across all sectors.
R&D 6/29 (R&D, News Agency dedicated to research and development, 6/29/12 “Study: Computing advances vital to sustainability efforts,” http://www.rdmag.com/News/2012/06/Environment-Computing-Advances-Vital-To-Sustainability-Efforts, azp)


This bottom up approach creates a network effect that ripples through other industries and creates innovation in high-tech manufacturing.
Belcher 2009 (Scott Belcher, CEO and President of Intelligent Transportation Society of America, “Congress: Invest in Intelligent Transportation Systems to create jobs”, The Hill, http://thehill.com/blogs/congress-blog/technology/70623-congress-invest-in-intelligent-transportation-systems-tocreate-jobs, 12/04/09, RM)


Nanotech development is inevitable by 2020; it’s only a question of effectiveness. The plan’s empowerment of high-tech manufacturing and computational science is key to developing safe and useful technology.
Francis 2004 (Michael Francis is a scientist at Acc’elrys a software company, The Big Picture for Nanotechnology and Computational Science, http://www.scientific-computing.com/features/feature.php?feature_id=66, azp)


Nanotech development is internationally inevitable – the US is lagging – the plan’s improved redundancy is critical to sustain a commitment that will rejuvenate the industry.
Morse 2010 (Jeffery, PhD, is the Managing Director of the National Nanomanufacturing Network, a new organization sponsored by the National Science Foundation and coordinated through the Center for Hierarchical Manufacturing, at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. Previously, Jeff has been a Senior Scientist in the Center for Micro and Nano Technology at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory since 1985. “Challenges for Sustaining Leadership in Nanotechnology in the U.S.” http://www.internano.org/content/view/441/251/, azp)


The plan’s safe nanotech prevents the worst catastrophe – lack of effective regulation triggers every impact.
Vandermolen 2006 (Thomas D., BS, Louisiana Tech University; MA, Naval War College), is officer in charge, Maritime Science and Technology Center, Yokosuka, Japan. He was previously assigned as a student at the Naval War College, Newport Naval Station, Rhode Island. http://www.airpower.maxwell.af.mil/airchronicles/apj/apj06/fal06/vandermolen.html, azp)


US sustained commitment alongside other nations creates opportunities for multilateralism and regulation.
Vandermolen 2006 (Thomas D., BS, Louisiana Tech University; MA, Naval War College), is officer in charge, Maritime Science and Technology Center, Yokosuka, Japan. He was previously assigned as a student at the Naval War College, Newport Naval Station, Rhode Island. http://www.airpower.maxwell.af.mil/airchronicles/apj/apj06/fal06/vandermolen.html, azp)


Safe nanotech development contains their impacts –

First, nanotech eliminates the motives to go to war.
McCarthy 2000 (Thomas McCarthy, Assistant Director of Graduate Admissions at Boston University’s Graduate School of Management 2000. “Molecular Technology and the World System.”http://www.mccarthy.cx/WorldSystem/intro.htm [Bapodra])


Second, nanotech makes nuclear conflict survivable.
Gubrud1997 (Mark Avrum Gubrud. Superconductivity Researcher at U of MD. 1997. “Nanotechnology and International Security.” Foresight 5th Conference Paper. http://www.csr.umd.edu/~mgubrud/nanosec1.html [Bapodra])


Federal oversight is critical to effective deployment – devolutions of authority miss the boat entirely.
Ezell 2010 (Stephen Ezell, senior analyst with the information technology and innovation foundation (itif), Explaining International IT Application Leadership: Intelligent Transportation Systems, http://www.itif.org/files/2010-1-27-ITS_Leadership.pdf, azp)


Centralized decision-making is the controlling factor– even if the plan might theoretically be possible under another agent – sustaining commitment would fall apart immediately – every empiric goes aff.
Ezell 2010 (Stephen Ezell, senior analyst with the information technology and innovation foundation (itif), Explaining International IT Application Leadership: Intelligent Transportation Systems, http://www.itif.org/files/2010-1-27-ITS_Leadership.pdf, azp)

And, even when funds are available states can’t bring together skilled labor to complete the project.
GAO2012 [March 2012, US Government Accountability Office, “INTELLIGENT TRANSPORTATION SYSTEMS Improved DOT Collaboration and Communication Could Enhance the Use of Technology to Manage Congestion”, http://www.gao.gov/assets/590/589430.pdf, azp)
