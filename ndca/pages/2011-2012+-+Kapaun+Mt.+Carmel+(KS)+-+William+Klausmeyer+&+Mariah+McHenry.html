Plan: The United States federal government should deploy ground-based lasers to remediate space debris. Funding and enforcement guaranteed through normal means. Aff speeches will clarify intent.

Adv 1: Space Access

There is a desire to settle space and efforts are being made.
Joshua**Dalzelle**, engineer in the aerospace industry with over 10 years of experience and currently works for a leading defense contractor. earned degrees in both aeronautics and avionics, served in the Unites States Air Force, 8/ 2, 2011. “Private firms eye future of manned space exploration.” Examiner. Accessed on August 2, 2001. <http://www.examiner.com/aviation-and-aerospace-in-cincinnati/private-firms-eye-future-of-manned-space-exploration>

Unchecked space debris causes a cascade effect – we’re already at critical mass and future collisions are inevitable – makes space travel and colonization impossible

Imburgia, ’11 [Lieutenant Colonel Joseph S. Imburgia, “Space Debris and Its Threat to National Security: A Proposal for a Binding International Agreement to Clean Up the Junk”, April 4, Vanderbilt Journal of Transnational Law Vol. 44:589, (accessed June 11, 2011), CMR]

WE MUST ACT NOW. EVEN WITH NO NEW LAUNCHES, DEBRIS WILL CONTINUALLY GROW

Charles Q. Choi, SPACE.com Contributor, March 17, 2011, “Earth-based Lasers Could Zap Space Junk Clear From Satellites,” http://www.space.com/11157-nasa-lasers-shooting-space-junk.html, ACC. 5-31-11, JT//JEDI

Space is key to survival --- multiple planets, terraforming, and biodomes prove it’s feasible.

Falconer, 6/26. Joel, “What Would Colonization of the Final Frontier Look Like?”, http://thenextweb.com/industry/2011/06/26/what-would-colonization-of-the-final-frontier-look-like/, CMR

Robert Shapiro, Professor Emeritus and Senior Research Scientist in the Chemistry Department of New York University, March 19, 2007, “Why the Moon? Human survival!,” The Space Review, http://www.thespacereview.com/article/832/1

We control magnitude – asteroid collision is inevitable and 50 times worse than the worst scale of nuclear war

Lewis ’96 [John S, PhD at the University of Arizona, “Mining the sky: Untold Riches from the Asteroids, Comets, and Planets]

Burrows, ‘7 [William E. Burrows, M.A. is cofounder of the Alliance to Rescue Civilization, “The Survival Imperative: Using Space to Protect Earth”, page number, CMR]
