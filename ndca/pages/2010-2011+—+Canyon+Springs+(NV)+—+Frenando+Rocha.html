Affirmative

I affirm the resolution.

The resolution implies legalization of drugs since:
1. The resolution refers to illegal drugs to differentiate them from pharmacological drugs. So debating on the status of the drugs whether they are illegal or legal is irrelevant.
2. The affirmative is still topical if the issue is treated as a matter of public health by legalizing it. Legalization can be applied to illegal drugs as long as the sentence "illegal drugs ought to be legalized" makes logical sense legalization is topical.
3. The affirmative position is to separate the connection between the abuse of illegal drugs and criminal justice and replacing that connection with a greater emphasis on the abuse as a public health issue.

Negative

I negate the resolution

Value: Morality

Negative achieves morality, defined by the Encarta English Dictionary as, “accepted moral standards: standards of conduct that are generally accepted as right or proper”. A moral society strives to achieve the greatest good for the greatest number of people.

Criterion: Enhancement of Human Life

If human life has no value, then there is no reason for morality as there is no reason to treat other humans in any particular way if their lives have no value. Illicit drugs have a destructive effect upon the lives of those who abuse them. They devalue human life, thus the goal of any drug policy must be to reduce illegal drug use as this would reduce the lives devalued by drugs.

First, the Counter-Plan: Governments should increase the funding for drug rehabilitation programs within prisons and jails.

Contention 1: Competition

The NEGATIVE is mutually exclusive
The negative and affirmative plan cannot exist at the same time because;

Criminal Justice measures will still be implemented at some point, thus violating the Affirmative’s advocacy of the resolution.
