Observation Three – Our Response

Transportation policies, imaginations of this topic, intrinsically produce modes of homeless dwelling. Transportation policy, which, for the “homed” is a policy of convenience, speed, efficiency and progress, is simultaneously a “dwelling policy” for the homeless.
Some make a comfortable home for themselves in both mind and body by purifying their environment, eliminating unwanted thoughts from creeping in, building four walls and a sturdy roof, and by organizing their lives such that interaction with “undesirables” is kept to a minimum. The transportation system is one of the few ways that this comfortable sense of home is disrupted by figures of the homeless who make transportation infrastructure their place of dwelling. Rather than confront this reality, those desiring home, avert their eyes, wait for the light to turn green, and drive on.
The topic itself is a kind of conceptual transportation infrastructure. The topic is not this or that thing, rather it is a crossing place, a “crossroads,” for multiple imaginations and ways to affirm/negate. When we say the topic is only one thing we close ourselves off to points of difference and try to construct the topic as a “home.” But the topic is not a home; it is a site for multiple forms of dwelling. Homelessness is the heart of this topic both in form and content. Refusal to confront this performs the invisibility that renders the homeless figure as an absolute other of our society. Thus we affirm the topic as a site of multiple forms of dwelling and as an opportunity to come to terms with homelessness. Thus, Resolved: The United States federal government should substantially increase its transportation infrastructure investment in the United States.

Out method and advocacy is based in nomadism and is the only approach that solves – other strategies reach a “home” or a redemptive endpoint, while ours continually acts to prevent injustice
Ilan Gur-Ze’ev 2005 – professor at the University of Haifa ADORNO AND HORKHEIMER: DIASPORIC PHILOSOPHY, NEGATIVE THEOLOGY, AND COUNTER-EDUCATION)

The affirmation of the bus as home is a form of pluralization that disrupts the political order of the status quo. Voting affirmatives establish an ethic of responsiveness that respects the homeless.
