OBSERVATION ONE IS FRAMEWORK:

V: Morality

Before we can adopt a moral framework, we must first determine the meta-ethical nature of morality itself. Without answering questions about where morality comes from, there is no way to ever determine what is moral and immoral. Meta-ethics literally determines what counts as a warrant for a normative theory. Morality is a social force that exists to guide decision-making. It is not a descriptive system of truth and falsity. As such, all moral statements prescribe action. Moral statements do not describe reality. Even if phrased as apparently truth functional statements, moral propositions are ultimately meaningful as action guiding prescriptions.
