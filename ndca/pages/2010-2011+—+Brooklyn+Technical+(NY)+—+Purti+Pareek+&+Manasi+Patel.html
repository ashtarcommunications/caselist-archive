LISTEN CLOSELY, IT'S NOT GOING TO CHANGE: THE FRAMEWORK FOR THIS ROUND IS WHOSE ADVOCACY BEST CHALLENGES SEXISM.

NARRATIVE

In ancient India, Women and men were viewed. They had access to the same privileges in society. It was until western imperialism when things started to change. Women were seen as a means to an end and patriarchal oppression became justifiable. What are we? Two Indian women living in the US because it offers more opportunities and freedom. However, after being exposed to the real world in the US, we realized that this is all a facade. No matter where we live, we will always be looked by men as some creature, not a human, not a woman. Our bodies are perceived as a an commodity, to be ravaged and exploited in order to preserve an order that looks to us as property. Definitely not equality. Being a woman just gives the man the right to look at me like that. When a man has sex every night with different women he is called a Pimp, applauded. But now if a woman does the same she is called the worst names possible, and degraded to the lowest level of the social ladder.

The status quo prefers that we are to be complacent to the wrongs that are done around us. I can walk down the street it will be perfectly fine to see a man call a woman a bitch or a whore for no reason at all. Whether you’re African American Asian pacific islander Hispanic or Hindustani, if you’re a woman, you’re always inferior. I see the looks I get and how my partner and I are talked about by certain people just because we are two brown girls raging against the machine. And then we walk into a debate round and there’s some people trying to shut our voices?! WRONG.


Part 1: the truth untold of militarism

our current military presences establishes the united states as the arbiters of the free world, protecting the freedoms of all. the military preaches the ideology of patriotism and freedom, being “all that we can be”, but for whom? the freedoms and liberties that are protected are all under the assumptions that freedom and liberty is accessible to all. but that is all a lie. all over the world, women are exploited, including women of the united states military itself. they are threatened, sexually harassed, and raped by male superiors as masculinity is an internal battle that exists, masking the foundational violence.

embedded into the current status of military culture inclucates hyper-masculinity. the normative element to hyper masculinity relies on the image of toughness and dominance. those that fail to meet the criterion are shunned, creating the status of docility.

militarism and masculinity are inherently interwined. the military state reflects their dominance by establishing the ideal image of a solidier, in the realms of military presence.

callahan, 2004 [professor of international relations and director of the center of chines studies at the university of durham]

Nation-states and sovereignty emerged in East Asia as part of the reversals of imperialism from Chinese empire to Euro-American-Japanese empire. Theory did not emerge solely out of administrative imperialism: the spread of "civilization," the conceptual cultural imperialism of "diplomacy," the "family of nations," and now the "international community" (see Gong 1984; Keene Zoor).

Thus, though it is common to frame "Communist China" and illiberal Southeast Asian regimes in terms of human rights violations, this book will argue that power also works in productive and multiple ways in Greater China, and will show how the state, the family, and the market work through regimes of power/knowledge. Cultural governance and biopower regulate not just the individual but other spaces: kinship networks, public and private schools, the capitalist workplace, the nation-state, and transnational networks
