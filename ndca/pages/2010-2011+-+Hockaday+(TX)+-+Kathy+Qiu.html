Possess is defined as to own or to gain ownership of. [Oxford English Dictionary, OED, “possess,” pg 1, Oxford English Dictionary, June 2010] “To own ... hold as property.”

Ought is defined as moral obligation. It is the most frequent use throughout. [Oxford English Dictionary, OED, “ought,” pg 1, Oxford English Dictionary, 2009, http://dictionary.oed.com/cgi/entry/00335248?query_type=word&queryword=ought&first=1&max_to_show=10 &sort_type=alpha&result_place=3&search_id=EIlK-nRvYnR-2745&hilite=00335248] “bound or under moral obligation.” And “The most frequent use throughout.”

Value is Morality

Morality respects individuals equally. [Kant, Immanuel. German Philosopher. Fundamental Principles of the Metaphysics of Morals. Google Books. Pg 43-45. 2007] “For all rational beings … as an end in itself.”


Moral actions respect human dignity. Kant, Immanuel. German Philosopher. Fundamental Principles of the Metaphysics of Morals. Google Books. Pg 43-45. 2007] “Reason then refers every … have the desired effect.”


The conception of using one for another ultimately destroys the inherent human worth of individuals. [Germain Grisez and Russell Shaw. Flynn Professor of Christian Ethics at Mount Saint Mary’s College and Columnist for Catholic Herald. “Absolutism and its Consequentialist Critics” ed. Joram Graf Haber. 1994. Pg. 25-27] “If there are no ethical … weighing, and balancing.”

The standard is treating people as ends in themselves.

Human dignity demands that we regard each individual as inviolable. [Kamm, Frances. Philosophy Professor at Harvard. “Intricate Ethics: Rights, Responsibilities, and Permissible Harm. Google Books. Pg. 28. 2007] “Consequently, I advocate … people in this way.”

Contention 1: Deterrence is an inevitable immoral use from possessing nuclear weapons. Nuclear weapons have existential deterrence. [Freedman, Lawrence; Bundy, McGeorge. Professor of War Studies at King’s College, London; Professor of Government at Harvard University. “I Exist; Therefore, I Deter”. International Security Vol. 13 No. 1. JSTOR. Pg. 184. 1998] “This argues for a … called passive deterrence.”

Nuclear deterrence uses foreign citizens as tools to change decisions of a few leaders. [Churchill, Robert. Associate Professor of Philosophy at George Washington University. “Nuclear Arms as a Philosophical and Moral Issue”. Annals of the American Academy of Political and Social Science Vol. 469 Nuclear Armament and Disarmament. SAGE JSTOR. Pg. 46-57. 1983] “Among the principles … the drivers of automobiles.”

Contention 2: Nuclear testing is an immoral side effect that results from possession of nuclear weapons. Testing is needed to maintain a nuclear arsenal. [Medalia, Jonathan. Specialist in National Defense, Foreign Affairs, Defense, and Trade Division. “Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty: Pro and Con”. Congressional Research Service Report for Congress. Pg 3. 2005] “The President’s National Security … people who maintain them.”
BUT Nuclear testing treats people as guinea pigs. [Barkas, Jessica. Knauss Marine Policy Fellow with the National Oceanographic and Atmospheric Administration. “Testing Bomb and Indigenous People”. University of Baltimore Journal of Environmental Law. LexisNexis. Pg. 6. 2005] “The United States used its careless …to move them - it refused.”

﻿NC Moral Permissibility Burden


Observation 1: Use is distinct from possession because possess is defined as to have as property. [Random House Dictionary. “possess”. www.dictionary.com. Pg 1. 2010] “to have as belonging to one; have as property. ”

‘Ought not’ means a prohibition. [Richard Robinson. Philosophy Professor at Oriel College Oxford. “Ought and Ought Not.” Philosophy, Vol. 46, No. 177 (Jul., 1971), pp. 193-202. Cambridge University Press on behalf of Royal Institute of Philosophy. http://www.jstor.org] “The moral ought … they can both be false.”

Morally permissible means not prohibited. [Shelly Kagan. Professor of Philosophy at Yale University. The Limits of Morality (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1989), pp. 65-66] “For a given reaction … sufficient to ground a prohibition.”

NC burden is to prove that possession of nuclear weapons is morally permissible.

1. Possession of an object does not entail any moral evaluation. An object is morally neutral because its moral state changes depending on how it was intended to be used or the consequences it produced. [Lewis, Julian. Shadow Defence Minister for the Royal Navy, the nuclear deterrent and strategic issues; Conservative MP for New Forest East; “Nuclear Disarmament Versus Peace In the 21st Century”. RUSI Journal. Pg. 1April 2006; This article is the 2005 winner of the Trench Gascoigne Essay Prize competition awarded by Royal United Services Institute for Defence and Security Studies.] “The dawning of the atomic … to which they were put.”

2. States are not moral actors. [George Kennan, Professor Emeritus at the Institute for Advanced Study at Princeton, Foreign Affairs Vol. 64, No. 2, “Morality and Foreign Policy”, pg. 207, JSTOR 1985, http://www.jstor.org/stable/20042569] “Second, let us recognize … the basis of them.”


﻿NC Self Defense

Ought is defined as expressing obligation on part of an agent who is contextually implied. Prefer this definition because it is contextual. [Oxford English Dictionary, OED, “ought”, pg 1, Oxford English Dictionary, 2010, http://dictionary.oed.com/cgi/entry/00335248?query_type=word&queryword=ought&first=1&max_to_show=10&sort_type=alpha&result_place=3&search_id=Fz1g-fiMMKO-11913&hilite=00335248] “Expressing duty or obligation … but contextually implied”

Value is governmental legitimacy.

Governmental legitimacy is only concerned with protecting the government’s citizens. [George Kennan, Professor Emeritus at the Institute for Advanced Study at Princeton, Foreign Affairs Vol. 64, No. 2, “Morality and Foreign Policy”, pg. 207, JSTOR 1985, http://www.jstor.org/stable/20042569] “Second, let us recognize … acting on the basis of them.”

The standard is maintaining self defense.

States must maintain their ability of self defense in the international arena. [Mearsheimer, John. Professor and Chair of the Department of Political Science at the University of Chicago. “Back to the Future: Instability in Europe after the Cold War.” International Security Vol. 15, No. 1. Pg. 5-56. 1990 JSTOR] “Conflict is common … the expense of rivals.”

Contention 1: Possession of nuclear weapons is an act of self defense. Disarming completely places states at incredible risk. [Berkowitz, Bruce. Department of Political Science University of Minnesota. “Proliferation, Deterrence, and the Likelihood of Nuclear War.” The Journal of Conflict Resolution, Vol. 29, No. 1. Pg.112-136. 1985 JSTOR] “Furthermore, as several writers … nuclear powers is unlikely.”
