Affirmative



SOAL AC


V- Justice
The respect principle passes a number of meta-ethical tests.
Regan, 83. Tom Regan. The Case for Animal Rights. University of California Press: Berkeley and Los Angeles, CA. ISBN: 978-520-24386-6. Pp. 259-260
“One wants more…accepting any principle. “
First, moral patients and moral agents both have equal inherent value:
Regan, 83. Tom Regan. The Case for Animal Rights. University of California Press: Berkeley and Los Angeles, CA. ISBN: 978-520-24386-6. Pp. 240-241
“Some might concede … come in degrees. “
Second, even if moral patients are less important than moral agents, this does not deny a direct duty to moral patients:
Regan, 83. Tom Regan. The Case for Animal Rights. University of California Press: Berkeley and Los Angeles, CA. ISBN: 978-520-24386-6. Pp. 188.
“In reply it … fails to recognize.”
Unlike moral agency, life, etc. the subject-of-a-life criterion provides a non-arbitrary standard for assigning moral worth:
Regan, 83. Tom Regan. The Case for Animal Rights. University of California Press: Berkeley and Los Angeles, CA. ISBN: 978-520-24386-6. Pp. 244-245
“The subject-of-a-life criterion … intelligible and nonarbitrary.“
VC- Subjects-of-a-life
My sole contention is that animals are subjects-of-a-life.
First, evolutionary theory suggests that animals are subjects of a life because of their similarity to humans physiologically.
Regan, 83. Tom Regan. The Case for Animal Rights. University of California Press: Berkeley and Los Angeles, CA. ISBN: 978-520-24386-6. Pp. 26
A third point … in principle mysterious.
Second, Regan provides the Cumulative argument in favor of animal consciousness:
Regan, 83. Tom Regan. The Case for Animal Rights. University of California Press: Berkeley and Los Angeles, CA. ISBN: 978-520-24386-6. Pp. 27-28
There is, then… than human beings.
Finally, the burden of proof lies on those that deny animals are subjects-of-a-life.
Regan, 83. Tom Regan. The Case for Animal Rights. University of California Press: Berkeley and Los Angeles, CA. ISBN: 978-520-24386-6. Pp. 34-35
The Cumulative Argument …. that they do.
