Affirmative 1

My advocacy is a literary analysis of nuclear language.
P1: The Framework
Ought-->moral obligation
The resolution is a moral statement that needs to be understood so our project is to understand what morality means.
We understand concepts through language. Roland Bleiker, “Popular dissent, human agency, and global politics.” Cambridge University Press (2000), 135. “It is within…acquired over time.”
The standard is rejecting language that makes understanding morality impossible.
P2: The Problem
1.
The first problem is that nuclear language isn’t objective to fully understand the effects of nuclear weapons. Carol Cohn, “Sex and Death in the Rational World of Defense Intellectuals.” Signs. University of Chicago Press (1987), 691. “ ‘Clean bombs’ are…targets show factories.”
Cohn 2, “Sex and Death in the Rational World of Defense Intellectuals.” Signs. University of Chicago Press (1987), 704. “A more subtle…of nuclear war”
http://people.reed.edu/~ahm/Courses/Stan-PS-314-2009-Q1_PNP/Syllabus/EReadings/Cohn1987Sex-and-Death.pdf
Thus the metaphors distort the language and hinder understanding of morality.
2.
The second problem is that it explores worlds of thought.
Cohn 3, “Sex and Death in the Rational World of Defense Intellectuals.” Signs. University of Chicago Press (1987), 708. “I found, however…be taken seriously.”
Morality requires moral contention, so when people’s thoughts are excluded we cannot understand it.
3.
The third problem is that it makes the subject the weapon rather than the human being.
Cohn 4, “Sex and Death in the Rational World of Defense Intellectuals.” Signs. University of Chicago Press (1987), 711-712. “The fact that…remains hermetically sealed.”
Cohn 5, “Sex and Death in the Rational World of Defense Intellectuals.” Signs. University of Chicago Press (1987), 712. “Finally, the third…become more incomprehensible.”
Morality is the distinction between right and wrong conduct of human beings. A language that eliminates human considerations, thus makes understanding morality impossible.
P3: The Decision Calculus
The language derives from the possession. Cohn 6, “Sex and Death in the Rational World of Defense Intellectuals.” Signs. University of Chicago Press (1987), 706-707. “Finally, then, I…threats to it.”
Post-Fiat Version:
As long as we are still beholden to them, nuclear weapons will control and define our understanding of morality. Thus, we must dispossess them to move towards understanding morality.
Pre-Fiat Version:
The only way to liberate discourse is to reconceptualize our interpretation of morality. Signing the ballot for the affirmative gives access to then debating what actions states ought to take.

Ought-->moral obligation

a. dictionaries reflect common usage which is key to predictability because it’s a check on differences in topic lit.
b. It is the first definition in the OED which implies it is the most commonly used.
